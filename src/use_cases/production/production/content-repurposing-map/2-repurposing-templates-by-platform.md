## Twitter Thread Template

### Structure (7 tweets maximum)

**Tweet 1: The Hook**
```

[Main thesis in one sentence]

[Thread 🧵]
```

**Tweet 2-4: Supporting Points**
```
[Point 1]

[Explanation or example]

[1/5]
```

**Tweet 5: Case Study or Example**
```
[Real example that proves the point]

[Specific results or outcome]

[Proof it works]
```

**Tweet 6: Action Step**
```
[One thing to do today]

[Practical application]

[Make it actionable]
```

**Tweet 7: CTA**
```
[Summarize main insight]

[Question to engage]

[Link to full content]
```

### Platform Best Practices:
- **Optimal timing:** 8-10am, 12-1pm, 5-6pm (your audience's time zone)
- **Character limit:** 280 per tweet (draft shorter, edit longer)
- **Visuals:** Add relevant images to tweets 1, 4, 7
- **Engagement:** Reply to every comment in first hour
- **Hashtags:** 1-2 max, placed in last tweet

## LinkedIn Post Template

### Structure (400 words max)

**Opening Hook (first 2 lines critical)**
```
[Counter-intuitive statement or surprising stat]

[Or: Relatable pain point]

[Or: Bold claim]
```

**Body Paragraphs (3-5)**
```
[Paragraph 1: Set context, explain why it matters]

[Paragraph 2: Framework or insight]

[Paragraph 3: Real example or case study]

[Paragraph 4: Practical application]
```

**Engagement Element**
```
[Question to prompt comments]

[Or: Ask for experience]

[Or: Request perspective]
```

**Soft CTA (end)**
```
[Subtle mention of full resource]

[Or: Invitation to connect]

[No aggressive selling]
```

### Filled Example (Core: Same time-blocking content)

**Most productivity advice is wrong.**

It tells you to optimize for doing more. Faster. Better. Harder.

Here's the problem: That approach assumes you're a machine.

You're not.

The most productive people I know don't focus on doing more. They focus on doing what matters.

Here's the framework:

**The Night Before**

Every evening, write down your top 3 priorities for tomorrow.

Not 10. Not 5. Three.

Why? Because if everything is important, nothing is. When you have 10 priorities, you have zero priorities.

When you have 3, you have focus.

**Block Your Peak Hours**

Assign your #1 priority to your peak energy hours.

For most people, this is 9-11am.

Protect this time like it's a meeting with your most important client. Because it is—your most important client is your future self.

**Batch Everything Else**

Emails? Do them all at 11am and 4pm.

Meetings? Cluster them on Tuesdays and Thursdays.

Deep work? Monday, Wednesday, Friday mornings.

Context switching kills productivity. Every interruption costs 23 minutes of focus.

**The Results**

I used to respond to emails all day. Result: Constant interruptions, fractured focus, 2.5 hours of deep work daily.

Now: Email at 11am and 4pm only. Result: 47% more deep work time, same email responsiveness.

This simple system added 10 hours to my week.

Not by working longer. By working smarter.

What time of day are you most productive?

Tell me below, and I'll share a template you can use tonight.

### Platform Best Practices:
- **Length:** 200-400 words optimal (1,000-1,500 characters)
- **Formatting:** Use line breaks, avoid walls of text
- **Media:** Add image or PDF (uploaded natively, not link)
- **Engagement:** Reply to every comment within 1 hour
- **Timing:** 8-10am, 12-1pm, 5-6pm (your audience's timezone)
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 max, relevant to content + industry
- **External links:** Only in comments, not post body
