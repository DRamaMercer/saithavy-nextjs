**Your presence is your primary leadership tool**

## Overview

This quarterly review transforms your decision journal from a tracking tool into a learning system. By systematically reviewing your decisions, you'll identify patterns, recognize blind spots, and continuously improve your judgment.

**Time commitment:** 60-90 minutes per quarter
**Best practice:** Schedule during a quiet week, not during crunch time

## Sample Review Summary

**Quarter:** Q1 2025
**Decisions reviewed:** 28

### Key Findings
- **Best decisions:** Made when calm and well-rested (85% success rate)
- **Worst decisions:** Made under time pressure (40% success rate)
- **Confidence accuracy:** Overconfident in people decisions, underconfident in strategic decisions
- **Values alignment:** 85% overall - struggled with "transparency" vs "protecting the team"

### What Worked
1. Sleeping on big decisions before finalizing
2. Seeking dissenting opinions before deciding
3. Writing down the expected outcome - forced clarity

### What Didn't
1. Deciding during afternoon energy slumps (2-4 PM)
2. Skipping the "options considered" step when feeling confident
3. Not revisiting decisions after new information emerged

### Three Insights
1. I consistently underestimate emotional impact on people decisions
2. My strategic intuition is better than I give myself credit for
3. I need to build in more time pressure buffers for important decisions

### Next Quarter Commitments
1. No major decisions between 2-4 PM without 30-minute reflection
2. Always write 3 options, even if I think I know the answer
3. Schedule decision review sessions 2 weeks after major choices
