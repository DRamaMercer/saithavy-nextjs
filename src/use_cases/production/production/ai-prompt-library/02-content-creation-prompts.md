# Content Creation Prompts Collection

**Innovation isn't about new tools—it's about better questions**

## Overview

This collection contains tested prompts for content creation across blogs, newsletters, social media, videos, podcasts, and email marketing. Each prompt has been validated to produce engaging, audience-resonant content.

## 1. Blog Post Ideation

### Purpose
Generate compelling blog post ideas that align with your audience's interests, your expertise, and current trends.

### Prompt Template
```
I need blog post ideas for [BLOG/AUDIENCE].

**Context:**
- My blog/expertise area: [TOPIC]
- Target audience: [WHO THEY ARE, EXPERIENCE LEVEL]
- Audience's biggest challenges: [PAIN POINTS]
- My unique perspective/angle: [WHAT MAKES ME DIFFERENT]
- Content goals: [TRAFFIC/LEADS/THOUGHT LEADERSHIP/ENGAGEMENT]
- My best-performing posts (topics, not titles): [EXAMPLES]

**I'm Looking For:**
- Number of ideas: [QUANTITY]
- Post format preference: [HOW-TO/LISTICLE/OPINION/CASE STUDY/COMPARISON/ANY]
- SEO focus: [KEYWORDS IF ANY]
- Current trends/seasonal: [ANY RELEVANT TRENDS]
- Content depth: [QUICK READS/COMPREHENSIVE GUIDES/MIX]

**What I Don't Want:**
- [TOPICS/ANGLES TO AVOID]

Please generate [NUMBER] blog post ideas with:
1. **Working Title** (compelling, clear, click-worthy but not clickbait)
2. **Target Keyword** (primary SEO term)
3. **Content Angle** (what makes this interesting, not generic)
4. **Brief Outline** (3-5 key points to cover)
5. **Audience Hook** (why they should care, what problem this solves)
6. **Unique Value** (what makes this worth reading vs. other content on this topic)
7. **Content Format** (how-to, listicle, opinion, case study, etc.)

**Prioritize ideas that:**
- Address real audience pain points
- Have clear search demand
- Leverage my unique perspective
- Are evergreen (not tied to a specific moment)
- I'm actually qualified to write well
```

### Example Usage
```
I need blog post ideas for a productivity blog.

**Context:**
- My blog/expertise area: Personal productivity for knowledge workers
- Target audience: Knowledge workers (software engineers, designers, marketers) with 3-10 years experience
- Audience's biggest challenges: Meeting fatigue, context switching, deep work scarcity, burnout
- My unique perspective: Former PM at a tech company, diagnosed with ADHD, approach productivity from a neurodivergent perspective
- Content goals: Build email list, establish thought leadership
- My best-performing posts: "The meeting-free day experiment," "How I manage my ADHD without medication," "Deep work rituals from remote workers"

**I'm Looking For:**
- Number of ideas: 10
- Post format preference: Mix of how-to, case study, and opinion pieces
- SEO focus: Productivity, remote work, ADHD, time management
- Current trends: Return-to-office mandates, AI productivity tools
- Content depth: Mix, but lean toward comprehensive guides

**What I Don't Want:**
- Generic "productivity hacks" that don't acknowledge neurodiversity
- Content that shames people for struggling
- Overly simplistic advice that doesn't address systemic issues

Please generate 10 blog post ideas with: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- 10 detailed post ideas, each with working title, keyword, angle, outline, hook, unique value, and format
- Ideas that balance search demand with originality
- Mix of content formats (how-to, case study, opinion, etc.)
- Alignment with your unique perspective and expertise
- Clear differentiation from existing content

### Customization Tips
- **For SEO-focused blogs**: Add search volume tools (Ahrefs, SEMrush) data
- **For thought leadership**: Emphasize opinion pieces and contrarian takes
- **For lead generation**: Focus on problems that your product/service solves
- **For niche audiences**: Add specific subculture/industry language and references

### Limitations
- **No real-time search data** - Cannot provide current search volumes or keyword difficulty
- **Limited competitive analysis** - Cannot scan existing content to ensure differentiation
- **Trend timing** - May not know if a topic is currently over-saturated
- **Audience validation** - Ideas seem good but only your audience can confirm what resonates

## 2. Newsletter Drafting

### Purpose
Create engaging newsletters that subscribers actually want to read, open, and forward to others.

### Prompt Template
```
I need to write a newsletter edition about [TOPIC/THEME].

**Newsletter Context:**
- Newsletter name/focus: [BRAND/TOPIC]
- Subscriber count: [SIZE]
- Audience: [WHO THEY ARE, WHY THEY SUBSCRIBED]
- Typical open rate: [PERCENTAGE]
- Send frequency: [WEEKLY/BI-WEEKLY/MONTHLY]
- My newsletter's unique value: [WHAT SUBSCRIBERS CAN'T GET ELSEWHERE]

**This Edition:**
- Main topic: [WHAT THIS EDITION IS ABOUT]
- Why this topic now: [TIMELINESS/RELEVANCE]
- Goal: [EDUCATE/ENTERTAIN/INSPIRE/PROMOTE]
- Secondary goals: [OTHER OUTCOMES]
- Tone preference: [PROFESSIONAL/CASUAL/INSPIRING/CONTROVERSIAL]
- Length target: [WORD COUNT OR READING TIME]
- Call to action (if any): [WHAT YOU WANT THEM TO DO]

**Key Content I Want to Include:**
- Main point 1: [POINT]
- Main point 2: [POINT]
- Main point 3: [POINT]
- Personal story/example (optional): [STORY]
- Resource/tool recommendation: [RECOMMENDATION]

**Subject Line Options:**
I need [NUMBER] subject line options that are:
- Compeling but not clickbait
- Clear about what's inside
- Personal and authentic
- Testable (I'll A/B test them)

**My Challenges:**
- [WHAT'S HARD ABOUT WRITING THIS - E.G., "I struggle with openings" or "I tend to ramble"]

Please help me by:

1. **Subject Lines**: Generate [NUMBER] subject line options with different angles (curiosity, benefit, urgency, personal)

2. **Opening Hook**: Write 3 opening options that hook readers immediately (question, story, statistic, provocative statement)

3. **Body Structure**: Organize my key points into a flow that keeps readers engaged. Consider:
   - Scannable sections (subheads, bullet points)
   - Pacing (when to go deep, when to keep it light)
   - Transitions between points

4. **Content Draft**: Draft the full newsletter body incorporating my key points, optimized for readability (short paragraphs, clear language, varied sentence structure)

5. **Voice Check**: Ensure this sounds like [ME/MY BRAND], not generic AI. I'm [DESCRIBE YOUR VOICE - e.g., "wry, self-deprecating, practical, slightly irreverent"]

6. **P.S. Section**: Write a P.S. that reinforces the main point or adds value (stats show many readers scan to the P.S.)

7. **Call to Action**: If I have a CTA, how do I make it compelling without being salesy?

8. **Subject Line Testing**: Which subject line do you think will perform best? Why?
```

### Example Usage
```
I need to write a newsletter edition about "Why productivity advice fails for neurodivergent people."

**Newsletter Context:**
- Newsletter name: "Productivity for the Rest of Us" (3,200 subscribers)
- Audience: Knowledge workers, 60% diagnosed with ADHD/autism/dyslexia, tired of generic productivity advice
- Typical open rate: 42% (above industry average)
- Send frequency: Weekly
- My unique value: I write about productivity from a neurodivergent perspective, no "just try harder" advice

**This Edition:**
- Main topic: Why most productivity advice is designed for neurotypical brains and fails for the rest of us
- Why now: I've gotten 50+ emails from readers saying "nothing works for me"
- Goal: Validate their experience, offer actually useful alternatives
- Secondary goals: Drive traffic to my course on neurodivergent productivity systems
- Tone: Empathetic, slightly frustrated with the industry, practical, hopeful
- Length: 800 words (5-minute read)
- CTA: "If you're tired of advice that doesn't work, my course might help. But no pressure - read this first."

**Key Content:**
- Point 1: Most productivity advice assumes executive function works consistently (it doesn't for us)
- Point 2: "Willpower" and "discipline" are harmful concepts for neurodivergent people
- Point 3: What actually works: systems, tools, medications, accommodations - not "trying harder"
- Personal story: How I blamed myself for years until my ADHD diagnosis at 32
- Resource: "How to ADHD" YouTube channel

**Subject Line Options:**
I need 5 subject lines.

**My Challenges:**
- I tend to get too ranty and negative; need to balance frustration with hope
- Openings are hard for me - I either start too slow or jump in too fast

Please help me by: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- 5 subject lines with different psychological angles
- 3 distinct opening hooks (question, story, provocative statement)
- Structured body with scannable sections and smooth transitions
- Full 800-word draft in your authentic voice
- P.S. section that reinforces value
- Compelling, non-salesy CTA
- Recommendation on best subject line with rationale

### Customization Tips
- **For curated newsletters**: Add "links to include" section with annotation prompts
- **For product updates**: Add "feature explanation" and "customer benefit" sections
- **For personal newsletters**: Emphasize storytelling and vulnerability
- **For B2B newsletters**: Add "business value" and "actionable takeaway" focus

### Limitations
- **No access to your analytics** - Cannot use your past performance data to optimize
- **Voice approximation** - Takes a few iterations to truly capture your unique voice
- **Audience feedback loop** - Only your subscribers can tell you what resonates
- **Platform constraints** - Email clients render differently; HTML not considered

## 3. Social Media Content

### Purpose
Create scroll-stopping social media content that drives engagement, builds community, and aligns with platform best practices.

### Prompt Template
```
I need social media content for [PLATFORM].

**Account Context:**
- Platform: [INSTAGRAM/TIKTOK/LINKEDIN/TWITTER/X/THREADS/YOUTUBE SHORTS]
- Niche/expertise: [TOPIC]
- Follower count: [SIZE]
- Account vibe: [PROFESSIONAL/CASUAL/EDUCATIONAL/ENTERTAINING/INSPIRING]
- My content pillars: [TOPIC 1, TOPIC 2, TOPIC 3]
- Best-performing content types: [FORMATS THAT WORK]
- Growth goals: [FOLLOWERS/ENGAGEMENT/COMMUNITY/CONVERSIONS]

**Content Request:**
- Topic: [WHAT THIS POST IS ABOUT]
- Content format: [IMAGE/CAROUSEL/VIDEO/TEXT/THREAD]
- Goal: [ENGAGEMENT/FOLLOWS/TRAFFIC/SALES/AWARENESS]
- Call to action: [WHAT YOU WANT THEM TO DO]
- Posting time: [IF THERE'S URGENCY OR TIMING]
- Link in bio destination (if applicable): [WHERE YOU'RE SENDING THEM]

**Key Message:**
- Main point I want to make: [CORE MESSAGE]
- Why this matters to my audience: [RELEVANCE]
- Something unique or surprising: [HOOK]

**Visual/Format Details:**
- For image posts: Describe visual concept or describe what you have
- For carousels: Number of slides and key points per slide
- For video: Length limit, style (talking head, b-roll, screen recording)
- For text: Character limit, hashtag strategy

**My Brand Voice:**
- [3-5 ADJECTIVES - E.G., "Witty, slightly irreverent, practical, no-fluff"]
- Things I never say/do: [BOUNDARIES]

**Engagement Strategy:**
- Question for comments: [PROMPT TO START CONVERSATION]
- How I'll respond: [MY COMMUNITY MANAGEMENT STYLE]

Please help me:

1. **Hook**: Write 5 hook options (first line/3 seconds) that stop the scroll

2. **Content Body**: Create the full post optimized for this platform:
   - Platform-appropriate length and format
   - Native to the platform (doesn't feel reposted from elsewhere)
   - Optimized for retention (keeps them watching/reading)

3. **Visual Direction**: For visual posts, describe what should be on-screen:
   - Image: Composition, text overlays, color scheme
   - Carousel: Slide-by-slide breakdown with visuals + text
   - Video: Shot list, text overlays, pacing

4. **Caption**: Write the caption with:
   - Compelling opening (repurposed from hook)
   - Valuable body (not fluff)
   - Clear CTA
   - Strategic hashtags (10-15 for IG/LinkedIn, 2-3 for Twitter)

5. **CTA Optimization**: How do I make the call to action compelling without being pushy?

6. **Engagement Bait**: What question will generate quality comments (not just emoji reactions)?

7. **Platform Optimization**: What platform-specific features should I use? (polls, quotes, threading, tagging, etc.)

8. **A/B Testing**: What's one variable I could test to improve performance?
```

### Example Usage
```
I need social media content for LinkedIn.

**Account Context:**
- Platform: LinkedIn
- Niche: Leadership development for new managers (0-3 years experience)
- Follower count: 2,400
- Account vibe: Professional but approachable, practical, anti-corporate-speak
- Content pillars: Managing people, difficult conversations, personal growth as a manager
- Best-performing content: Personal stories about management failures, "here's what I'd do differently"
- Growth goals: Build community, establish thought leadership, drive consulting clients

**Content Request:**
- Topic: The mistake I made giving feedback to a direct report for the first time
- Content format: Text post (no visuals)
- Goal: Engagement (comments/shares) + demonstrate vulnerability/relatability
- CTA: "What's a management mistake you've made? Share in the comments"
- Link in bio: "Book a consultation" (but not the focus of this post)

**Key Message:**
- Main point: Being "nice" in feedback is actually unkind - clarity is kindness
- Why it matters: New managers are terrified of hurting feelings, so they're vague and unhelpful
- Unique/surprising: I thought I was being compassionate, but I was actually being cowardly

**My Brand Voice:**
- Vulnerable, self-deprecating, practical, learned-the-hard-way, no corporate jargon

**Engagement Strategy:**
- I'll respond to every comment with a question to keep conversation going

Please help me by: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- 5 hook options optimized for LinkedIn (professional but personal)
- Full LinkedIn post (1,300 characters max, ideal length determined)
- Structured for readability (short paragraphs, line breaks)
- Engaging opening that's vulnerable, not clickbait
- Clear story arc: situation → mistake → realization → lesson
- Compelling CTA that invites sharing
- Strategic hashtags (3-5 for LinkedIn)
- A/B test suggestion (e.g., test different opening lines)

### Customization Tips
- **For Instagram**: Add visual descriptions, carousel breakdowns, hashtag strategy (15-25 tags)
- **For TikTok**: Add script format with visual directions, trending sound suggestions, text overlay timing
- **For Twitter/X**: Add thread structure, hook testing, tweet-by-tweet breakdown
- **For YouTube Shorts**: Add retention strategies (pattern interrupts, hooks every 3 seconds)

### Limitations
- **Platform algorithm changes** - Best practices shift; AI may not know latest updates
- **Trend timing** - Cannot access real-time trending topics or sounds
- **Your analytics** - Doesn't know what has/hasn't worked for your specific account
- **Visual creation** - Can describe visuals but cannot create images/videos
- **Community management** - Can suggest responses but cannot read comment sentiment

## 4. Video Script Outlining

### Purpose
Create structured, engaging video scripts that retain viewers, deliver value, and drive desired actions.

### Prompt Template
```
I need a video script outline for [VIDEO TYPE].

**Video Context:**
- Platform: [YOUTUBE/YOUTUBE SHORTS/TIKTOK/IG REELS/LINKEDIN VIDEO/COURSE]
- Video topic: [WHAT THIS VIDEO IS ABOUT]
- Video type: [TUTORIAL/OPINION/STORY/INTERVIEW/REVIEW/LISTICLE/EXPLANATION]
- Target length: [MINUTES/SECONDS]
- Target audience: [WHO THEY ARE, EXPERIENCE LEVEL]
- Video goal: [VIEWS/ENGAGEMENT/SUBSCRIBERS/SALES/EDUCATION]
- My channel's unique value: [WHAT MAKES ME DIFFERENT]

**Content Strategy:**
- Main message: [THE ONE THING VIEWERS SHOULD REMEMBER]
- Key points to cover (3-5): [POINTS]
- Evidence/examples: [PROOF, STORIES, DATA]
- My perspective/angle: [WHAT MAKES THIS INTERESTING, NOT GENERIC]

**Script Requirements:**
- Hook style: [PROVOCATIVE QUESTION/BOLD STATEMENT/STORY/TEASER]
- Pacing preference: [FAST-PACED/MEASURED/CONVERSATIONAL]
- Visual style: [TALKING HEAD/SCREEN RECORDING/B-ROLL/ANIMATION/MIXED]
- Energy level: [HIGH/MODERATE/CALM/INTIMATE]
- Humor: [YES/NO - IF YES, WHAT STYLE]

**Call to Action:**
- Primary CTA: [WHAT YOU WANT THEM TO DO]
- CTA timing: [BEGINNING/MIDDLE/END/MULTIPLE]
- CTA approach: [DIRECT/SOFT/CURIOUSITY-DRIVEN]

**Production Constraints:**
- Equipment: [WHAT YOU'RE WORKING WITH - PHONE/DSLR/MIC/LIGHTING]
- Editing skill: [BEGINNER/INTERMEDIATE/ADVANCED]
- Filming location: [HOME STUDIO/OFFICE/ON-LOCATION/GREEN SCREEN]
- B-roll availability: [WHAT B-ROLL YOU HAVE OR CAN SHOOT]

**My On-Camera Presence:**
- My personality: [ADJECTIVES - E.G., "NERVOUS BUT ENTHUSIASTIC, AUTHENTIC, SLIGHTLY AWKWARD"]
- What I struggle with: [E.G., "RAMBLING," "SOUNDING SCRIPTED," "FORGETTING POINTS"]
- What viewers like about me: [BASED ON COMMENTS/FEEDBACK]

Please help me:

1. **Hook Options**: Write 5 hooks (opening 3-10 seconds) that grab attention immediately

2. **Script Structure**: Create a beat-by-beat outline including:
   - Hook (0:00-0:XX)
   - Intro/context (XX:XX-XX:XX)
   - Content delivery (organized in digestible chunks)
   - Pattern interrupts (to prevent drop-off)
   - CTA (placement and wording)
   - Outro (sign-off)

3. **Scene-by-Scene Breakdown**: For each section, specify:
   - What I say (talking points, not full script unless requested)
   - What's on screen (talking head, b-roll, screen recording, text overlays, graphics)
   - Approximate timing
   - Energy/pacing notes

4. **Retention Strategies**: Identify where viewers might drop off and how to keep them engaged:
   - Pattern interrupts (visual or verbal changes)
   - Open loops (tease what's coming)
   - Curiosity gaps
   - Energy shifts

5. **Visual Direction**: Describe what should be on screen throughout:
   - Text overlays (key phrases, stats, quotes)
   - B-roll suggestions (what to film)
   - Graphics/visual aids
   - Screen recording sections

6. **CTA Scripting**: Write compelling CTA copy that feels natural, not salesy

7. **Thumbnail Ideas**: Describe 3-5 thumbnail concepts that would make people click

8. **Title/Description Optimization**: Suggest:
   - 5 title options (click-worthy but not clickbait)
   - Description template with key sections (hook, what you'll learn, who this is for, links)
   - Tag suggestions

9. **Filming Notes**: Any tips for my specific setup/skill level?
```

### Example Usage
```
I need a video script outline for a YouTube tutorial.

**Video Context:**
- Platform: YouTube
- Video topic: How to build a personal knowledge management system in Notion (beginner-friendly)
- Video type: Tutorial (step-by-step)
- Target length: 12-15 minutes
- Target audience: Knowledge workers who feel overwhelmed by information, new to Notion
- Video goal: Views + drive people to my free Notion template (link in bio)
- My unique value: I'm a productivity consultant who specializes in simple, non-overwhelming systems (not Notion power users who make everything complicated)

**Content Strategy:**
- Main message: You don't need a complex system - start simple, iterate later
- Key points:
  1. Why most people fail with PKM (overcomplicating from day one)
  2. The 3 essential components (capture, organize, retrieve)
  3. Live build: Setting up a simple system in Notion
  4. How to actually use it daily (habits, not just tools)
- Evidence: Client success stories, my own evolution from complex to simple
- Perspective: Anti-power-user, start simple philosophy

**Script Requirements:**
- Hook style: Provocative statement about why complicated systems fail
- Pacing: Conversational but deliberate (I tend to ramble when nervous)
- Visual style: Screen recording + talking head (face in corner)
- Energy: Friendly, encouraging, not hyper
- Humor: Light self-deprecation about my former over-complicated systems

**Call to Action:**
- Primary: "Grab the free template I built that matches this system"
- CTA timing: End (main CTA) + middle soft mention
- Approach: "If you want to skip the setup, I've built this for you free" (not aggressive)

**Production Constraints:**
- Equipment: iPhone 14, decent lighting, lapel mic
- Editing skill: Intermediate (comfortable with cuts, basic transitions)
- Filming location: Home office (decent but not studio quality)
- B-roll: Can film myself using Notion, screen recording

**My On-Camera Presence:**
- Personality: Warm, knowledgeable, slightly awkward but authentic
- Struggle with: Rambling when explaining technical concepts
- Viewers like: Practicality, simplicity focus, I don't make them feel dumb for not being a Notion expert

Please help me by: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- 5 hook options (provocative statements about overcomplicated systems)
- Beat-by-beat outline with timing (hook, intro, problem, solution, demo, CTA, outro)
- Scene-by-scene breakdown with:
  - Talking points (not word-for-word script)
  - Visual directions (screen recording, text overlays, when to show face)
  - Timing estimates
  - Energy notes (when to slow down for clarity)
- Retention strategies (pattern interrupts every 3-4 minutes, open loops before demo)
- Visual direction (text overlays for key concepts, screen recording flow)
- Natural CTA language that matches tone
- 5 thumbnail concepts (before/after, simple system vs complex, etc.)
- 5 title options (optimized for clicks but not misleading)
- Filming tips for nervous rambling (shorter sentences, pause after key points)

### Customization Tips
- **For YouTube Shorts/TikTok**: Condense to one key point, add viral hooks every 2-3 seconds
- **For course videos**: Add learning objectives, practice prompts, knowledge checks
- **For vlogs**: Add story arc, emotional beats, personal reflection moments
- **For reviews**: Add criteria, verdict format, affiliate disclosure language

### Limitations
- **No search data** - Cannot provide SEO-optimized titles based on real search volume
- **Algorithm shifts** - Best practices change; may not know latest algorithm preferences
- **Your editing style** - Cannot see your actual editing to suggest improvements
- **Audience retention data** - Doesn't know where your past videos have dropped off
- **Thumbnail creation** - Can describe but cannot create actual visual assets

## 5. Podcast Interview Questions

### Purpose
Generate insightful podcast questions that reveal fascinating stories, practical wisdom, and authentic moments.

### Prompt Template
```
I need interview questions for a podcast episode.

**Podcast Context:**
- Podcast name/focus: [SHOW/TOPIC]
- Episode theme: [WHAT THIS EPISODE IS ABOUT]
- Episode type: [INTERVIEW/STORY/DEBATE/TEACHING/CONVERSATION]
- Target audience: [WHO LISTENS]
- Episode length: [MINUTES]
- Episode goal: [EDUCATE/ENTERTAIN/INSPIRE/PROMOTE]

**Guest Profile:**
- Guest name: [NAME]
- Guest expertise: [WHAT THEY'RE KNOWN FOR]
- Guest accomplishments: [NOTABLE ACHIEVEMENTS]
- Guest background: [CAREER/LIFE CONTEXT]
- What makes them interesting: [WHY THIS GUEST, WHY NOW]
- Guest's communication style: [STORYTELLER/ANALYTIC/INTROVERTED/ENERGETIC/RESERVED]

**Interview Goals:**
- What listeners should learn: [KEY TAKEAWAYS]
- Stories I want to elicit: [SPECIFIC MOMENTS/EXPERIENCES]
- Topics to cover: [SUBJECTS]
- Topics to avoid: [OFF-LIMITS]
- Promotional balance: [HOW MUCH TO PROMOTE THEIR WORK]

**Pre-Interview Research:**
- Key things I know about them: [FACTS, ACCOMPLISHMENTS, CONTROVERSIES]
- Something surprising about them: [INTERESTING DETAIL]
- Their latest work/project: [WHAT THEY'RE PROMOTING]

**My Interviewing Style:**
- My strengths: [E.G., "DEEP LISTENER," "GOOD FOLLOW-UPS," "COMFORTABLE WITH SILENCE"]
- My weaknesses: [E.G., "TEND TO MONOPOLIZE," "AWKWARD PAUSES," "STICK TO SCRIPT"]
- Vibe I create: [WARM/CHALLENGING/PLAYFUL/INTIMATE/JOURNALISTIC]

**Episode Flow Preference:**
- Structure: [CHRONOLOGICAL/THEMATIC/JUMP-AROUND/STORY-DRIVEN]
- Tone: [SERIOUS/LIGHTHEARTED/INSPIRING/EDUCATIONAL/CONVERSATIONAL]

Please help me:

1. **Opening Questions**: Generate 5 opening questions that:
   - Make the guest comfortable
   - Hook listeners immediately
   - Set up the episode theme
   - Are better than "tell me about yourself"

2. **Core Questions** (20-30): Create questions organized by topic:
   - **Origin Story**: How they got started, early influences
   - **Turning Points**: Pivotal moments, failures, breakthroughs
   - **Key Insights**: What they've learned, their unique perspective
   - **Practical Wisdom**: Actionable advice for listeners
   - **Behind the Scenes**: Stories that haven't been told elsewhere
   - **Controversies/Challenges**: Difficult moments, criticism, conflicts
   - **Future Vision**: What's next, their predictions, hopes

3. **Story-Eliciting Questions**: Craft questions that start with:
   - "Tell me about the time..."
   - "What was going through your mind when..."
   - "Take me back to..."
   - "What nobody knows is..."

4. **Follow-Up Questions**: Prepare 10 follow-up questions based on anticipated answers:
   - "Wait, can you explain that more?"
   - "What happened next?"
   - "How did that feel?"
   - "What did you learn from that?"

5. **Deep-Dive Questions**: Create 5 questions that get beneath the surface:
   - Vulnerability prompts
   - Contrarian perspectives
   - Unpopular opinions
   - Regrets or mistakes
   - What they'd do differently

6. **Lighter Moments**: Prepare 5 fun/easy questions for:
   - Pacing relief
   - Personality reveal
   - Audience engagement
   - Memorable clips for social media

7. **Closing Questions**: Generate 5 closing questions that:
   - Synthesize key themes
   - Leave listeners inspired
   - Provide clear next steps
   - Create a natural ending

8. **Backup Questions**: Prepare 5 questions in case:
   - Conversation stalls
   - You need to change direction
   - You have extra time
   - Their answers are brief

9. **Promotional Integration**: How do I weave in their project/work without it feeling salesy?

10. **Listener Engagement**: What questions should I ask to encourage listener interaction?
```

### Example Usage
```
I need interview questions for a podcast episode.

**Podcast Context:**
- Podcast name: "Creative Unemployment" - stories of people who quit traditional jobs for creative paths
- Episode theme: Leaving a prestigious career to pursue art
- Episode type: Interview + storytelling
- Target audience: People in unfulfilling jobs who dream of doing something creative
- Episode length: 45 minutes
- Episode goal: Inspire listeners, provide practical roadmap, demystify the leap

**Guest Profile:**
- Guest name: Sarah Chen
- Guest expertise: Former corporate lawyer turned full-time painter (left biglaw 3 years ago)
- Guest accomplishments: Now sells paintings for $5k-$20k, featured in Art in America, has 80k Instagram following
- Guest background: Stanford Law, practiced at a top firm in NYC for 7 years, miserable the whole time
- Interesting: Her parents were immigrants who sacrificed everything for her education; they were furious when she quit law
- Communication style: Thoughtful, vulnerable, good storyteller, slightly nervous but authentic

**Interview Goals:**
- What listeners should learn: That it's possible, what the transition actually looks like, financial reality
- Stories to elicit: The moment she decided to quit, telling her parents, first sale, terrifying moments
- Topics: The decision, the transition, the financial reality, the artistic doubt
- Topics to avoid: None - she's been open about everything
- Promotional: She has a workshop for aspiring artists - mention but don't center it

**Pre-Interview Research:**
- Key facts: Graduated Stanford Law 2014, quit firm job 2021, first profitable year as artist 2023
- Surprising: She still does contract legal work 5 hours/week for stability
- Latest: Launching "Creative Leap" mentorship program next month

**My Interviewing Style:**
- Strengths: Good listener, ask "why" a lot, comfortable with emotion
- Weaknesses: Sometimes too polite, don't push enough on tough topics
- Vibe: Intimate, like two friends having a deep conversation

**Episode Flow:**
- Structure: Story-driven (chronological but with thematic asides)
- Tone: Inspiring but honest (not sugarcoating how hard it is)

Please help me by: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- 5 opening questions (better than "tell me about yourself")
- 25-30 core questions organized by topic:
  - Origin story (growing up, pressure to succeed, why law)
  - The misery years (what it felt like to hate something she was good at)
  - The turning point (when she decided to quit, what triggered it)
  - The leap (telling parents, the actual quitting moment)
  - The transition (financial reality, doubt, first sale)
  - Current reality (what her days look like now, what's hard)
  - Advice (for listeners considering similar leaps)
- 10 story-eliciting questions ("Take me back to the moment you told your father...")
- 10 follow-up questions ("How did that conversation actually go?")
- 5 deep-dive questions (vulnerability prompts about doubt, privilege, fear of failure)
- 5 lighter questions (first painting sale, surprising aspects of artist life, etc.)
- 5 closing questions (what she wishes she knew, what she'd say to her former self)
- Backup questions in case conversation stalls
- Natural ways to mention her workshop without being salesy

### Customization Tips
- **For celebrity guests**: Add questions that go beyond the standard press tour talking points
- **For academic experts**: Add practical application questions ("how does this apply to everyday life?")
- **For controversial figures**: Add balanced questions that address criticism without being attack-dog
- **For solo episodes**: Repurpose as reflection prompts to structure your monologue

### Limitations
- **No real-time conversation** - Cannot adapt to what the guest actually says in the moment
- **Over-preparation risk** - Too many questions can make you rigid, not present
- **Guest research gaps** - Only knows what you provide; may miss relevant context
- **Chemistry unpredictability** - Cannot predict actual conversational flow
- **Sensitivity issues** - Cannot read guest discomfort; you must navigate in real-time

### Purpose
Create email campaigns that get opened, read, and clicked without feeling spammy or salesy.

### Prompt Template
```

**This Email:**
- Email topic: [WHAT THIS EMAIL IS ABOUT]
- Segment (if applicable): [WHO'S RECEIVING THIS]
- Goal: [PRIMARY OUTCOME]
- Secondary goals: [OTHER OUTCOMES]
- Send timing: [WHY SEND NOW - URGENCY, SEASONAL, ETC.]

**Offer/Product:**
- What I'm promoting: [PRODUCT/SERVICE/OFFER]
- Price: [COST OR PRICE RANGE]
- Key benefits: [OUTCOMES/TRANSFORMATION]
- Social proof: [TESTIMONIALS, NUMBERS, CREDIBILITY]
- What makes it different: [UNIQUE VALUE PROPOSITION]
- Objections to address: [COMMON CONCERNS]
- Urgency/scarcity: [IF ANY - DEADLINE, LIMITED SPOTS, ETC.]

**Audience Context:**
- Who they are: [DEMOGRAPHICS, PSYCHOGRAPHICS]

- Their biggest pain points: [WHAT KEEPS THEM UP AT NIGHT]
- What they care about most: [PRIORITIES]
- What they're skeptical about: [OBJECTIONS]

**My Brand Voice:**
- [ADJECTIVES - E.G., "FRIENDLY, SLIGHTLY IRREVERENT, NO-FLUFF, TRANSPARENT"]
- Things I never do: [BOUNDARIES - E.G., "NEVER SAY 'HURRY,' NEVER USE FAKE URGENCY"]

**This Email's Structure:**
I need help with:
- Subject line: [NUMBER] options
- Preview text: [NUMBER] options
- Opening hook
- Body copy (organized for scannability)
- Call to action
- P.S. (if applicable)

**My Challenges:**
- [WHAT'S HARD - E.G., "I WRITE TOO LONG," "I SOUND TOO SALESY," "OPENINGS ARE HARD"]

Please help me:

1. **Subject Lines**: Generate [NUMBER] subject line options with different angles:
   - Benefit-driven
   - Curiosity-driven
   - Urgency (if applicable, without being spammy)
   - Personal/conversational
   - Story-driven

2. **Preview Text**: Create [NUMBER] preview text options that complement (not repeat) the subject line

3. **Opening Hook**: Write 3 opening options that:
   - Hook immediately
   - Connect to the reader's situation
   - Avoid "Hi [Name], how are you?" genericness

4. **Email Body**: Draft the full email with:
   - Clear structure (one main idea per paragraph)
   - Scannable (short paragraphs, subheads, bullets)
   - Conversational tone (sounds like a human, not marketing copy)
   - Story or example to illustrate the point
   - Social proof woven in naturally
   - Objections handled without being defensive

5. **Call to Action**: Create a compelling CTA that:
   - Is clear and specific
   - Creates urgency (if appropriate) without manipulation
   - Focuses on the next step, not the final outcome
   - Uses action-oriented language

6. **P.S. Section**: Write a P.S. that reinforces:
   - Key benefit
   - Scarcity/urgency (if applicable)
   - Additional social proof
   - Or a final thought

7. **Length Optimization**: Is this email the right length? Should it be longer/shorter for my goals?

8. **Send Time Strategy**: When should I send this for maximum opens/clicks?

9. **A/B Testing**: What variable should I test to improve performance?
```

### What Doesn't Work

**Common failures:**
- **Insufficient audience detail**: "People interested in marketing" is too broad; "Junior marketers at startups who feel overwhelmed by growth expectations" works better

- **Over-reliance on AI voice**: Content that doesn't sound like you feels inauthentic; audiences can tell
- **One-and-done**: Best content requires iteration, testing, and refinement
- **Missing the "why"**: Focusing on what you're creating without why it matters to your audience

**Real failures:**
- Used social media prompt without mentioning platform nuances → Generic content that got 0 engagement
- Used email marketing prompt without brand voice details → Sounded like a spam bot, high unsubscribe rate

## Best Practices

1. **Know your audience deeply**: The more specific you can be about who you're creating for, the better the output

2. **Provide brand voice examples**: Share what you sound like (and what you don't) to get authentic content

3. **Focus on value, not volume**: One great piece of content that resonates beats five mediocre pieces

4. **Test and iterate**: Use AI to generate options, then A/B test to see what your audience actually responds to

5. **Stay platform-native**: Each platform has its own norms; adapt content accordingly

6. **Engage, don't broadcast**: Social media and newsletters are conversations, not megaphones

7. **Be honest about AI use**: Audiences appreciate transparency; hiding AI use erodes trust

8. **Document what works**: When something performs well, note why and use those insights

---

**Remember**: These prompts are starting points. The best content comes from combining AI assistance with your unique voice, deep audience understanding, and willingness to experiment.
