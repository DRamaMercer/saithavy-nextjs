## 🚨 Quick Start: Crisis Severity Assessment

**If you're in crisis right now, start here. This takes 3 minutes.**

### Severity Scale (1-5)

**Level 1: Discomfort Zone**
- Something is wrong, but you're safe and stable
- Timeline to resolve: Days to weeks
- Example: Didn't get a promotion, minor disagreement, car repair expense
- **Your approach:** Standard problem-solving

**Level 2: Challenge Zone**
- Significant disruption, but you have resources and support
- Timeline to resolve: Weeks to months
- Example: Laid off with savings, breakup with support system, injury with insurance
- **Your approach:** Activate support network, use this template

**Level 3: Crisis Zone**
- Major disruption, resources stretched, emotional flooding
- Timeline to resolve: Months
- Example: Laid off with no savings, divorce, major health diagnosis
- **Your approach:** Full crisis response, professional support recommended

**Level 4: Emergency Zone**
- Severe disruption, survival mode activated, multiple systems failing
- Timeline to resolve: 6-12 months
- Example: Job loss + health crisis + relationship end simultaneously
- **Your approach:** Immediate professional support, crisis team activated

**Level 5: Catastrophe Zone**
- Existential threat, most resources gone, long-term rebuilding required
- Timeline to resolve: 1-3 years
- Example: Business bankruptcy, major disability, total life collapse
- **Your approach:** Professional crisis management + long-term rebuilding plan

## Part 3: Decision-Making Framework

### The 10-10-10 Rule for Crisis Decisions

**When you're overwhelmed, every decision feels high-stakes. This framework helps.**

**For any decision, ask:**
- How will I feel about this in 10 minutes?
- How will I feel about this in 10 months?
- How will I feel about this in 10 years?

**Example:** "Should I tell my family about this crisis?"
- 10 minutes: Scared and vulnerable
- 10 months: Grateful I had support
- 10 years: Glad I didn't isolate myself

### Decision Speed Matrix

**Decide NOW (<1 hour):**
- Immediate safety concerns
- Medical emergencies
- Legal deadlines
- Childcare/dependent care
- Time-sensitive financial decisions (bills due today)

**Decide TODAY (within 24 hours):**
- Communication plan (who to tell, when)
- Short-term financial moves
- Housing next steps
- Work/business communication
- Support team activation

**Decide THIS WEEK (within 7 days):**
- Financial planning (30-60-90 day outlook)
- Career strategy
- Living situation adjustments
- Treatment/therapy plans
- Major purchases or sales

**Decide THIS MONTH (within 30 days):**
- Long-term financial plans
- Major life changes (moving, career pivots)
- Relationship decisions
- Business strategy
- Legal strategies

### The "Good Enough" Decision Protocol

**In crisis, perfect decisions aren't possible. Aim for "good enough."**

**Good enough criteria:**
1. It doesn't make things worse
2. It addresses an immediate need
3. You can adjust course later
4. It aligns with your core values

**The 70% Rule:** If you're 70% confident and it won't cause irreversible harm, decide. Action > perfection in crisis.

## Part 4: Communication System

### Who to Tell, When, and How

**Inner Circle (Tell within 24-48 hours):**
- **Who:** Partner, immediate family, best friend
- **What:** Full truth, emotions included
- **Why:** They need to know to support you effectively
- **Script:** "I'm going through a crisis with [brief description]. I don't need you to fix it. I need you to listen and be present. Can you do that?"

**Support Circle (Tell within first week):**
- **Who:** Close friends, extended family, trusted colleagues
- **What:** High-level overview, clear boundaries
- **Why:** Creates broader support network
- **Script:** "I wanted you to know I'm dealing with [crisis type]. I'm managing it, but things might be different for a while. I appreciate your support and understanding."

**Professional Circle (Tell strategically):**
- **Who:** Employer, clients, professional network (if applicable)
- **What:** Professional, minimal detail, focus on solutions
- **Why:** Protects your professional reputation
- **Script:** "I wanted to inform you of [situation]. I'm taking steps to address it, including [specific actions]. I'm committed to [continued work/transition plan/next steps]."

### What to Say: Crisis Communication Templates

**To Family:**
"I'm going through a difficult time with [brief description]. I'm working on it and have support. I wanted you to know so you understand if I seem distant/overwhelmed. I'll keep you updated on big developments. I love you and appreciate your support."

**To Friends:**
"Hey, I wanted to share something personal. I'm dealing with [crisis]. I'm okay, but things are tough right now. I might not be as available/social as usual. Please don't take it personally—just focusing on getting through this. Thanks for being a friend."

**To Employer:**
"I need to discuss a personal situation that affects my work. [Brief, professional description]. I'm taking steps to manage this, including [specific actions]. I'm committed to my role and want to discuss how to navigate this period effectively."

**To Creditors/Landlords:**
"I'm experiencing a temporary hardship due to [brief description]. I want to work with you on a payment plan/adjustment. Here's what I can do: [specific proposal]. Can we discuss options?"

### What NOT to Share

**Protect yourself from:**
- Oversharing with people who have hurt you before
- Sharing details that could be used against you legally
- Sharing before you've processed enough to be clear
- Sharing with people who drain vs. support you

**Red flag responses:** If someone responds with judgment, blame, or "I told you so," limit future sharing with them.

- [ ] Emotional processing (allow yourself to feel shocked/angry/afraid)
- [ ] Document what happened (facts only, for legal/reference purposes)
- [ ] Secure your belongings and data (if applicable)
- [ ] Do NOT make major public announcements yet

**First 72 Hours:**
- [ ] Activate support system (3-5 key people)
- [ ] Review finances (runway, cuts needed, emergency funds)
- [ ] Apply for unemployment (if applicable)
- [ ] Research COBRA/health insurance options
- [ ] Update LinkedIn (change to "Open to opportunities")

**First Week:**
- [ ] Communicate with family (practical + emotional)
- [ ] Reach out to professional network (personal, targeted messages)
- [ ] Identify your transferable skills and value proposition
- [ ] Create job search strategy (target roles, companies, approach)
- [ ] Set daily routine (structure prevents spiraling)

**First Month:**
- [ ] Apply to 10-20 targeted opportunities (quality > quantity)
- [ ] Attend 2-3 networking events or virtual meetups
- [ ] Consider contract/freelance work (income + bridge)
- [ ] Upskill in one high-value area (shows proactive growth)
- [ ] Protect your mental health (therapy, support groups, routine)

### Personal Crisis (Relationship End, Divorce)

**First 24 Hours:**
- [ ] Allow emotional release (cry, scream, journal—process it)
- [ ] Do NOT contact your ex (give yourself space)
- [ ] Activate your most supportive friend
- [ ] Ensure your basic needs are met (food, sleep, safety)

**First 72 Hours:**
- [ ] Create physical distance (if living together, plan separation)
- [ ] Inform your support system (you don't have to manage this alone)
- [ ] Secure your finances (separate accounts, document shared assets)
- [ ] Establish boundaries (what you will/won't discuss)
- [ ] Focus on one day at a time (future planning comes later)

**First Week:**
- [ ] Consult with a lawyer (if married/shared assets)
- [ ] Establish new routines (morning, evening, meals)
- [ ] Join a support group (divorce, breakup, grief)
- [ ] Plan social support (regular check-ins with friends)
- [ ] Avoid major decisions (moving, job changes, big purchases)

**First Month:**
- [ ] Begin legal process (if applicable)
- [ ] Establish co-parenting plan (if children involved)
- [ ] Start therapy (individual, not couples)
- [ ] Reconnect with your identity (hobbies, friends, goals)
- [ ] Focus on self-care (sleep, nutrition, movement)

### Health Crisis (Diagnosis, Injury, Mental Health)

**First 24 Hours:**
- [ ] Allow yourself to feel (fear, anger, grief—whatever comes up)
- [ ] Gather facts (write down what doctors told you, record conversations)
- [ ] Identify your medical team (primary doctor, specialists)
- [ ] Do NOT Google your condition (reliable sources only)

**First 72 Hours:**
- [ ] Second opinion (for major diagnoses or treatment plans)
- [ ] Research your condition (reputable sources only: Mayo Clinic, NIH)
- [ ] Inform your support system (practical + emotional support)

**First Week:**
- [ ] Create a medical question list (write down everything)
- [ ] Appoint a healthcare advocate (someone to accompany you to appointments)
- [ ] Research treatment options (with medical guidance, not internet alone)
- [ ] Understand work implications (disability, FMLA, accommodations)
- [ ] Establish a support system (practical help: meals, rides, childcare)

**First Month:**
- [ ] Begin treatment plan (as recommended by medical team)
- [ ] Establish new routines around treatment/medication
- [ ] Join a patient support group (condition-specific community)
- [ ] Communicate with employer (if work-affected)
- [ ] Focus on what you can control (treatment adherence, self-care)

### Financial Crisis (Debt, Bankruptcy, Financial Ruin)

**First 24 Hours:**
- [ ] Stop the bleeding (cancel subscriptions, pause unnecessary spending)
- [ ] Face the numbers (total debt, income, expenses—no avoiding)
- [ ] Secure cash (what money do you have access to right now?)
- [ ] Do NOT make major financial decisions yet

**First 72 Hours:**
- [ ] Prioritize expenses (food, housing, utilities, medications first)
- [ ] Contact creditors (ask for hardship programs, payment plans)
- [ ] Research debt relief options (consolidation, settlement, bankruptcy)
- [ ] Protect your income (what can you do to earn/keep money coming in?)
- [ ] Consult a financial counselor or bankruptcy attorney

**First Week:**
- [ ] Create bare-bones budget (survival budget only)
- [ ] Sell unnecessary items (raise cash quickly)

- [ ] Communicate with family (transparency about financial reality)
- [ ] Establish a cash management system (every dollar allocated)

**First Month:**
- [ ] Execute debt relief strategy (as advised by professional)
- [ ] Increase income (side gig, sell items, ask for hours at work)
- [ ] Build emergency fund (even $100 starts the habit)

- [ ] Focus on progress, not perfection (small wins build momentum)

### Existential Crisis (Identity, Meaning, Purpose)

**First 24 Hours:**
- [ ] Allow the questioning (this is normal, not a problem to solve)
- [ ] Do NOT make major life decisions (no quitting everything today)
- [ ] Ground yourself in basics (sleep, food, movement)

- [ ] Identify what feels meaningless (specific areas of life)
- [ ] Identify what still feels meaningful (even small things)
- [ ] Talk to someone who gets it (therapist, mentor, wise friend)

- [ ] Focus on present moment (future can wait 72 hours)

**First Week:**
- [ ] Explore values (what actually matters to you?)

- [ ] Connect with something bigger (nature, art, service, spirituality)

- [ ] Work with a therapist or coach (specialized in existential issues)

- [ ] Take meaningful action (volunteer, create, connect—do something that matters)

- [ ] Embrace uncertainty (meaning isn't found, it's created)

## Part 8: Troubleshooting Crisis Complications

### When Crisis Gets Worse: Escalation Protocol

**Signs your crisis is escalating:**
- You're not sleeping more than 4 hours per night for 3+ nights
- You're having panic attacks daily
- You're having thoughts of self-harm
- You're unable to complete basic tasks (eating, hygiene, safety)
- You feel completely hopeless or trapped

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **Call for backup:** Contact your crisis team immediately
2. **Professional support:** Call a therapist, crisis line, or go to ER
3. **Safety first:** Remove yourself from dangerous situations
4. **Simplify:** Cancel everything non-essential
5. **Accept help:** This is not the time to "handle it alone"

### When Recovery Stalls: Momentum Reset Protocol

**Signs recovery has stalled:**
- You've been at the same phase for 4+ weeks without progress
- You're avoiding key tasks (procrastination, denial, overwhelm)
- You feel stuck in a loop (same problems, no forward movement)
- Your motivation has disappeared completely

**Momentum Reset Actions:**
1. **Reassess severity:** Has your crisis level changed?
2. **Simplify the plan:** Break stalled tasks into smaller steps
3. **Accountability:** Share your plan with someone who will check in
4. **Celebrate small wins:** Acknowledge EVERY bit of progress
5. **Professional support:** Therapy, coaching, or support group

### When Support Systems Fail: Backup Protocol

**When support isn't supportive:**
- People are judging instead of helping
- Friends/family are absent or unavailable
- Professional support isn't accessible or effective
- You feel more alone after reaching out

**Backup Actions:**
1. **Diversify support:** Don't rely on one person or system
2. **Online communities:** Find anonymous support online
3. **Professional backup:** Get a second opinion or new provider
4. **Paid support:** Sometimes coaching or therapy is worth the investment
5. **Be your own support:** Journaling, self-soothing, self-compassion

### When Multiple Crises Hit: Compound Crisis Protocol

**When you're dealing with 2+ crises simultaneously:**
1. **Prioritize by severity:** Which crisis is Level 4-5? Focus there first
2. **Find intersections:** Can one action address multiple crises?
3. **Extend timeline:** Recovery will take longer—adjust expectations
4. **Increase support:** You need more help, not less
5. **Accept limits:** You cannot fix everything at once

## Part 10: Crisis Prevention for the Future

### Build Your Crisis-Resistant Life

**Financial Resilience:**
- [ ] Emergency fund (3-6 months expenses)
- [ ] Multiple income streams (even small ones)
- [ ] Insurance coverage (health, disability, life)
- [ ] Low debt burden or debt-free
- [ ] Professional network (always nurturing)

**Emotional Resilience:**
- [ ] Daily mental health practices (meditation, journaling, therapy)
- [ ] Strong support network (diverse relationships)
- [ ] Healthy coping mechanisms (not avoidance, not numbing)
- [ ] Self-awareness (you know your triggers and patterns)
- [ ] Meaning and purpose (beyond work or status)

**Practical Resilience:**
- [ ] Diversified identity (not all eggs in one basket)
- [ ] Up-to-date documents (legal, financial, medical)
- [ ] Crisis contacts identified (you know who to call)
- [ ] Skills and education (always learning)
- [ ] Flexibility and adaptability (you can pivot)

### Early Warning System

**Signs you're drifting toward crisis:**
- Sleep is disrupted for 2+ weeks
- You're avoiding important tasks or conversations
