## Values as a Compass, Not a Map

Values aren't a map. They don't tell you exactly where to go or what to do.

Values are a compass. They tell you which direction is true north for YOU.

When you're lost, you check your compass.
When you're facing a decision, you check your compass.
When you're feeling off track, you check your compass.

**Values are your reference point.**

## Values Change Over Time

One of the biggest mistakes people make is treating values as static.

**Your values will change.**

What I valued at 25 isn't what I value at 35. What I value now won't be what I value at 45.

This isn't failure. This is growth.

**Examples of values evolution:**
- 25: Achievement, Status, Excitement
- 35: Balance, Family, Growth
- 45: Legacy, Service, Wisdom

**This is natural. This is healthy.**

This is why you should revisit your values annually.

Not to judge past you. To understand current you.

## When Values Compete

We talked about this briefly, but let's go deeper.

Sometimes your values will be in conflict. You'll have to choose.

**Example:**
You value both **Security** and **Freedom**.
- Opportunity: Job offer with startup (high freedom, low security)
- Current job: Stable position (high security, low freedom)

Both options honor one value and violate another.

**How to choose:**

1. **Which values are relevant?**
   - Security vs. Freedom

2. **Which option honors more of my values?**
   - Maybe Growth is also a value, and the startup honors that too
   - Maybe Family is a value, and current job's stability supports them

3. **Can I live with the values compromise?**
   - Can I live with less security for more freedom?
   - Can I live with less freedom for more security?

4. **What would future me be proud of?**
   - 5 years from now, which decision will I respect more?

**There's no "right" answer.**
**There's only the answer that's right for YOU.**

## Your Values Commitment

I want you to make a 30-day values commitment.

Not a lifelong pledge. Not a vague resolution. A focused 30-day experiment.

**Why 30 days?**
- Long enough to see results
- Short enough to feel doable
- Specific enough to measure

**Choose 2-3 values to focus on.**

Don't try to fix everything. Don't try to honor all 5 values equally.

Choose the 2-3 values that:
- Matter most right now
- Will have the biggest impact
- Feel most urgent to live

## The 30-Day Values Commitment

**Which 2-3 values will I focus on?**
- Be specific
- These should be from your top 5
- These should be values that need attention

**What's one behavior I'll START?**
- Actionable
- Specific
- Measurable
- Example: "I'll spend 30 minutes every morning learning" (Learning value)

**What's one behavior I'll STOP?**
- Clear boundary
- Specific situation
- Example: "I'll stop saying yes to every request" (Boundaries value)

**What's one boundary I'll SET?**
- Protects your values
- Communicated to others
- Example: "No meetings before 11am" (Learning value)

**How will I track progress?**
- Daily check-in
- Weekly review
- What will you measure?

**What's my 30-day review date?**
- Put it on your calendar
- What will you assess?
- What happens after 30 days?

## Values Decision Framework

I'm giving you a simple framework for making values-aligned decisions:

When facing any decision, ask:

1. **Which values are relevant?**
   - Which of my top 5 values are involved?

2. **Which option honors my values?**
   - Which choice aligns with what matters to me?

3. **Can I live with the values compromise?**
   - If there's a values conflict, which will I prioritize?

4. **What would future me be proud of?**
   - 6 months from now, which choice will I respect?

**This framework works for big decisions:**
- Career changes
- Relationship choices
- Major life transitions

**And small decisions:**
- How to spend your weekend
- Whether to say yes to a request
- What to prioritize today

## The Quarterly Review

Values aren't set-it-and-forget-it.

Schedule a quarterly values review:

1. **Are these still my top 5 values?**
   - Some values may have shifted
   - Some priorities may have changed

2. **Where am I living my values?**
   - What's working?
   - What should I continue?

3. **Where am I violating my values?**
   - What needs attention?
   - What's one thing to improve?

4. **What's my next 30-day commitment?**
   - Choose 2-3 values to focus on
   - Set specific actions and boundaries
