## The Values Test

Here's the uncomfortable truth about values:

**Your values are not what you say they are. Your values are what you actually do.**

I can say I value "Health" all day long. But if I eat fast food three times a week, never exercise, and sleep 5 hours a night...

I don't value health. I say I do. I wish I did. But I don't.

**How to Test If a Value Is Real**

Look at two things:
1. Your calendar
2. Your bank statement

Where you spend your time and money = what you actually value.

Everything else is just what you *wish* you valued.

## The Aspirational Values Trap

Most of us have aspirational values—things we wish we valued but don't actually live.

This isn't bad. It's just important to know the difference.

**Aspirational values** are who you want to become.
**Actual values** are who you are now.

Growth happens when you bridge the gap between them.

But you can't bridge a gap you don't acknowledge.

## Today's Exercise: Values Audit

For each of your top 5 values from yesterday, answer these questions honestly:

1. **When did I live this value recently?**
   - Specific example from the last 30 days
   - What did you do? What choice did you make?

2. **When did I violate this value recently?**
   - Specific example from the last 30 days
   - What happened? What did you choose instead?

3. **Does my calendar reflect this value?**
   - Look at how you spend your time
   - Give specific examples
   - Rate alignment 1-10

4. **Does my spending reflect this value?**
   - Look at where your money goes
   - Give specific examples
   - Rate alignment 1-10

5. **Is this value real or aspirational?**
   - Based on evidence, not feelings
   - Be honest

## What You'll Discover

Three things happen during this exercise:

**1. Confirmation:**
Some values will pass the test easily. You'll see clear evidence in your calendar and spending. These are genuine core values.

**2. Discomfort:**
Some values will fail the test. You'll realize they're aspirational—things you wish were true but aren't. This is uncomfortable but necessary.

**3. Surprise:**
You might discover values you didn't choose yesterday but are clearly real based on your behavior. That's okay—add them to your list.

## The Values Misalignment Cost

Living with values misalignment has a cost:
- **Guilt:** You know you're not being true to yourself
- **Frustration:** You keep making choices that don't feel right
- **Burnout:** You're working against your own nature
- **Confusion:** You can't understand why you're not satisfied

**The opposite is also true:**

When your values are aligned, decisions feel easier. Work feels more meaningful. Relationships feel more fulfilling.

You're not fighting yourself anymore.
