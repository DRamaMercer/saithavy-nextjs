## By Difficulty

### 🟢 Easy Scripts (Start Here)

**Work:**
- "I need more time to decide, thank you" (Decision making)
- "I'm heading out at [time] today. I can pick this up tomorrow" (Leaving on time)
- "I'm blocking focus time until [time]. Will respond after" (Focus time)
- "No thank you, I don't need one today" (Declining offers)

**Personal:**
- "Thank you for the invite! I'm prioritizing rest and won't be able to make it" (Social plans)
- "I need to get going now, but it was good talking to you" (Ending conversations)
- "I'll think about it and get back to you" (Buying time)

**Digital:**
- "I check email [times] and respond within [timeframe]" (Email)
- "I'm not responding to texts right now. If urgent, please call" (Phone)

---

### 🟡 Medium Scripts (Building Muscle)

**Work:**
- "I'm at capacity with current commitments and can't take this on" (Saying no to work)
- "I want to ensure quality. Given scope, I need [timeline]" (Pushing back on deadlines)
- "I don't check work messages on weekends. I'll respond Monday" (Weekend boundaries)
- "I can't cover your work right now. Have you talked to [manager]?" (Not covering others)

**Family:**
- "I love you and I need [time/space] to take care of myself" (Family boundaries)
- "I won't be able to make it home for [holiday]. I'd love to visit [other time]" (Holiday boundaries)
- "I'd rather not talk about [topic]. How are you doing?" (Avoiding topics)

**Friends:**
- "I value our friendship, and our conversations have felt one-sided lately" (Imbalance)
- "I'm feeling drained by how negative our conversations have been" (Negativity)
- "I can't lend money to friends. I value our friendship too much" (Money)

**Self:**
- "I want to help, and I also need to honor my own needs" (People-pleasing)
- "I'm excited about these opportunities. I need to be realistic about capacity" (Overcommitment)
- "Done is better than perfect. This is good enough" (Perfectionism)

---

### 🔴 Hard Scripts (Advanced Practice)

**Family:**
- "When you comment on [topic], it feels hurtful. I'd appreciate avoiding it" (Critical parents)
- "I've mentioned [boundary] multiple times and it continues to happen" (Repeated crossings)
- "I care about you, and I'm not in a position to lend money. That's a boundary I need to hold" (Financial boundaries)

**Relationships:**
- "I've been reflecting on our relationship and it's not meeting my needs. I need to take space" (Ending relationships)
- "I need to talk to you about something. When you [behavior], it feels violating. I need you to stop" (Respect violations)

**Work:**
- "I work most effectively with autonomy, with check-ins at [milestones]. Can we try that?" (Micromanagement)
- "I need to report [behavior]. This has created a [hostile/unsafe] environment" (Harassment)

## By Relationship Type

### Work → Manager

**Saying no to work:**
"I'm at capacity with my current commitments. I want to be realistic about what I can deliver well."

**Unrealistic deadlines:**
"I want to ensure quality. Given the scope, I'd need [timeline] to do this right. If we need it faster, let's discuss what to de-prioritize."

**Leaving on time:**
"I make it a practice to wrap up at [time] to maintain my energy and effectiveness."

**Micromanagement:**
"I've found I work most effectively with autonomy on [tasks], with check-ins at [milestones]. Can we try that approach?"

---

### Work → Colleagues

**Covering their work:**
"I understand you're in a tight spot. I'm at capacity with my own deliverables and can't take this on. Have you talked to [manager]?"

**Chatty coworkers:**
"I enjoy catching up! I also need to get back to work right now. Let's continue this [later time/at lunch]."

**Venting sessions:**
"I can hear that you're frustrated. I've noticed our conversations often turn into venting, and I'm trying to stay more positive. Can we focus on [solutions/actionable topic]?"

---

### Work → Clients

**Unrealistic timelines:**
"I want to deliver work that meets your standards. To do that well, I need [timeline]. If we need to move faster, let's discuss what to reduce from scope."

**After-hours contact:**
"I received your message at [time]. I make it a practice to respond to non-urgent matters during work hours. I'll get back to you by [time tomorrow]."

**Scope creep:**
"I understand you want to add [new request]. That's outside our original scope. Let's discuss how this impacts timeline and budget."

---

### Family → Parents

**Critical comments:**
"I love you, and I need to share something. When you comment on [my weight/job/relationship], it feels hurtful. I'd appreciate it if we could avoid those topics."

**Over-involvement:**
"I appreciate that you care. I also need space to make my own decisions. I'll come to you if I want advice. Otherwise, I'd appreciate you trusting me to handle this."

**Guilt-tripping:**
"I hear that you're disappointed. I also need to make the decision that's right for me. I love you, and I'm not going to change my mind on this."

---

### Family → Partner

**Household imbalance:**
"I'm feeling overwhelmed by [household responsibility]. I need us to rebalance this. Can we sit down and divide things more evenly?"

**Alone time:**
"I love spending time with you, and I also need time alone to recharge. I'm going to take [evening/afternoon] for myself."

**Emotional boundary crossing:**
"I need to talk to you about something. When you [specific behavior], it feels violating. I need you to stop doing that."

---

### Family → Siblings

**Borrowing money:**
"I care about you, and I'm not in a position to lend money right now. That's a boundary I need to hold for my own financial wellbeing."

**Comparison/competition:**
"I notice that when we talk, it often turns into comparisons. That doesn't feel good to me. I'd love for us to celebrate each other's paths without competing."

---

### Friends → General

**Declining plans:**
"Thank you for thinking of me! I'm prioritizing rest right now and won't be able to make it. Hope you have a wonderful time."

**One-sided conversations:**
"I value our friendship, and I've noticed I do most of the listening. I'd love for our conversations to feel more balanced."

**Negative friend:**
"I value our friendship, and I've been feeling drained by how negative our conversations have been lately. Can we focus on more positive topics when we hang out?"

---

### Friends → Ending Friendship

**Draining friendship:**
"I've been doing some reflecting on our friendship, and I've realized it's not meeting my needs anymore. I need to take some space. I wish you the best."

---

### Self → Internal Boundaries

**Perfectionism:**
"Done is better than perfect. This is good enough for now. I can always iterate later."

**Overcommitment:**
"I'm excited about all these opportunities. I need to be realistic about my capacity. I'll choose [top priority] and politely decline the rest."

**People-pleasing pause:**
"I want to help, and I also need to honor my own needs. Let me think about this and get back to you."

**Social media:**
"I'm noticing this app is making me feel [anxious/inadequate]. I need to set a limit for my own wellbeing."

---

### Digital → General

**Email:**
"I check email [times] to stay focused on deep work. I'll respond to non-urgent messages within [timeframe]."

**Text messages:**
"I'm trying to be less reactive to my phone. I might not respond to texts right away. If it's urgent, please call me."

**Work communication after hours:**
"I make it a practice not to check [Slack/Teams/Email] after [time] so I can recharge. If something is urgent, please call me."

## By Situation Type

### Time Boundaries

**Leaving work on time:**
"I make it a practice to wrap up at [time] to maintain my energy. I can pick this up tomorrow."

**Protecting focus time:**
"I'm blocking [time] for deep work and won't be available for meetings then."

**Weekend boundaries:**
"I don't check work messages on weekends so I can recharge. If anything urgent comes up, please call me."

**Ending a conversation:**
"I need to get going now, but it was good talking to you."

---

### Energy Boundaries

**Social battery drained:**
"I've had a long day and my social battery is drained. I need some quiet time to recharge."

**Emotional capacity:**
"I care about you and I want to support you. I'm also feeling [overwhelmed/at capacity] right now. Can we find a different time to talk?"

**After intense project:**
"That project took a lot out of me. I'm planning to take [time] at a lighter pace to recover."

---

### Emotional Boundaries

**Not emotional dumping ground:**
"I can hear that you're frustrated. I've noticed our conversations often turn into venting sessions, and I'm trying to stay more positive. Can we focus on solutions?"

**Not therapist for friend:**
"I care about you, and I'm not able to support you through this specific situation. It's beyond what I can handle. Have you considered talking to a therapist?"

**Setting emotional limit:**
"I want to be here for you, and I'm running on empty right now. Can we have this conversation [later] when I can be more present?"

---

### Physical Boundaries

**Unwanted touch:**
"I'm not comfortable with that kind of physical contact. I'd appreciate it if you wouldn't [behavior]."

**Personal space:**
"I need a bit more personal space. Could you step back a bit?"

**Physical affection:**
"I'm not feeling up for [hugs/touching] right now. A [handshake/wave] works better for me."

---

### Financial Boundaries

**Lending money:**
"I care about you, and I'm not in a position to lend money. That's a boundary I need to hold for my own financial wellbeing."

**Splitting costs:**
"I'm happy to split the bill, but I can't cover the whole thing."

**Financial expectations:**
"I understand you have expectations about [financial topic], and I need to make decisions that work for my financial situation."

---

### Digital Boundaries

**Phone boundaries:**
"I'm setting boundaries around my phone use to be more present. I might be slower to respond to texts."

**Email boundaries:**
"I batch my email to protect focus time. I check messages at [times] and respond within [timeframe]."

**Social media:**
"I'm taking a break from social media for [timeframe] to focus on [mental health/presence]."

**Video call fatigue:**
"I've been on video calls all day and my energy is drained. Can we do this as a phone call instead?"

## Repair Scripts

### First Time Boundary Crossed

"I mentioned that I [boundary], and that happened again. I need you to [expected behavior]. Can we agree on that?"

---

### Repeated Boundary Crossing

"We've talked about [boundary] multiple times, and it continues to happen. This isn't working for me. I need [specific change]."

---

### Enforcing Consequence

"I've asked for [boundary] multiple times. Since it's not being respected, I need to [consequence]. I don't want to do this, but I will if the pattern continues."

---

### You Didn't Hold Boundary (Self-Repair)

"I didn't hold my boundary there. That's okay - I'm still learning. I forgive myself. Next time, I'll [what I'll do differently]."

---

### Re-establishing After Inconsistency

"I realize I let [boundary] slide when you [crossed it]. I'm not willing to continue that. Going forward, I need [boundary held]."

---

## Pushback Responses

### "You're being selfish"

"I understand it feels that way to you. I also need to take care of my own [needs/energy]. This isn't about you - it's about what I need to be healthy."

---

### "You've changed"

"You're right - I have changed. I'm learning to prioritize my [needs/wellbeing]. That's not a bad thing."

---

### "You're being too sensitive"

"I hear that you see it differently. My feelings are valid, and this is what I need. I'd appreciate your respect even if you don't agree."

---

### Guilt-tripping

"I hear that you're disappointed. I also need to [hold this boundary]. I love you/care about you, and I'm not going to change my mind."

---

### They get angry

"I can see you're upset. I understand this is an adjustment. This boundary is important to me, and I'm willing to have this conversation when we're both calm."

## Emergency Scripts

### When You Feel Unsafe

**In the moment:**
"I'm not comfortable continuing this conversation. I'm going to leave now."

**If threatened:**
"I'm leaving. If you continue, I will call the police."

---

### When Someone Is Being Abusive

**Naming it:**
"This behavior is abusive. I will not tolerate it."

**Leaving:**
"I'm leaving. Do not follow me."

## Quick Tips

1. **Keep it simple:** More words = weaker boundary
2. **Don't over-explain:** "No" is a complete sentence
3. **Stay calm:** Emotion weakens delivery
4. **Expect testing:** People will check if you meant it
5. **Follow through:** Enforce stated consequences
6. **Be consistent:** Inconsistency confuses people
7. **Practice out loud:** It feels different in your mouth
8. **Start small:** Build muscle with easy boundaries first

---

**Remember:** Your boundaries teach others how to treat you. Start small. Be clear. Stay firm.
