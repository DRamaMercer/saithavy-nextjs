# Letting Go Protocol

**Weeks 17-20: Final Phase Resource**

*Before you can begin something new, you must complete what was.*

---

## Understanding Endings

**William Bridges taught us that every transition starts with an ending.** Not the physical ending (the last day, the final goodbye), but the psychological ending—the internal process of letting go of who you were, what you knew, and how you operated.

**The ending phase is often harder than the beginning.** Because beginnings are filled with hope and possibility, while endings are filled with loss, grief, and uncertainty.

**You're allowed to grieve your ending.** Even if it's:
- A "good" change (promotion, move, new opportunity)
- A "necessary" change (layoff, breakup, health diagnosis)
- A "wanted" change (retirement, parenthood, new business)

**Every ending contains loss.** This workbook helps you honor it, learn from it, and let it go with intention.

## Part 5: Rituals for Honoring Endings

**Rituals help your brain and heart process endings.** They create psychological completion and mark the transition from "then" to "now."

**Choose one or more of these rituals, or create your own:**

### 1. The Goodbye Letter
**Write a letter to:**
- The version of yourself you're leaving behind
- The job/role you're ending
- The relationship that's changing
- The chapter that's closing

**Include:**
- What I loved about you
- What I learned from you
- What I'll miss
- What I'm ready to release
- My blessing as we part ways

**Destroy it after writing** (burn, bury, shred, dissolve in water) to symbolize release.

### 2. The Gratitude Ceremony
**Create a gratitude list or jar:**
- Write each person, experience, or lesson on separate slips of paper
- Read them aloud
- Say thank you for each one
- Keep the list as a reminder, or release the slips

### 3. The Object Ritual
**Gather objects that represent this chapter:**
- Work badge, business card, notebook
- Photos, mementos, tokens
- Tools, equipment, supplies

**Options:**
- Create a shadow box or display (to honor, not dwell)
- Donate or pass on items (completing the cycle)
- Store them away (when you're ready)
- Bury or destroy (symbolizing release)

### 4. The Physical Release
**Physical movement helps process emotions:**
- Go to the place you're leaving and say goodbye
- Take a final walk through familiar spaces
- Do something symbolic: plant something, release balloons, light a candle
- Exercise intensely to move emotion through your body

### 5. The Sharing Circle
**Gather people who understand:**
- Share stories from this chapter
- Express gratitude and grief
- Receive acknowledgment and support
- Mark the ending together

### 6. The Solo Retreat
**Take intentional time alone:**
- Go away for a day or weekend
- Reflect on this chapter
- Feel what you need to feel
- Set intentions for what's next

## Part 6: Letting Go Practice

### Daily Release Practice (5 minutes)

**Each evening for the next week:**

1. **Write down:** "Today I release..."
   - One thought
   - One emotion
   - One expectation
   - One piece of the past

2. **Say aloud:** "I release this. It is complete."

3. **Take a breath** and visualize it leaving your body

4. **Write:** "Tomorrow I'm ready for..."
   - One intention
   - One hope
   - One possibility

### Weekly Release Practice (30 minutes)

**Once a week for the next month:**

1. **Review your week:** What came up from the past?
2. **Feel it fully:** Give yourself permission to grieve
3. **Write it down:** Name what you're releasing
4. **Burn, bury, or dissolve:** Physically release the paper
5. **Replace it:** What new energy are you inviting in?

## Weekly Check-In

**Ask yourself each week:**

1. What am I still holding onto?
2. What am I ready to release?
3. What am I grateful for from this chapter?
4. What am I carrying forward?

**Until nothing new comes up.** Then you know you've completed the ending.

---

**Remember:** Letting go is not a one-time event. It's a process. Be patient with yourself. Some endings layer on top of others. You may think you're done, then grief resurfaces. That's normal. That's human. That's transition.

**Every season is training.** This ending is teaching you something about beginnings.
