# Transition Support Plan

**Weeks 17-20: Final Phase Resource**

*Transition is not a solo sport. You don't have to do this alone.*

---

## Why You Need A Transition Support Plan

**Transitions are vulnerable times.** You're:
- Letting go of the familiar
- Navigating uncertainty
- Becoming someone new
- Facing old patterns
- Managing stress and emotion

**Trying to do this alone is like:** climbing a mountain without a map, gear, or guide. Possible? Maybe. Wise? No.

**A Transition Support Plan ensures you have:**
- People to call when it gets hard
- Resources to draw on when you're stuck
- Practices to return to when you're overwhelmed
- A safety net for when you fall
- Celebration partners for when you succeed

**This document helps you build your support system before you need it.**

## Part 8: Communication With Your Support Team

### How To Ask For What You Need

**Most people want to help but don't know how.** Be specific.

**Templates for reaching out:**

**When you need emotional support:**
> "Hey [Name], I'm navigating a transition and having a hard time. I don't need you to fix anything, just to listen. Can you spare 20 minutes to vent?"

**When you need practical help:**
> "Hey [Name], I'm overwhelmed with [specific situation]. Would you be able to help with [specific task] on [day/time]?"

**When you need perspective:**
> "Hey [Name], I'm facing [situation] and feeling stuck. Can I get your perspective? You're good at seeing things I miss."

**When you need encouragement:**
> "Hey [Name], I'm doubting myself right now. Can you remind me what you see in me?"

**When you need distraction:**
> "Hey [Name], I'm heavy right now. Want to [activity] and talk about anything but my transition?"

### Setting Boundaries With Support

**It's okay to set boundaries with your support team:**

**When you're not ready to talk:**
> "I appreciate your checking in. I'm not ready to talk about it yet. I'll reach out when I am."

**When you need a different kind of support:**
> "Thanks for the advice. Right now I just need to be heard. Can you just listen?"

**When you need space:**
> "I love you and I need some space to process on my own. I'll reach out when I'm ready."

## Final Reminder

**Transition is not a solo sport.** You don't have to do this alone.

**Your support plan is a living document.** It changes as your transition changes.

**Reach out BEFORE you're in crisis.** Build your team when you're strong, not when you're struggling.

**You deserve support.** You don't have to earn it. You don't have to be "bad enough" to ask for it.

**Asking for help is brave.** Receiving support is wise. Navigating transition with others is sustainable.

**Every season is training.** This transition is teaching you how to receive, how to trust, how to be supported.

**You don't have to do this alone.**
