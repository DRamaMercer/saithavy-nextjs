# Performance Templates

*Dashboard and reporting templates for tracking platform health, content performance, and resource optimization.*

---

## Overview

This directory contains templates for:

1. **Platform Health Dashboard** - Comprehensive platform performance tracking
2. **Content Performance Report** - Deep-dive into content analytics
3. **ROI Report** - Return on investment analysis

---

## Templates

### Platform Health Dashboard (`platform-health-dashboard.md`)

**Purpose**: Comprehensive dashboard tracking overall platform health, top performers, underperformance analysis, and resource recommendations.

**What It Tracks**:
- **Platform Health Scores** - Overall health metrics per platform (0-100)
- **Component Scores** - Reach, engagement, growth, quality, consistency
- **Top Performing Content** - Best content across all platforms
- **Underperformance Analysis** - Diagnose and fix poor performers
- **Platform Comparison** - Cross-platform performance analysis
- **Resource Allocation** - Data-driven time and budget recommendations
- **Growth Opportunities** - High-impact opportunities to pursue
- **Risk Assessment** - Platform-specific risks and mitigation

**When to Use**:
- Weekly performance reviews
- Monthly strategy planning
- Quarterly business reviews
- Before resource allocation decisions

**How to Use**:
1. Update metrics at end of reporting period
2. Calculate health scores for each platform
3. Identify top and bottom performers
4. Analyze patterns and insights
5. Make data-driven recommendations
6. Distribute to stakeholders

**Health Score Calculation**:
```
Overall Health Score = (Reach + Engagement + Growth + Quality + Consistency) / 5

Component Scores (0-100 each):
- Reach: How many people see your content
- Engagement: How people interact with content
- Growth: How fast you're gaining followers
- Quality: Content production value
- Consistency: How regularly you post
```

## Best Practices

### 1. Consistency

- Use same metrics across reports
- Report on consistent schedule
- Use same time periods for comparison
- Maintain consistent formatting

### 2. Accuracy

- Pull real data from platforms
- Don't estimate or guess
- Verify calculations
- Source all metrics

### 3. Actionability

- Every insight should have recommendation
- Every problem should have solution
- Every opportunity should have action plan
- Every question should lead to investigation

### 4. Visualization

- Use charts and graphs where helpful
- Color-code performance (green/yellow/red)
- Highlight key metrics
- Make data easy to scan

## Health Score Components

### Reach Score (0-100)

**What It Measures**: How many people are seeing your content

**Metrics**:
- Impressions (total views)
- Unique reach
- Reach vs. follower ratio
- Reach growth rate

**Calculation**:
```
Reach Score = ((Reach / Follower Count) × 50) + (Reach Growth Rate × 50)
```

**Benchmarks**:
- Excellent: 90-100 (reach exceeds followers significantly)
- Good: 70-89 (healthy reach)
- Fair: 50-69 (average reach)
- Poor: Under 50 (low reach)

---

### Engagement Score (0-100)

**What It Measures**: How people interact with your content

**Metrics**:
- Engagement rate ((likes + comments + shares) / reach)
- Comment quality (meaningful vs. emoji-only)
- Share rate
- Save/bookmark rate

**Calculation**:
```
Engagement Score = (Engagement Rate × 40) + (Share Rate × 30) + (Comment Quality × 30)
```

**Benchmarks**:
- Excellent: 90-100 (10%+ engagement rate)
- Good: 70-89 (5-10% engagement rate)
- Fair: 50-69 (2-5% engagement rate)
- Poor: Under 50 (under 2% engagement rate)

---

### Growth Score (0-100)

**What It Measures**: How fast you're gaining followers

**Metrics**:
- Follower growth rate
- Follower acquisition cost
- Follower retention rate
- Unfollow rate

**Calculation**:
```
Growth Score = (Growth Rate × 50) + (Retention Rate × 30) + (Unfollow Rate Penalty × 20)
```

**Benchmarks**:
- Excellent: 90-100 (10%+ monthly growth)
- Good: 70-89 (5-10% monthly growth)
- Fair: 50-69 (2-5% monthly growth)
- Poor: Under 50 (under 2% monthly growth)

---

### Quality Score (0-100)

**What It Measures**: Production value of your content

**Metrics**:
- Visual quality (subjective assessment)
- Audio quality (for video)
- Writing quality (for text)
- Professional polish

**Calculation**:
```
Quality Score = (Visual Quality × 40) + (Audio Quality × 30) + (Content Quality × 30)
```

**Benchmarks**:
- Excellent: 90-100 (professional broadcast quality)
- Good: 70-89 (high quality, some rough edges)
- Fair: 50-69 (acceptable quality)
- Poor: Under 50 (low quality)

---

### Consistency Score (0-100)

**What It Measures**: How regularly you post

**Metrics**:
- Posting frequency adherence
- Content calendar completion
- On-time posting rate
- Missed posts

**Calculation**:
```
Consistency Score = (Posts Completed / Posts Planned) × 100
```

**Benchmarks**:
- Excellent: 90-100 (95%+ completion)
- Good: 70-89 (80-95% completion)
- Fair: 50-69 (60-80% completion)
- Poor: Under 50 (under 60% completion)

## Action Item Priority Matrix

### High Priority (This Week)

**Criteria**:
- Algorithm changes requiring immediate action
- Viral trends (time-sensitive)
- Critical underperformance (health score under 60)
- Crisis management

**Examples**:
- "TikTok algorithm changed - adjust content strategy"
- "Viral trend emerging - create content today"
- "Instagram health score dropped - diagnose and fix"

### Medium Priority (Next 2-4 Weeks)

**Criteria**:
- Optimization opportunities
- Testing new formats
- Competitive responses
- Platform improvements

**Examples**:
- "Test YouTube Shorts format"
- "Optimize posting times based on data"
- "Respond to competitor's new content series"

### Low Priority (Next 1-3 Months)

**Criteria**:
- Long-term strategy considerations
- Nice-to-have improvements
- Exploratory projects
- Research and learning

**Examples**:
- "Explore Pinterest as new platform"
- "Develop new content series"
- "Research emerging platform features"

## Growth Opportunity Assessment

### Opportunity Scoring

```
Opportunity Score = (Potential Impact × 0.4) + (Ease of Implementation × 0.3) + (Time Sensitivity × 0.3)

Scoring:
- High Potential Impact: 8-10
- Medium Potential Impact: 5-7
- Low Potential Impact: 1-4

- Easy Implementation: 8-10
- Moderate Implementation: 5-7
- Difficult Implementation: 1-4

- High Time Sensitivity: 8-10
- Medium Time Sensitivity: 5-7
- Low Time Sensitivity: 1-4

Total Score:
- 20-30: Pursue immediately
- 15-19: High priority
- 10-14: Medium priority
- Under 10: Low priority
```

---

## Risk Assessment Framework

### Risk Scoring

```
Risk Score = (Probability × 0.5) + (Impact × 0.5)

Probability:
- High: 8-10 (very likely to occur)
- Medium: 5-7 (somewhat likely)
- Low: 1-4 (unlikely)

Impact:
- Critical: 8-10 (major damage)
- High: 6-7 (significant damage)
- Medium: 4-5 (moderate damage)
- Low: 1-3 (minor damage)

Total Score:
- 15-20: Critical risk (immediate action required)
- 10-14: High risk (plan and mitigate)
- 5-9: Medium risk (monitor)
- Under 5: Low risk (awareness only)
```
