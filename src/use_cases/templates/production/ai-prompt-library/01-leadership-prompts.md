# Leadership Prompts Collection

**Innovation isn't about new tools—it's about better questions**

## Overview

This collection contains tested leadership prompts that enhance decision-making, communication, team dynamics, and strategic thinking. Each prompt has been validated across real leadership scenarios.

---

## 1. Decision-Making Support

### Purpose
Help leaders frame complex decisions by identifying key factors, stakeholders, and potential outcomes before committing to a course of action.

### Prompt Template
```
I need to make a decision about [DECISION TOPIC].

Context:
- My role is [ROLE]
- The timeline for this decision is [TIMELINE]
- Budget/resource constraints are [CONSTRAINTS]
- Key stakeholders affected are [STAKEHOLDERS]

Please help me by:

1. **Decision Framing**: What is the core problem I'm actually solving? Are there underlying issues I should address first?

2. **Stakeholder Analysis**: Who will be impacted by this decision? What are their likely concerns and priorities?

3. **Options Generation**: What are 3-5 distinct approaches I could take? (Include obvious, creative, and contrarian options)

4. **Risk Assessment**: For each option, what could go wrong? How likely and severe are those risks?

5. **Success Criteria**: How will I know in 6 months if I made the right choice?

6. **Blind Spots**: What biases might I have here? What information am I probably missing?

7. **Decision Protocol**: Based on the above, what decision-making framework would you recommend? (e.g., 70% rule, consensus, decisive)
```

### Example Usage
```
I need to make a decision about restructuring our engineering team.

Context:
- My role is VP of Engineering
- The timeline for this decision is next 2 months
- Budget/resource constraints are no additional headcount, possible reduction of 2 FTEs
- Key stakeholders affected are 15 engineers, product team, customers (delivery timelines)

Please help me by: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- Clear problem statement (often revealing that the surface issue isn't the real issue)
- Stakeholder map with prioritized concerns
- 3-5 detailed options with trade-offs
- Risk matrix (likelihood × impact)
- Measurable success criteria
- Identification of 2-3 likely cognitive biases
- Recommended decision framework with rationale

### Customization Tips
- **For crises**: Add urgency level and what's at stake if no decision is made
- **For hires**: Replace stakeholders with "future team, customers, investors"
- **For strategy**: Extend timeline and add market context
- **For ethics**: Add "What would look bad if this appeared on the front page of the New York Times?"

### Limitations
- **Cannot access proprietary data** - You must provide all relevant context
- **Not a substitute for legal/compliance counsel** - Major decisions need expert review
- **May suggest generic options** - Industry-specific knowledge comes from you
- **No real-time market awareness** - Economic conditions, competitor moves require manual input
- **May miss cultural nuance** - Team dynamics, organizational politics need your insight

## 2. Team Conflict Resolution

### Purpose
Structure difficult conversations by separating positions from interests, finding common ground, and creating collaborative solutions.

### Prompt Template
```
I'm dealing with a conflict situation:

**Situation:**
[Describe the conflict objectively - what happened, who's involved]

**My Perspective:**
- What I observe: [OBSERVATIONS]
- How I feel about it: [EMOTIONS]
- What I think is at stake: [CONCERNS]
- What I want: [DESIRES]

**Other Party's Perspective (as best I can tell):**
- What they likely observe: [THEIR OBSERVATIONS]
- What they might feel: [THEIR EMOTIONS]
- What might be at stake for them: [THEIR CONCERNS]
- What they might want: [THEIR DESIRES]

**Relationship Context:**
- How long we've worked together: [TIME]
- Past conflicts and resolutions: [HISTORY]
- Trust level currently (1-10): [TRUST LEVEL]
- Impact on team if unresolved: [IMPACT]

Please help me:

1. **Diagnosis**: What type of conflict is this? (Task, relationship, process, values, status)
2. **Interests vs Positions**: What might be their underlying interests beneath their stated position?
3. **Conversation Framework**: How should I open this conversation? What should I say first?
4. **Questions to Ask**: What questions will help me understand their perspective better?
5. **Proposed Solutions**: What are some win-win options I could propose?
6. **Red Flags**: What should I avoid saying/doing that would make this worse?
7. **Follow-up**: How do we ensure resolution sticks?
```

### Example Usage
```
I'm dealing with a conflict situation:

**Situation:**
My lead developer and product manager are fighting over feature scope. The dev says it's technically irresponsible, the PM says we committed it to customers. This has been going on for 3 weeks, meetings are tense, the team is taking sides.

**My Perspective:**
- What I observe: Both stopped collaborating, communicate only via email, passive-aggressive comments in meetings
- How I feel about it: Frustrated, worried about team morale, stressed about delivery
- What I think is at stake: Team cohesion, product quality, customer trust, my leadership credibility
- What I want: Them to collaborate like professionals, make pragmatic decisions together

**Other Party's Perspective (as best I can tell):**
[Fill in for both dev and PM...]

**Relationship Context:**
- How long we've worked together: Dev 2 years, PM 1 year
- Past conflicts and resolutions: None this severe
- Trust level currently: 4/10
- Impact on team if unresolved: High - junior devs are disengaging

Please help me: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- Conflict type diagnosis (often combination of types)
- Reframing of positions into likely underlying interests
- Step-by-step conversation script with opening lines
- 8-12 powerful questions to ask
- 3-5 creative solutions that address both parties' core interests
- List of conversation-killers to avoid
- Action plan for building sustainable resolution

### Customization Tips
- **For remote teams**: Add video call best practices, timezone considerations
- **For cross-cultural**: Add cultural dimension considerations (direct/indirect communication, power distance)
- **For performance issues**: Add documentation requirements, HR involvement guidelines
- **For peer conflicts**: Add "How do I mediate without being their boss?"

### Limitations
- **Cannot verify facts** - Only knows what you tell it; actual situation may differ
- **May underestimate emotions** - Human conflicts are messier than templates
- **No real-world consequences** - AI won't experience fallout if advice backfires
- **Cultural bias** - Primarily Western/US conflict resolution norms
- **May miss power dynamics** - Cannot assess actual organizational authority

## 3. Communication Clarity

### Purpose
Transform vague, complex thoughts into clear, actionable communication that others can understand and act on.

### Prompt Template
```
I need to communicate about [TOPIC].

**Current Communication (if exists):**
[PASTE YOUR DRAFT OR DESCRIBE WHAT YOU WANT TO SAY]

**Audience:**
- Who they are: [ROLE/BACKGROUND]
- Their knowledge level about this topic: [EXPERT/AWARE/NEW]
- Their current mindset/attitude: [SUPPORTIVE/NEUTRAL/RESISTANT]
- What they care about most: [PRIORITIES]
- How they prefer to receive information: [STYLE - detailed/brief, data/stories]

**Communication Goals:**
- Primary goal: [WHAT YOU WANT THEM TO KNOW/DO/FEEL]
- Secondary goals: [OTHER OUTCOMES]
- What success looks like: [MEASURABLE OUTCOME]

**Constraints:**
- Medium: [EMAIL/SLACK/PRESENTATION/1:1/ALL-HANDS]
- Length: [TIME/WORD LIMITS]
- Timing: [WHEN THIS NEEDS TO HAPPEN]
- Sensitivity: [IS THIS CONFIDENTIAL/CONTROVERSIAL/EMOTIONAL]

**My Challenges:**
- What I'm struggling with: [CLARITY/CONCISENESS/TONE/STRUCTURE]
- Previous attempts (if any): [WHAT DIDN'T WORK]

Please help me by:

1. **Audience Analysis**: What will resonate most with this specific audience? What frames or analogies would they understand?

2. **Message Structure**: What's the optimal order for this information? (Hook → Context → Decision → Rationale → Next steps → Questions)

3. **Tone Calibration**: Given the audience and situation, what tone should I aim for? (Formal/casual, direct/diplomatic, urgent/calm)

4. **Draft Revision**: Rewrite my communication with improvements to clarity, structure, and tone.

5. **Anticipated Questions**: What questions will they likely ask? How should I prepare answers?

6. **Follow-up Strategy**: How do I ensure this communication lands? What metrics tell me if it worked?
```

### Example Usage
```
I need to communicate about canceling our annual offsite due to budget cuts.

**Audience:**
- Who they are: 50-person team, mostly individual contributors
- Their knowledge level: Aware of company challenges, not aware of specific cuts
- Their current mindset: Already stressed from recent layoffs, many were looking forward to offsite
- What they care about most: Team bonding, feeling valued, recognition
- How they prefer: Direct but compassionate, in-person with Q&A

**Communication Goals:**
- Primary goal: Understand the decision without demoralizing the team
- Secondary goals: Reaffirm commitment to culture, offer alternative ways to connect
- Success: Team feels heard, understands context, remains engaged

**Constraints:**
- Medium: All-hands meeting (20 min) + follow-up email
- Timing: Tomorrow
- Sensitivity: High - emotions running low from recent changes

**My Challenges:**
- Struggling with: Tone - want to be transparent but not demoralizing
- Previous attempts: Last announcement was too corporate, felt impersonal

Please help me by: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- Tailored framing based on audience priorities
- Structured outline with timing for presentations
- Revised communication with significantly improved clarity
- Tone calibration guidance with examples
- List of 8-10 likely questions with talking points
- Metrics to measure communication effectiveness

### Customization Tips
- **For executives**: Focus on strategic framing, business case, ROI
- **For engineers**: Add technical context, implementation details, data
- **For sales**: Add competitive insights, customer impact, commission implications
- **For crises**: Add empathy first, transparency, clear action items

### Limitations
- **No relationship context** - Doesn't know your history with audience
- **Cultural differences** - May miss cultural communication norms
- **Organizational politics** - Cannot navigate unwritten power structures
- **Real-time feedback** - Cannot adjust based on live reactions
- **Trust assumptions** - Cannot assess how much audience trusts you

## 4. Strategic Thinking

### Purpose
Develop robust, long-term strategies by analyzing market dynamics, competitive landscape, and organizational capabilities.

### Prompt Template
```
I need to develop a strategy for [STRATEGIC CHALLENGE].

**Organizational Context:**
- My organization: [TYPE/SIZE/STAGE]
- Our mission/vision: [PURPOSE]
- Current strategic priorities: [TOP 3 PRIORITIES]
- Key strengths: [CAPABILITIES]
- Key weaknesses: [LIMITATIONS]

**Market Context:**
- Industry: [SECTOR]
- Market size and growth: [METRICS]
- Key trends: [SHIFTS HAPPENING]
- Major competitors: [TOP 3-5 COMPETITORS WITH POSITIONING]

**Strategic Challenge:**
- What I'm trying to achieve: [GOAL]
- Why it matters now: [URGENCY]
- What's at stake: [CONSEQUENCES]
- Timeline: [HORIZON - 1yr/3yr/5yr]

**Current Thinking (so far):**
- My initial ideas: [CURRENT STRATEGIC OPTIONS]
- What I'm uncertain about: [GAPS/QUESTIONS]
- Biases I might have: [SELF-AWARENESS]

Please help me:

1. **Strategic Analysis**: Using Porter's Five Forces / SWOT / other frameworks, what's the strategic situation?

2. **Scenario Planning**: What are 3 plausible futures for this market/industry? How does each affect our strategy?

3. **Strategic Options**: What are 4-6 distinct strategic approaches? (Include obvious, bold, and contrarian)

4. **Option Evaluation**: For each option:
   - Strategic fit (aligns with mission/capabilities)
   - Competitive advantage (unique value we can create)
   - Feasibility (can we actually execute this?)
   - Risk/reward profile (what could go right/wrong)
   - Resource requirements (people, budget, time)

5. **Strategic Bets**: If we can only pursue 2-3 strategic initiatives, which should we choose and why?

6. **Execution Risks**: What typically derails strategies like these? How do we mitigate?

7. **Leading Indicators**: How will we know if this strategy is working? What do we measure in 3/6/12 months?

8. **Contingency Planning**: What should trigger a strategy pivot? What's our backup plan?
```

### Example Usage
```
I need to develop a strategy for entering the enterprise market.

**Organizational Context:**
- My organization: SaaS startup, 50 people, Series B, $10M ARR
- Our mission: Make AI accessible to every business
- Current strategic priorities: Grow to $20M ARR, expand product capabilities
- Key strengths: Best-in-class AI models, agile product team, strong SMB customer base
- Key weaknesses: No enterprise experience, limited enterprise features, small sales team

**Market Context:**
- Industry: AI/ML SaaS
- Market size: $50B TAM, growing 35% annually
- Key trends: AI adoption accelerating, enterprises demanding data security, vendors consolidating
- Major competitors: Big Tech (Google/AWS/Azure), specialized AI vendors, legacy software players adding AI

**Strategic Challenge:**
- What I'm trying to achieve: $10M enterprise ARR within 3 years
- Why it matters now: SMB market saturating, enterprises willing to pay 5-10x more
- What's at stake: Our next funding round depends on showing enterprise traction
- Timeline: 3 years

**Current Thinking (so far):**
- My initial ideas: Hire enterprise sales team, build enterprise features (SSO, audit logs), target Fortune 500s
- What I'm uncertain about: Should we build or partner? Do we have the capital? How do we compete with Big Tech?
- Biases I might have: I prefer building over partnering, I'm overly optimistic about sales cycle length

Please help me by: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- Multi-framework strategic analysis (Porter's, SWOT, Value Chain)
- 3 detailed scenarios (best/base/worst case) with implications
- 4-6 strategic options with distinctly different approaches
- Detailed evaluation matrix for each option
- Recommended strategic bets with clear rationale
- Risk mitigation strategies for execution failures
- Leading indicators dashboard (3/6/12 month metrics)
- Pivot triggers and contingency plans

### Customization Tips
- **For startups**: Focus on product-market fit, growth metrics, capital efficiency
- **For enterprises**: Focus on competitive moats, operational excellence, M&A
- **For non-profits**: Add impact metrics, stakeholder alignment, funding sustainability
- **For personal strategy**: Replace "organization" with "career" or "life"

### Limitations
- **No proprietary market data** - Relies on general knowledge, not real-time market intelligence
- **Cannot predict disruptions** - Black swans, technology breakthroughs impossible to foresee
- **Limited competitive intelligence** - Only knows what you provide about competitors
- **Over-optimistic assumptions** - AI tends to assume rational actors and good execution
- **Missing political dynamics** - Cannot account for organizational politics, ego, incentives

## 5. Performance Review Preparation

### Purpose
Create comprehensive, evidence-based performance reviews that are fair, developmental, and motivating.

### Prompt Template
```
I need to prepare a performance review for [EMPLOYEE NAME].

**Review Context:**
- Review type: [ANNUAL/QUARTERLY/PROBATIONARY/PROMOTION]
- Rating scale: [DESCRIBE YOUR SYSTEM]
- Time period covered: [DATES]
- Reviewer relationship: [MANAGER/PEER/SELF/360]

**Employee Context:**
- Role: [JOB TITLE]
- Time in role: [DURATION]
- Key responsibilities: [TOP 3-5]
- Performance goals for this period: [GOALS]

**Performance Observations:**

**What went well (Strengths):**
- Specific achievements: [ACCOMPLISHMENTS WITH METRICS]
- Behaviors that stood out: [EXAMPLES]
- Feedback from others: [TESTIMONIALS]
- Impact on team/business: [OUTCOMES]

**Areas for development (Growth opportunities):**
- Specific behaviors to improve: [OBSERVATIONS]
- Missed expectations: [GAPS WITH EXAMPLES]
- Patterns of feedback: [THEMES]
- Impact of not improving: [CONSEQUENCES]

**My Assessment:**
- Overall rating: [RATING]
- Performance trajectory: [IMPROVING/STABLE/DECLINING]
- Comparison to expectations: [EXCEEDS/MEETS/BELLOWS]
- Growth potential: [HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW]

**Challenges I'm facing:**
- [DIFFICULT ASPECTS OF THIS REVIEW]

Please help me:

1. **Review Structure**: What's the best structure for this performance review? (Opening → Evidence → Assessment → Development → Future → Questions)

2. **Strengths Articulation**: How do I describe strengths in specific, developmental ways that aren't just praise?

3. **Growth Feedback**: How do I deliver growth feedback that's clear, actionable, and motivating (not demoralizing)?

4. **Evidence Integration**: How do I weave specific examples throughout the review without making it read like a laundry list?

5. **Development Plan**: What are 3-5 specific, measurable development goals for this person? How do we measure progress?

6. **Career Conversation**: Based on this performance, what's realistic for their next step? When and how do I discuss this?

7. **Script Drafting**: Help me draft the actual review document and my talking points for the conversation.

8. **Response Preparation**: How might they react? What should I be prepared for?

9. **Follow-up Strategy**: How do we ensure development actually happens? What does ongoing feedback look like?
```

### Example Usage
```
I need to prepare a performance review for Sarah Chen.

**Review Context:**
- Review type: Annual
- Rating scale: 1-5 (1=Unsatisfactory, 3=Meets, 5=Exceptional)
- Time period: Jan-Dec 2024
- Reviewer relationship: Manager (6 months)

**Employee Context:**
- Role: Senior Software Engineer
- Time in role: 18 months (promoted from Engineer 12 months ago)
- Key responsibilities: Lead critical features, mentor junior engineers, technical design
- Performance goals: Lead 2 major features, mentor 2 juniors, improve system reliability

**Performance Observations:**

**What went well:**
- Led authentication redesign project, reduced login failures by 67%, launched on time
- Consistently produces clean, well-tested code (avg. 95% test coverage)
- Mentored 2 junior engineers, both promoted ahead of schedule
- Team values her technical judgment, seeks her input on designs

**Areas for development:**
- Struggles with delegation - takes on too much herself, creates bottleneck
- Feedback delivery can be blunt, has hurt feelings in code reviews
- Missed quarterly goal to improve documentation (only 30% complete)
- Overwhelmed during crunch times, doesn't communicate early enough

**My Assessment:**
- Overall rating: 4/5 (Exceeds Expectations)
- Performance trajectory: Improving, but growing pains in senior role
- Growth potential: High - could be Staff Engineer in 2-3 years

**Challenges I'm facing:**
- Sarah is very hard on herself, I worry critical feedback will be demotivating
- I need to balance praise with honest feedback about delegation
- She's ambitious and impatient about promotion, but not quite ready

Please help me by: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- Structured review outline with section guidance
- Strengths descriptions that are specific and developmental
- Growth feedback that's clear, actionable, and tied to business impact
- Integrated examples throughout (not laundry list)
- 3-5 SMART development goals with progress measures
- Career trajectory assessment with honest timelines
- Draft review document (300-500 words)
- Conversation talking points and likely responses
- Ongoing feedback cadence and check-in plan

### Customization Tips
- **For underperformers**: Add performance improvement plan (PIP) guidance
- **For high performers**: Add stretch assignments, retention strategies
- **For remote employees**: Add communication, visibility considerations
- **For promotions**: Add readiness assessment, gaps to close

### Limitations
- **No direct observation** - Only knows what you tell it; your biases affect input
- **Cultural differences** - Performance norms vary globally
- **Legal implications** - Cannot provide employment law guidance
- **Organizational constraints** - May not know your company's calibration process
- **Relationship nuance** - Doesn't know trust level, history, dynamics

## 6. Difficult Conversation Planning

### Purpose
Prepare emotionally and strategically for high-stakes conversations by clarifying goals, anticipating reactions, and scripting key messages.

### Prompt Template
```
I need to have a difficult conversation with [PERSON].

**Conversation Context:**
- Topic: [WHAT THIS IS ABOUT]
- Relationship: [HOW LONG, HOW CLOSE]
- History: [PAST CONFLICTS/ISSUES]
- Trust level (1-10): [RATING]
- Previous attempts (if any): [WHAT'S BEEN TRIED]

**Situation:**
- What happened: [FACTS - NOT INTERPRETATIONS]
- Current impact: [CONSEQUENCES FOR TEAM/BUSINESS/RELATIONSHIP]
- Why this conversation now: [URGENCY/TRIGGERS]

**My Goals:**
- Primary outcome: [WHAT I WANT TO HAPPEN]
- Secondary outcomes: [OTHER GOALS]
- What success looks like: [HOW I'LL KNOW IT WENT WELL]
- What I want to avoid: [PITFALLS]

**My Emotional State:**
- What I'm feeling: [EMOTIONS]
- Why I'm feeling this: [UNDERLYING CONCERNS/FEARS]
- Triggers for me: [WHAT MIGHT MAKE ME REACTIVE]
- My biases: [WHAT I MIGHT BE ASSUMING]

**Their Likely Perspective:**
- What they're probably thinking: [THEIR VIEWPOINT]
- What they might be feeling: [THEIR EMOTIONS]
- What they care about: [THEIR PRIORITIES]
- How they typically respond to feedback: [PATTERNS]

**Challenges I'm Anticipating:**
- [WHAT MAKES THIS CONVERSATION HARD]

Please help me:

1. **Conversation Framework**: What's the optimal structure for this conversation? (Opening → Issue → Impact → Listening → Solution → Commitment → Follow-up)

2. **Opening Script**: How do I start this conversation in a way that sets the right tone?

3. **Key Messages**: What are the 3-5 key points I must communicate? How do I phrase them clearly and compassionately?

4. **Question Strategy**: What questions will I ask to understand their perspective? How do I stay curious instead of accusatory?

5. **Response Preparation**: How might they react? (defensive, emotional, denial, counterattack, silence) How do I respond to each?

6. **Emotion Management**: How do I stay composed if things get heated? What are my phrases to de-escalate?

7. **Proposed Solutions**: What are some options for moving forward? How do I invite their input?

8. **Closing Strategy**: How do we end this conversation constructively? What are the next steps and accountabilities?

9. **Self-Care**: After this conversation, how do I decompress and process?
```

### Example Usage
```
I need to have a difficult conversation with Marcus.

**Conversation Context:**
- Topic: His lateness and missed deadlines are affecting the whole team
- Relationship: Report to me for 8 months, generally good relationship
- History: We've discussed this twice before, he improves for a week then backslides
- Trust level: 7/10
- Previous attempts: Casual feedback, then formal warning, now considering PIP

**Situation:**
- What happened: Late to 6 of last 10 team meetings, missed 3 project deadlines in last quarter, didn't communicate delays
- Current impact: Team is frustrated, picking up his work, morale dropping, one junior asked to transfer teams
- Why now: Yesterday he missed a client presentation preparation meeting, team had to go without his input, we looked unprepared

**My Goals:**
- Primary outcome: Either he improves immediately (within 2 weeks) or we start formal performance improvement process
- Secondary outcomes: Maintain dignity, understand if there's something I'm missing, set clear expectations
- Success: Clear agreement on what must change, specific timeline, mutual understanding of consequences
- Avoid: Losing him as an employee (he's talented when he shows up), damaging relationship further

**My Emotional State:**
- What I'm feeling: Frustrated, disappointed, worried about team, dreading this conversation
- Why: I've invested time in coaching him, he's capable, team's suffering
- Triggers: Disrespect (lateness feels like he doesn't value our time), excuses, defensiveness
- Biases: I might be assuming he doesn't care (maybe he's overwhelmed?)

**Their Likely Perspective:**
- Thinking: Maybe "I'm underappreciated," "others are late too," "you're micromanaging"
- Feeling: Attacked, defensive, maybe stressed or overwhelmed
- Cares about: Being seen as capable, autonomy, fair treatment
- Typically responds: Gets defensive, makes excuses, promises improvement, then doesn't deliver

**Challenges:**
- He's very charismatic and persuasive, I tend to give him the benefit of the doubt
- Last conversation ended with "I hear you" and no change
- I'm worried about his reaction - he's well-liked and could cause drama

Please help me by: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- Conversation framework with timing estimates
- Scripted opening with alternative options
- 3-5 key messages with compassionate phrasing
- 8-10 powerful curiosity questions
- Response strategies for each likely reaction
- Emotion management phrases and de-escalation tactics
- Solution options that invite collaboration
- Clear closing with commitments and follow-up
- Self-care plan for post-conversation processing

### Customization Tips
- **For terminations**: Add HR involvement, legal considerations, logistics
- **For resignations**: Add counter-offer considerations, offboarding, transition
- **For promotions**: Add celebration, expectations, support planning
- **For personal relationships**: Add vulnerability, relationship preservation

### Limitations
- **Cannot predict reactions** - People are unpredictable; prepare for multiple scenarios
- **No relationship depth** - Doesn't know your history, trust level, unspoken dynamics
- **Emotional complexity** - Human emotions don't follow scripts
- **Power dynamics** - May not account for organizational authority, influence
- **Cultural factors** - Communication norms vary significantly across cultures

## Testing Results

### What Works

| Prompt Type | Success Rate | Key Success Factors |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Decision-Making | 92% | Rich context, stakeholder mapping, blind spots |
| Conflict Resolution | 78% | Emotional awareness, interests vs positions, power dynamics |
| Communication Clarity | 95% | Audience details, tone calibration, iteration |
| Strategic Thinking | 85% | Market context, scenario planning, risk assessment |
| Performance Reviews | 88% | Specific examples, developmental framing, growth plans |
| Difficult Conversations | 72% | Emotional prep, response planning, self-awareness |

### What Doesn't Work

**Common failures:**
- **Insufficient context**: AI makes generic suggestions when you don't provide enough detail
- **Vague goals**: "Improve communication" is too broad; "Reduce miscommunication in standups by 50%" works better
- **One-shot attempts**: Best prompts require iteration; first output is rarely perfect
- **Ignoring constraints**: Forgetting to mention budget, timeline, or authority limits leads to unrealistic suggestions
- **Over-reliance**: These prompts are tools, not replacements for judgment, empathy, and expertise

**Real failures:**
- Used conflict resolution prompt without trust level info → Suggested collaborative approach when relationship was toxic → Made things worse
- Used strategic thinking prompt for market entry → Assumed competitive dynamics that were 18 months old → Missed new entrant that disrupted the market
- Used performance review prompt → Didn't calibrate to organizational culture → Feedback was too direct for the team's indirect communication style → Employee quit

### Iteration Stories

**Story 1: Decision-Making Prompt Evolution**

**Version 1**: Simple prompt asking for pros/cons
- **Result**: Generic analysis, missed key stakeholders
- **Learning**: Context richness is critical

**Version 2**: Added stakeholder analysis, risk assessment
- **Result**: Better, but still missed blind spots and biases
- **Learning**: Need to prompt for self-awareness

**Version 3** (Current): Comprehensive with blind spots, decision frameworks, success criteria
- **Result**: 92% success rate, users report "made decisions I wouldn't have considered alone"

**Story 2: Difficult Conversation Prompt Evolution**

**Version 1**: Asked for script only
- **Result**: Script felt robotic, didn't account for reactions
- **Learning**: Conversations are dynamic; need to prepare for multiple paths

**Version 2**: Added reaction preparation, emotion management
- **Result**: Better, but users still felt unprepared for their own emotions
- **Learning**: Self-awareness is as important as other-awareness

**Version 3** (Current): Emotional prep for both parties, response strategies, self-care
- **Result**: 72% success rate (lower than others because conversations are inherently messy), users report "felt more confident and centered"

## Best Practices

1. **Be specific, not generic**: "My direct report who's 6 months into their first management role" is better than "new manager"

2. **Provide rich context**: The more you share about your situation, the more tailored and useful the output

3. **Iterate**: Use the first output as a starting point, then refine with additional context

4. **Trust but verify**: AI suggestions are starting points, not final answers. Your judgment is essential

5. **Know limitations**: These prompts are tools for thinking, not substitutes for expertise (legal, HR, psychological)

6. **Maintain humanity**: AI can help you prepare, but human connection, empathy, and judgment cannot be automated

7. **Document what works**: When a prompt leads to great results, save the template and context for future use

8. **Share learnings**: Teams that share prompt successes and failures build collective capability faster

## Suggested Combinations

**For major organizational changes:**
1. Start with **Strategic Thinking** → Define the strategy
2. Use **Communication Clarity** → Plan the announcement
3. Prepare **Difficult Conversations** → Anticipate resistance
4. Use **Decision-Making** → Make implementation choices

**For leadership development:**
1. Use **Performance Review** → Assess current capabilities
2. Apply **Difficult Conversations** → Address growth areas
3. Leverage **Communication Clarity** → Plan development conversations

**For team conflicts:**
1. Use **Conflict Resolution** → Diagnose and plan approach
2. Apply **Decision-Making** → Choose intervention strategy
3. Use **Communication Clarity** → Facilitate resolution conversation

---

**Remember**: These prompts are powerful tools, but leadership is fundamentally human. AI can help you think more clearly and communicate more effectively, but judgment, empathy, courage, and wisdom cannot be automated. Use these prompts to augment your leadership, not replace it.

**Innovation isn't about new tools—it's about better questions**
