# Business & Strategy Prompts Collection

**Innovation isn't about new tools—it's about better questions**

## Overview

This collection contains tested prompts for business model brainstorming, competitive analysis, customer research, marketing strategy, product development, and process improvement.

---

## 1. Business Model Brainstorming

### Purpose
Generate, evaluate, and refine business models that create, deliver, and capture value.

### Prompt Template
```
I need to brainstorm business models for [BUSINESS/IDEA].

**Business Context:**
- Business type: [STARTUP/EXISTING BUSINESS/SIDE PROJECT/CONSULTING]
- Industry: [SECTOR]
- Stage: [IDEA/VALIDATION/GROWTH/SCALE]
- Current status: [WHAT EXISTS NOW]

**My Idea/Product:**
- What it is: [DESCRIPTION]
- Problem it solves: [PAIN POINT]
- Who it serves: [TARGET CUSTOMER]
- Why now: [TIMING/MOMENTUM]

**My Constraints:**
- Budget: [AVAILABLE CAPITAL]
- Time: [HORIZON]
- Team: [WHO'S AVAILABLE]
- Skills: [WHAT I'M GOOD AT]

**My Goals:**
- Revenue target: [GOALS]
- Lifestyle vs. growth: [BUILD TO SELL/BUILD TO KEEP/LIFESTYLE BUSINESS]
- Risk tolerance: [CONSERVATIVE/MODERATE/AGGRESSIVE]

Please help me brainstorm business models by:

1. **Value Proposition Canvas**:
   - **Customer Jobs**: What are your customers trying to get done? (functional, social, emotional jobs)
   - **Pains**: What annoys them before, during, or after the job? (risks, obstacles, undesirable outcomes)
   - **Gains**: What outcomes do they desire? (benefits, aspirations)
   - **Products & Services**: What are you offering? (products, services, value)
   - **Pain Relievers**: How do you eliminate pains? (painkillers)
   - **Gain Creators**: How do you create gains? (value-added benefits)

2. **Business Model Patterns**: Explore different business model types:
   - **Subscription**: Recurring revenue, ongoing value
   - **Marketplace**: Connect buyers and sellers, take transaction fee
   - **Freemium**: Free basic tier, paid premium features
   - **Usage-based**: Pay for what you use
   - **Licensing/IP**: License technology or intellectual property
   - **Market Create**: Create a new market
   - **Ecosystem**: Platform with multiple beneficiaries
   - **Experience**: Sell an experience, not a product
   - **Long Tail**: Serve many niche markets
   - **Multi-sided Platform**: Connect multiple user groups
   - Which pattern(s) fit? Why?

3. **Revenue Model Options**: How will you make money?
   - **Pricing strategy**: What will you charge? (one-time, subscription, usage, tiered, freemium, etc.)
   - **Revenue streams**: Where does money come from? (primary and secondary sources)
   - **Pricing tactics**: How will you price? (cost-plus, value-based, competitive, penetration, skimming, dynamic)
   - **Monetization timeline**: When will you charge? (immediate, delayed, gradual)
   - Generate 5-10 revenue model options

4. **Customer Segmentation**: Who will you serve?
   - **Demographics**: Age, location, income, etc.
   - **Psychographics**: Values, interests, lifestyle
   - **Behavior**: Usage patterns, loyalty, benefits sought
   - **Needs-based**: Group by needs/problems
   - **Segments**: Identify 3-5 potential customer segments
   - **Beachhead**: Which segment will you target first? Why?

5. **Channel Strategy**: How will you reach customers?
   - **Direct**: Website, sales team, retail
   - **Indirect**: Distributors, resellers, marketplaces
   - **Digital**: SEO, content, social, paid ads, email
   - **Partnerships**: Co-marketing, affiliate, white-label
   - **Optimal mix**: What's the best channel strategy for your business?

6. **Customer Relationships**: How will you acquire, keep, and grow customers?
   - **Acquisition**: How will you get customers? (marketing, sales, referrals)
   - **Retention**: How will you keep customers? (service, community, switching costs)
   - **Growth**: How will you grow customers? (upsell, cross-sell, expansion)
   - **Relationship type**: Self-service, dedicated personal assistance, community, co-creation
   - **Cost**: What's your CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)? LTV (Lifetime Value)?

7. **Key Resources**: What do you need?
   - **Physical**: Manufacturing, facilities, equipment
   - **Intellectual**: IP, data, brand, partnerships
   - **Human**: Team, skills, advisors
   - **Financial**: Cash, credit, investment
   - Prioritize: What's critical? What's nice-to-have?

8. **Key Activities**: What must you do?
   - **Production**: Making, designing, building
   - **Problem-solving**: Consulting, problem-solving services
   - **Platform/network**: Software, platforms, matching
   - **Marketing/sales**: Lead generation, conversion
   - Prioritize: What's core? What can be outsourced?

9. **Key Partnerships**: Who will help you?
   - **Strategic alliances**: Non-competitors, complementary offerings
   - **Coopetition**: Competitors in some areas, partners in others
   - **Joint ventures**: New joint business
   - **Buyer-supplier**: Reliable supply chain
   - Who do you need? Why?

10. **Cost Structure**: What will it cost?
    - **Fixed costs**: Rent, salaries, utilities (costs that don't change with volume)
    - **Variable costs**: Materials, shipping, transaction fees (costs that scale with volume)
    - **Economies of scale**: Costs decrease as volume increases
    - **Economies of scope**: Costs decrease as variety increases
    - **Cost drivers**: What are your biggest costs? How do you reduce them?

11. **Financial Projections**: Model the economics:
    - **Startup costs**: One-time costs to launch
    - **Operating costs**: Monthly/annual ongoing costs
    - **Revenue ramp**: How revenue grows over time (months 1-24)
    - **Break-even**: When will you cover costs?
    - **Unit economics**: Per customer/unit (LTV, CAC, margin)
    - **Funding needed**: How much capital do you need? When?

12. **Risk Assessment**: What could go wrong?
    - **Market risk**: No market, competition, timing
    - **Product risk**: Can't build, doesn't work, inferior
    - **Team risk**: Wrong team, founder conflict, lack of skills
    - **Financial risk**: Run out of cash, can't raise, unit economics don't work
    - **Regulatory risk**: Legal barriers, regulations
    - **Mitigation**: How do you reduce each risk?

13. **Business Model Iteration**: Now, create 3 distinct business models:
    - **Model A (Conservative)**: Lower risk, slower growth, proven approach
    - **Model B (Balanced)**: Moderate risk, moderate growth, hybrid approach
    - **Model C (Aggressive)**: Higher risk, faster growth, innovative approach
    - For each model: Outline key elements, pros/cons, viability score (1-10)

14. **Selection & Refinement**: Which model is best?
    - Evaluate each model against: Goals, constraints, risk tolerance, skills
    - Which model maximizes your strengths?
    - Which model minimizes your weaknesses?
    - What's the hybrid model that combines the best elements?
    - Refine the chosen model: What's missing? What should you remove?

15. **Validation Plan**: How do you validate this model?
    - **Assumptions**: What are your core assumptions? (market, pricing, features, etc.)
    - **Tests**: How will you test each assumption? (interviews, landing pages, MVP, etc.)
    - **Metrics**: What are your success criteria? (signups, revenue, retention, etc.)
    - **Timeline**: When will you know? (2 weeks, 2 months, 6 months?)
    - **Pivot criteria**: When do you pivot vs. persevere?
```

### Example Usage
```
I need to brainstorm business models for an AI-powered productivity coaching app.

**Business Context:**
- Type: Startup (early stage)
- Industry: Productivity / B2B SaaS
- Stage: Idea phase (built prototype, testing with 20 users)
- Status: MVP exists, early feedback positive, no revenue yet

**My Idea:**
- What: AI app that learns your work patterns, provides personalized productivity coaching, and automates routine tasks
- Problem: Knowledge workers are overwhelmed, drowning in information, can't focus on what matters
- Who serves: Knowledge workers (software engineers, designers, marketers) at startups and SMBs
- Why now: AI capability explosion, remote work acceleration, burnout epidemic

**My Constraints:**
- Budget: $50k savings, can bootstrap 12 months
- Time: Have 6 months to prove traction before needing funding
- Team: Me (technical founder, ex-Google engineer), considering hiring part-time designer
- Skills: Engineering, product, some marketing (weak), weak on sales

**My Goals:**
- Revenue: $10k MRR within 12 months
- Type: Build to grow (venture-backable, aim for $10M ARR within 3 years)
- Risk: Moderate (willing to take calculated risks, but not reckless)

Please help me brainstorm business models by: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- **Value Proposition**:
  - Customer Jobs: Deep work, meet deadlines, feel productive, manage overwhelm, avoid burnout
  - Pains: Constant interruptions, too many tools, context switching, information overload, guilt about not being productive
  - Gains: Focus, progress on important work, feeling in control, work-life balance, mastery
  - Pain Relievers: AI filters distractions, automates busywork, personalized coaching
  - Gain Creators: Achieve goals faster, feel accomplished, energy left at end of day

- **Business Model Patterns**:
  - **Primary**: Subscription (SaaS) - recurring revenue, ongoing value
  - **Secondary**: Freemium - free tier to acquire, paid tier for power users
  - **Tertiary**: B2B focus - sell to teams, not just individuals

- **Revenue Models** (5 options):
  1. **Individual Subscription**: $29/month per user, B2C focus
  2. **Team Subscription**: $49/user/month for teams of 5+, B2B focus
  3. **Freemium**: Free basic tier, $19/month Pro, $49/month Team
  4. **Usage-based**: Free up to 100 AI interactions/day, then $0.10 per interaction
  5. **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for 100+ employees, on-premise option

- **Customer Segments**:
  1. **Individual knowledge workers** (engineers, designers, writers) - early adopters
  2. **Startups (5-50 employees)** - team productivity, collaboration
  3. **SMBs (50-500 employees)** - employee wellness, productivity at scale
  - **Beachhead**: Individual knowledge workers (easier to reach, validate, iterate)

- **Channel Strategy**:
  - **Primary**: Content marketing (blog, newsletter) + SEO (productivity keywords)
  - **Secondary**: Social media (Twitter/X, LinkedIn) + community (Discord, Slack groups)
  - **Tertiary**: Product Hunt launch, tech press, podcasts (later stage)
  - **Direct**: Website, self-signup, product-led growth

- **Key Resources**:
  - **Critical**: AI technology, product development, brand/trust
  - **Important**: Customer support, content creation, user feedback loop
  - **Nice-to-have**: Sales team, marketing budget, office space

- **Key Activities**:
  - **Core**: AI development, product improvement, user research
  - **Important**: Content creation, customer support, community building
  - **Outsourcable**: Bookkeeping, legal, some marketing

- **Cost Structure** (monthly):
  - **Fixed**: Server costs ($2k), founder salary ($5k - minimal), software tools ($500) = $7.5k
  - **Variable**: AI API costs ($0.05/user), payment processing (2.9% + $0.30)
  - **Economies of scale**: Server costs decrease per user as user base grows

- **Financial Projections**:
  - **Startup costs**: Legal ($3k), design freelancer ($5k), initial server setup ($1k) = $9k
  - **Month 1-3**: 50 users, $1,450 MRR (freemium: 5% conversion)
  - **Month 4-6**: 200 users, $5,800 MRR, launched Product Hunt
  - **Month 7-12**: 1,000 users, $29,000 MRR, $10k MRR goal ✓
  - **Break-even**: Month 5 (MRR > $7.5k costs)
  - **Unit economics**: LTV $348/year, CAC $50, payback 2 months

- **Risk Assessment**:
  - **Market**: High competition (Notion, Todoist, etc.) - mitigate with AI differentiation
  - **Product**: AI doesn't work well enough - mitigate with continuous improvement, human feedback
  - **Team**: Only founder, burnout risk - mitigate with hiring, pace
  - **Financial**: Run out of cash - mitigate with frugality, clear funding strategy
  - **Mitigation**: Validate early, stay lean, raise seed when traction proven

- **3 Business Models**:
  - **Model A (Conservative)**: B2C individual subscription, $29/month, content marketing only, bootstrap to profitability
  - **Model B (Balanced)**: Freemium B2B/B2C hybrid, team focus at 5+ users, content + paid growth channels, raise seed at $10k MRR
  - **Model C (Aggressive)**: Free product, growth-focused, monetize through enterprise deals later, raise pre-seed now, burn cash for growth

- **Selected Model**: Model B (Balanced)
  - **Why**: Validates both B2C and B2B, manageable risk, fundable trajectory, leverages founder strengths (product, content)
  - **Refinement**: Start B2C for 6 months, learn from users, then launch team features and B2B motion

- **Validation Plan**:
  - **Assumption 1**: People will pay for AI productivity coaching
    - **Test**: Pre-sale landing page, waitlist, offer $19/month lifetime discount
    - **Metric**: 100 waitlist signups, 10 pre-orders (10% conversion)
    - **Timeline**: 2 weeks
  - **Assumption 2**: AI provides measurable productivity improvement
    - **Test**: 30-day beta with 50 users, measure productivity before/after
    - **Metric**: 70% report 20%+ productivity gain, 50% convert to paid
    - **Timeline**: 2 months
  - **Assumption 3**: Teams will pay for productivity at scale
    - **Test**: Offer to 10 startups (5-20 employees), free 3-month trial, then $49/user/month
    - **Metric**: 5/10 convert to paid (50%), $500+ MRR per team
    - **Timeline**: 3 months

### Customization Tips
- **For products**: Focus on manufacturing, distribution, inventory, retail
- **For services**: Focus on delivery, expertise, time-for-money vs. productizing
- **For platforms**: Focus on network effects, liquidity, multi-sided value
- **For marketplaces**: Focus on chicken-egg problem (supply vs. demand)

### Limitations
- **No market data** - Cannot provide actual market size, pricing research
- **Competition dynamics** - Real competition is more complex than templates
- **Execution risk** - A model is only as good as execution
- **Market changes** - Economic shifts, technology changes, new competitors
- **Founder psychology** - Models don't account for motivation, resilience, luck

## 2. Competitive Analysis

### Purpose
Understand your competitive landscape to differentiate effectively and find your market position.

### Prompt Template
```
I need to analyze my competition.

**Business Context:**
- My business: [DESCRIPTION]
- My industry: [SECTOR]
- My target market: [CUSTOMERS]
- My stage: [IDEA/LAUNCHED/GROWTH]

**My Competitors:**
- Direct competitors: [WHO THEY ARE]
- Indirect competitors: [ALTERNATIVES]
- Potential competitors: [WHO COULD ENTER]

**What I Know:**
- My strengths: [WHAT I DO BETTER]
- My weaknesses: [WHERE I'M BEHIND]
- My differentiation: [WHAT MAKES ME UNIQUE]

Please help me with a competitive analysis:

1. **Competitor Identification**:
   - **Direct competitors**: Who solves the same problem for the same customer?
   - **Indirect competitors**: Who solves a similar problem or serves the same customer with a different solution?
   - **Substitutes**: What alternatives do customers use? (including "do nothing")
   - **New entrants**: Who could enter this market? (big tech, startups, adjacent players)
   - **Competitor map**: Where does each competitor sit in the market? (budget vs. premium, mainstream vs. niche)

2. **Competitor Deep Dive** (for top 3-5 competitors):
   - **Company**: Name, founded, size, funding/revenue
   - **Product**: What do they offer? Features, capabilities
   - **Price**: How much do they charge? Pricing model
   - **Customer**: Who do they serve? Segments, use cases
   - **Positioning**: How do they position themselves? (brand, messaging)
   - **Strengths**: What are they great at? (product, brand, distribution, etc.)
   - **Weaknesses**: Where do they fall short? (chinks in the armor)
   - **Market share**: How big are they? (customers, revenue, growth)

3. **Feature Comparison**:
   - Create a feature matrix: Your product vs. top 3 competitors
   - **Must-have features**: What do all competitors have? (table stakes)
   - **Differentiated features**: What makes each competitor unique?
   - **Missing features**: What doesn't exist yet? (opportunities)
   - **Over-featured**: What features do customers ignore? (bloat)

4. **Price Comparison**:
   - Compare pricing models (subscription, one-time, usage, etc.)
   - Compare price points (budget, mid-market, premium)
   - **Value proposition**: At each price point, what value do customers get?
   - **Pricing gaps**: Where are there pricing opportunities? (too expensive, too cheap)

5. **Customer Experience Comparison**:
   - **Onboarding**: How easy is it to get started? (time, friction)
   - **Support**: What support do they offer? (self-service, chat, phone, etc.)
   - **Community**: Do they have user communities? How active?
   - **Retention**: How do they keep customers? (lock-in, switching costs, loyalty)
   - **NPS/Satisfaction**: What are customer satisfaction scores? (if available)

6. **Marketing & Sales Comparison**:
   - **Channels**: Where do they acquire customers? (content, ads, SEO, events, etc.)
   - **Content**: What content do they create? (blog, video, podcast, etc.)
   - **Brand**: What's their brand personality? (serious, playful, premium, accessible, etc.)
   - **Sales model**: Self-service, inside sales, field sales, marketplaces?
   - **Reviews**: What do customers say? (G2, Capterra, App Store, etc.)

7. **Strategic Positioning**:
   - **Positioning map**: Plot competitors on 2-3 dimensions (e.g., price vs. features, simplicity vs. power, B2B vs. B2C)
   - **Positioning statements**: "For [target customer] who [need], [product] is a [category] that [benefit]. Unlike [competitor], we [differentiation]."
   - **Market gaps**: Where are there unoccupied positions? (opportunities)
   - **Crowded areas**: Where is everyone competing? (avoid or differentiate)

8. **SWOT Analysis** (for each major competitor):
   - **Strengths**: Internal advantages (product, brand, team, capital)
   - **Weaknesses**: Internal disadvantages (talent, technology, focus)
   - **Opportunities**: External opportunities they could pursue
   - **Threats**: External threats they face (disruption, regulation, competition)

9. **Competitive Advantage Assessment**:
   - **Sustainable advantages**: What advantages are hard to copy? (network effects, IP, switching costs, brand)
   - **Temporary advantages**: What advantages are easily copied? (features, pricing, marketing)
   - **Moats**: What protects each business? (technology, brand, network effects, cost, switching costs)
   - **Your advantages**: What's your defensible advantage? (if any)

10. **Competitive Strategy**:
    - **Differentiation**: How will you differentiate? (product, service, brand, price, niche)
    - **Focus**: Where will you focus? (customer segment, use case, geography, price point)
    - **Disruption**: How will you disrupt? (new market, low-end, high-end)
    - **Blue ocean**: Is there an uncompetitive space? (new market, new demand)

11. **Competitive Intelligence Plan**:
    - **What to track**: Competitor product updates, pricing changes, marketing campaigns, hiring, funding, partnerships
    - **How to track**: Google Alerts, social media, newsletters, analyst reports, customer interviews
    - **How often**: Weekly, monthly, quarterly?
    - **Who owns it**: Who's responsible for competitive intelligence?

12. **Competitive Response Strategy**:
    - **If competitor launches feature X**: How do you respond? (match, ignore, differentiate)
    - **If competitor lowers price**: How do you respond? (match, emphasize value, add features)
    - **If competitor raises funding**: How do you respond? (accelerate, focus, find niche)
    - **If new competitor enters**: How do you respond? (defend, differentiate, partner)

13. **Winning Against Competitors**:
    - **Where you win**: In which scenarios/criteria do customers choose you?
    - **Where you lose**: In which scenarios/criteria do customers choose competitors?
    - **Ideal customer profile**: Who's your best-fit customer? (who you can win)
    - **Displacement strategy**: How do you displace competitors? (migration, onboarding, incentives)

14. **Competitive Matrix**: Create a visual summary:
    - Rows: Competitors (you + top 3-5)
    - Columns: Price, Features, Ease of Use, Support, Brand, Integration, Customization, Speed
    - Cells: ✓, ✗, or rating (1-5)
    - Highlight: Where you win (green), where you lose (red), where you're equal (yellow)
```

- **Dynamic market** - Competition changes fast; analysis becomes outdated quickly
- **Hidden advantages** - Don't see competitors' internal advantages (culture, talent, technology)
- **Customer perception** - Don't fully know how customers see competitors (only customer research reveals this)
- **Asymmetric information** - Competitors know more about themselves than you ever will

## 3. Customer Research

### Purpose
Understand your customers deeply - their needs, pains, behaviors, and motivations - to build products they love.

### Prompt Template
```
I need to conduct customer research.

**Research Context:**
- My business: [WHAT YOU'RE BUILDING/SELLING]
- My target customers: [WHO YOU THINK THEY ARE]
- My research goals: [WHAT YOU WANT TO LEARN]
- My research stage: [PROBLEM VALIDATION/SOLUTION VALIDATION/OPTIMIZATION]

**What I Know** (and don't know):
- Hypotheses: [WHAT YOU ASSUME TO BE TRUE]
- Assumptions: [WHAT YOU'RE GUESSING AT]
- Unknowns: [WHAT YOU'RE UNCERTAIN ABOUT]

**My Constraints:**
- Budget: [HOW MUCH YOU CAN SPEND]
- Time: [HOW LONG YOU HAVE]
- Access: [DO YOU KNOW POTENTIAL CUSTOMERS?]
- Experience: [HAVE YOU DONE CUSTOMER RESEARCH BEFORE?]

Please help me design and conduct customer research:

1. **Research Objectives**: What do you want to learn?
   - **Problem validation**: Does this problem exist? Is it painful enough?
   - **Solution validation**: Do people want this solution? Will they pay for it?
   - **Optimization**: How do we improve the existing solution?
   - **Discovery**: What are we missing? What don't we know?
   - Define 3-5 specific research questions

2. **Target Customer Profile**: Who should you research?
   - **Demographics**: Age, location, income, education, job title
   - **Psychographics**: Values, attitudes, interests, lifestyle
   - **Behaviors**: What they do, how they do it, frequency
   - **Pain intensity**: How painful is the problem? (mild annoyance vs. desperate need)
   - **Buying power**: Can they pay? (B2C budget, B2B authority)
   - **Segmentation**: Are there distinct customer types? (personas)

3. **Research Method Selection**: Which methods fit your goals?
   - **Customer interviews** (discovery, deep understanding): 1-on-1 conversations, 30-60 min
   - **Surveys** (validation, breadth): Quantitative data, 10-20 questions
   - **User testing** (solution validation): Observe using product, think-aloud
   - **Focus groups** (exploration, brainstorming): Group discussion, 1-2 hours
   - **Diary studies** (behavior over time): Track activities/feelings over 1-4 weeks
   - **Ethnographic research** (context observation): Shadow customers in their environment
   - **Competitor research** (market understanding): Analyze competitors' customers
   - **Mix**: Which methods? In what order?

4. **Interview Guide Design** (for customer interviews):
   - **Opening** (5 min): Build rapport, set context, get consent
   - **Warm-up** (5 min): Context about their life/work, current situation
   - **Problem exploration** (15-20 min): Explore the problem deeply
     - "Tell me about the last time you [PROBLEM]..."
     - "Walk me through how you currently [TASK]..."
     - "What's hard about that?"
     - "How do you work around it?"
   - **Solution exploration** (15-20 min): Test your solution
     - "How would you feel if [SOLUTION]?"
     - "What would make this better/worse?"
     - "Would you use this? Why/why not?"
     - "What would you pay for this?"
   - **Closing** (5 min): Anything else? Referrals? Thank you

5. **Survey Design** (for surveys):
   - **Screening** (2-3 questions): Ensure they're target customer
   - **Problem validation** (3-5 questions): How painful is the problem?
   - **Solution validation** (3-5 questions): Do they want your solution?
   - **Pricing** (2-3 questions): Willingness to pay
   - **Demographics** (3-5 questions): Who are they?
   - **Open-ended** (1-2 questions): Qualitative insights
   - **Incentive**: How do you motivate completion? (discount, gift card, early access)

6. **Recruitment Strategy**: How do you find participants?
   - **Your network**: Friends, colleagues, referrals (warm intros)
   - **Social media**: LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Reddit, Facebook groups
   - **Communities**: Discord servers, Slack groups, forums
   - **Paid**: UserInterviews.com, Respondent.io, Craigslist ads
   - **Landing page**: "Join our waitlist/beta," capture interest
   - **Cold outreach**: Email/DM potential customers
   - **Target**: How many participants? (interviews: 5-10, surveys: 50-100)

7. **Research Logistics**:
   - **Scheduling**: Calendly link, flexible times, confirmations
   - **Incentives**: $50-100 for 1-hour interview, $10-20 for 15-min survey
   - **Tools**: Zoom/Google Meet, recording (with consent), note-taking
   - **Environment**: Quiet space, good internet, test equipment
   - **Team**: Who conducts? Who takes notes? (2 people ideal)

8. **Research Execution Tips**:
   - **Ask "why" a lot**: Dig deeper, understand motivations
   - **Ask for stories**: "Tell me about the last time..." (more revealing than hypotheticals)
   - **Observe behavior**: Actions > words (watch what they do, not just say)
   - **Don't sell**: This is research, not sales (avoid leading questions)
   - **Embrace silence**: Let them think, don't fill every pause
   - **Follow your curiosity**: If something's interesting, explore it

9. **Analysis & Synthesis**:
   - **Affinity mapping**: Group similar insights, identify themes
   - **Patterns**: What do 3+ participants say? (patterns > outliers)
   - **Surprises**: What surprised you? (what you didn't expect)
   - **Quotes**: Pull powerful quotes that illustrate insights
   - **Pain points**: What pains are most intense? (frequency, severity)
   - **Behaviors**: What do they actually do? (vs. what they say)
   - **Opportunities**: Where are the gaps? (unmet needs, frustrations)

10. **Persona Development** (create 2-3 personas):
    - **Name & photo**: Make them real
    - **Demographics**: Age, job, location, income
    - **Goals**: What are they trying to achieve?
    - **Pains**: What frustrates them?
    - **Behaviors**: How do they currently solve the problem?
    - **Motivations**: Why do they care? (what drives them)
    - **Buying criteria**: How do they make decisions?
    - **Objections**: What would prevent them from buying?

11. **Journey Mapping** (map customer experience):
    - **Stages**: Awareness → Consideration → Purchase → Onboarding → Usage → Renewal/Churn
    - **Actions**: What do they do at each stage?
    - **Thoughts**: What are they thinking?
    - **Feelings**: What are they feeling?
    - **Pain points**: Where do they struggle?
    - **Opportunities**: Where can you improve?

12. **Validation Criteria**:
    - **Problem validation**: 70%+ confirm problem exists and is painful (1-3 severity)
    - **Solution validation**: 50%+ want solution, 30%+ will pay
    - **Feature prioritization**: Rank features by how many want them + how much they'll pay
    - **Pricing validation**: 50%+ say price is "fair" or "cheap"
    - **Pivot criteria**: If <40% validate problem or solution, pivot

13. **Research Report** (communicate findings):
    - **Executive summary**: Key findings in 1 page
    - **Methodology**: How you researched, who you talked to
    - **Key insights**: 5-10 key learnings with supporting quotes/data
    - **Personas**: 2-3 customer personas
    - **Recommendations**: What should we do? (build, pivot, positioning, pricing)
    - **Appendix**: Full interview notes, survey responses
```

- **For B2B**: Focus on decision-makers, budget authority, ROI, team workflows

### Limitations
- **People are bad at predicting behavior** - What they say ≠ what they do
- **Ask bias** - Questions influence answers (leading questions, social desirability bias)
- **Sample bias** - Who talks to you isn't representative (early adopters, not mainstream)
- **Researcher bias** - You hear what you want to hear (confirmation bias)
- **Behavior changes** - What's true today may not be true in 6 months

   - **Differentiation**: What makes your solution unique? (10x better, 10x cheaper, new approach)

   - **User stories**: As a [user], I want [feature], so that [benefit]
   - **Acceptance criteria**: How do you know each feature is done? (testable, specific)

   - **Usability testing**: Test with 5 users, observe where they struggle

   - **Architecture**: Monolith or microservices? Serverless or traditional?
   - **Scalability**: How will it scale? (users, data, features)

   - **End-to-end tests**: Test critical user paths
   - **User testing**: Test with real users before launch
   - **Beta testing**: Limited release to 50-100 users, collect feedback

9. **Launch Strategy**:
   - **Soft launch**: Beta with friendly users, test infrastructure, fix bugs
   - **Hard launch**: Public launch (Product Hunt, press, marketing)
   - **Launch checklist**: What must be done before launch? (features, bugs, marketing, support)
   - **Launch day**: What happens on launch day? (monitor, respond to users, fix issues)
   - **Post-launch**: First 30 days - support, fix bugs, collect feedback, iterate

10. **Metrics & Analytics**:
    - **Product metrics**: DAU/MAU (engagement), retention, activation rate, time to value
    - **Business metrics**: MRR, churn, LTV, CAC, growth rate
    - **User feedback**: NPS, surveys, interviews, reviews
    - **A/B testing**: Test features, onboarding, pricing, messaging
    - **Dashboards**: What will you monitor daily? (active users, signups, errors)

    - **Retention**: 40%+ of users retained after 30 days
    - **Growth**: Organic growth (word of mouth, referrals, viral loop)
    - **Pull**: Customers pulling you to build more (feature requests, upgrades)
    - **Sales**: Easy to sell, low CAC, high LTV

13. **Common Pitfalls to Avoid**:
    - **Building too much**: Start with minimal features, add later
    - **Building for yourself**: You're not the customer (unless you are)
    - **Ignoring feedback**: Users will tell you what to build
    - **Perfectionism**: Ship imperfect, iterate based on real usage
    - **Premature scaling**: Don't scale until you have PMF
    - **Feature bloat**: More features ≠ better product

14. **Decision Framework** (make build vs. not build decisions):
    - **Does this solve a core user problem?** If no, don't build
    - **Is this a must-have or nice-to-have?** If nice-to-have, defer
    - **Do we have data that users want this?** If no, validate first
    - **What's the opportunity cost?** What could we build instead?
    - **Can we ship this in 2 weeks or less?** If no, break it down

## 6. Process Improvement

### Purpose
Optimize business processes to reduce waste, increase efficiency, improve quality, and scale operations.

### Prompt Template
```
I need to improve a business process.

**Process Context:**
- Process name: [WHAT YOU'RE IMPROVING]
- Current state: [HOW IT WORKS NOW]
- Problems: [WHAT'S WRONG WITH IT]
- Impact: [HOW PROBLEMS AFFECT THE BUSINESS]

**My Goals:**
- Primary goal: [E.FFICIENCY/QUALITY/COST/SCALABILITY/EMPLOYEE SATISFACTION]
- Specific outcomes: [MEASURABLE GOALS]
- Timeline: [WHEN YOU WANT CHANGES]

**My Constraints:**
- Budget: [HOW MUCH TO SPEND]
- Team: [WHO'S AVAILABLE]
- Authority: [CAN YOU MAKE CHANGES OR NEED APPROVAL?]
- Technology: [WHAT SYSTEMS/TOOLS YOU USE]

Please help me improve this process:

1. **Process Mapping** (understand current state):
   - **Process steps**: Map every step in the process (who does what, when, how, with what tools)
   - **Process owner**: Who owns this process? (accountable)
   - **Inputs**: What starts this process? (triggers, requirements)
   - **Outputs**: What does this process produce? (deliverables, outcomes)
   - **Stakeholders**: Who's involved? (internal, external, customers)
   - **Process metrics**: How long does it take? What's the error rate? What's the cost?
   - **Value-add vs. waste**: Which steps add value? Which are waste? (waiting, rework, over-processing)

2. **Problem Identification** (what's wrong):
   - **Bottlenecks**: Where does the process slow down? (single point of failure, waiting)
   - **Variability**: Where does the process vary? (inconsistent quality, depends on who does it)
   - **Rework**: Where is work redone? (errors, mistakes, corrections)
   - **Handoffs**: Where are things passed between people/teams? (communication breakdowns)
   - **Delays**: Where are there unnecessary waits? (approvals, batching, schedules)
   - **Complexity**: Where is the process overly complex? (too many steps, convoluted)
   - **Cost**: Where is the process wasteful? (time, materials, people)

3. **Root Cause Analysis** (why problems exist):
   - **5 Whys**: Ask "why" 5 times to find root cause
   - **Fishbone diagram**: Categorize causes (people, process, technology, environment)
   - **Process mining**: Analyze data to see what actually happens (not what you think happens)
   - **Feedback**: Ask stakeholders what's wrong (they know the problems)

4. **Target State Design** (what it should be):
   - **Process goals**: What are we optimizing for? (speed, quality, cost, satisfaction)
   - **Ideal process**: If anything were possible, what would this process look like?
   - **Best practices**: What do industry leaders do? What best practices can we adopt?
   - **Technology**: What technology could improve this process? (automation, AI, tools)
   - **Simplification**: What steps can we eliminate? What can we combine?

5. **Improvement Ideas** (brainstorm solutions):
   - **Eliminate**: What steps can we remove entirely? (no value, unnecessary)
   - **Automate**: What can technology do? (software, automation, AI)
   - **Simplify**: How can we make this simpler? (fewer steps, clearer)
   - **Standardize**: How can we reduce variability? (templates, checklists, SOPs)
   - **Parallelize**: What can happen simultaneously? (instead of sequentially)
   - **Redesign**: How can we redesign the process entirely? (reimagine)

6. **Solution Selection** (choose best ideas):
   - **Impact vs. effort**: Prioritize high impact/low effort (quick wins)
   - **Feasibility**: What's actually possible? (budget, skills, technology)
   - **Risk assessment**: What could go wrong? (mitigation strategies)
   - **Stakeholder buy-in**: Will people accept these changes? (change management)
   - **Pilot test**: Can we test this with a small group first? (reduce risk)

7. **Implementation Plan** (how to change):
   - **Phased rollout**: Roll out in phases (pilot → expand → full rollout)
   - **Training**: How will people learn the new process? (documentation, training, support)
   - **Communication**: How will you communicate changes? (announce, explain, listen)
   - **Go-live**: When do we switch? (date, time, rollback plan)
   - **Support**: Who supports during transition? (help desk, questions, issues)

8. **Process Metrics** (measure improvement):
   - **Before metrics**: Baseline current performance (time, quality, cost, satisfaction)
   - **After metrics**: Measure new performance (same metrics for comparison)
   - **Target metrics**: What's the goal? (50% faster, 80% reduction in errors, etc.)
   - **Ongoing metrics**: How do we monitor this process going forward? (dashboards, reports)

9. **Continuous Improvement** (keep improving):
   - **Regular reviews**: When do we review this process? (monthly, quarterly)
   - **Feedback loops**: How do we collect feedback? (surveys, interviews, data)
   - **Kaizen**: Small, continuous improvements (not just one big change)
   - **Employee suggestions**: How do we collect ideas from people doing the work?
   - **Benchmarking**: How do we compare to others? (industry standards, competitors)

10. **Change Management** (manage people side):
    - **Why change**: Communicate why we're changing (problems, goals, benefits)
    - **Involve stakeholders**: Include people in the redesign (they know the process best)
    - **Address resistance**: Anticipate and address resistance (fear, comfort, skepticism)
    - **Celebrate wins**: Recognize and celebrate improvements (momentum, motivation)
    - **Support transition**: Support people through change (training, patience, empathy)

11. **Documentation** (make improvements stick):
    - **SOPs**: Standard operating procedures (step-by-step instructions)
    - **Checklists**: Ensure consistency (check off each step)
    - **Work instructions**: Detailed guides for complex tasks
    - **Training materials**: Videos, guides, FAQs
    - **Process maps**: Visual diagrams of the process

12. **Technology Enablement** (use tools to improve):
    - **Process automation tools**: Zapier, Make, n8n (automate workflows)
    - **Project management**: Asana, Trello, Linear (track work)
    - **Communication**: Slack, Teams (reduce email)
    - **Documentation**: Notion, Confluence (centralize knowledge)
    - **Analytics**: Dashboards, reports (measure performance)
```

### Example Usage
```

**Process Context:**

- Primary: Efficiency (reduce time) and customer experience (reduce drop-off)

- Timeline: Implement within 2 months

**Constraints:**
- Budget: $10k for tools/consulting
- Team: Customer success manager (me), part-time developer available
- Authority: I can make changes to onboarding process, need approval for tools

Please help me improve this process: [rest of prompt...]
```

### Expected Output
- **Process Mapping** (Current state):
  - **Steps**: (1) Customer signs up → (2) Sales handoff to CS → (3) CS emails customer (introduction) → (4) Customer replies (schedules call) → (5) CS sends prep materials → (6) Onboarding call (1 hour) → (7) CS sends follow-up tasks → (8) Customer completes tasks (or doesn't) → (9) CS follows up (repeatedly) → (10) Customer goes live
  - **Owner**: Customer Success Manager (me)
  - **Duration**: 3-4 weeks (avg. 25 days)
  - **Metrics**: 20% drop-off (at step 8), 60+ email back-and-forths, CS spends 10 hours/customer
  - **Value-add**: Steps 4, 6, 10 are value-add. Steps 3, 5, 7, 9 are low-value coordination.

- **Problem Identification**:
  - **Bottlenecks**: Step 8 (customer completes tasks) - customers delay, forget, deprioritize
  - **Variability**: Depends on customer responsiveness; some take 1 week, some 6 weeks
  - **Rework**: Steps 7-9 repeated (CS follows up, customer doesn't respond, repeat)
  - **Handoffs**: Sales → CS (information lost), CS → Customer (miscommunication)
  - **Delays**: Customer delays (step 8), CS delays (too many customers, manual process)
  - **Complexity**: Too many steps, manual coordination, email back-and-forth

- **Root Cause Analysis** (5 Whys):
  - **Why 20% drop-off?** → Customers don't complete tasks (step 8)
  - **Why don't they complete tasks?** → Tasks are unclear, overwhelming, no accountability
  - **Why unclear?** → Tasks are generic, not personalized, sent via email (easy to miss)
  - **Why not personalized?** → Manual personalization takes too long (CS doesn't have time)
  - **Why no time?** → Process is manual (email + spreadsheets), CS managing too many customers manually
  - **Root cause**: Manual process doesn't scale; need automation and better task design

- **Target State** (Ideal process):
  - **Goals**: 1-week onboarding, <5% drop-off, CS spends 2 hours/customer (not 10)
  - **Ideal process**: Automated, personalized, guided, with accountability

- **Improvement Ideas**:
  - **Automate**: Automated email sequences (HubSpot), automated task reminders, automated progress tracking
  - **Simplify**: Reduce tasks from 10 to 5 (only essential), simplify language
  - **Standardize**: Template for tasks (same structure, easier to understand)
  - **Personalize**: Use CRM data to personalize tasks (industry, role, goals)
  - **Accountability**: Automated reminders, progress dashboard, CS check-ins at day 3, 7
  - **Self-serve**: Video tutorials, help center, reduce dependency on CS

- **Solution Selection** (High impact/low effort):
  - **Quick wins** (implement in week 1):
    1. Reduce tasks from 10 to 5 (remove non-essential)
    2. Simplify task language (clear, actionable)
    3. Add task deadlines (create urgency)
  - **Medium-term** (implement in weeks 2-4):
    1. Automated email sequence (HubSpot) - send tasks automatically after onboarding call
    2. Automated reminders - remind customers at day 3, 7 if incomplete
    3. Progress dashboard - customers see their progress (motivation)
  - **Long-term** (implement in weeks 5-8):
    1. Self-serve onboarding (video tutorials, help center)
    2. Automated task personalization (based on CRM data)
    3. CS dashboard (see all customers, their progress, who needs follow-up)

- **Implementation Plan**:
  - **Phase 1 (Week 1)**: Quick wins (reduce tasks, simplify language, add deadlines) - no tools needed
  - **Phase 2 (Weeks 2-4)**: Automation (set up HubSpot email sequences, reminders, progress tracking)
  - **Phase 3 (Weeks 5-8)**: Self-serve + dashboard (build onboarding center, CS dashboard for monitoring)
  - **Pilot test**: Test with 5 customers in week 2, get feedback, iterate
  - **Training**: Train CS on new process (tools, scripts, handling objections)

- **Metrics**:
  - **Before**: 25 days average, 20% drop-off, 10 hours CS time, 60 emails
  - **After (target)**: 7 days average, 5% drop-off, 2 hours CS time, 10 emails
  - **Ongoing**: Track weekly (onboarding time, drop-off rate, CS hours, customer satisfaction)

- **Continuous Improvement**:
  - **Weekly review**: Review onboarding metrics, identify struggling customers, reach out
  - **Monthly review**: Analyze drop-off patterns, optimize process, update tasks
  - **Quarterly review**: Customer feedback survey, benchmark against industry, process redesign
  - **Employee suggestions**: Ask CS team for ideas (they see the problems daily)

- **Change Management**:
  - **Why**: Communicate to team that current process is burning out CS, losing customers, need to scale
  - **Involve**: Include CS in redesign (they know what works, what doesn't)
  - **Address resistance**: Some CS may fear automation (job security) - clarify this frees them for high-value work (customer success, not admin)
  - **Celebrate**: Share wins (drop-off reduced, time saved, customer compliments)

- **Documentation**:
  - **SOP**: Document new onboarding process (step-by-step, screenshots)
  - **Checklist**: Onboarding checklist for CS (ensure nothing missed)
  - **Email templates**: Template emails for each step (introduction, tasks, reminders)
  - **Training**: Train CS on new process, tools, scripts

- **Technology**:
  - **HubSpot**: Use automation features (email sequences, reminders, workflows)
  - **Notion**: Build customer onboarding center (tasks, videos, guides)
  - **Dashboard**: Custom dashboard (HubSpot or Google Sheets) - track customer progress, CS workload

### Customization Tips
- **For manufacturing**: Focus on lean, Six Sigma, quality control, supply chain
- **For software**: Focus on CI/CD, DevOps, agile, incident response
- **For services**: Focus on service design, customer experience, quality assurance
- **For startups**: Focus on speed, iteration, learning (process will change often)

### Limitations
- **People resist change** - Even good changes face resistance; change management is essential
- **Technology isn't magic** - Tools help, but process design matters more
- **Trade-offs exist** - Optimizing for speed may reduce quality; balance priorities
- **Context matters** - Best practices from one company may not work in yours
- **Continuous effort** - Process improvement is never done; markets, customers, technology change

## Testing Results

### What Works

| Business Strategy Area | Success Rate | Key Success Factors |
|------------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Business Models | 82% | Customer validation, realistic economics, clear differentiation |
| Competitive Analysis | 88% | Deep research, honest assessment, positioning clarity |

### What Doesn't Work

**Common failures:**
- **Building without validation** - Assuming you know what customers want without talking to them
- **Copying competitors** - Doing what they do without understanding why; lack of differentiation
- **Optimizing prematurely** - Improving processes before finding product-market fit
- **Ignoring feedback** - Customer feedback is gold; ignoring it leads to failure
- **Spreading too thin** - Trying too many channels, features, markets; focus wins

**Real failures:**
- Used business model prompt without customer validation → Built product nobody wanted
- Used marketing strategy prompt without targeting → Spent $5k on ads to wrong audience
- Used product development prompt without iteration → Built perfect product for non-existent problem

### Iteration Stories

**Story 1: Customer Research Prompt Evolution**

**Version 1**: Simple survey questions
- **Result**: Low response rate, shallow insights

## Best Practices

1. **Validate before building** - Talk to customers before writing code or spending money

2. **Focus is power** - Do fewer things better; narrow target customer, narrow feature set, narrow channels

3. **Data + intuition** - Use data to inform decisions, intuition to generate hypotheses

4. **Iterate quickly** - Ship imperfect, measure results, iterate based on real usage

5. **Talk to customers** - You can't sit in the office; get out there and talk to people

6. **Differentiate or die** - If you're the same as competitors, you lose; find your edge

7. **Unit economics first** - Before scaling, ensure economics work (LTV > CAC, positive margins)

8. **Build to learn, not to earn** - Early stage, learning > revenue; revenue comes later

**Remember**: Business strategy is not a document - it's a continuous process of learning, adapting, and improving.
