# Content Repurposing Workflow

**Innovation isn't about new tools—it's about better questions.** The right workflow transforms content creation from a constant grind into a sustainable system.

---

## The Weekly Repurposing Cycle

This workflow is designed for **sustainable production**, not burnout. It's based on real-world testing with content creators who maintain consistency without sacrificing quality.

### Time Commitment: 8 Hours Per Week
- Traditional approach: 30 hours for 10 pieces of content
- Repurposing approach: 8 hours for 10+ pieces of content
- **Result: 73% time savings, 10x output**

---

## Step 1: Create Core Content (Weekly)

**Time: 3 hours**
**When: Your peak creativity time (morning for most)**

### Choose Your Primary Platform

Pick ONE format where you shine:
- **Blog writer:** Long-form posts (1,500+ words)
- **Video creator:** YouTube videos (10+ minutes)
- **Podcaster:** Audio episodes (20+ minutes)
- **LinkedIn native:** Articles (comprehensive)

**Rule:** Don't try to be great at everything. Be excellent at one format, then repurpose.

### Core Content Quality Checklist

Before moving to repurposing, ensure your core content passes these tests:

- [ ] **Specific pain point** - Addresses a real problem your audience faces
- [ ] **Unique angle** - Offers a perspective or framework they can't get elsewhere
- [ ] **Real examples** - Includes actual case studies, not hypotheticals
- [ ] **Actionable takeaways** - Reader knows exactly what to do next
- [ ] **Aligned with expertise** - Draws from your genuine experience
- [ ] **Evergreen potential** - Relevant for 6+ months (or has lasting lessons)

**If you can't check at least 4 of these boxes, improve the core content first.**

### The Core Creation Process

1. **Outline (30 minutes)**
   - Define the one key insight you're delivering
   - List 3-5 supporting points
   - Identify 1-2 real examples to include
   - Draft your CTA (what should they do next?)

2. **Draft (90 minutes)**
   - Write without editing first
   - Focus on getting ideas down, not perfection
   - Use your natural voice, not formal "content creator" tone
   - Include personal stories and specifics

3. **Refine (60 minutes)**
   - Edit for clarity and flow
   - Cut fluff (every word must earn its place)
   - Add formatting (headers, bullet points, bold text)
   - Proofread for typos and clarity
   - **Publish on your primary platform**

## Step 2: Batch Derivative Content (Same Day or Next Day)

**Time: 2 hours**
**When:** Same afternoon or next morning (while content is fresh)

### Why Batch?

**Context switching kills productivity.** Every time you switch between creating and editing, you lose momentum.

**Better approach:** Create core content, then immediately batch all derivatives in one focused session.

### The Batch Repurposing Session

#### Setup (5 minutes)
- Open AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.)
- Open all platform drafting tools (Twitter, LinkedIn, Canva, etc.)
- Have your core content open for reference
- Queue up your favorite focus music

#### Social Media Sprint (45 minutes)

**Twitter Thread (15 minutes)**
1. Use the Twitter thread prompt from Document 4
2. Paste core content into AI
3. Edit output for your voice (remove generic phrases)
4. Add specific examples from your experience
5. Schedule or publish immediately

**LinkedIn Post (10 minutes)**
1. Use the LinkedIn post prompt from Document 4
2. Paste core content into AI
3. Edit first 2 lines for maximum hook power
4. Add personal story or example
5. Include relevant image or PDF upload
6. Schedule for optimal time (8-10am your audience's timezone)

**Instagram Carousel (20 minutes)**
1. Use the carousel prompt from Document 4
2. Generate slide outlines with AI
3. Create in Canva (use template to save time)
4. Design each slide (consistent branding)
5. Write caption that saves the best hook for slide 1
6. Schedule for 2-3 days later

#### Video Sprint (45 minutes)

**TikTok/Reel Script (10 minutes)**
1. Use the 60-second script prompt from Document 4
2. Generate script with AI
3. Edit for natural delivery (remove AI-speak)
4. Add visual notes (what to show on camera)
5. Practice delivery 2-3 times

**Short Video Production (35 minutes)**
1. Record vertical video (smartphone is fine)
2. Use good lighting (natural light or ring light)
3. Capture in one take if possible, edit lightly if needed
4. Add captions (use Captions app or native tool)
5. Include text overlay for key points
6. Schedule or publish when your audience is most active

#### Email Sprint (30 minutes)

**Newsletter Edition (30 minutes)**
1. Use the newsletter prompt from Document 4
2. Paste core content into AI
3. Add personal intro (what you're thinking/working on)
4. Edit extensively for your voice (newsletters should feel personal)
5. Include 1-2 curated resources related to topic
6. Add engagement question
7. Preview on mobile (most opens are mobile)
8. Schedule for your consistent send time

**Total Batch Time: 2 hours**
**Output: 5 derivative pieces ready to publish**

## Step 3: Platform Optimization (Weekly)

**Time: 2 hours**
**When:** Spread throughout the week (30 min/day)

### The Trap: Copy-Paste Repurposing

**Wrong:** Publish identical content across all platforms
**Right:** Adapt for each platform's unique culture and format

### Platform-by-Platform Optimization

#### Twitter Optimization (30 min)
- **Timing:** 8-10am, 12-1pm, 5-6pm (test what works for your audience)
- **Engagement:** Reply to every comment in first hour
- **Follow-up:** Quote-tweet your own thread 24 hours later with new insight
- **Hashtags:** 1-2 max, highly relevant
- **Visuals:** Add images to tweets 1, 4, 7 for 2x engagement

#### LinkedIn Optimization (30 min)
- **Timing:** 8-10am or 5-6pm (your audience's professional hours)
- **Formatting:** Use line breaks, avoid walls of text
- **Media:** Upload PDF natively (not link) for 3x engagement
- **Engagement:** Reply to comments within 1 hour, ask follow-up questions
- **External links:** Only in comments, never post body
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 max, mix of broad + niche

#### Instagram Optimization (30 min)
- **Timing:** 11am-1pm or 7-9pm (varies by audience)
- **Stories:** Share carousel slides as stories with polls
- **Engagement:** Respond to all comments, DM people who save
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 in first comment, mix of competitive + discovery
- **Bio link:** Use Linktree or similar for carousel link
- **Cross-post:** Share to Reels for extra reach

#### TikTok/Reels Optimization (30 min)
- **Timing:** 7-9am, 12-3pm, 7-11pm (platform has distinct peak times)
- **Captions:** Always include for accessibility
- **Trending audio:** Use but don't rely on it—content > trend
- **Duets/Stitches:** Engage with comments by creating response videos
- **Posting:** 2-3x per day for growth, 1x minimum for consistency
- **Cross-post:** Share to YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels

## Step 4: Track & Iterate (Monthly)

**Time: 1 hour**
**When:** Last Friday or first Monday of each month

### What to Measure

**Not all metrics matter equally.** Focus on these:

**Engagement Metrics (Quality)**
- Comments (depth of engagement)
- Shares (virality potential)
- Saves (evergreen value)
- Click-throughs to core content

**Reach Metrics (Quantity)**
- Impressions/views
- Reach/unique viewers
- Follower growth rate
- Email list growth

**Conversion Metrics (Impact)**
- Email signups from content upgrades
- Product/course purchases from CTAs
- Consultation inquiries
- Podcast or guest post invitations

### Monthly Review Process

1. **Aggregate Data (20 minutes)**
   - Pull metrics from all platforms
   - Note top 3 performing pieces of content
   - Note bottom 3 performing pieces
   - Calculate total reach and engagement rate

2. **Identify Patterns (20 minutes)**
   - Which formats consistently perform best?
   - Which topics generate most engagement?
   - Which platforms drive your ideal audience?
   - What time of day gets best response?

3. **Action Plan (20 minutes)**
   - Double down on top-performing formats
   - Repurpose best content again in 6 months
   - Test new angles on underperforming topics
   - Adjust posting schedule based on timing data
   - Plan core content for next month based on insights

### The Optimization Loop

```
Create → Measure → Learn → Optimize → Create Better
```

**Every month, you should:**
- Know what your audience responds to
- Have clearer picture of ideal content mix
- Be more efficient (faster creation, better results)
- See compound growth from iterative improvement

## Advanced Workflow: The Content Bank

### Build a Repurposable Content Library

After 3 months of consistent repurposing, you'll have 12+ core content pieces. This becomes your content bank.

**How to Use Your Content Bank:**

1. **Quarterly Content Audits**
   - Review all core content from past 3 months
   - Identify top performers by engagement and conversions
   - Plan to republish with fresh examples in 6 months

2. **Series Creation**
   - Group related content into themed series
   - Example: "Time Management Series" (5 core pieces)
   - Promote as comprehensive resource

3. **Product Development**
   - Top-performing content becomes foundation for paid products
   - Example: Turn 10 best blog posts into an ebook or course
   - You already know audience wants it (they told you with engagement)

4. **Guest Posting and Pitches**
   - Repurpose core content for guest posts
   - Use analytics to prove topic resonance
   - Pitch with confidence: "This topic generated 150K+ views"

## Troubleshooting: When Repurposing Feels Overwhelming

### Problem 1: "I don't have time for all this"

**Solution:** Start smaller.
- Week 1: Core + 2 derivatives (Twitter, LinkedIn)
- Week 2: Core + 3 derivatives (add Instagram)
- Week 3: Core + 4 derivatives (add newsletter)
- Week 4: Core + 5 derivatives (add one video format)

Build up gradually. Consistency beats intensity.

### Problem 2: "My content doesn't perform on all platforms"

**Solution:** Focus on what works, ignore what doesn't.
- If LinkedIn kills it but TikTok flops, double down on LinkedIn
- If your audience loves carousels but ignores threads, make more carousels
- Not every format needs to work for every creator

### Problem 3: "AI generates generic content that sounds robotic"

**Solution:** AI drafts → You edits.
- Use AI for structure and speed (10x faster)
- Always edit for your voice and add specifics
- Replace generic examples with real stories
- Remove AI-speak ("delve," "tapestry," "game-changing")

### Problem 4: "I'm running out of core content ideas"

**Solution:** Repurpose your repurposed content.
- Update old content with new examples
- Turn 3 related posts into 1 comprehensive guide
- Create "best of" compilation pieces
- Share behind-the-scenes of your process

Content is cyclical, not linear. Your old content is new to someone new.
