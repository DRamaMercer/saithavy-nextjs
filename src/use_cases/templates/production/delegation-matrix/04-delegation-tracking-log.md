# Delegation Tracking Log

> **"What gets tracked gets done. What gets measured gets improved."**

Use this log to track all delegated tasks, monitor progress, and capture lessons learned for continuous improvement.

---

## Why Track Delegations?

**Without tracking:**
- You forget what you delegated and to whom
- Checkpoints get missed
- Deadlines sneak up on you
- You don't learn from your delegation patterns

**With tracking:**
- Clear visibility on all delegated work
- Proactive issue identification
- Better planning and workload management
- Data to improve your delegation skills

## Tracking System Options

### Option 1: Spreadsheet (Simple)
- Google Sheets or Excel
- One tab for master log
- One tab per detailed task
- Share with your team

### Option 2: Project Management Tool (Robust)
- Asana, Monday, Notion, ClickUp
- Create delegation dashboard
- Track tasks, deadlines, checkpoints
- Automate reminders

### Option 3: Notebook (Personal)
- Physical notebook or note-taking app
- Quick daily/weekly reviews
- Manual tracking
- Good for small teams or light delegation

### Option 4: Hybrid (Recommended)
- Spreadsheet for master log and metrics
- Project tool for active task tracking
- Notebook for reflections and lessons learned

## Anti-Pattern: The "Inbox Zero" Tracker

**What it looks like:**
- You delegate tasks
- Don't track them
- Forget about them until deadline
- Panic when you remember
- Micromanage to catch up

**Why it fails:**
- No visibility into progress
- Issues caught too late
- Team feels abandoned
- You lose trust in delegation

**Fix:**
- Spend 5 minutes updating tracking log daily
- Spend 15 minutes reviewing weekly dashboard
- Trust the system so you can trust your team

---

## Daily Tracking Routine (5 minutes)

Every morning:

1. **Review upcoming deadlines** (next 7 days)
2. **Check at-risk tasks** (what needs attention?)
3. **Confirm today's checkpoints** (are they still on?)
4. **Update status** (mark progress, flag issues)

**That's it. Five minutes.**

---

## Weekly Review (15 minutes)

Every week:

1. **Review all active delegations** (status, deadlines)
2. **Identify at-risk tasks** (what needs help?)
3. **Check team capacity** (who's overloaded?)
4. **Capture lessons learned** (what went well? What didn't?)
5. **Plan next week's delegations** (what to hand off? To whom?)

---

**Your delegation tracking system is only as good as your consistency in using it.**

**Pick a system. Use it daily. Review weekly. Improve continuously.**

*Delegation without tracking is hope. Delegation with tracking is strategy.*
