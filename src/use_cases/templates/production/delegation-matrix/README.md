# Delegation Matrix Template Package

> **"You don't have to do it all yourself. That's not leadership, that's burnout."**

This 4-document template package supports mindful leadership by providing practical tools for effective delegation.

---

## What's Included

### 1. Delegation Decision Matrix
A 4-quadrant framework to decide what to delegate, what to keep, and what to eliminate.

- **Must delegate**: High skill + Low leadership value
- **Should delegate**: Medium skill + Medium leadership value (growth opportunities)
- **Can't delegate**: High skill + High leadership value (your core work)
- **Shouldn't do**: Low skill + Low leadership value (eliminate or automate)

**Use when:** Auditing your workload, deciding what to delegate, periodic role review

---

### 2. Delegation Brief Template
A comprehensive brief template for handing off important tasks with clarity and context.

Sections:
- Context (why it matters)
- Outcome definition (what done looks like)
- Resources & authority (what they can access and decide)
- Checkpoints (when to check in)
- Learning (skills development connection)

**Use when:** Delegating any important task, setting someone up for success

---

### 3. Delegation Readiness Checklist
An 8-item checklist to ensure both you and your team member are ready before delegating.

Checklist items:
- Task clarity
- Success criteria
- Resources available
- Team member capacity
- Skills or learning curve
- Time for proper handoff
- Checkpoint schedule
- Feedback approach

**Use when:** Preparing to delegate, onboarding new team members, building delegation habits

---

### 4. Delegation Tracking Log
A tracking system for monitoring delegated tasks, progress, and capturing lessons learned.

Components:
- Master log template
- Detailed task tracking
- Quarterly review framework
- Monthly dashboard
- Delegation journal

**Use when:** Managing multiple delegations, improving delegation skills, team capacity planning

---

## How to Use This Package

### Step 1: Audit Your Workload (Document 1)

Spend 30 minutes with the Delegation Decision Matrix:

1. List everything you worked on last week
2. Categorize each task into the 4 quadrants
3. Identify:
   - 3 tasks to delegate immediately (Must delegate)
   - 2 tasks to plan for delegation (Should delegate)
   - Tasks to protect time for (Can't delegate)
   - Tasks to eliminate (Shouldn't do)

### Step 2: Prepare for Delegation (Document 3)

For each task you're delegating:

1. Run through the 8-item Readiness Checklist
2. Fix any gaps before handoff
3. Confirm team member capacity
4. Schedule proper handoff time (minimum 30 minutes)

### Step 3: Create the Brief (Document 2)

Before the handoff meeting:

1. Fill out the Delegation Brief Template
2. Be specific about outcomes and success criteria
3. Clarify resources and authority boundaries
4. Schedule checkpoints
5. Connect the work to their development

### Step 4: Track and Improve (Document 4)

After delegating:

1. Add task to Delegation Tracking Log
2. Monitor progress via checkpoints
3. Capture lessons learned after completion
4. Review patterns quarterly
5. Improve your delegation approach over time

## Common Delegation Mistakes

### ❌ Dumping, Not Delegating
**What it looks like:** Hand off tasks with zero context, no brief, just "handle this"

**Why it fails:** No clarity, no context, no setup for success

**Fix:** Always use the Delegation Brief Template. Take 15 minutes to write it, 30 minutes to discuss it.

---

### ❌ Abdicating Responsibility
**What it looks like:** Delegate and disappear. "It's their problem now."

**Why it fails:** You're still accountable. Radio silence until deadline is dangerous.

**Fix:** Scheduled checkpoints. Available for support, not hovering. Own the outcome even if you're not doing the work.

---

### ❌ Micromanaging
**What it looks like:** Delegate but hover over every detail. Constant status checks. Rewriting their work.

**Why it fails:** Defeats the purpose. Demotivates the team. No development happens.

**Fix:** Clear success criteria upfront. Scheduled checkpoints (not constant checking). Focus on outcomes, not methods.

---

### ❌ Under-Delegating
**What it looks like:** Only delegate grunt work. Keep all interesting, high-visibility work for yourself.

**Why it fails:** Your team can't grow. They leave. You become the bottleneck.

**Fix:** Look at "Should delegate" quadrant. These are growth opportunities. Your job is to develop your team, not to do all the rewarding work yourself.

---

### ❌ Over-Delegating
**What it looks like:** Delegate everything, including your core leadership responsibilities.

**Why it fails:** You're not leading. You're passing the buck. Key decisions don't get made.

**Fix:** Protect "Can't delegate" work ruthlessly. That's your job. Build capacity so you can focus on it.

---

### ❌ No Feedback Loop
**What it looks like:** Don't check in until deadline. Only give feedback if something goes wrong.

**Why it fails:** Issues caught too late. No learning. Same mistakes repeated.

**Fix:** Scheduled checkpoints. Post-completion review (what worked? What didn't?). Continuous improvement.

## Effective Delegation Pattern

✅ **Clear brief + resources**
- Take 15 minutes to write a proper brief
- Confirm resources are available
- Clarify authority boundaries

✅ **Handoff with time for questions**
- Schedule 30+ minutes for handoff conversation
- Answer their questions
- Confirm understanding (ask them to summarize back)

✅ **Scheduled checkpoints**
- Set checkpoints before they start
- Frequent enough to catch issues early
- Infrequent enough to avoid hovering
- Typical pattern: Outline → Draft → Final

✅ **Available for support (not hovering)**
- "I'm available on Slack anytime"
- "Let's review at our scheduled checkpoint"
- Trust them to work autonomously between checkpoints

✅ **Feedback on completion**
- Debrief after delivery
- Discuss what went well
- Discuss what to improve next time
- Capture lessons learned in tracking log

## Real Talk About Delegation

### I've Made All These Mistakes

**Dumping:** I once delegated a major proposal via email while rushing to a meeting. No brief, no conversation. The result? Two weeks of rework because my assumptions didn't match reality. That 30-minute conversation would have saved 20 hours.

**Abdicating:** I delegated a client relationship and didn't check in for 6 weeks. Assumed "no news is good news." Reality: Client was unhappy and nobody told me. By the time I found out, they were halfway out the door. Scheduled checkpoints would have caught it.

**Micromanaging:** I delegated a presentation but kept "just making a few edits" until it was basically my work again. My team member learned nothing, and I did the work anyway. Should have focused on clear success criteria and trusting them to deliver.

**Under-delegating:** I used to keep all the interesting strategy work and only delegate routine tasks. Wondered why my team wasn't developing. Realized I was hogging all the growth opportunities. Started delegating "Should delegate" work and my team grew faster.

**Over-delegating:** In a moment of overwhelm, I delegated hiring decisions to my team lead. That's my job. It backfired. Now I protect "Can't delegate" work ruthlessly and build capacity so I have time for it.

**No feedback:** I used to just say "thanks" when work was done and move on. No feedback, no learning. Now I always do a 15-minute debrief: "What worked? What didn't? What would you do differently?" That's where the growth happens.

## Quick Start Guide

### If you have 1 hour:

1. **Read the Decision Matrix** (10 min)
2. **Audit your last week's work** (10 min)
3. **Identify 3 tasks to delegate** (5 min)
4. **Run Readiness Checklist for each** (10 min)

4. **Schedule checkpoints** (30 min)

2. **Delegate the tasks**
3. **Monitor via checkpoints**
4. **Do post-completion debriefs**
5. **Capture lessons learned**
6. **Plan next round of delegations**
