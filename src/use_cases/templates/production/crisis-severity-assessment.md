# Crisis Severity Assessment Tool

**Quick Reference: Determine Your Crisis Level in 3 Minutes**

---

## Instructions
Circle your response to each question. Add up your score. See the Severity Scale below.

---

## Part 1: Impact Assessment

**1. How many areas of your life are affected?**
- 1 area only (work OR health OR relationships, not multiple) = 1 point
- 2 areas = 2 points
- 3-4 areas = 3 points
- 5+ areas = 4 points

**2. How severe is the disruption to your daily life?**
- Minor inconvenience (life continues mostly normally) = 1 point
- Significant disruption (major routines changed) = 2 points
- Major disruption (most routines upended) = 3 points
- Complete disruption (life as you knew it has stopped) = 4 points

**3. How long will this crisis impact you?**
- Days to weeks = 1 point
- 1-3 months = 2 points
- 3-6 months = 3 points
- 6+ months = 4 points

---

## Part 2: Resource Assessment

**4. How stable are your finances?**
- Fully stable (savings 6+ months, steady income) = 1 point
- Somewhat stable (savings 1-3 months, income at risk) = 2 points
- Unstable (minimal savings, income disrupted) = 3 points
- Critical (no savings, no income, debts due) = 4 points

**5. How strong is your support system?**
- Very strong (multiple people actively helping) = 1 point
- Moderate (1-2 people available) = 2 points
- Weak (isolated, limited support) = 3 points
- Nonexistent (completely alone) = 4 points

**6. How is your physical health?**
- Excellent (no health concerns) = 1 point
- Good (manageable health concerns) = 2 points
- Poor (health issues complicating crisis) = 3 points
- Critical (health crisis is the emergency) = 4 points

---

## Part 3: Emotional Capacity Assessment

**7. How are you functioning emotionally?**
- Stable (can think clearly, make decisions) = 1 point
- Shaken (emotional but functional) = 2 points
- Overwhelmed (hard to think, decisions feel impossible) = 3 points
- Flooded (panic, shutdown, cannot function) = 4 points

**8. How much sleep are you getting?**
- Normal sleep (6-8 hours, resting okay) = 1 point
- Disrupted sleep (waking often, nightmares) = 2 points
- Poor sleep (4-5 hours, difficulty falling/staying asleep) = 3 points
- No sleep (less than 4 hours, or unable to sleep at all) = 4 points

**9. Are you having thoughts of self-harm?**
- No = 1 point
- Passive thoughts (wish you weren't here, no plan) = 3 points
- Active thoughts (thinking about how, making a plan) = 5 points
- **STOP: Call 988 (Suicide Crisis Line) immediately**

## Severity Scale

### Level 1: Discomfort Zone (Score: 9-13)
**Status:** Something is wrong, but you're safe and stable.

**Your Approach:**
- Standard problem-solving
- No emergency response needed
- Timeline to resolve: Days to weeks

**Typical Situations:**
- Didn't get a promotion
- Minor disagreement
- Car repair expense
- Temporary work setback

**Immediate Action:**
- [ ] Acknowledge the inconvenience
- [ ] Make a plan to address it
- [ ] Talk to a friend if you need perspective
- [ ] Move forward—this is temporary

---

### Level 2: Challenge Zone (Score: 14-18)
**Status:** Significant disruption, but you have resources and support.

**Your Approach:**
- Activate support network
- Use crisis response template
- Consider professional support if helpful
- Timeline to resolve: Weeks to months

**Typical Situations:**
- Laid off with savings
- Breakup with support system
- Injury with insurance
- Temporary disability

**Immediate Action:**
- [ ] Complete Crisis Severity Assessment (done!)
- [ ] Activate your support system
- [ ] Use the Crisis Response Plan template
- [ ] Focus on one day at a time
- [ ] Celebrate small wins

---

### Level 3: Crisis Zone (Score: 19-24)
**Status:** Major disruption, resources stretched, emotional flooding.

**Your Approach:**
- Full crisis response protocol
- Professional support strongly recommended
- Activate all resources
- Timeline to resolve: Months

**Typical Situations:**
- Laid off with no savings
- Divorce or separation
- Major health diagnosis
- Financial crisis with debt

**Immediate Action (Next 24 hours):**
- [ ] Stabilize your nervous system (see Crisis Response Plan)
- [ ] Document the crisis facts
- [ ] Activate crisis team (3-5 key people)
- [ ] Secure your fundamentals (food, housing, safety)
- [ ] Access professional support (therapy, financial, legal)
- [ ] Use full Crisis Response Plan template

---

### Level 4: Emergency Zone (Score: 25-30)
**Status:** Severe disruption, survival mode activated, multiple systems failing.

**Your Approach:**
- Immediate professional support
- Crisis team activated
- Survival focus only
- Timeline to resolve: 6-12 months

**Typical Situations:**
- Job loss + health crisis simultaneously
- Multiple crises hitting at once
- Severe mental health crisis
- Major financial collapse

**Immediate Action (Next 1 hour):**
- [ ] Call for professional help (therapist, crisis line, doctor)
- [ ] Contact your primary support person
- [ ] Secure your immediate safety
- [ ] Do NOT make any major decisions alone
- [ ] Focus on survival needs only
- [ ] Accept all help offered

---

### Level 5: Catastrophe Zone (Score: 31-36)
**Status:** Existential threat, most resources gone, long-term rebuilding required.

**Your Approach:**
- Professional crisis management
- Long-term rebuilding plan
- Complete life reconstruction
- Timeline to resolve: 1-3 years

**Typical Situations:**
- Business bankruptcy with personal debt
- Major disability or life-threatening illness
- Total life collapse (multiple Level 4 crises)
- Death of partner/primary support person

**Immediate Action (Right Now):**
- [ ] Call emergency services if you're in danger (911)
- [ ] Call a crisis hotline (988 for mental health)
- [ ] Contact every support person you have
- [ ] Get professional help TODAY (not tomorrow)
- [ ] Focus on surviving the next hour
- [ ] Accept that your life will rebuild, but it takes time

## Special Cases

### Mental Health Emergency
**If you're having thoughts of self-harm:**
- **Call 988** (National Suicide Prevention Lifeline)
- **Text HOME to 741741** (Crisis Text Line)
- Go to the nearest emergency room
- Do NOT stay alone
- This is not weakness—this is emergency medical care

### Physical Safety Emergency
**If you're in immediate physical danger:**
- **Call 911** immediately
- Leave the dangerous situation if possible
- Go to a safe location (police station, hospital, fire station)
- Call a support person once you're safe

### Financial Emergency
**If you have no money for food/housing:**
- Call 211 (United Way emergency assistance)
- Contact local food banks
- Call your local Department of Social Services
- Ask about emergency housing assistance
- Do NOT let pride prevent you from accessing help
