# Communication Style Assessment
## Understanding How You Show Up in Relationships

**Welcome to your communication style assessment.** I used to think I was a "direct communicator" - until someone I respected told me I was actually "blunt." I thought I was "being honest," but I was actually being careless with people's feelings. I thought I was "avoiding conflict," but I was actually eroding trust through silence.

This assessment revealed my blind spots. The way I communicated when calm was different from how I communicated under stress. The way I gave feedback wasn't the way I received it. My conflict style was making everything worse.

Understanding your communication patterns isn't about fixing what's broken. It's about seeing clearly so you can choose how you show up.

## Part 4: Conflict Style

How do you approach and navigate conflict?

**My Conflict Style is:**

**Fight (Competing):**
- ☐ I stand my ground firmly
- ☐ I value winning the argument
- ☐ I'm direct about my position
- ☐ I can be dismissive of others' needs
- Risk: Relationships damaged, my way or the highway

**Flight (Avoiding):**
- ☐ I withdraw from conflict
- ☐ I hope it will resolve itself
- ☐ I prioritize peace over resolution
- ☐ I don't address issues directly
- Risk: Problems fester, resentment builds, nothing changes

**Freeze (Accommodating):**
- ☐ I give in to keep harmony
- ☐ I sacrifice my needs to avoid tension
- ☐ I agree even when I disagree
- ☐ I smooth over real differences
- Risk: I build resentment, my needs go unmet

**Appease (Compromising):**
- ☐ I look for middle ground
- ☐ I want everyone to feel okay
- ☐ I give up something to get something
- ☐ I value quick resolution
- Risk: Neither side fully satisfied, root issues unaddressed

**Collaborate (Problem-Solving):**
- ☐ I seek to understand all perspectives
- ☐ I work toward mutual satisfaction
- ☐ I address underlying needs, not just positions
- ☐ I invest time in real resolution
- Risk: Time-intensive, requires others' participation
