# 5-Minute Boundary Setting Meditation

*A guided practice to strengthen your capacity to hold boundaries with kindness and firmness.*

---

## Audio Companion Guide

**Duration:** 5 minutes
**Purpose:** Practice holding a boundary in your body before your next conversation
**Use:** Before setting a boundary, or daily to build the muscle

---

## Script

**(0:00-0:30) Setup**

"Find a comfortable position, either sitting or lying down. Close your eyes if that feels comfortable, or soften your gaze on a spot in front of you.

Let's take three deep breaths together.

Inhale deeply... [pause]
Exhale slowly... [pause]

Inhale again... [pause]
And release... [pause]

One more breath. Inhale... [pause]
And exhale completely. [pause]

---

**(0:30-1:30) Body Awareness**

"Bring your attention to your body. Notice how you're feeling right now.

Without judgment, just notice:
Is there tension anywhere?
Is your chest tight or your jaw clenched?
Does your stomach feel knotted?

These are often where we store our difficulty with boundaries - the fear of disappointing others, the guilt of saying no, the worry about conflict.

Just notice what's present for you right now. [pause]

There's nothing to fix. You're just awareness, noticing what's here.

---

**(1:30-2:30) Locate the Urge to People-Please**

"Now, let's explore where you feel the urge to say yes when you mean no.

Recall a recent situation where you wanted to say no, but felt pressured to say yes. It could be at work, in a relationship, or even with yourself.

Where do you feel that memory in your body?
- Is it a tightness in your throat?
- A heaviness in your chest?
- A sinking feeling in your stomach?
- Tension in your shoulders?

Just notice. [pause]

This sensation is your body telling you that a boundary is needed.

---

**(2:30-3:30) Breathe Into the Sensation**

"Now, imagine you could breathe directly into that sensation.

As you inhale, imagine breathing spaciousness into that tightness. [pause]

As you exhale, imagine releasing some of the tension. [pause]

Let's do that again.

Inhale... creating space. [pause]
Exhale... letting go. [pause]

One more time.

Inhale... you are allowed to take up space. [pause]
Exhale... you are allowed to have needs. [pause]

---

**(3:30-4:30) Affirm Your Truth**

"Now, repeat these truths silently to yourself:

My needs matter. [pause]

My time is mine to steward. [pause]

I am allowed to say no. [pause]

My boundaries are an act of self-respect. [pause]

I can be kind AND firm. [pause]

Let each truth settle into your body. Feel the strength of these words. [pause]

You don't have to earn the right to boundaries. You have it by virtue of being human.

---

**(4:30-5:00) Carry It Forward**

"As we prepare to end this practice, imagine your next conversation where you'll need to set a boundary.

See yourself handling it with clarity and kindness. [pause]

Feel what it's like to speak your truth without over-explaining. [pause]

Trust that you can handle whatever response comes. [pause]

Take one last deep breath together.

Inhale... [pause]
Exhale... [pause]

When you're ready, open your eyes and carry this strength with you into your next conversation.

You've got this."

---

## Practice Tips

**Before a boundary conversation:**
- Do this meditation to ground yourself
- Focus on Step 1 (Get Clear) of the framework
- Remind yourself: "My needs matter"

**Daily practice:**
- Do this meditation for 7 days to build the muscle
- Notice if it becomes easier to hold boundaries
- Journal about what shifts in your body awareness

**When you feel resistant:**
- That's normal. Your people-pleasing patterns will push back.
- Stay with the practice. Repetition is how you rewire.
- Self-compassion is part of the practice.

---

**Remember:** Boundaries aren't just words - they're a stance you take with yourself. This meditation helps you embody that stance so it's available when you need it.

*Your needs matter. You're allowed to say no. And this practice will help you do both with more ease.*
