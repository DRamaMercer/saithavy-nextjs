# Complete Boundary Scripts Collection
## 50+ Scripts for Every Context

*Real scripts for real conversations. Your boundaries teach others how to treat you.*

---

## How to Use This Collection

**Each script includes:**
- **Difficulty:** 🟢 Easy | 🟡 Medium | 🔴 Hard
- **When to use:** Specific situations
- **What to expect:** Likely reactions
- **Why it works:** The psychology behind it

**Pro tip:** Practice out loud before using. It feels weird at first. That's normal.

## Part 1: Work Boundaries (18 Scripts)

### Manager & Leadership Boundaries

#### 1. Saying No to Additional Work
**Difficulty:** 🟢 Easy

**Script:**
"It means a lot that you thought of me for this. I'm at capacity with my current commitments and can't take this on without dropping the ball on [project X]. I want to be realistic about what I can deliver well."

**When to use:** Your manager assigns you work beyond your capacity.

**What to expect:** Most managers will appreciate your honesty about capacity. They may ask you to prioritize.

**Why it works:** You're protecting your existing work quality while acknowledging the opportunity.

---

#### 2. Pushing Back on Unrealistic Deadlines
**Difficulty:** 🟡 Medium

**Script:**
"I want to make sure we deliver quality work on this. Given the scope, I'd need [more realistic timeline] to do this right. If we need it faster, let's discuss what we can de-prioritize or simplify."

**When to use:** Someone sets a deadline that isn't achievable with quality.

**What to expect:** Pushback initially, then usually a conversation about trade-offs.

**Why it works:** You're positioning it as commitment to quality, not resistance.

---

#### 3. Protecting Focus Time
**Difficulty:** 🟢 Easy

**Script:**
"I'm blocking focus time on [day/time] to make progress on [key project]. I'll be unavailable for meetings then, but I'll respond to messages after [time]."

**When to use:** You need uninterrupted time for deep work.

**What to expect:** Most colleagues will respect this if you communicate when you'll be available.

**Why it works:** Clear expectations about when you're unavailable AND when you'll be back.

---

#### 4. Leaving Work on Time
**Difficulty:** 🟡 Medium

**Script:**
"I make it a practice to wrap up at [time] to maintain my energy and effectiveness. I can pick this up first thing tomorrow morning."

**When to use:** You're consistently staying late and need to protect your time.

**What to expect:** Some pushback initially, especially if late nights are normalized.

**Why it works:** You're framing it as sustainability, not checking out.

---

#### 5. Weekend Boundaries
**Difficulty:** 🟡 Medium

**Script:**
"I don't check work messages on weekends so I can recharge and be fully present when I'm back on Monday. If anything urgent comes up, please call me at [number]. Otherwise, I'll respond Monday."

**When to use:** Your workplace expects weekend availability.

**What to expect:** Adjustment period as people learn your new pattern.

**Why it works:** You're setting expectations while providing an emergency channel.

---

#### 6. Saying No to Meetings Without Agendas
**Difficulty:** 🟢 Easy

**Script:**
"I want to make sure our meeting is productive. Could you share the agenda and desired outcomes beforehand? That will help me prepare and make the best use of our time."

**When to use:** You're invited to meetings that lack clear purpose.

**What to expect:** People will either provide agendas or you'll be removed from unnecessary meetings.

**Why it works:** You're framing it as wanting to be productive, not avoiding work.

---

#### 7. Declining After-Hours Events
**Difficulty:** 🟡 Medium

**Script:**
"Thank you for the invite! I make it a practice to keep evenings for personal time, so I won't be able to join. I hope you all have a great time."

**When to use:** Work extends into personal time with social events.

**What to expect:** Some may try to guilt you, especially if it's "team building."

**Why it works:** Clear no without over-explaining, with well-wishes.

---

#### 8. Interrupting a Micromanager
**Difficulty:** 🔴 Hard

**Script:**
"I appreciate your attention to detail. I've found I work most effectively when I have autonomy on [tasks], with check-ins at [milestones]. Can we try that approach?"

**When to use:** Someone is excessively controlling your work.

**What to expect:** Defensiveness, pushback about "oversight."

**Why it works:** You're acknowledging their need for information while proposing a better way.

## Quick Tips for Using These Scripts

1. **Make them yours**: Adjust wording to sound like you. Authenticity matters more than perfect scripts.

2. **Start with easy**: Build the muscle with low-stakes boundaries before the hard ones.

3. **Expect awkwardness**: First few times feel weird. That's normal. It gets easier.

4. **Some will land, some won't**: That's about them, not you. You're not responsible for reactions.

5. **Celebrate attempts**: Even if it comes out messy, you're building the skill.

6. **Practice out loud**: Seriously. It feels different in your mouth than in your head.

7. **Consider context**: Adapt scripts for culture, relationship, hierarchy.

8. **You don't owe explanations**: "No" is a complete sentence. Over-explaining weakens boundaries.

---

**Remember:** Your boundaries teach others how to treat you. You're allowed to start small. Just don't stay unclear.

*These scripts are tools, not rules. Take what works, leave what doesn't, and keep practicing.*
