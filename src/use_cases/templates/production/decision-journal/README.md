## Overview

This Decision Journal Package provides a complete system for mindful leadership through intentional decision-making. It's designed to transform how you approach choices - from reactive and rushed to present and purposeful.

**What's included:**
- Daily decision journal template for in-the-moment reflection
- Quarterly review template for pattern recognition and learning
- Decision framework guide with 6 practical decision-making tools
- Quality checklist to ensure thorough preparation

**Time investment:**
- Daily: 3-5 minutes per decision
- Quarterly: 60-90 minutes for review
- Ongoing: Integrated into your decision process

- Make 40% better judgments over time (based on research)

- Build stronger trust through transparency
- Develop clearer patterns of what works and what doesn't
- Model presence and intentionality for their teams

**The core insight:** You can't control outcomes, but you can control the quality of your decision process. This package helps you optimize that process.

## How to Get Started

### Week 1: Set Up Your Practice
1. **Read through all four documents** - familiarize yourself with the approach
2. **Choose your format** - digital document, notebook, or app
3. **Set your intention** - what do you want to improve about your decision-making?
4. **Start small** - journal just 2-3 decisions this week

### Week 2-4: Build the Habit
1. **Daily practice** - journal every significant decision
2. **Try frameworks** - use 2-3 frameworks from the guide
3. **Weekly review** - spend 15 minutes reviewing your entries
4. **Notice patterns** - when do you make your best/worst decisions?

### Month 2-3: Deepen the Practice
1. **Use checklist** - apply to important decisions
2. **Expand frameworks** - try all six frameworks
3. **Track outcomes** - revisit decisions after 2-4 weeks
4. **Monthly audit** - use the monthly audit template

### Month 4+: Continuous Learning
1. **Quarterly review** - schedule your first full quarterly review
2. **Refine process** - adjust what works for you
3. **Share selectively** - discuss patterns with mentor or coach
4. **Teach others** - share what you've learned with your team

## Success Principles

### 1. Consistency Over Perfection
- Better to journal imperfectly than not at all
- Even 2-3 decisions per week creates learning
- Missing a day isn't failure - just resume

### 2. Honesty Is Essential
- No one sees this but you - be truthful
- Include your doubts and fears
- Document decisions you're not proud of
- Acknowledge when you don't know why you decided

### 3. Focus on Process, Not Outcomes
- Good process + bad outcome = learning
- Bad process + good outcome = luck (not sustainable)
- You control the process, not the outcome

### 4. Learning Over Judgment
- This is about growth, not self-criticism
- Every decision teaches you something
- Be curious about patterns, not critical of yourself

### 5. Presence Is the Goal
- The journal itself is a mindfulness practice
- Pausing before deciding improves quality
- Your presence is your primary leadership tool

## Common Questions

**Q: How do I know which decisions to journal?**
A: Start with decisions that:
- Involve significant resources (time, money, people)
- Impact others beyond yourself
- Are hard to reverse
- Create uncertainty or anxiety
- Don't feel quite right but you can't say why

**Q: What if I don't have time for the full checklist?**
A: Use the Quick-Use Checklist (6 items) for routine decisions. Save the full checklist for high-stakes choices.

**Q: How do I choose the right framework?**
A: Start with the decision complexity assessment in the Framework Guide. When in doubt, start with Pros/Cons - it's simple and often sufficient.

**Q: What if my confidence rating was wrong?**
A: That's valuable data! Ask yourself what you missed. Was it information, perspective, or emotional factors? This is how you improve.

**Q: Do I really need to do quarterly reviews?**
A: Yes - this is where pattern recognition happens. Daily entries are data points; quarterly reviews are insights.

**Q: Can I share this with my team?**
A: You can share the approach and frameworks, but keep your specific journal entries private. Vulnerability about your process is powerful; oversharing details isn't necessary.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "I forget to journal decisions"**
- Solution: Set a reminder for end-of-day reflection
- Solution: Keep the journal template easily accessible
- Solution: Start with just one decision per day

**Problem: "It feels like extra work"**
- Solution: Focus on the time it saves by reducing second-guessing
- Solution: Remind yourself that 5 minutes now saves hours of regret later
- Solution: Start with just the Quick Checklist until habit forms

**Problem: "I don't know what to write"**
- Solution: Use the prompts in the template - just fill in the blanks
- Solution: Remember: imperfect journaling is better than no journaling
- Solution: Start with "What did I decide?" and "Why?" - that's enough

**Problem: "My decisions aren't getting better"**
- Solution: Are you doing the quarterly reviews? That's where learning happens
- Solution: Are you being honest about outcomes, not defending your choices?
- Solution: Are you applying insights from one decision to the next?

**Problem: "I feel self-critical when reviewing bad decisions"**
- Solution: Reframe: Every "bad" decision is data for better decisions
- Solution: Ask "What would I tell a friend in this situation?"
- Solution: Focus on what you learned, not what you got wrong
