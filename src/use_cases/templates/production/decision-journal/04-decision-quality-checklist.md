# Decision Quality Checklist

**Your presence is your primary leadership tool**

---

## Overview

This checklist ensures you've done the necessary preparation before making important decisions. It's not about making perfect decisions - it's about making decisions with intention, clarity, and presence.

**When to use:** Before any decision that involves significant resources, impacts others, or is hard to reverse.

**Time commitment:** 5-15 minutes, depending on decision complexity

---

## Pre-Decision Checklist

### Phase 1: Clarity

#### □ 1. Clear Problem Definition
Before deciding, can you articulate:
- [ ] What problem are you actually solving? (Not just the symptom)
- [ ] Why is this decision necessary now?
- [ ] What would happen if you didn't decide?
- [ ] What does "success" look like?

**Test:** Can you explain the problem in one clear sentence that a 12-year-old would understand?

---

#### □ 2. Stakeholder Identification
- [ ] Who will be impacted by this decision?
- [ ] Have you consulted key stakeholders?
- [ ] Have you sought diverse perspectives?
- [ ] Who needs to be informed after deciding?
- [ ] Who needs to approve this decision?

**Key stakeholders to consider:**
- Team members who will implement
- Customers/users who will experience
- Leaders who need to support
- Partners who will be affected

---

#### □ 3. Sufficient Information Gathered
- [ ] What facts do you have?
- [ ] What facts are you missing?
- [ ] Can you get the missing information? (Is it worth the delay?)
- [ ] What are your assumptions? Are they tested?
- [ ] What does past experience or data suggest?

**Information quality test:**
- [ ] Is this information reliable and current?
- [ ] Am I seeing the whole picture or just what I want to see?
- [ ] What would someone who disagrees with me say about this information?

---

### Phase 2: Options

#### □ 4. Multiple Options Considered
- [ ] Have you generated at least 3 distinct options?
- [ ] Have you considered "do nothing" as an option?
- [ ] Have you considered a hybrid/compromise option?
- [ ] Have you asked "what else?" at least twice?

**Option generation techniques:**
- Reverse thinking: "What would make this fail?"
- Perspective shift: "What would [respected leader] do?"
- Constraint removal: "What if time/money weren't constraints?"
- First principles: What would I do if starting from scratch?

---

#### □ 5. Options Evaluated Against Criteria
- [ ] What criteria matter most for this decision?
- [ ] Have you weighed the criteria by importance?
- [ ] How does each option perform against each criterion?
- [ ] Are you comparing apples to apples?

**Common criteria:**
- Impact (magnitude of outcome)
- Feasibility (can we actually do this?)
- Risk (what could go wrong?)
- Alignment (with values/strategy)
- Resources required (time, money, energy)
- Timeline (how long will it take?)

---

### Phase 3: Consequences

#### □ 6. Short-term Consequences Weighed
- [ ] What happens in the next day/week/month?
- [ ] Who is impacted in the short term?
- [ ] What resources are required immediately?
- [ ] What could go wrong right away?
- [ ] How will you measure initial success?

---

#### □ 7. Long-term Consequences Weighed
- [ ] What happens in 6 months? 1 year? 5 years?
- [ ] Does this decision enable or limit future options?
- [ ] What are the second-order effects?
- [ ] How does this compound over time?
- [ ] Will you be proud of this decision in retrospect?

**Second-order effects to consider:**
- If this succeeds, what then?
- If this fails, what then?
- What precedent does this set?
- How does this change systems/culture?
- What becomes easier/harder because of this?

---

#### □ 8. Worst-Case Scenario Considered
- [ ] What's the worst realistic outcome?
- [ ] How likely is the worst case? (Not "possible" but "probable")
- [ ] Can you recover if the worst case happens?
- [ ] What would make the worst case less likely or less damaging?
- [ ] Are you willing to accept the worst case?

**Recovery planning:**
- [ ] What early warning signs would indicate things are going wrong?
- [ ] What's your exit strategy if things go badly?
- [ ] What resources do you need in reserve?

---

### Phase 4: Alignment

#### □ 9. Values Alignment Checked
- [ ] Which of your core values are relevant to this decision?
- [ ] Does each option align with those values?
- [ ] Would you be proud to explain this decision publicly?
- [ ] Does this decision reflect the leader you want to be?
- [ ] How would you feel if this decision was headline news?

**Values test:**
- [ ] What would happen if everyone in the organization made decisions this way?
- [ ] Does this decision feel energizing or depleting when you imagine it?
- [ ] What would your future self thank you for?

---

#### □ 10. Strategic Alignment Checked
- [ ] How does this decision support your broader goals?
- [ ] Does this move you toward or away from your vision?
- [ ] Is this consistent with previous decisions? (If not, why?)
- [ ] Does this create or reduce complexity in the system?
- [ ] What tradeoffs are you making strategically?

---

#### □ 11. Resource Alignment Checked
- [ ] Do you have the time required to implement this?
- [ ] Do you have the budget required?
- [ ] Do you have the team capacity needed?
- [ ] Do you have the energy to sustain this?
- [ ] What are you NOT doing because you're doing this?

**Resource reality check:**
- [ ] Be honest: Do you have capacity for this, or are you already over-extended?
- [ ] What's the opportunity cost of using these resources here?
- [ ] Is this the highest and best use of these resources?

---

### Phase 5: Commitment

#### □ 12. Implementation Plan Considered
- [ ] What are the next 3 steps after deciding?
- [ ] Who owns each step?
- [ ] What's the timeline for implementation?
- [ ] What resources are needed?
- [ ] What could derail implementation?

---

#### □ 13. Measurement Plan Established
- [ ] How will you know if this decision was successful?
- [ ] What metrics will you track?
- [ ] When will you review the results?
- [ ] What would cause you to reconsider this decision?
- [ ] How will you capture learning from this decision?

---

#### □ 14. Communication Plan Ready
- [ ] Who needs to know about this decision?
- [ ] How will you communicate it to each group?
- [ ] What context do they need?
- [ ] What questions will they have?
- [ ] How will you handle disagreement or pushback?

---

#### □ 15. Personal Commitment Assessed
- [ ] Are you fully committed to this decision?
- [ ] Are you deciding or just going through motions?
- [ ] What part of you is resisting this decision?
- [ ] If you're not fully committed, what's missing?
- [ ] Are you ready to stand behind this decision even when it's hard?

---

## Quick-Use Checklist (For Smaller Decisions)

For routine decisions, use this abbreviated version:

- [ ] **Problem:** What am I solving? (One sentence)
- [ ] **People:** Who's impacted? Have I consulted them?
- [ ] **Options:** Do I have at least 3 options?
- [ ] **Consequences:** What's the worst case?
- [ ] **Alignment:** Does this match my values?
- [ ] **Commitment:** Am I ready to own this?

## Quality Scoring

Score each phase from 1-5 (1 = poor, 5 = excellent):

**Phase 1: Clarity** _____ / 25
**Phase 2: Options** _____ / 10
**Phase 3: Consequences** _____ / 15
**Phase 4: Alignment** _____ / 15
**Phase 5: Commitment** _____ / 15

**Total Score:** _____ / 80

**Interpretation:**
- 70-80: Excellent preparation - high confidence
- 55-69: Good preparation - moderate confidence
- 40-54: Adequate preparation - proceed with caution
- Below 40: Insufficient preparation - pause and do more work

## Sample Completed Checklist

**Decision:** Whether to hire a senior developer or promote from within

**Phase 1: Clarity (Score: 23/25)**
- ✓ Problem defined: Need senior technical leadership, current team lacks experience
- ✓ Stakeholders identified: Current team, candidate, company budget, clients
- ✓ Information gathered: Team skills assessment, budget, market rates
- ✓ Missing: Haven't discussed with current team members about their career goals (-2)

**Phase 2: Options (Score: 10/10)**
- ✓ Option 1: External senior hire
- ✓ Option 2: Promote from within + training
- ✓ Option 3: Contract senior mentor + promote from within
- ✓ Options evaluated against: Cost, timeline, team morale, capability growth, risk

**Phase 3: Consequences (Score: 14/15)**
- ✓ Short-term: External hire = faster results, higher cost. Promote = slower, cheaper
- ✓ Long-term: External = knowledge retention risk. Promote = loyalty building
- ✓ Worst-case: External hire doesn't fit culture. Promote person struggles
- ✓ Haven't fully considered second-order effects on team culture (-1)

**Phase 4: Alignment (Score: 13/15)**
- ✓ Values: "Growth" and "Loyalty" favor promotion. "Excellence" could favor external
- ✓ Strategy: Building internal capacity aligns with long-term vision
- ✓ Resources: Budget allows external hire, but tight. Promote fits budget better
- ✓ Team capacity: Do we have bandwidth to train? Need to verify (-2)

**Phase 5: Commitment (Score: 12/15)**
- ✓ Implementation plan: Job description ready if external. Development plan if promote
- ✓ Measurement: Performance metrics, team satisfaction, retention
- ✓ Communication: Plan for both scenarios
- ✓ Personal commitment: Leaning toward promotion but fear of slower results (-3)

**Total Score: 72/80**

**Interpretation:** Good preparation but need to:
1. Discuss with current team members about their goals (missing information)
2. Consider second-order cultural effects (incomplete consequence analysis)
3. Verify team capacity for training (resource alignment)
4. Address personal fear about slower results (emotional factor)

**Next steps:** Complete missing items before deciding

## Common Decision Traps

### Trap 1: Sunk Cost Fallacy
**Sign:** "We've already invested so much..."
**Checklist item:** Focus on future consequences, not past investments
**Question:** If you hadn't already invested, would you make this decision now?

### Trap 2: Confirmation Bias
**Sign:** Only seeing information that supports your preference
**Checklist item:** Explicitly seek disconfirming evidence
**Question:** What would someone who disagrees with me say?

### Trap 3: Anchoring
**Sign:** Overweighting the first piece of information
**Checklist item:** Generate multiple options before evaluating
**Question:** What if I started with a different assumption?

### Trap 4: Availability Bias
**Sign:** Overweighting recent or vivid examples
**Checklist item:** Seek broader historical data
**Question:** What does the full data set say, not just recent examples?

### Trap 5: Overconfidence Bias
**Sign:** Underestimating risk and uncertainty
**Checklist item:** Explicitly consider worst-case scenarios
**Question:** What if I'm wrong about this?

### Trap 6: Status Quo Bias
**Sign:** Preferring current state, even if suboptimal
**Checklist item:** Treat "do nothing" as an active option to evaluate
**Question:** If we were starting from scratch, would we choose the current approach?

### Trap 7: Groupthink
**Sign:** Team converging too quickly on one option
**Checklist item:** Assign a "devil's advocate" to challenge consensus
**Question:** What dissenting perspectives haven't been heard?

## Before You Decide

**Final check:**

- [ ] Have I slept on it? (For significant decisions)
- [ ] Is my current emotional state appropriate for deciding?
- [ ] Am I deciding too quickly or too slowly?
- [ ] Do I need outside perspective before finalizing?
- [ ] What would make this decision better if I waited 24 hours?

**If you can't check these boxes, pause.**

---

## After You Decide

**Decision made? Now commit.**

- [ ] Document the decision in your journal
- [ ] Communicate decision clearly to stakeholders
- [ ] Set review date to assess outcomes
- [ ] Let go of alternatives - no second-guessing
- [ ] Focus on execution, not regret

---

## Notes

- This checklist is a tool for preparation, not perfection
- Not every item applies to every decision - use judgment
- The goal is intentionality, not paralysis by analysis
- Good decisions with poor execution still fail - commit to implementation
- Great decisions can have bad outcomes - that's why we review

---

**The quality of your decisions depends on the quality of your preparation.**
