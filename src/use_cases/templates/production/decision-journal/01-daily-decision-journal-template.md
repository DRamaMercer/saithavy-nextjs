# Daily Decision Journal Template

**Your presence is your primary leadership tool**

## Overview

This daily decision journal helps you develop mindfulness in your leadership by creating a practice of pausing, reflecting, and learning from your decisions. Research shows that leaders who consistently document their decisions improve their judgment by 40% over time.

**Time commitment:** 3-5 minutes per decision
**Best practice:** Complete within 1 hour of making the decision

## Quick Reference: Common Decision Patterns

### Pattern 1: The "Should've Known Better" Decision
**Sign:** You're ignoring information that doesn't fit your preferred outcome
**Fix:** List all evidence that contradicts your preferred choice

### Pattern 2: The "Good Enough" Decision
**Sign:** Settling for the first acceptable option
**Fix:** Force yourself to generate 3 distinct options

### Pattern 3: The "Emotional Spike" Decision
**Sign:** Making decisions when emotional intensity is high
**Fix:** Implement a 2-hour rule for any decision made when triggered

### Pattern 4: The "Analysis Paralysis" Decision
**Sign:** Seeking more information when you already have enough
**Fix:** Set a decision deadline before you start gathering information

## Sample Completed Entry

**Date:** January 15, 2025
**Time:** 2:30 PM

**Decision:** Whether to launch the new product feature next week or delay for additional testing.

**Context Available:**
- Beta testing showed 12 minor bugs (non-critical)
- Competitor launching similar feature next month
- Team consensus: split (3 ready, 2 want more testing)
- Time constraint: Marketing campaign already scheduled

**Options Considered:**
1. **Launch on schedule** - Pros: First to market, meet commitments; Cons: Risk of bugs, team stress
2. **Delay 2 weeks** - Pros: More testing time, team comfort; Cons: Lose competitive advantage, marketing waste
3. **Launch with known issues disclosed** - Pros: Transparency, market timing; Cons: Reputation risk, customer dissatisfaction

**Decision Made:** Launch on schedule with extended support coverage.

**Rationale:** Values alignment - we prioritize learning speed over perfection. The bugs are minor, and real-world feedback will be more valuable than another week of internal testing. This aligns with our value of "progress over perfection."

**Expected Outcome:** Some customer complaints about bugs, but faster learning and iteration. Support team will be busier than usual for 2 weeks.

**Confidence Level:** 7/10
*Why:* Confident in the strategic alignment, but uncertain about bug impact magnitude.

**Follow-up Date:** January 29, 2025

**Gut Check:** Yes, this aligns with values. I'd be comfortable explaining this publicly. I'm not avoiding or rushing - this feels right.
