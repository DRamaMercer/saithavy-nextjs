# Meeting Communication Guide

**Your presence is your primary leadership tool.**

Most meetings are terrible. They don't have to be. This guide transforms meetings from time-wasters into relationship-builders and decision-makers.

I've attended thousands of meetings. I've run hundreds of them. The best ones share common patterns. The worst ones share common mistakes. This guide captures both.

## Pre-Meeting Prep Checklist

### Before You Schedule the Meeting

**Question 1: Is this meeting necessary?**

**Don't schedule if:**
- [ ] Information could be shared in an email or document
- [ ] You're still figuring out the purpose
- [ ] The decision could be made async
- [ ] No clear outcome or agenda

**Schedule if:**
- [ ] Complex discussion requiring real-time interaction
- [ ] Decision that benefits from diverse perspectives
- [ ] Relationship-building or team bonding
- [ ] Work that requires collaboration or co-creation

**Test:** If the meeting has no clear outcome, cancel it.

### Meeting Purpose Framework

**Define the meeting type:**

| Meeting Type | Purpose | Outcome | Recommended Length |
|--------------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **Decision** | Make a choice | Decision made | 30-45 min |
| **Brainstorm** | Generate ideas | List of ideas | 30-60 min |
| **Status** | Share updates | Alignment | 15-30 min |
| **Problem-Solving** | Address issue | Solution or next steps | 45-60 min |
| **Team Building** | Build relationships | Stronger connections | 30-60 min |
| **Retrospective** | Learn and improve | Action items | 60-90 min |

### Essential Pre-Meeting Elements

#### 1. Clear Purpose Statement

**Template:**

"By the end of this meeting, we will have [specific outcome]."

**Examples:**

❌ **Bad:** "Discuss the project"
✅ **Good:** "Decide on project timeline and identify risks"

❌ **Bad:** "Team sync"
✅ **Good:** "Align on Q2 priorities and assign ownership"

❌ **Bad:** "Review feedback"
✅ **Good:** "Identify top 3 action items from customer feedback"

#### 2. Participant List

**Ask for each invitee:**

- [ ] Why is this person necessary?
- [ ] What perspective do they bring?
- [ ] Can their role be fulfilled by someone else?
- [ ] Do they need to be there the whole time?

**Rule:** If you can't explain why someone is invited, don't invite them.

#### 3. Agenda with Times

**Template:**

| Time | Topic | Owner | Outcome |
|------|-------|-------|---------|
| 0:00-0:05 | Opening & context | [Name] | Purpose aligned |
| 0:05-0:15 | Topic 1 | [Name] | [Specific outcome] |
| 0:15-0:25 | Topic 2 | [Name] | [Specific outcome] |
| 0:25-0:30 | Decision & next steps | [Name] | Owners assigned |
| 0:30-0:35 | Buffer | - | - |

**Total:** 35 minutes scheduled, 30 minutes planned

#### 4. Pre-Work (If Applicable)

**Template:**

"Please review before the meeting:
- [Document 1] (5 min read)
- [Document 2] (10 min review)
- Come prepared to discuss [specific question]"

**Send pre-work:** Minimum 24 hours in advance

#### 5. Preparation Checklist (For You)

**Meeting Owner Prep:**

- [ ] Purpose defined and clear
- [ ] Agenda created with times
- [ ] Right people invited (and only the right people)
- [ ] Pre-work sent 24+ hours in advance
- [ ] Technology tested (if virtual)
- [ ] Materials prepared or shared
- [ ] Note-taker identified
- [ ] Timekeeper identified
- [ ] Decision criteria defined (if decision meeting)

**Participant Prep:**

- [ ] Accepted or declined (with reason if declined)
- [ ] Reviewed agenda
- [ ] Completed pre-work
- [ ] Prepared questions or input
- [ ] Reviewed background materials

## In-Meeting Facilitation Prompts

### Opening the Meeting

**Strong Opening Template:**

"Welcome everyone. I'm [your name] and I'll be facilitating today.

**Purpose:** We're here to [clear outcome statement]

**Agenda:**
1. [Topic 1] - [Time] - [Owner]
2. [Topic 2] - [Time] - [Owner]
3. [Topic 3] - [Time] - [Owner]

**Norms for today:**
- [Norm 1: e.g., One person speaks at a time]
- [Norm 2: e.g., Disagree with ideas, not people]
- [Norm 3: e.g., Silence means thinking, not agreement]

**Roles:**
- Facilitator: [Name]
- Note-taker: [Name]
- Timekeeper: [Name]

Let's get started."

### When People Aren't Paying Attention

**Gentle Redirect:**

"I want to make sure we're all together. [Name], I'd love to hear your thoughts on this."

"Let's pause for a moment. I want to make sure everyone is with us before we move on."

**Direct Approach:**

"I'm noticing some side conversations. Can we bring our full attention to the group? There are only [X] minutes left."

### When the Conversation Goes Off-Track

**Parking Lot Template:**

"That's an important topic, and I want to make sure we address it. It's not the focus of today's meeting, so I'm going to add it to the parking lot. Let's capture that and return to [agenda item]."

**Return to Purpose:**

"I want to remind us of our goal: [purpose]. How does this conversation help us achieve that?"

"Let me bring us back to the agenda. We're on topic [X], and we have [Y] minutes remaining."

### When One Person Dominates

**Direct Redirect:**

"[Name], I appreciate your enthusiasm. I want to hear from others who haven't spoken yet. Let's hold that thought and come back if there's time."

**Round-Robin Approach:**

"I want to hear from everyone. Let's go around the room. [Name], start us off with your thoughts."

**Structured Participation:**

"Before we continue, let's use silent writing. Take 2 minutes to write your thoughts, then we'll share."

### When There's Conflict

**De-escalation Script:**

"I can see we have different perspectives on this. That's valuable - it means we're thinking deeply. Let's make sure we're debating the idea, not the person."

**Finding Common Ground:**

"Let me see if I can find where we agree. It sounds like we both agree that [shared concern]. Is that right? Let's build from there."

**Structured Dialogue:**

"[Name A], share your perspective. [Name B], your role is to listen, then repeat back what you heard before responding."

### When No One Speaks

**Silence is Okay:**

"Take your time. Silence means we're thinking."

**Specific Questions:**

Instead of "Any questions?", try:
- "What's unclear about what I just shared?"
- "What concerns do you have about this approach?"
- "What would make this better?"
- "Who sees this differently?"

**Call on Specific People:**

"[Name], I'd love to hear your thoughts on this."

### When Making a Decision

**Clarify Decision Criteria:**

"Before we decide, let's agree on the criteria:
- [Criterion 1]
- [Criterion 2]
- [Criterion 3]

Our decision must meet these criteria."

**Test for Consensus:**

"I want to test where we are. On a count of 3, show your level of support:
- 5 fingers: Full support, will champion it
- 3 fingers: Can live with it, won't block it
- 1 finger: Strong objections, will block it

Ready? 3, 2, 1..."

**Fist-to-Five (Alternative):**

"Show fingers:
- 5: Love it
- 4: Like it
- 3: Neutral
- 2: Have concerns
- 1: Strong concerns
- Fist: Will block it"

**Address Objections:**

"[Name], you showed [number] fingers. Can you share your concerns?"

**Decision Statement:**

"Based on our discussion, we've decided to [decision]. This decision meets [criteria] and addresses [concerns raised]."

### Virtual Meeting Prompts

**Check Technology:**

"Can everyone see my screen? Use the reaction buttons to show yes/no."

**Ensure Participation:**

"I want to hear from everyone. We'll go in alphabetical order in the participants list."

**Combat Zoom Fatigue:**

"Let's take a 5-minute break. Stand up, stretch, move away from your screen. Back at [time]."

**Read the Room:**

"I can't see your faces, so help me out. Use reactions to show agreement (thumbs up) or disagreement (thumbs down)."

## Post-Meeting Follow-Up Templates

### Meeting Notes Template

**Send within 24 hours**

**Subject:** Notes & Action Items: [Meeting Name]

**Attendees:** [List]

**Purpose:** [Meeting purpose]

**Discussion Summary:**

**[Topic 1]**
- Key points: [Bullet points]
- Decision: [Decision made, or "None"]
- Owner: [If applicable]

**[Topic 2]**
- Key points: [Bullet points]
- Decision: [Decision made, or "None"]
- Owner: [If applicable]

**Action Items:**

| Action | Owner | Due Date |
|--------|-------|----------|
| [Specific action] | [Name] | [Date] |
| [Specific action] | [Name] | [Date] |

**Next Meeting:** [Date/Time, or "To be scheduled"]

**Parking Lot Items (to be addressed later):**
- [Item 1]
- [Item 2]

**Notes:** [Any additional context or links to documents]

---

### Action Item Follow-Up Template

**Send 2-3 days before deadline**

**Subject:** Reminder: Action Item Due [Date]

Hi [Name],

This is a friendly reminder that your action item from [meeting name] is due [date].

**Action item:** [Specific action]

**Context:** [Brief reminder of why this matters]

**Questions or blockers?**
- [ ] On track, will complete by [date]
- [ ] Need support with [specific challenge]
- [ ] Need more time until [new date]

Let me know how I can help.

Best,

[Your name]

### Decision Communication Template

**Use for decisions that affect people not in the meeting**

**Subject:** Decision Update: [Topic]

Hi [Team/Stakeholders],

I want to share a decision made in [meeting/context].

**Decision:** [Clear statement of what was decided]

**Context:** [Brief background on why the decision was needed]

**Options Considered:**
- Option 1: [Description] - [Why not chosen]
- Option 2: [Description] - [Why chosen]

**What This Means For You:**
[Specific impact on recipients]

**Next Steps:**
- [Step 1]
- [Step 2]

**Implementation:** [Timeline and owner]

**Questions?**
[Contact person or channel for questions]

Best,

[Your name]

### Meeting Debrief Template

**Use for recurring meetings to improve them over time**

**Ask at the end of the meeting:**

"Before we wrap up, I want to do a quick check-in on this meeting itself.

**What worked well:**
[Ask for specific feedback]

**What could be better:**
[Ask for specific feedback]

**One thing to change next time:**
[Ask for specific suggestion]

Thank you for the feedback. I'll incorporate it into the next meeting."

## Communication Norms Template

### Creating Team Meeting Norms

Don't assume norms—create them explicitly. Here's a template to start with, then customize with your team.

### Meeting Norms Agreement

**Purpose:** These norms create a foundation for effective, inclusive meetings.

#### Participation Norms

- [ ] **Everyone speaks, everyone listens:** We actively seek diverse perspectives
- [ ] **Step up, step back:** Those who tend to speak a lot make space; those who tend to be quiet contribute
- [ ] **Disagree with ideas, not people:** We challenge thinking, not character
- [ ] **Silence is agreement:** We explicitly ask if silence means agreement or thinking
- [ ] **One person speaks at a time:** We don't interrupt or have side conversations

#### Preparation Norms

- [ ] **Read beforehand:** We come prepared, having reviewed materials
- [ ] **On time:** We start and end on time
- [ ] **Right people:** Only essential people attend
- [ ] **No pre-work, no meeting:** If pre-work isn't done, we reschedule

#### Technology Norms

- [ ] **Phones away:** Unless needed for the meeting, phones are put away
- [ ] **Laptops closed:** Unless needed for the meeting work, laptops stay closed
- [ ] **Video on:** For virtual meetings, cameras are on (unless bandwidth is an issue)
- [ ] **Mute when not speaking:** For virtual meetings, we mute to reduce background noise

#### Decision-Making Norms

- [ ] **Clear decision criteria:** We know how decisions will be made before discussing
- [ ] **Dissent is recorded:** If we disagree, we capture it
- [ ] **Disagree and commit:** Once decided, we move forward together
- [ ] **Communicate decisions:** We document and share decisions with those affected

#### Follow-Up Norms

- [ ] **Notes within 24 hours:** Meeting notes are sent within a day
- [ ] **Action items tracked:** All action items have owners and due dates
- [ ] **Parking lot addressed:** Items in the parking lot are addressed or removed
- [ ] **Feedback loop:** We regularly review meeting effectiveness

### Norms Adoption Process

**Step 1: Draft**

Share these as a starting point, not a final version.

**Step 2: Customize**

"Let's discuss these norms as a team. What should we add, remove, or change to make these work for us?"

**Step 3: Commit**

After discussion, ask for explicit agreement: "Can we all commit to these norms going forward?"

**Step 4: Review**

Every 3-6 months, ask: "How are these norms working? What should we adjust?"

## Meeting Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)

### Common Meeting Mistakes

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Fix |
|--------------|--------------|-----|
| No clear agenda | Wastes time, unclear purpose | Always have agenda with times |
| Wrong people | Decision makers missing, too many bystanders | Carefully curate attendee list |
| Late starts | Disrespects time, creates lateness culture | Start on time, don't recap for latecomers |
| No owner | No facilitation, conversation drift | Always have designated facilitator |
 | No notes | Forgetting decisions, no follow-up | Always take notes, send within 24 hours |
 | No outcomes | Meeting for meeting's sake | Every meeting has clear outcome |
 | Virtual treated same as in-person | Different dynamics, needs different approach | Adapt for virtual, not identical |
 | Dominant voices unchecked | Groupthink, lost perspectives | actively manage participation |

### Meeting Types to Avoid

- **Status meetings where everyone reports to the manager:** Make them peer-to-peer or async
- **Brainstorming that's actually planning:** Separate idea generation from evaluation
- **Decision meetings that are actually information sharing:** Decide if it's decide or discuss
- **Large meetings where small groups would work:** Break into smaller groups

---

**Communication is relationship, not transmission.**

Every meeting is an opportunity to build trust, alignment, and shared understanding. Approach meetings not as events to manage, but as relationships to nurture.

**Your presence is your primary leadership tool.**

The best meetings aren't about perfect facilitation techniques—they're about creating space for authentic conversation, diverse perspectives, and shared commitment to action.

Meetings are where culture is created. Make them count.
