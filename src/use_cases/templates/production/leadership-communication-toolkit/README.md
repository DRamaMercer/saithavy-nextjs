# Leadership Communication Toolkit

**Your presence is your primary leadership tool.**

This comprehensive toolkit provides real-world tested templates, scripts, and frameworks for the most challenging communication moments in leadership. These aren't theoretical exercises—they're battle-tested resources drawn from actual leadership conversations, mistakes included.

## What's Inside

This toolkit contains 6 comprehensive modules designed to help you navigate difficult conversations, deliver effective feedback, audit team communication, and build stronger relationships through communication.

### Modules

1. **Difficult Conversation Script Template** - Ready-to-use scripts for 5 challenging scenarios
2. **Feedback Delivery Frameworks** - Complete system for giving and receiving feedback
3. **Team Communication Audit** - Diagnose and fix communication breakdowns
4. **Written Communication Templates** - Email scripts for 10 common scenarios
5. **Meeting Communication Guide** - Transform meetings from time-wasters to relationship-builders
6. **Non-Verbal Communication Awareness** - Master the 93% of communication that's not words

## How to Use This Toolkit

### Before the Conversation

1. **Choose your template** - Scan the directory and find the scenario that matches your situation
2. **Customize the script** - These are frameworks, not rigid scripts. Adapt to your voice and context
3. **Practice aloud** - Read the script out loud. It will feel different spoken vs. read
4. **Prepare your mindset** - Remember: Communication is relationship, not transmission

### During the Conversation

1. **Stay present** - Your presence matters more than perfect words
2. **Listen actively** - Use the listening prompts provided
3. **Adapt as needed** - These templates are guides, not rules
4. **Check for understanding** - Use the clarity questions included

### After the Conversation

1. **Document key points** - Use the follow-up templates provided
2. **Reflect on what worked** - Note what felt authentic and what didn't
3. **Schedule follow-up** - Communication is ongoing, not one-time
