## The Values Gap

There's a gap between who you are and who you want to be.

There's a gap between the values you claim and the values you live.

There's a gap between your intentions and your reality.

This is normal.

Everyone has a values gap. Everyone has areas of alignment and areas of misalignment.

**The question isn't whether you have a values gap.**
**The question is: How big is it, and what are you doing about it?**

## The Cost of Values Misalignment

Living with misaligned values has a cost:

**Emotional Cost:**
- Guilt: You know you're not being true to yourself
- Resentment: You're doing things that violate who you are
- Anxiety: You're constantly fighting your own nature
- Burnout: You're working against yourself, not with yourself

**Behavioral Cost:**
- Procrastination: You avoid things that don't align
- Self-sabotage: You undermine situations that violate your values
- People-pleasing: You betray yourself to avoid conflict
- Escapism: You numb the discomfort of misalignment

**Opportunity Cost:**
- Wrong career: Years in work that doesn't fit
- Wrong relationships: Connections that drain you
- Wrong life path: Decisions that don't feel right
- Lost time: Months and years living out of alignment

**The bigger the gap, the higher the cost.**

## The Six Life Domains

Let's look at your values across six life domains:

**1. Career**
- How you earn money
- How you spend 40+ hours per week
- Your professional identity
- Your contribution to the world

**2. Relationships**
- Partner/spouse
- Family
- Friends
- Community

**3. Health**
- Physical health
- Mental health
- Energy and vitality
- Self-care

**4. Personal Growth**
- Learning and development
- Skills and abilities
- Knowledge and wisdom
- Becoming your best self

**5. Finances**
- How you earn
- How you spend
- How you save
- Your relationship with money

**6. Community**
- Contribution to others
- Volunteering and service
- Impact and legacy
- Connection to something bigger

For each domain, some values will be honored, some will be violated.

## The Areas of Alignment

Celebrate the areas where your values are honored.

These are your strengths. These are where life feels good. These are your foundations.

Build on these.

**Example:** If your career honors "Creativity" and "Growth," you're in the right field. Maybe you need to adjust role or company, but the path is correct.

## The Areas of Misalignment

This is where you need to focus.

For each domain where values are violated, ask:

1. **Is this temporary or permanent?**
   - Temporary misalignment (startup grind, building something) is okay for a season
   - Permanent misalignment (career that violates your values) is unsustainable

2. **How big is the violation?**
   - Minor violations are manageable
   - Major violations are toxic

3. **What's one action I can take?**
   - Not "fix everything"
   - Just "improve alignment by 10%"

## The Hierarchy of Domains

Not all domains are equal.

Some people prioritize career over relationships. Some prioritize family over growth. Some prioritize health over wealth.

**That's okay.**

You don't need perfect alignment in all six domains.

You need to know which domains matter most to you, and focus there.

**My priority hierarchy:**
1. Health (without this, nothing else works)
2. Relationships (these are my foundation)
3. Career (this enables everything else)
4. Personal Growth (this keeps me growing)
5. Finances (this is a tool, not a goal)
6. Community (this is meaningful but not primary)

**Your hierarchy will be different.**

The key is knowing your hierarchy—so you can focus your energy where it matters most to YOU.
