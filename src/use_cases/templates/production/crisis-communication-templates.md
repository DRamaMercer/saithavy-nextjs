# Crisis Communication Templates

**What to Say, When, and to Whom**

---

## Quick Reference: Who to Tell When

| Circle | Tell Within | What to Share | Privacy Level |
|--------|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Inner Circle** | 24-48 hours | Full truth, emotions | Very Low |
| **Support Circle** | First week | High-level overview | Low |
| **Professional Circle** | Strategically | Minimal detail, focus on solutions | High |
| **Public Circle** | Only if necessary | Brief, controlled statement | Very High |

---

## Inner Circle Templates

### Partner/Spouse

**Initial conversation (within 24 hours):**

"[Name], I need to talk to you about something serious. I'm going through a crisis with [brief description: job loss/health issue/family situation]. I wanted you to know because you're my partner and I don't want to hide this from you. I'm still processing it myself. Can we sit down and talk about this?"

**Follow-up conversation (when you have more information):**

"I wanted to give you an update on what's happening. Here's what I know: [facts]. Here's what I'm feeling: [emotions]. Here's what I need from you right now: [specific requests]. I don't expect you to fix this—I just need you to know and be present with me."

**Ongoing communication:**

"I'm having a hard day with [crisis]. Can you just listen? I don't need solutions, just support."

---

### Parents/Family

**For adult children telling parents:**

"Mom/Dad, I wanted to share something with you. I'm dealing with [crisis]. I'm managing it and have a plan, but things are going to be tough for a while. I wanted you to know so you understand what's going on. I'll keep you updated on major developments. I love you and appreciate your support."

**For parents telling adult children:**

"Kids, I need to let you know about something happening in my life. [Share facts]. I'm handling it, but I wanted you to know in case you notice changes or if I need support down the line. I don't want you to worry—I'm taking steps to address this."

---

### Best Friend/Close Friend

**The vulnerability ask:**

"Hey [Name], can I talk to you about something personal? I'm going through a crisis with [brief description]. I'm okay, but it's really hard. I don't need you to fix it—I just need a safe person to talk to. Do you have space for that right now?"

**The emotional download:**

"I need to vent for 5 minutes without feedback. Can you just hold space for me? [Then share emotions freely.]"

**The specific request:**

"I'm dealing with [crisis] and I need help with [specific thing: child care, meals, a place to stay, someone to talk to. Would you be able to [specific request]? No pressure if you can't—I completely understand."

## Support Circle Templates

### Extended Family

"I wanted to inform you of a personal situation. I'm dealing with [high-level description]. I'm working on it and have support. I wanted you to know so you understand if I seem distant or if things are different for a while. I appreciate your support and understanding."

**If they ask for details you don't want to share:**

"I appreciate your concern. I'm focusing on managing this right now, so I'm keeping the details private. What would help most is just knowing you're in my corner."

---

### Close Colleagues/Work Friends

"Hey [Name], I wanted to share something with you. I'm going through a personal challenge [optional: very brief description]. I might seem a bit off for a while, but I'm still committed to my work. I appreciate your understanding and support."

**If they ask for details:**

"I appreciate you asking. I'd prefer to keep the details private for now, but thank you for your concern."

---

### Community Members (Religious, Hobby, Social)

"I wanted to let you know that I'm going through a difficult time right now [optional: brief description]. I might not be as active/available as usual for a while. Please don't take it personally—I'm just focusing on managing this challenge. I appreciate your support and understanding."

---

## Professional Circle Templates

### Employer/Manager

**Requesting emergency meeting:**

"Hi [Name], I need to schedule a brief meeting with you to discuss a personal situation that affects my work. Can we meet [timeframe]? Thank you."

**Meeting script:**

"[Name], I need to inform you of a personal situation. [Brief, professional description: I've experienced a health challenge/family emergency/personal crisis]. I wanted to be transparent with you about what's happening."

**I'm taking steps to manage this, including:**
- [Specific actions you're taking: medical treatment, legal counsel, financial planning]
- [Timeline: short-term/ongoing/uncertain]
- [Impact on work: any changes in availability, performance, or schedule]

**I'm committed to my role and want to discuss:**
- How to navigate this period effectively
- Any accommodations or adjustments I might need
- The best way to keep you updated

**I value my position here and want to ensure we handle this professionally. Thank you for your understanding and support.**

---

### HR/Benefits Department

"I'm experiencing a personal situation [brief description]. I'd like to understand my options for:
- [FMLA/medical leave if applicable]
- [Health insurance/COBRA]
- [Employee assistance programs]
- [Disability benefits if applicable]
- [Any other applicable benefits]

Can you walk me through what's available and the process for accessing these resources?"

---

### Clients/Customers (If Self-Employed)

**Initial communication:**

"[Name], I'm reaching out to inform you of a personal situation that may affect our work together. [Brief, professional description]. I'm committed to ensuring your needs are met and wanted to discuss how to navigate this period."

**Options to present:**
- "I'm able to continue working, but my availability may be [changed/limited]."
- "I'll need to [pause/adjust] our work together until [date]."
- "I can [refer you to a colleague/offer alternative solutions]."

**I value our working relationship and want to ensure you're supported during this time. Let's discuss what works best for you.**

---

### Professional Network

**Selective outreach (only to trusted connections):**

"Hi [Name], I hope you're well. I'm reaching out because I'm navigating a career transition [or other professional change]. I've always respected your perspective and would value any insights you might have about [specific question or area].

**If appropriate, I'd also appreciate:**
- [Any referrals or connections you might suggest]
- [Advice about next steps]
- [Information about opportunities in [area]]

**No pressure at all—I completely understand if you're not able to help. Thank you for your time and consideration.**

## Creditor/Landlord Templates

### Landlord/Property Manager

"Hi [Name], I'm experiencing a temporary financial hardship due to [brief, honest description: job loss, medical emergency, etc.]. I want to work with you on a payment plan.

**Here's what I can do:**
- [Pay $X amount now]
- [Pay remaining balance by date]
- [Set up a payment plan of $X per month]

**I value my tenancy and want to maintain a good relationship. Can we discuss options for managing this situation? Thank you for your understanding.**

---

### Creditors/Credit Card Companies

"I'm experiencing a temporary financial hardship due to [brief description]. I want to work with you on a payment arrangement.

**I'm able to:**
- [Pay $X amount per month]
- [Make a lump sum payment of $X on date]
- [Set up automatic payments for $X]

**Are there hardship programs, payment plans, or temporary reductions available? I want to pay what I owe and maintain good standing. Thank you for your help.**

---

### Utility Companies

"I'm experiencing a financial hardship and need to discuss payment options. Are there:
- Budget billing programs available?
- Payment extensions I can access?
- Low-income assistance programs?
- Deferred payment arrangements?

**I want to maintain service and pay what I owe. What options are available?**

## Sensitive Situation Templates

### When You Don't Want to Share Details

"I'm dealing with a personal matter that I prefer to keep private right now. I appreciate your concern and ask for your understanding and privacy. What would help most is knowing you're in my corner."

**If pressed:**
"I understand you're concerned, and I appreciate that. I'm not ready to discuss the details. What I need most right now is your support and understanding."

---

### When Someone's Response Is Hurtful

"I appreciate that you're trying to help, but that response [didn't feel supportive/was hurtful/wasn't what I need right now]. What would help me most is [specific need: listening, presence, lack of judgment]."

**Or simply:**
"I hear you, and I know you mean well. I'm processing this in my own way, and I need to do it my way right now."

---

### When You Need to Set Boundaries

"I love you and appreciate your support, but I need to set a boundary. [Specific boundary: I can't talk about this topic right now/I need space/I'm not taking advice at this moment]. I hope you can understand and respect that."

**Or:**
"I'm overwhelmed with advice and solutions right now. What I really need is for you to just listen and be present. Can you do that for me?"

---

## Social Media Templates

### If You Choose to Share Publicly

**Minimal approach:**

"I'm navigating a personal challenge right now. I'm taking some time away from social media to focus on my health/well-being/family. I appreciate your understanding and support. I'll be back when I'm ready. [Optional: Please respect my privacy during this time.]"

**More transparent approach:**

"Hey everyone, I wanted to share some personal news. I'm dealing with [brief description]. I'll be taking some time away to focus on [healing/recovery/managing this]. I appreciate your support and understanding. I'll share updates when I'm ready. In the meantime, please respect my privacy."

---

### How to Respond to Comments

**To supportive comments:**
"Thank you so much for your kindness and support. It means a lot to me."

**To invasive comments:**
"I appreciate your concern. I'm keeping the details private right now, but thank you for thinking of me."

**To unsolicited advice:**
"I appreciate you trying to help. I'm working with professionals/support system and focusing on what I need right now. Thanks for understanding."

## Children Communication Templates

### Age-Appropriate Scripts

**Young Children (Ages 3-7):**

"Mommy/Daddy is going through a hard time right now. It's kind of like when you have a really bad day, but for grown-ups. Everything is going to be okay, but things might be a little different for a while. You're safe, and you're loved, and we're a team."

**School-Age Children (Ages 8-12):**

"I wanted to talk to you about something happening in our family. [Give brief, honest, age-appropriate explanation.] This means things might be different for a while. You might see me sad/upset/stressed, and I want you to know that's okay and normal. You can ask me questions anytime. You're safe, and we'll get through this together."

**Teenagers (Ages 13-18):**

"I want to be transparent with you about something happening. [Be honest about the situation.] This is going to affect our family in [specific ways]. I want you to know what's going on so you're not confused or scared. You can ask me anything, and I'll tell you what I can. We'll navigate this together."

## Rejection/No-Response Templates

### When Someone Doesn't Respond

"Hey [Name], I know you're busy, but I'm going through a tough time and could really use your support. No pressure, but if you could [specific request], it would mean a lot. Let me know if you can help."

**Or simply:**
"I'm going through a hard time and could use support. Are you available to talk this week?"

---

### When Someone Declines to Help

"Thank you for letting me know. I understand. If anything changes and you're able to help, please let me know. Take care."

**Or:**
"I appreciate your honesty. No worries at all—I'll manage. Hope you're doing well."

## Post-Crisis Update Templates

### To Inner Circle (When You're Ready)

"I wanted to give you an update on my situation with [crisis]. Here's where things stand: [brief update]. I'm feeling [better/improving/stabilizing]. I really appreciate your support through this—it made a huge difference. I wanted to let you know I'm doing okay and getting back to normal."

---

### To Professional Circle

"I wanted to update you on my situation. I'm now [recovered/stabilizing/back to normal]. I really appreciate your understanding and support during this time. I'm fully engaged and ready to [move forward/continue our work together]. Thank you for your patience."

## Communication Guidelines

### Do's

- **Be honest but selective:** Share what's necessary, withhold what's harmful
- **Set clear boundaries:** You control what you share and with whom
- **Ask for what you need:** People can't read your mind
- **Express gratitude:** Thank people for their support
- **Protect your peace:** Limit sharing with people who drain you
- **Use "I" statements:** Speak from your experience, not accusations

### Don'ts

- **Don't overshare:** You don't owe everyone the full story
- **Don't justify or defend:** Your crisis is valid, period
- **Don't let others dictate your healing:** Process at your own pace
- **Don't accept unsolicited advice:** Unless you asked for it
- **Don't share to get validation:** Your crisis is valid regardless of others' reactions
- **Don't compare your crisis to others:** Your experience is yours

---

## Remember

**You don't owe anyone an explanation.** You choose what to share, when, and with whom.

**People's reactions are about them, not you.** If someone responds poorly, that's on them.

**Support is not weakness.** Asking for help is a strength, not a character flaw.

**You've survived 100% of your bad days.** This communication is part of surviving this one.
