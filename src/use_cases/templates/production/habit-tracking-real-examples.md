## My 10-Year Habit Journey {#my-journey}

### By The Numbers

**Habits Attempted:** 73
**Habits That Stuck:** 51
**Habits That Failed:** 22
**Success Rate:** 70%
**Current Automatic Habits:** 38
**Habits Still Requiring Effort:** 13

**Time to Automatic (Average):**
- Simple habits (floss, drink water): 21 days
- Medium habits (exercise, meditation): 45 days
- Complex habits (creative practice, learning): 90+ days

### What I Got Wrong (First 3 Years)

❌ **Too many habits at once**
- Tried to build 7 habits simultaneously
- Result: All of them failed within 2 weeks
- Lesson: One habit at a time beats three poorly

❌ **Starting too big**
- "I'll exercise for an hour daily"
- Result: Lasted 4 days, quit
- Lesson: Start with 10 minutes, build from there

❌ **No tracking**
- "I'll just remember to do it"
- Result: Forgot 50% of the time
- Lesson: Tracking is non-negotiable

❌ **Focusing on outcomes, not identity**
- "I need to lose 20 pounds"
- Result: Lost motivation when scale didn't move
- Lesson: "I'm someone who exercises" works better

❌ **Quitting after one missed week**
- "I missed 5 days, I failed"
- Result: Started over constantly
- Lesson: Never miss twice > perfection

### What I Got Right (Last 7 Years)

✓ **One habit at a time**
- Add new habit only after current one is 80% automatic
- Result: 70% success rate
- Lesson: Patience compounds

✓ **Stupidly small starts**
- "I'll meditate for 2 minutes" (not 30)
- Result: Built to 20 minutes over 6 months
- Lesson: Lower bar = higher completion = momentum

✓ **Daily tracking**
- Simple checkbox in journal
- Result: Can't stop tracking now
- Lesson: What gets measured gets managed

✓ **Habit stacking**
- Attach everything to existing routines
- Result: Automatic triggers
- Lesson: Environment > willpower

✓ **Identity-based habits**
- "I'm someone who writes daily" vs "I need to write a book"
- Result: Published first book at year 7
- Lesson: Identity shift happens before results

## Failure Stories (And What I Learned) {#failure-stories}

### Failure 1: Cold Shower Every Morning

**The Habit:** Take a cold shower every morning
**Duration:** Lasted 5 days
**Why It Failed:**
- hated every second of it
- No intrinsic motivation (was doing it because "successful people do it")
- Never felt good, only felt bad
- Eventually just didn't do it one day, then quit

**What I Learned:**
- You need to not hate the habit
- External motivation ("successful people do it") isn't enough
- Some habits just aren't for you
- It's OK to quit and try something different

**Alternative That Worked:**
- 30-second cold blast at END of shower (tolerable)
- Eventually built to 60 seconds
- Now do it sometimes (not every day)

---

### Failure 2: Wake Up at 5 AM

**The Habit:** Wake up at 5 AM every day
**Duration:** Lasted 2 weeks
**Why It Failed:**
- I'm a night owl by biology
- Was exhausted by 2pm
- Productivity actually decreased
- Eventually started sleeping in

**What I Learned:**
- Biology matters (night owl vs. early bird)
- You can't fight your chronotype forever
- "5 AM club" isn't for everyone
- Sleep consistency > wake time

**What Works Instead:**
- Wake up at same time daily (for me: 7 AM)
- Get natural light immediately
- Go to bed at same time
- Focus on sleep consistency, not early rising

---

### Failure 3: Read 1 Book Per Week

**The Habit:** Read 1 book per week (52 books/year)
**Duration:** Lasted 3 months, read 12 books
**Why It Failed:**
- Felt like a chore
- Was skimming to finish, not enjoying
- Quality suffered for quantity
- Eventually burned out

**What I Learned:**
- Goals should be about who you want to be, not arbitrary numbers
- "I'm someone who reads" vs "I need to read 52 books"
- Quality > quantity
- Some books deserve slow reading

**What Works Instead:**
- Read 10 pages daily (no finish pressure)
- Sometimes 1 book/week, sometimes 1 book/month
- Focus on learning, not finishing
- Now read 30-40 books/year and enjoy all of them

---

### Failure 4: No Social Media for 30 Days

**The Habit:** Complete social media detox
**Duration:** Lasted 8 days
**Why It Failed:**
- Too extreme (all-or-nothing)
- Felt isolated (FOMO)
- No replacement for connection needs
- Eventually caved and binged

**What I Learned:**
- Extreme habits rarely stick
- Need replacement behaviors
- Willpower is finite
- Gradual reduction > cold turkey

**What Works Instead:**
- No social media until 10 AM (working well)
- No social media during meals (working well)
- 30-minute limit/day (working well)
- Reduced usage 70% without feeling deprived

---

### Failure 5: Gym 6 Days Per Week

**The Habit:** Go to gym 6 days/week
**Duration:** Lasted 3 weeks
**Why It Failed:**
- Too much, too soon
- Felt like a part-time job
- Eventually started dreading it
- Missed one day, then quit

**What I Learned:**
- Start with less than you think
- You can always add more later
- It's better to do 3 days consistently than 6 days for 3 weeks
- Never miss twice > never miss

**What Works Instead:**
- Started with 3 days/week (now automatic)
- Built to 4 days/week over 6 months
- Now at 5 days/week and enjoy it
- Quality > quantity always

**Base:** Pour coffee (automatic)
```

   ↓ Stack: While coffee brews (3 minutes) → Meditate
   ↓ Result: Daily meditation practice

2. Drink coffee
   ↓ Stack: While drinking coffee (10 minutes) → Write morning pages
   ↓ Result: Daily writing practice

3. Finish coffee
   ↓ Stack: Immediately after → Drink 16oz water
   ↓ Result: Daily hydration habit

4. Walk to kitchen
   ↓ Stack: While in kitchen → Take vitamins
   ↓ Result: Daily supplement habit

5. Finish kitchen routine
   ↓ Stack: Before leaving kitchen → Plan top 3 priorities
   ↓ Result: Daily planning habit
```

**Total Time:** 30 minutes
**Habits Built:** 5
**All Automatic:** Yes (after 6 months)

## Troubleshooting Real Situations {#real-troubleshooting}

### Situation: Travel Completely Disrupted My Routine

**What Happened:**
- Business trip for 1 week
- All habits fell apart
- Felt like "I failed, what's the point"

**What I Did Wrong:**
- Had no "minimum viable version" of habits
- All-or-nothing thinking
- Let one bad week become three bad weeks

**What I Do Now:**
- Create "travel versions" of habits
- Example: "Hotel room meditation" (2 minutes on edge of bed)
- Example: "Hotel room workout" (10 minutes, no equipment)
- Example: "Travel planning" (5 minutes on phone)
- Focus on maintenance, not growth
- Rebuild routine gradually when back

**Lesson:** Have "emergency" versions of your habits for chaotic times.

---

### Situation: I Got Sick and Missed a Week

**What Happened:**
- Bad flu for 1 week
- Couldn't do any habits
- Felt guilty about "losing progress"

**What I Did Wrong:**
- Tried to resume at full intensity immediately
- Burned out again
- Felt like I was "starting over"

**What I Do Now:**
- Accept that sick week = 0% progress lost (seriously)
- Resume at 50% intensity for one week
- Example: Instead of 20-minute meditation, do 5 minutes
- Example: Instead of 30-minute workout, do 10 minutes
- Build back to 100% over 2-3 weeks

**Lesson:** Never go from 0 to 100. Ramp back up gradually.

---

### Situation: Life Got Overwhelming (Work Crisis)

**What Happened:**
- Major work crisis for 3 weeks
- All mental bandwidth went to crisis
- Habits fell apart

**What I Did Wrong:**
- Tried to maintain all habits
- Failed at all of them
- Felt overwhelmed

**What I Do Now:**
- Identify "core habits" (can't drop these)
- Identify "nice-to-have habits" (pause these)
- Core habits for me: meditation, sleep, basic movement
- Pause: creative practice, learning projects, extra workouts
- Rebuild gradually after crisis passes

**Lesson:** Know which habits are essential and which are optional. Drop the optional during crisis.

---

### Situation: I Just Got Bored With the Habit

**What Happened:**
- Had been doing habit for 6 months
- Felt stale, unmotivated
- Started missing days

**What I Did Wrong:**
- Just quit (all-or-nothing)
- Lost all progress
- Had to start over later

**What I Do Now:**
- "Level up" the habit instead of quitting
- Example: 10-minute meditation → 15-minute meditation
- Example: Same walking route → New walking route
- Example: Sol hobby reading → Try audiobooks
- Add variety while maintaining consistency

**Lesson:** Boredom means it's time to evolve, not quit.

## Key Takeaways from 10 Years

### What Actually Matters

**1. Consistency > Intensity**
- 10 minutes daily > 60 minutes once per week
- You build identity through showing up, not through heroic efforts

**2. Environment > Willpower**
- Design your space to make the habit easy
- Remove friction from good habits
- Add friction to bad habits

**3. Identity > Goals**
- "I'm someone who exercises" > "I need to lose 20 pounds"
- Identity change happens around 6 months of consistency
- Results follow identity

**4. Tracking > Motivation**
- What gets measured gets managed
- Even a simple checkbox works
- Tracking activates your brain's reward system

**5. Never Miss Twice > Perfection**
- One missed day = 0% progress lost
- Two missed days = start of a new pattern
- Three missed days = habit is dying

### What Doesn't Matter

❌ Perfect streaks (missed days are part of the process)
❌ Starting on Monday/start of month (start today)
❌ Building 10 habits at once (one at a time)
❌ Extreme habits (gradual > radical)
❌ How you feel about it (do it anyway)

### The Secret

There is no secret.

There's just:
- showing up
- doing the work
- tracking your progress
- adjusting when needed
- repeating until it's automatic

**Build strength through the reps.**

Not because you're special. Because you're consistent.
