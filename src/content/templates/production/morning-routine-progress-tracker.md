# Progress Tracker

**Track your consistency, measure your impact, and optimize your morning routine over time.**

---

## How to Use This Tracker

**The goal**: Track not just whether you did your routine, but HOW it affected your day.

**The insight**: Data helps you optimize. Without tracking, you're guessing.

**The method**: 2 minutes of logging after your morning routine. That's it.

## Digital Tracker Options

**Prefer digital over paper? Here are options:**

### Apps
- **Habitica**: Gamify your habit tracking with RPG elements
- **Streaks**: Simple, beautiful habit tracking (iOS)
- **HabitHub**: Free, open-source habit tracker with charts
- **Notion**: Build your own custom tracker with templates

### Spreadsheets
- **Google Sheets**: Create a simple grid with dates and habits
- **Excel**: Use conditional formatting to color-code consistency

### Notion Template
- Create a database with these fields:
  - Date
  - Routine completed? (checkbox)
  - Which elements? (multi-select)
  - Energy level (1-10)
  - Notes (text)
- Use different views: Calendar view, Kanban view, Dashboard

## Final Thoughts on Tracking

**Tracking serves three purposes**:

1. **Accountability**: Seeing your streak motivates you to continue
2. **Insight**: Data reveals patterns you'd otherwise miss
3. **Optimization**: You can't improve what you don't measure

**But tracking can become a trap**:

- Don't let perfect tracking become more important than the routine itself
- If you miss logging for a few days, it's okay—just pick it back up
- The tracker is a tool, not a taskmaster

**The best tracker is the one you'll actually use.**

If that's a fancy app, great. If it's a checklist on a sticky note, that's great too.

**What matters is not how you track, but that you track.**

**Systems before willpower. Build strength through the reps.**

Start tracking today.
