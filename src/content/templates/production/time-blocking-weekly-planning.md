# Weekly Planning Template for Time-Blocking

**15-20 Minutes Weekly to Transform Your Productivity**

I've tested weekly planning systems for 10 years. This is what works. Daily planning is too reactive. Monthly planning is too abstract. Weekly hits the sweet spot.

Systems before willpower. Your environment shapes your behavior.

---

## When to Do Your Weekly Planning

Choose one and stick to it:

- **Sunday evening** (7-8pm): Prepare for Monday, reduce weekend anxiety
- **Monday morning** (7-8am): Fresh start, clear mind from weekend rest
- **Friday afternoon** (4-5pm): Review week, plan next week, end workday strong

**Most successful:** Sunday evening. You sleep better knowing Monday is planned.

## Weekly Planning Quick Checklist

Use this checklist every week until it becomes automatic (usually 2-3 weeks):

- [ ] Reviewed last week's wins and challenges
- [ ] Identified top 3 priorities for this week
- [ ] Calculated deep work hours needed vs. available
- [ ] Scheduled deep work blocks during peak energy times
- [ ] Batched shallow work into dedicated blocks
- [ ] Consolidated meetings into specific time blocks
- [ ] Scheduled life blocks (exercise, meals, family)
- [ ] Added daily buffer blocks (15-30 min)
- [ ] Scheduled next week's planning session
- [ ] Felt confident and clear about the week ahead

**If you didn't check all boxes, your planning isn't complete. Spend 5 more minutes.**

## Common Weekly Planning Mistakes

### Mistake #1: Skipping the Review
You jump straight to planning next week without reviewing last week. **Spend 5 minutes reviewing.** The data will improve your planning.

### Mistake #2: Too Many Priorities
You list 5-10 priorities instead of 3. **Pick 3 only.** If you finish them early, add more. Better to under-plan and over-deliver.

### Mistake #3: Ignoring Energy Data
You schedule deep work whenever, not during peak energy. **Check your energy audit.** Schedule deep work when your energy is 7-10.

### Mistake #4: Over-Scheduling
You fill every minute with work. **Schedule 60-70% max.** Leave 30-40% for buffers, breaks, and unexpected events.

### Mistake #5: Forgetting Life Blocks
You schedule work but forget exercise, meals, family. **Schedule life first.** Work fills the remaining time.

### Mistake #6: No Buffer Blocks
You schedule back-to-back with no overflow time. **Add 15-30 min buffers daily.** Life happens.

## Printable Weekly Planning Template

To create a printable version:

1. **Copy this template** into a document (Google Docs, Word, Notion)
2. **Add space for handwritten notes** (leave blank lines)
3. **Print multiple copies** (one for each week)
4. **Fill in by hand** during your weekly planning session

**Pro tip:** Create a weekly planning binder with 52 templates (one for each week). Review at year-end to see patterns and progress.

## Digital Weekly Planning Setup

### Google Calendar Setup

1. **Create a "Weekly Planning" recurring event:**
   - Sunday 7-7:20pm OR Monday 7-7:20am
   - Set reminder: 15 minutes before
   - Add agenda template in event description

2. **Create calendars for each category:**
   - Deep Work (blue)
   - Shallow Work (green)
   - Meetings (yellow)
   - Breaks (orange)
   - Life (red)
   - Buffer (gray)

3. **Use Tasks for priorities:**
   - Add your top 3 priorities as Google Tasks
   - Link tasks to specific deep work blocks
   - Check off when complete

4. **Set up weekly review template:**
   - Create Google Doc with template
   - Link in calendar event description
   - Fill in during weekly planning session

### Notion Template Setup

1. **Create a "Weekly Planning" database:**
   - Properties: Week start date, priorities, deep work hours, completion rate
   - Template: The exact template from this document
   - Views: Weekly calendar, kanban board, list view

2. **Create related databases:**
   - Daily Time Blocks (linked to weekly planning)
   - Priorities (linked to weekly and daily)
   - Progress Tracking (linked to weekly data)

3. **Add automations:**
   - Auto-create new week template every Sunday
   - Roll over incomplete priorities to next week
   - Calculate deep work hours automatically

**Pro tip:** Use Notion's template button to instantly generate a new weekly planning page with all sections pre-filled.
