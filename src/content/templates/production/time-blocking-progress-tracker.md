# Time-Blocking Progress Tracker

**Measure What Matters: Weekly Metrics Dashboard**

I've tracked productivity metrics for 10 years. One truth emerges consistently: What gets measured gets managed. Most people don't track their time-blocking because they're afraid of what the data will show.

Don't be. The data is your friend. It reveals what's working, what isn't, and where to optimize.

Systems before willpower. Your environment shapes your behavior.

---

## How to Use This Tracker

### Weekly Review (15 minutes every Sunday or Monday)

1. **Fill out the metrics** (be honest, not idealistic)
2. **Review your 4-week trends** (look for patterns)
3. **Celebrate wins** (even small ones)
4. **Identify 1-2 adjustments** for next week

### Monthly Review (30 minutes at month-end)

1. **Review all 4-5 weeks** of data
2. **Identify major patterns** and breakthroughs
3. **Update your baseline targets** (as you improve)
4. **Plan next month's focus** (what to optimize)

## Digital Progress Tracker Setup

### Google Sheets Setup

1. **Create a "Time-Blocking Progress" spreadsheet**

2. **Create tabs:**
   - Weekly Dashboard (one tab per week)
   - Monthly Summary (one tab per month)
   - Yearly Overview (one tab)
   - Charts & Graphs (visualize trends)

3. **Weekly Dashboard tab columns:**
   - Week start date
   - Deep work hours (target, actual, %)
   - Priorities completed (target, actual, %)
   - Schedule adherence (target, actual)
   - Energy alignment (target, actual)
   - Notes

4. **Create formulas:**
   - Calculate % of target automatically
   - Calculate averages across weeks
   - Calculate month-to-date totals

5. **Create charts:**
   - Deep work hours trend (line graph)
   - Priorities completed trend (bar chart)
   - Schedule adherence trend (line graph)
   - Energy alignment trend (line graph)

**Pro tip:** Use Google Sheets' "Protected Ranges" to prevent accidentally overwriting formulas.

### Notion Setup

1. **Create a "Time-Blocking Progress" database**

2. **Properties:**
   - Week start date (date)
   - Deep work hours (number)
   - Deep work target (number)
   - Priorities completed (number)
   - Priorities target (number)
   - Schedule adherence (number)
   - Energy alignment (number)
   - Notes (text)

3. **Create views:**
   - Weekly dashboard (table view)
   - Monthly summary (calendar view)
   - Trends (gallery view with charts)

4. **Add templates:**
   - Weekly entry template (pre-filled with targets)
   - Monthly review template (pre-filled with questions)

5. **Create relations:**
   - Link to daily time blocks database
   - Link to weekly planning database
   - Link to energy audit database

**Pro tip:** Use Notion's "Rollup" property to calculate monthly and yearly averages automatically.

## Tracking Tips

### Tip 1: Be Honest, Not Idealistic

Track what actually happened, not what you wish happened. If deep work was 2 hours, log 2 hours. Not 4 hours "because I should have done more."

### Tip 2: Track Weekly, Not Daily

Daily tracking is too reactive and discouraging. Weekly tracking reveals patterns and smooths out daily variation.

### Tip 3: Focus on Trends, Not Individual Weeks

One bad week doesn't mean the system failed. Look at 4-week trends. That's where the real data lives.

### Tip 4: Celebrate Small Wins

Increased deep work by 10%? Celebrate. Improved schedule adherence by 5%? Celebrate. Momentum builds on small wins.

### Tip 5: Adjust Targets as You Improve

Once you consistently exceed targets for 3-4 weeks, raise your baseline. That's how you progress from beginner to master.
