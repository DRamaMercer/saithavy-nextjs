### [0:10-2:30] WELCOME + PERSONAL STORY + TEACHING

[Voice warm, slightly more energized, like sharing a discovered secret]

"Welcome back to the Resilience Audio Series. So glad you're here for Part 2.

Last time, we talked about understanding resilience—how it's about integration, not bouncing back. How real resilience means carrying your experiences forward with wisdom, not denial.

Today, we're getting practical. Because here's what I've learned: Understanding resilience in theory is great. But building resilience in practice? That's what actually changes your life.

Let me tell you something I learned during those dark years I mentioned in Part 1. You know, when everything felt like it was falling apart?

I used to think resilience was this big, dramatic thing. Like summiting a mountain or surviving a crisis. I thought you either had it or you didn't. And when I was struggling, I thought, 'Guess I just don't have what it takes.'

But I was wrong.

Here's what I discovered: Real resilience isn't built in moments of crisis. It's built in ordinary moments. It's built on Tuesdays when you're tired but still take care of yourself. It's built when you practice small habits, consistently, even when nothing dramatic is happening.

I share this because I wasted so much time waiting for a big breakthrough, not realizing that the small things were already building my resilience muscles.

You don't need to be perfect. You need to be consistent.

And that's what today's episode is about: The micro-practices that build resilience daily. Not when everything is falling apart—but right now, in your ordinary life.

Because here's the truth: If you wait until crisis hits to start building resilience, you're too late. You don't wait until you're drowning to learn how to swim. You practice when the water is calm.

So today, I'm sharing something I call the 'Five-Minute Resilience Protocol.' These are practices I've used—and still use—every single day. They take less than five minutes. But done consistently, they transform your relationship with difficulty.

Adversity reveals what you're made of—but daily practice determines what that is.

Let me walk you through the protocol."

---

### [2:30-12:30] GUIDED PRACTICE: DAILY RESILIENCE BUILDING

**Action:** Music shifts to steady, supportive background

"The Five-Minute Resilience Protocol has four parts. We're going to practice each one right now, so you can experience it directly.

**PART 1: The Morning Check-In (60 seconds)**

This is the first thing I do every morning, before coffee, before checking my phone, before anything.

Sit comfortably. Close your eyes if that feels right.

Take a deep breath.

[PAUSE 5 seconds]

Now, ask yourself three questions:

1. 'How am I feeling right now?' Not how should I feel. How do I actually feel?

[PAUSE 10 seconds for reflection]

2. 'What do I need today?' Not what do you want to accomplish. What do you actually need?

[PAUSE 10 seconds for reflection]

3. 'What's one small thing I can do to take care of myself today?'

[PAUSE 10 seconds for reflection]

Don't overthink this. Your first instinct is usually right. Maybe you need rest. Maybe you need movement. Maybe you need connection. Maybe you need to say 'no' to something.

**PART 2: The Body Reset (60 seconds)**

This one is crucial because stress lives in the body. You can't think your way out of it—you have to move.

Stand up if you can. If not, you can do this seated.

Shake out your hands.

[PAUSE 3 seconds]

Shake out your feet.

[PAUSE 3 seconds]

Roll your shoulders—big circles, backwards, forwards.

[PAUSE 5 seconds]

Now, shake your whole body—like you're shaking off stress. Shake your arms, your legs, your torso. Let yourself be a little silly.

[PAUSE 10 seconds]

Now take a deep breath, reach your arms overhead, and exhale with a big sigh. Let it all out.

[PAUSE 5 seconds]

Feel that? Even in 60 seconds, you've shifted your state. That's the body reset.

**PART 3: The Difficult Truth Practice (90 seconds)**

This one is harder. But it's powerful.

I want you to identify one difficult truth you've been avoiding. Something you know, deep down, but haven't wanted to face. It could be:

- A relationship that's not working
- A habit you need to change
- A conversation you've been putting off
- A pattern you keep repeating

[PAUSE 10 seconds]

Got it? Good.

Now, instead of spiraling into shame or avoidance, I want you to practice saying this to yourself:

'I am facing [difficult truth], and I am still worthy.'

[PAUSE 10 seconds]

Say it again, out loud if you can:

'I am facing [difficult truth], and I am still worthy.'

[PAUSE 10 seconds]

Now ask yourself: 'What is one small step I can take to address this?'

Not the giant solution. Just one small step.

[PAUSE 15 seconds for reflection]

That's it. You don't have to fix it today. You just have to acknowledge it and identify one step.

**PART 4: The Resilience Anchor (60 seconds)**

This last one is about connecting with your inner strength. And when I say strength, I don't mean toughness. I mean that part of you that has survived every difficult day of your life so far.

Place your hand on your heart.

[PAUSE 3 seconds]

Feel your heartbeat. That's your life force. That's what has carried you through everything.

Now, I want you to recall a time when you faced something difficult—and you got through it. It doesn't have to be dramatic. Maybe you got through a really hard week. Maybe you handled a crisis. Maybe you just kept going when you wanted to give up.

[PAUSE 10 seconds]

Remember that feeling. Remember that you DID it. You figured it out. You survived.

Now say to yourself:

'I have faced difficult things before, and I can face this too.'

[PAUSE 10 seconds]

'I don't have to do it perfectly. I just have to do it.'

[PAUSE 10 seconds]

'I am stronger than I think, and I don't have to do this alone.'

[PAUSE 10 seconds]

Let that sink in. That's your resilience anchor.

**PUTTING IT ALL TOGETHER**

So that's the full protocol:

1. Morning Check-In: How am I? What do I need? What's one small thing for self-care?
2. Body Reset: Shake it out, move, breathe
3. Difficult Truth: Face one hard thing, identify one small step
4. Resilience Anchor: Remember your strength

Total time? Less than five minutes.

But here's the key: You have to do it every day. Or at least, most days. You don't need to be perfect. You need to be consistent.

[PAUSE 10 seconds]

Before we close this practice, I want you to commit to yourself: When will you do this protocol tomorrow? Morning? Afternoon? Evening?

[PAUSE 15 seconds for commitment]

Pick a time. And hold that commitment gently.

Now, slowly bring your attention back to the room.

[PAUSE 5 seconds]

Open your eyes when you're ready.

**Action:** Music gently swells

You've just learned the daily resilience building practice."

### [12:30-15:00] CLOSING + ENCOURAGEMENT + NEXT STEPS

**Action:** Inspiring, forward-looking music

"Thank you for practicing with me today.

I know what some of you are thinking: 'Five minutes doesn't sound like much.' And you're right—it's not much. But done consistently, over days and weeks and months? It changes everything.

Remember: I'm not asking you to meditate for an hour or run five miles or overhaul your entire life. I'm asking you to spend five minutes a day checking in with yourself, moving your body, facing truth, and remembering your strength.

That's doable. That's sustainable. And that's what builds real resilience.

**Here's what I want you to remember this week:**

**Resilience is not a destination. It's a daily practice.**

You don't 'achieve' resilience and then you're done. You practice it, day after day, in small ways, in ordinary moments.

You don't need to be perfect. You need to be consistent.

**For this week:**
Commit to doing the Five-Minute Protocol at least four times. Pick a specific time of day and protect it. Treat it like an important appointment—because it is.

Track your progress. Just a simple checkmark each time you do it. Notice how it affects your days.

**Coming up next:**
In Part 3, we're going to talk about what to do when everything falls apart. Because here's the truth: Even with daily practice, crisis will come. And when it does, you need a different set of tools. I'll share my Emergency Resilience Protocol—the practices that got me through my darkest days, and that can help you through yours.

Remember: Adversity reveals what you're made of—and daily practice determines what that is.

You've got this. And you don't have to do it alone.

Until next time.

**Action:** Music fades out over 15 seconds
