# Resilience Audio Series - Quick Reference Guides

## Five-Minute Daily Resilience Protocol

**Use this protocol every day for building sustainable resilience**

---

### Part 1: Morning Check-In (60 seconds)

**Ask yourself three questions:**

1. **How am I feeling right now?**
   - Not how should I feel
   - How do I actually feel?

2. **What do I need today?**
   - Not what to accomplish
   - What do I actually need?

3. **What's one small thing for self-care?**
   - Rest, movement, connection, boundaries?

---

### Part 2: Body Reset (60 seconds)

**Move to release stress from the body:**

1. Shake out your hands
2. Shake out your feet
3. Roll shoulders (big circles, forward and back)
4. Shake whole body (like shaking off stress)
5. Deep breath, reach arms overhead, big exhale with sigh

---

### Part 3: Difficult Truth Practice (90 seconds)

**Face one avoided truth:**

1. Identify one difficult truth you've been avoiding
   - Relationship not working?
   - Habit to change?
   - Conversation needed?
   - Pattern repeating?

2. Say to yourself:
   *"I am facing [difficult truth], and I am still worthy"*

3. Ask yourself:
   *"What is one small step I can take?"*

---

### Part 4: Resilience Anchor (60 seconds)

**Connect with your inner strength:**

1. Place hand on heart
2. Feel your heartbeat (your life force)
3. Remember a time you faced difficulty and got through it
4. Say to yourself:
   - *"I have faced difficult things before, and I can face this too"*
   - *"I don't have to do it perfectly. I just have to do it"*
   - *"I am stronger than I think, and I don't have to do this alone"*

---

## Emergency Crisis Protocol

**Use this protocol when everything feels overwhelming**

---

### Step 1: Reality Anchor (90 seconds)

**Ground yourself in the present moment:**

1. Ask: **Are you safe right now?**
2. If yes, say:
   - *"In this moment, I am safe"*
   - *"In this moment, I have what I need"*
   - *"In this moment, I can breathe"*

3. Name what's around you:
   - 3 things you can see
   - 2 things you can hear
   - 1 thing you can feel

---

### Step 2: Breathing Anchor (120 seconds)

**Override fight-or-flight response:**

**Box Breathing:**
- Inhale for count of 4
- Hold for count of 4
- Exhale for count of 4
- Hold empty for count of 4

Repeat for 3-4 cycles

---

### Step 3: One-Hour Rule

**Focus only on the next hour:**

Ask yourself:
*"What is the one thing I need in the next hour to take care of myself?"*

Examples:
- Drink a glass of water
- Call one person
- Take a shower
- Just breathe

Say to yourself:
*"For the next hour, my only job is [one thing]"*

You can handle one hour.

---

### Step 4: Resource Line-Up

**Know who to call before you need to call:**

Identify 3 people or resources:
- Family member
- Therapist/counselor
- Crisis hotline
- Friend good in emergencies
- Support group

**If currently in crisis: Reach out TODAY**

**Crisis Resources:**
- National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: **988** (US)
- Crisis Text Line: Text **HOME** to **741741**
- International: https://www.iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres/
- Emergency: **911** (US), **999** (UK), **112** (EU)

---

### Step 5: Survival Mantra (60 seconds)

**Repeat what's true when your mind turns against you:**

Choose your mantra:
- *"I have survived 100% of my difficult days so far"*
- *"I don't know how I'll get through this, but I will"*
- *"I don't have to be okay right now. I just have to keep going"*

Repeat as needed.

## Key Reminders

**About Resilience:**
- Resilience is about **integration**, not bouncing back
- Built in **ordinary moments**, not just crises
- You don't need to be **perfect**; you need to be **consistent**
- Your body has carried you through **100% of your bad days**

**In Crisis:**
- You only have to get through the **next hour**
- You don't have to do this **alone**
- You've survived **100% of your difficult days so far**
- Reach out for **real help** when needed

**Daily Practice:**
- Same time each day works best
- Track your progress (simple checkmark)
- Notice how it affects your days
- Be consistent, not perfect

---

## Practice Schedule

**Daily (Five-Minute Protocol):**
- Morning, afternoon, or evening
- Choose a specific time and protect it
- Treat it like an important appointment
- At least 4 times per week

**Emergency (Crisis Protocol):**
- Save this guide in your phone
- Know your 3 support contacts before crisis
- Practice once when calm so you know it
- Use when overwhelmed or in crisis
