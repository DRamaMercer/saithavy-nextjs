## Core Frameworks

### The 3 Types of Focus

**Type 1: Shallow Focus**
- Low-intensity, easy to switch
- Email, Slack, quick calls, routine tasks
- Sustainable: All day (but not for deep work)

**Type 2: Moderate Focus**
- Sustained attention on medium-complexity tasks
- Straightforward emails, reviewing docs, planning
- Sustainable: 2-3 hours

**Type 3: Deep Focus**
- Complete concentration on cognitively demanding tasks
- Complex problem-solving, creative work, learning
- Sustainable: 90 minutes max

---

### The 5 Phases of Deep Work

**Phase 1: Setup (2 minutes)**
- Clear workspace completely
- Phone in another room
- Close all browser tabs except what you need
- Water/tea ready beforehand

**Phase 2: Commitment (1 minute)**
- Say out loud what you're committing to
- "I am entering deep work for 90 minutes on [task]"
- Put your brain on notice

**Phase 3: Transition (10-15 minutes)**
- The discomfort zone
- Start EASY
- Set a timer for 15 minutes
- Push through resistance

**Phase 4: Deep Work (60-70 minutes)**
- You're in the zone now
- Keep a notepad for distractions
- Take 30-second breaks if stuck
- Trust the process

**Phase 5: Exit (5-10 minutes)**
- Complete your current thought
- Write down where you left off
- Stretch, full break
- THEN check phone/messages

---

### Distraction Handling System

**Type 1: External Distractions** (someone calls, notification)
- If urgent: Pause session
- If not urgent: "I'll get back to you in an hour"
- Return to work immediately

**Type 2: Internal Distractions** (urge to check phone, random thoughts)
- **NOTICE:** "Oh, distraction arising"
- **NAME:** "That's the urge to check my phone"
- **WRITE:** Jot it down (don't act on it)
- **RETURN:** Immediately, without judgment

**Key Insight:** Don't fight distractions. Handle them.

---

### The 3 Types of Recovery

**Type 1: Micro-Recovery (5-15 minutes)**
- Between deep work sessions
- Stand up, stretch, walk around
- Get water, tea
- Look out a window
- **No phone, no email**

**Type 2: Mid-Recovery (30-90 minutes)**
- After morning of deep work
- Real lunch, away from desk
- Walk outside, exercise
- 20-minute nap
- Read something unrelated

**Type 3: Deep Recovery (Evening & Weekend)**
- After day/week of deep work
- Evenings: No work, real downtime
- Weekends: At least one full day off
- Vacations: Actual time away
- Hobbies, connection, play

**Key Insight:** Recovery is not laziness. It's part of the system.

## The 30-Day Focus Challenge

### Week 1: Foundation (Days 1-7)
- **Goal:** One 30-minute deep work session per day
- **Focus:** Building the habit, using your system
- **Track:** Date, duration, task, quality (1-10)

### Week 2: Building (Days 8-14)
- **Goal:** One 60-minute deep work session per day
- **Focus:** Deepening practice, noticing patterns
- **Track:** Add energy level (1-10)

### Week 3: Stretching (Days 15-21)
- **Goal:** Two 60-minute OR one 90-minute session per day
- **Focus:** Expanding capacity, refining system
- **Track:** Note what worked/what didn't

### Week 4: Integration (Days 22-30)
- **Goal:** Find YOUR sustainable rhythm
- **Focus:** Consistency, system refinement
- **Result:** Your proven focus system

## Daily Focus Checklist

### Before Deep Work
- [ ] Phone in another room or on Do Not Disturb
- [ ] Notifications OFF (Slack, email, phone)
- [ ] Workspace cleared
- [ ] Only necessary tabs/tools open
- [ ] Water, tea, or coffee ready
- [ ] One clear task defined
- [ ] Say commitment out loud

### During Deep Work
- [ ] Push through transition (first 10-15 min)
- [ ] Keep notepad for distractions
- [ ] Notice, name, write, return (when distracted)
- [ ] Take 30-second breaks if stuck
- [ ] Stay in deep work for 60-70 min

### After Deep Work
- [ ] Complete current thought
- [ ] Write down where you left off
- [ ] Stretch, move body
- [ ] Take full break (micro-recovery)
- [ ] THEN check phone/messages

## Quick Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Can't get into deep work
**Solution:** Shorten the session. Try 30 minutes instead of 90. Build the habit first.

**Problem:** Constantly getting distracted
**Solution:** Check your environment. Is your phone away? Are notifications off? Remove the distraction source.

**Problem:** Too tired to focus
**Solution:** Work with your energy. Do deep work during your best hours. Respect your recovery.

**Problem:** Feel guilty about not doing enough
**Solution:** Remember: Practice over performance. Some days are harder. That's normal.

**Problem:** System feels too rigid
**Solution:** Adjust. Your system should work for YOUR life, not the other way around.

## Energy Management Template

**Morning (Peak Focus):** 8am-12pm
- Best 3-4 hours for deep work
- Hardest, most important tasks
- Phone away, notifications off

**Midday (Recovery):** 12pm-2pm
- Real break, away from screens
- Lunch, walk, exercise, nap

**Afternoon (Moderate Focus):** 2pm-5pm
- Meetings, calls, collaboration
- Email, Slack, communication
- Planning, admin, shallow work

**Evening (Rest):** After 5pm
- No deep work
- Personal time, family, relaxation
- Prepare for good sleep

## Key Numbers to Remember

- **90 minutes:** Maximum deep work session length
- **10-15 minutes:** Transition time before deep focus kicks in

- **5-15 min:** Micro-recovery between sessions
- **30-90 min:** Mid-recovery after morning deep work

- Focus will start to feel easier

- System will feel more natural

- Deep focus sessions get longer
- Quality of work improves
- System becomes automatic
- Skill compounds

**Month 6+:** Lasting transformation
- Deep work is your default
- System is unconscious
- Focus is a core skill
- You've built something real
