# Focus & Deep Work System - Production Guide

## Series Overview

**Title:** Focus & Deep Work System
**Format:** 4-part audio series with guided practices
**Duration:** 12-15 minutes per episode (48-60 minutes total)
**Target Audience:** Knowledge workers, creators, professionals wanting to build sustainable deep work practice

**Brand Voice:** Supportive + Practical
- Warm, conversational style
- Honest about the struggle (focus is hard)
- Vulnerable personal stories
- Real examples from practice
- Signature lines: "Systems before willpower", "Your environment shapes your behavior"

---

## Episode Structure (Consistent Across All 4 Parts)

### Time Breakdown (12-15 minutes each)

**0:00-2:30 (2-3 min): Welcome + Personal Story + Teaching Hook**
- Connect with listener
- Share personal vulnerability
- Set up episode promise

**2:30-6:00 (3-4 min): Teaching/Framework**
- Core concepts
- Real examples
- Clear explanations

**6:00-10:00 (4-5 min): Guided Practice**
- Step-by-step walkthrough
- Pause markers for exercises
- Real application time

**10:00-12:00 (1-2 min): Encouragement + Next Episode Preview**
- Validate progress
- Build excitement for next episode
- Clear call to action

### Production Elements

**Music:**
- Warm instrumental intro/outro (10-15 seconds)
- Background support during teaching
- Special focus music for practices
- Fade transitions between sections

**Voice Performance:**
- Warm, conversational tone
- Authentic vulnerability during personal stories
- Clear, structured during teaching
- Spacious, gentle during practices
- Proud, encouraging during closings

**Pacing:**
- Deliberate with natural pauses
- Longer pauses during guided practices
- 2-3 second pauses after key concepts
- Space for listener processing

## Recording Guidelines

### Technical Specifications

**Audio Quality:**
- Sample rate: 44.1kHz or 48kHz
- Bit depth: 24-bit
- Format: WAV (master), MP3 192kbps (distribution)

**Microphone:** High-quality condenser mic, 6-8 inches from mouth
**Environment:** Quiet room, minimal reverb
**Processing:** Light compression, de-essing, noise reduction only if needed

### Voice Performance Notes

**General Tone:**
- Speak as if to a friend over coffee
- Authentic, not performative
- Warm but practical
- Vulnerable about struggles

**Section-Specific Notes:**

**Personal Stories:**
- More intimate, quieter delivery
- Genuine emotion, not reading
- Slightly slower pace
- Natural pauses for emotional resonance

**Teaching Sections:**
- Clear, articulate, confident
- Slightly more energy
- Structured delivery
- Emphasize key phrases with tone, not volume

**Guided Practices:**
- Very spacious, lots of silence
- Gentle, supportive instruction
- Clear pause markers ("I'll give you 30 seconds")
- Don't rush the silence

**Closings:**
- Warm, proud, encouraging
- Build to emotional peak
- Signature lines delivered with conviction
- Final fade out is unhurried

## Music Guidelines

### Music Types

**1. Main Theme (Welcome/Intro)**
- Warm instrumental
- Familiar melody across all episodes (continuity)
- 10-15 seconds, fades to background
- Emotional tone: inviting, safe

**2. Teaching Background**
- Steady, focused
- Supports without distracting
- Mid-range volume
- Emotional tone: clarity, learning

**3. Guided Practice**
- Soft, meditative
- Lots of space
- Can have very subtle rhythm
- Emotional tone: spacious, safe

**4. Focus Music (Part 3 Practice)**
- Steady, supportive
- No distracting elements
- Loops seamlessly
- Emotional tone: concentration support

**5. Closing/Transition**
- Building energy
- Uplifting, encouraging
- Emotional tone: forward momentum, completion

### Music Cues in Script

All music cues are marked as:
**[MUSIC: Description - emotional tone]**

Performers should:
- Read the description for emotional context
- Adjust delivery to match the music energy
- Never speak over music that's too prominent
- Let music breathe during pauses

## Edit Guidelines

### Transitions

**Between Sections:**
- 1-2 second crossfade between music beds
- Clean start/stop for voice
- No jarring cuts

**During Guided Practices:**
- Music continues through pause periods
- No fading out and back in
- Consistent volume throughout

**Episode Endings:**
- Final chord gets full duration (5-7 seconds)
- Complete fade to silence
- 2 seconds silence before voiceover (if applicable)

### Pause Markers

**Strict Markers:**
- **[PAUSE: X seconds]** - Must observe exactly
- Practice pauses must be full duration
- Processing pauses are flexible but generous

**Natural Pauses:**
- 2-3 seconds after key concepts
- 5-10 seconds between major sections
- Space for listener digestion

### Volume Levels

**Voice:** -6dB to -3dB peak
**Music (behind voice):** -20dB to -15dB
**Music (alone):** -12dB to -10dB
**Silence:** True digital silence (no floor noise)

## Accessibility

### Transcript Requirements

All episodes must include:
- Full word-for-word transcript
- Music cue descriptions in brackets
- Pause timing noted
- Section headers for navigation

### Additional Accessibility

- Clear speech, not rushed
- Music never competes with voice
- Practice instructions are paced
- Pause periods are clearly marked

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

**Don't:**
- Rush through teaching sections
- Shorten pause markers during editing
- Make personal stories sound performative
- Use music that competes with voice
- End episodes abruptly
- Forget to repeat signature lines
- Skip vulnerability (it builds trust)
- Make it sound easy (it's hard)

**Do:**
- Honor all pause times fully
- Let personal stories be genuinely vulnerable
- Use music to support, not distract
- Build emotional arc through closing
- Validate that focus is difficult
- Emphasize systems over willpower
- Make practices feel safe and spacious
- End with clear call to action
