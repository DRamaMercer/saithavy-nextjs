### [2:00-5:30] TEACHING: Entering Deep Work Consistently

**[MUSIC: Focused, steady - teaching energy]**

**HOST:**
Alright. Let's talk about getting INTO deep work. Because here's the reality: entering deep work is the hardest part.

Once you're IN deep work, it's actually enjoyable. You get into a flow state, time distorts, you're completely absorbed. But the first 10-15 minutes? That's the transition zone. And it's uncomfortable.

**[MUSIC: Brief pause - 2 seconds]**

Here's the framework I use to consistently enter deep work:

**Phase 1: The Setup (2 minutes)**

Before you even start working:
- Clear your workspace completely
- Put your phone in another room
- Close ALL browser tabs except what you need
- Open ONLY the document or tool you'll work on
- Fill your water, get your tea, use the bathroom

**Do this the same way every time.** Remember - rituals train your brain.

**[MUSIC: Slight shift]**

**Phase 2: The Commitment (1 minute)**

This is THE most important step. Say out loud:

"I am entering deep work for 90 minutes on [specific task]. I will not check my phone, switch tabs, or multitask. If I get distracted, I'll return to focus immediately."

**Why say it out loud?** Because it creates commitment. It puts your brain on notice: "We're doing this now."

**[MUSIC: Shift - more serious]**

**Phase 3: The Transition (10-15 minutes)**

This is where most people quit. This is the discomfort zone.

Your brain will resist. You'll feel restless. You'll remember something you "need" to do. You'll feel bored.

**This is normal. This is not a sign to stop.**

Here's what to do during transition:
- Start EASY - don't dive into the hardest part first
- Make it concrete - write down ONE specific thing you'll do first
- Set a timer for 15 minutes - tell yourself "I'll just do 15 minutes"
- When distraction arises, notice it and return to the task

**The transition usually lasts 10-15 minutes.** Then you'll feel it - the resistance drops and you're IN.

**[MUSIC: Shift - more flowing]**

**Phase 4: Deep Work (60-70 minutes)**

Once you're IN, stay IN. You're in the zone now. Keep going.

**Some tips for staying in deep work:**
- Have water nearby (hydration helps focus)
- Keep a notepad for random thoughts ("buy milk", "email so-and-so") - write them down, don't act on them
- If you get stuck, don't switch tasks - take a 30-second break: stand up, stretch, breathe, then return
- Trust the process

**[MUSIC: Lighten slightly]**

**Phase 5: The Exit (5-10 minutes)**

Don't just stop abruptly. That's jarring for your brain.

Complete your current thought. Write down where you left off so you can pick up easily next time. Stretch. Take a full break.

Then - and only then - check your phone, your messages, whatever.

### [5:30-8:00] TEACHING: Managing Distractions

**[MUSIC: More practical, problem-solving energy]**

**HOST:**
Let's talk about distractions. Because they WILL happen.

Here's what I've learned: **Distractions are not failures. They're information.**

**[MUSIC: Brief pause]**

Let me give you my distraction handling system:

**Type 1: External Distractions**
Someone calls, someone interrupts, a notification slips through

**Handle it like this:**
- If it's urgent, pause your deep work session (you can resume later)
- If it's not urgent, say "I'm in the middle of something, can I get back to you in an hour?"
- Then return to your work immediately

**Type 2: Internal Distractions**
You remember something you need to do. You feel restless. You get bored. You want to check your phone.

**Handle it like this:**
- NOTICE it: "Oh, my brain is offering me a distraction"
- NAME it: "That's the urge to check my phone"
- WRITE it down: On your notepad
- RETURN to work: Immediately, without judgment

**[MUSIC: Shift - more reflective]**

Here's what's powerful about this system: **You're not fighting distractions. You're handling them.**

Fighting distractions takes energy. Handling distractions takes almost no energy.

And here's something I've noticed: The more I practice this, the fewer distractions arise. My brain has learned that distractions don't work anymore.

**[MUSIC: Transition to guided practice - soften]**

I'll give you 30 seconds.

**[PAUSE: 30 seconds]**

**[MUSIC: Return]**

Say out loud: "I am entering deep work for 10 minutes on [your task]. I will not check my phone, switch tabs, or multitask."

Say it now.

**[PAUSE: 5 seconds for them to speak]**

**[MUSIC: Focus music begins - steady, supportive background]**

You just did a mini deep work session. And you can do this again tomorrow, for longer, with your own tasks.

**[MUSIC: Warm, encouraging]**

Here's what I want you to notice: You didn't need more willpower. You needed a SYSTEM. Setup, commitment, begin, complete.

That's it. That's the practice.
