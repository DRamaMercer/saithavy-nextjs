## Template Breakdown

### 1:1 Meeting Template

Structure regular check-ins for maximum impact:

- **Connection check-in** - How are you really? Personal and professional pulse
- **Progress review** - Updates on goals, projects, and priorities
- **Blockers and support** - What's standing in the way? How can I help?
- **Feedback and growth** - Two-way feedback and development opportunities
- **Next steps** - Clear actions for both parties before next meeting

**Best for:** Weekly or bi-weekly direct report check-ins, mentorship sessions, performance conversations

### Team Meeting Template

Run purposeful team gatherings:

- **Objective** - Clear purpose and desired outcomes for this meeting
- **Agenda with timeboxes** - Topic, owner, and duration for each item
- **Decisions made** - Record choices and the reasoning behind them
- **Action items** - Task, owner, deadline, and success criteria
- **Parking lot** - Important topics tabled for future discussion
- **Next meeting prep** - Homework and advance reading for next time

**Best for:** Daily standups, weekly team syncs, sprint planning, retrospectives

### Client Meeting Template

Professional client-facing documentation:

- **Meeting details** - Date, attendees (both sides), and meeting type
- **Purpose** - Why we're meeting and what we hope to accomplish
- **Discussion summary** - Key points, client feedback, and questions raised
- **Decisions and next steps** - What was agreed and who's doing what
- **Client concerns** - Objections, risks, or areas needing attention
- **Follow-up items** - Deliverables, communications, and next meeting date

**Best for:** Client calls, presentations, proposal reviews, status updates, relationship-building meetings

### Decision Log Template

Build organizational memory:

- **Decision** - Clear statement of what was decided
- **Context** - Background and problem being solved
- **Options considered** - Alternatives discussed and why they were rejected
- **Rationale** - The reasoning behind this specific choice
- **Impact** - Who/what is affected and how
- **Review date** - When this decision should be revisited
- **Stakeholders** - Who was involved in making the decision

**Best for:** Strategic decisions, technical choices, policy changes, architectural decisions

### Brainstorming Template

Capture creative chaos and turn it into action:

- **Challenge or question** - Clear problem statement or opportunity
- **Ideas generated** - Raw list of all ideas (no judgment yet)
- **Categories** - Group related ideas together
- **Top selections** - Voted or ranked favorites with rationale
- **Action assignments** - Who's exploring/prototyping what
- **Follow-up plan** - How and when we'll review progress

**Best for:** Strategy sessions, feature planning, problem-solving workshops, innovation sprints

### Project Kickoff Template

Start projects with alignment and clarity:

- **Project overview** - Vision, goals, and success criteria
- **Scope and boundaries** - What's in and out of scope
- **Roles and responsibilities** - RACI matrix for team members
- **Timeline and milestones** - Key phases and target dates
- **Risks and dependencies** - Potential blockers and what we need from others
- **Communication plan** - How and when we'll share progress
- **Next steps** - Immediate actions and first milestone

**Best for:** New projects, cross-functional initiatives, client engagements, product launches

## Pro Tips for Better Meeting Notes

### Before the Meeting

- **Share the agenda** 24+ hours in advance
- **Clarify the goal** - What decision or outcome do we need?
- **Assign a note-taker** (rotate this role, it shouldn't always be the same person)
- **Review context** - Past notes, relevant documents, and attendee backgrounds

### During the Meeting

- **Capture verbatim quotes** for key decisions and agreements
- **Ask for clarification** if anything is unclear
- **Confirm action items** with owners and deadlines before moving on
- **Record dissents** - Note who disagrees and why
- **Use direct quotes** for commitments and promises

### After the Meeting

- **Distribute notes within 1 hour** while memory is fresh
- **Create action item tracking** in your project management tool
- **Update decision logs** for long-term reference
- **Follow up on commitments** and track completion rates
- **Review effectiveness** - Was this meeting necessary? What could improve?

## Building a Meeting Culture

Great notes are just one part of effective meetings. Build a culture that respects everyone's time:

- **Default to 15 minutes** - Most meetings can be shorter
- **Document first** - Share notes, then discuss
- **End with actions** - Every meeting ends with clear next steps
- **Review regularly** - Cancel recurring meetings that no longer serve a purpose
- **Measure effectiveness** - Track meeting quality and outcome achievement
