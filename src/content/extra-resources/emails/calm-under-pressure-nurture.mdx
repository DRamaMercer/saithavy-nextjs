## Email 2: Day 2 - The 30-Second Trick

**Subject:** The 30-second trick that changes everything

---

Hey!

Here's a 30-second technique you can use right now:

**Box Breathing.**

It's simple:
- Inhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Exhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Repeat 3 times

Total time: 90 seconds.

**Why it works:**

When you're stressed, your exhale is short. Your body thinks it's in danger.

By extending your exhale (and adding the hold), you signal safety to your brain. Your nervous system shifts from fight-or-flight to rest-and-digest.

**Try it right now.**

I'll wait.

The next time you feel pressure building, come back to this. 90 seconds can change everything.

## Email 3: Day 4 - What Your Body Is Telling You

**Subject:** What your body is trying to tell you

---

Hey!

Your body is always communicating with you.

Are you listening?

Every emotion has a physical signature. Here's what to look for:

**Anger:**
- Tight jaw
- Clenched fists
- Heat rising

**Fear:**
- Racing heart
- Tight chest
- Shallow breathing

**Overwhelm:**
- Heaviness
- Mental fog
- Physical fatigue

**The practice:**

When you notice a strong emotion, pause. Don't react. Just ask:

"What does my body feel right now?"

Name the sensation. Locate it in your body.

**That's regulation.**

You don't have to change the feeling. You just have to notice it.

More tomorrow,

Saithavy

## Email 4: Day 7 - The Mistake Most People Make

**Subject:** The mistake most people make

---

Hey!

Most people try to calm down when they're already upset.

That's like trying to stop a car by pressing on the gas.

**The mistake:** Waiting until you're overwhelmed to practice regulation.

**The fix:** Build your capacity when you're calm.

Think of it like exercise. You don't run a marathon without training. You don't regulate under pressure without practicing when you're okay.

**Daily practice:**
- 5 minutes of box breathing in the morning
- Notice your emotional state throughout the day
- Use small stressors as practice opportunities

**Weekly practice:**
- Put yourself in low-pressure situations on purpose
- Notice how you respond
- Adjust your toolkit

Regulation is a skill. Skills improve with practice.

Don't wait for a crisis to start training.

Your next email will be different. Stay tuned.

Saithavy
