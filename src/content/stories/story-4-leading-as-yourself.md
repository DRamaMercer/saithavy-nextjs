## TRANSCRIPT

### [0:00-0:45] INTRO
*(Warm, reflective piano)*

**Speaker:**
Welcome to the final story in this series.

We've talked about vulnerability. About failure. About hard conversations.

All of it pointing to one question:

Who are you as a leader?

Not who you should be. Not who you're trying to be. Who you actually are.

This is the story of my journey from performing to leading. From trying to be a leader to letting myself lead as myself.

It's the most important journey I've taken.

### [0:45-4:00] THE STORY

Five years into my leadership career, I was exhausted.

I was successful by every external measure. Promotions, recognition, good results.

And I was miserable.

*[PAUSE 3 seconds]*

Here's what nobody saw: I was performing every single day.

I had a leadership persona I'd carefully constructed. Confident. Decisive. Always prepared. Never uncertain.

I didn't swear. I didn't show anger. I didn't admit doubt. I didn't talk about my life outside work.

I was professional. Polished. Perfect.

And completely disconnected from who I actually am.

*[PAUSE 5 seconds]*

The crack in the facade appeared during a 360 feedback process.

One piece of feedback stopped me cold:

"I respect you as a leader. But I don't know you at all. You seem like a character, not a person."

It hurt. But I couldn't deny it.

I WAS performing. I HAD created a character. And I didn't know how to be any other way.

*[PAUSE 5 seconds]*

The real shift came during a team retreat.

We were doing an exercise where we shared our "leadership heroes" - people who inspired us.

My team went first. They named vulnerable leaders. Authentic leaders. People who admitted mistakes. People who showed up real.

Then it was my turn.

I could have performed. I could have named safe, impressive leaders.

Instead, I got honest.

"I realize I don't have leadership heroes in the traditional sense," I said. "The people who actually inspire me are just... themselves. They're not performing. They're not trying to be leaders. They're just leading as themselves."

I paused. Then I said: "And I realize I'm not doing that. I'm performing. And I'm exhausted by it."

*[PAUSE 5 seconds]*

The room was quiet.

Then one person said: "We can tell. We've been waiting for you to show up."

Another said: "We don't need the perfect version of you. We need the real version."

Another said: "Who are you when you're not performing?"

*[PAUSE 5 seconds]*

That question undid me.

I didn't know anymore. I'd been performing for so long.

That moment started a journey. A slow, imperfect journey of stopping the performance and learning to lead as myself.

### [4:00-7:00] WHAT I LEARNED

Here's what I learned on that journey:

**First: You can't lead from a place you're not inhabiting.**

For years, I was trying to lead from a place called "Professional Competent Leader." But I didn't live there. I was visiting.

You can only lead from where you actually are. From who you actually are.

*[PAUSE 5 seconds]*

**Second: Performance disconnects. Presence connects.**

My leadership persona was impressive but distant. It earned respect but not trust.

When I started showing up real - with my uncertainty, my quirks, my humanity - something shifted:

Trust deepened. Innovation increased. People felt safe to be real too.

Performance creates distance. Presence creates connection.

*[PAUSE 5 seconds]*

**Third: Your "leadership style" is just your personality, applied to leadership.**

I spent years trying to be the "right kind of leader." Visionary. Servant. Transformational.

None of them fit. Because they weren't me.

Here's what I've learned: The best leadership approach is the one that fits who you actually are.

Are you quiet? Lead from quiet.
Are you direct? Lead from direct.
Are you intuitive? Lead from intuition.
Are you analytical? Lead from analysis.

Don't try to be a visionary if you're practical. Don't try to be a servant leader if you're naturally directive.

Your personality is not a problem to solve. It's your leadership asset to leverage.

*[PAUSE 5 seconds]*

**Fourth: People follow leaders, not leadership techniques.**

My team didn't need better facilitation. More energetic check-ins. More visionary language.

They needed me. Actually me.

With my dry humor. My tendency to process out loud. My love of data. My occasional awkwardness.

The things I thought were weaknesses? Those were what made me followable.

Because they made me real.

*[PAUSE 5 seconds]*

**Fifth: Leading as yourself requires self-awareness, not self-improvement.**

I spent years trying to improve myself into a better leader.

What I actually needed was to know myself better.

To notice: How do I naturally communicate? What energizes me? What drains me? What are my patterns under stress?

Not to fix. To understand.

Self-awareness, not self-improvement. That's the path to authentic leadership.

### [7:00-10:30] GUIDED REFLECTION

*(Music becomes minimal, warm)*

Now I want to invite you to notice your own performance patterns.

Let's start with a question:

**Where are you performing as a leader?**

*[PAUSE 10 seconds]*

Not where are you growing or learning. Where are you performing?

Where are you trying to be something you're not?

Maybe it's how you communicate. How you show up in meetings. How you handle conflict.
Maybe it's your energy level. Your decision-making style. Your emotional expression.

Just notice.

*[PAUSE 10 seconds]*

Now, get curious about the performance:

**What are you trying to achieve with this performance?**

*[PAUSE 10 seconds]*

What story are you telling yourself about what you need to be?

Maybe: "I need to be confident to be respected."
Maybe: "I need to be directive to be effective."
Maybe: "I need to be energetic to be inspiring."

Notice the story. Notice if it's true.

*[PAUSE 10 seconds]*

Now I want you to ask yourself:

**Who are you when you're not performing?**

*[PAUSE 10 seconds]*

Not the idealized version. The real version.

How do you naturally communicate?
What energizes you?
What are your actual strengths (not the ones you think you should have)?
What are your quirks?

Let yourself see the real you.

*[PAUSE 15 seconds]*

And here's the deeper question:

**What would it look like to lead from THAT version of yourself?**

*[PAUSE 10 seconds]*

Not the improved version. The actual version.

If you lead from your natural strengths, not your aspirational ones?
If you communicate in your natural style, not your practiced one?
If you show up as you, not the leader you think you should be?

*[PAUSE 15 seconds]*

And finally:

**What's the cost of performing?**

*[PAUSE 10 seconds]*

For you? (Exhaustion? Disconnection? Burnout?)
For your team? (Distance? Lack of trust?)
For your leadership? (Inauthenticity? Misalignment?)

Let the cost land.

*[PAUSE 10 seconds]*

And then ask:

**What becomes possible when you lead as yourself?**

*[PAUSE 15 seconds]*

### [10:30-12:00] CLOSING

*(Piano swells gently, then fades)*

We've come to the end of this series.

We've talked about vulnerability. About failure. About hard conversations. About leading as yourself.

All of it pointing to one thing:

Your presence is your primary leadership tool.

Not your techniques. Not your frameworks. Not your performance.

You.

The real you. The messy you. The uncertain you. The learning-you.

That's who people follow. That's who creates trust. That's who leads.

*[PAUSE 3 seconds]*

Lead yourself first.

That's where leadership begins.

Not with leading others. With leading yourself.

With being honest with yourself. With knowing yourself. With accepting yourself.

Then, and only then, can you lead others well.

*[PAUSE 3 seconds]*

My hope for you:

That you stop performing and start leading.
That you stop trying to be impressive and start being real.
That you lead from who you actually are, not who you think you should be.

The world doesn't need more perfect leaders.

It needs real ones.

*[PAUSE 5 seconds]*

Thank you for being here with me.

For doing this work.

For leading as yourself.
