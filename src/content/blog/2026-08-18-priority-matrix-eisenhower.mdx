You have a list of things to do. Everything feels urgent. Everything feels important. You don't know where to start.

Sound familiar?

The problem isn't that you have too much to do. The problem is you don't have a system for deciding what to do first.

**Enter the Eisenhower Matrix.**

## What Is the Eisenhower Matrix?

Named after President Dwight D. Eisenhower, the matrix helps you prioritize based on two factors:

- **Urgent:** Requires immediate attention
- **Important:** Contributes to long-term goals

These form four quadrants:

### Q1: Urgent + Important (DO)
- Crises
- Deadlines
- Emergencies
- Problems needing immediate resolution

### Q2: Not Urgent + Important (DECIDE)
- Planning
- Prevention
- Relationship building
- Personal growth
- Strategy

### Q3: Urgent + Not Important (DELEGATE)
- Some emails
- Some meetings
- Other people's priorities
- Interruptions

### Q4: Not Urgent + Not Important (DELETE)
- Time wasters
- Busy work
- Distractions
- Social media (excessive)

## The Problem with Q1

Most people live in Q1.

They firefight constantly. Every day is a crisis. They're always reacting.

The problem with Q1:
- High stress
- Always on edge
- No time for strategy
- Eventually leads to burnout

## The Power of Q2

Q2 is where high-performers live.

Q2 activities:
- Prevent Q1 crises
- Build capabilities
- Create long-term success
- Improve relationships
- Personal development

**The goal: Spend more time in Q2.**

## Using the Matrix

### Step 1: List Everything
Write down every task, project, commitment.

### Step 2: Rate Each
For each item, ask:
- Is this urgent? (Needs immediate attention)
- Is this important? (Contributes to goals)

### Step 3: Place in Quadrants
Sort tasks into Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4.

### Step 4: Decide
- Q1: Do now
- Q2: Schedule
- Q3: Delegate or batch
- Q4: Delete

## The Regulated Decision Framework

When you're stressed, the matrix gets harder to use. Here's how to stay regulated:

### Before Prioritizing:
1. Take three deep breaths
2. Check your nervous system state
3. Ask: "What's actually urgent?"

### During Prioritizing:
1. One task at a time
2. Don't judge, just sort
3. Keep it simple

### After Prioritizing:
1. Pick ONE thing to start
2. Notice resistance
3. Start anyway

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Everything Feels Urgent
When overwhelmed, everything feels urgent.
**Fix:** Sleep, then revisit.

### Mistake 2: Q3 Looks Like Q1
Other people's urgency feels like your urgency.
**Fix:** Ask: "Is this my priority?"

### Mistake 3: Q4 Looks Like Q2
Distractions feel productive.
**Fix:** Ask: "Does this contribute to my goals?"

### Mistake 4: No Q2
"If it's not urgent, it doesn't get done."
**Fix:** Schedule Q2 like Q1.

## Key Takeaways

- Prioritize by urgent vs important
- Spend more time in Q2
- Q2 prevents Q1 crises
- Stay regulated when prioritizing
- Schedule Q2 like Q1
