If you have ADHD, you've probably tried every productivity system out there. You've read the books, downloaded the apps, bought the planners. And still, nothing sticks.

Here's why: most productivity systems are designed for neurotypical brains. They're built on assumptions that don't apply to yours.

**Your brain works differently. Your system should too.**

## Understanding ADHD Productivity

ADHD brains have different needs:

### What Doesn't Work
- Complex systems with many steps
- Relying on memory
- Rigid schedules
- Multi-tasking
- Long-term motivation without immediate feedback

### What Works
- External systems (your brain is external hard drive)
- Immediate rewards
- Novelty and interest
- Flexibility
- Body-based reminders

## The Externalize Everything Framework

The key to ADHD productivity: **externalize your executive function.**

Your brain isn't designed to hold your to-do list, remember your schedule, and track your goals. That's too much for working memory.

**Externalize everything:**

### 1. Write Everything Down
- Don't rely on memory
- Have one place for everything
- Check the list regularly

### 2. Use Physical Reminders
- Post-it notes
- Whiteboard
- Phone alarms
- Bullet journals

### 3. Create Visual Systems
- Color coding
- Pictures instead of words
- Visible task boards

### 4. Build in Accountability
- Buddy systems
- Public commitments
- Coach or mentor

## The Interest-Driven System

Here's a secret: ADHD brains are interest-driven, not importance-driven.

**Use that:**

### The Interest Hack
- Make tasks interesting
- Add challenges
- Create urgency
- Find the novelty

### TaskBatching by Interest
- Batch similar tasks
- Do when interest is highest
- Don't force when brain isn't there

## Simple Beats Perfect

The best ADHD productivity system is the one you'll actually use.

**Start simple:**
- One list
- One app
- One habit

**Add complexity only when needed:**
- Build on success
- Add only what's missing
- Don't optimize too early

## The ADHD Productivity Toolkit

### Daily Essentials:
1. One master task list
2. Time block your calendar
3. Set phone alarms for transitions
4. Body double (accountability partner)

### Weekly Rituals:
1. Weekly review (Sunday, 30 min)
2. Meal prep
3. System maintenance
4. Plan next week

### When Things Fall Apart:
1. Start fresh
2. One task at a time
3. Lower the bar
4. Try again tomorrow

---

## Key Takeaways

- Externalize executive function
- Simple beats perfect
- Interest-driven, not importance-driven
- Accountability is essential
- Start small, build gradually
