# Use Case Selection Matrix

## Identify High-Impact AI Opportunities by Department

This matrix helps you prioritize AI implementation based on **impact potential**, **implementation complexity**, and **resource requirements**. Use this to select your initial use cases and plan your implementation roadmap.

## How to Use This Matrix

1. **Review each department's opportunities**
2. **Score based on your organization's priorities**
3. **Prioritize High Impact/Low Complexity items for quick wins**
4. **Use the Priority Score Formula**: `(Business Impact × 3) - (Complexity × 2) + (Resource Availability)`

## Selection Framework

### Step 1: Identify Your Top 5-7 Use Cases

Use the criteria below to select your initial implementations:

#### Selection Criteria:
- **Business Impact**: Does this solve a significant pain point?
- **Feasibility**: Can we implement this in 30 days or less?
- **Resources**: Do we have the necessary team and budget?
- **Data Available**: Is the required data accessible?
- **Scalability**: Can this expand to other areas later?

#### Red Flags to Avoid:
- Requires massive data cleanup
- Needs specialized technical skills you don't have
- Involves sensitive customer data without compliance readiness
- Depends on integration with complex legacy systems
- Has unclear success metrics

### Step 2: Categorize Your Selection

**Quick Wins** (0-2 weeks, high impact):
- Implement immediately
- Build momentum and confidence
- Demonstrate value to stakeholders

**Strategic Projects** (3-6 weeks, high impact):
- Plan after initial quick wins
- Require cross-functional coordination
- Deliver substantial business value

**Long-term Initiatives** (7-12 weeks, very high impact):
- Part of Phase 2-3 roadmap
- Require significant investment
- Transform business operations

### Step 3: Create Your Implementation Plan

For each selected use case, document:

1. **Business Objective**: What problem are we solving?
2. **Success Metrics**: How will we measure success?
3. **Owner**: Who is responsible for implementation?
4. **Resources Needed**: Budget, tools, team members
5. **Timeline**: Key milestones and deadlines
6. **Risks**: Potential obstacles and mitigation strategies
7. **Dependencies**: What must happen first?
