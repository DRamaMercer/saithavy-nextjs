# Remote Work Troubleshooting Guide

## Executive Summary

Remote work has predictable problems. After 5+ years of working remotely and helping dozens of teams transition, I've seen the same issues repeatedly.

**This guide covers:**
- The most common remote work problems
- Why they happen
- Practical solutions that work
- How to prevent them in the future

**Use this as a reference guide.** When you encounter a problem, look it up, apply the solution, and move on.

---

## 🚨 Common Problems by Category

### Category 1: Focus & Productivity

### Problem: Constant Distractions at Home

**Symptoms:**
- Can't focus for more than 20 minutes
- Constantly getting interrupted by family/housemates
- Household chores calling your name
- End up working odd hours to find quiet time

**Root Causes:**
1. No dedicated workspace
2. No clear "work mode" signal to others
3. No boundaries with family/housemates
4. Trying to work in high-traffic areas

**Solutions (in order of effectiveness):**

**1. Create a dedicated workspace**
- Even if it's just a corner
- Make it work-only space
- Visual signal: "I'm here = I'm working"

**2. Set clear boundaries with family/housemates**
- Use the family communication template from the Boundary Tools guide
- Be explicit: "When I'm wearing headphones, I'm working"
- Create a signal: Closed door = working, open door = available

**3. Use the "fake commute"**
- Leave the house before starting work
- Walk around the block for 10 minutes
- Come back and go straight to workspace
- Signals to brain: "I'm at work now"

**4. Wear headphones**
- Even if you're not listening to anything
- Visual signal to others: "Don't interrupt"
- Use focus music or white noise if helpful

**5. Work in a different room**
- If possible, work in bedroom or office
- Not the living room or kitchen
- Less traffic, fewer distractions

**Prevention:**
- Establish a dedicated workspace from day 1
- Communicate boundaries early and often
- Be consistent with your work schedule

### Problem: Can't Stay Focused

**Symptoms:**
- Mind wanders constantly
- End up scrolling social media
- Work takes 2x longer than it should
- Feel guilty about lack of productivity

**Root Causes:**
1. No structured work day
2. Working in the wrong environment
3. Burnout (need rest, not more discipline)
4. Lack of clear priorities

**Solutions:**

**1. Time blocking**
- Structure your day with blocks
- Focus blocks: 2-4 hours, no interruptions
- Meeting blocks: Group all meetings together
- Admin blocks: Email, Slack, small tasks

**2. Pomodoro technique**
- Work for 50 minutes, break for 10
- Or work for 25, break for 5
- Use a timer (there are many free apps)
- Builds focus muscle over time

**3. Eliminate digital distractions**
- Block social media during work hours
- Use website blockers (Freedom, Cold Turkey)
- Put phone in different room
- Close unnecessary browser tabs

**4. Single-tasking**
- Do one thing at a time
- Multitasking reduces productivity by 40%
- Use the 2-minute rule: If it takes <2 minutes, do it now. Otherwise, schedule it.

**5. Clear priorities**
- Write down top 3 priorities for the day
- Do them first thing
- Everything else is secondary

**Prevention:**
- Structure your work day in advance
- Protect your focus time on your calendar
- Take real breaks (not just scrolling)

### Problem: Procrastination is Worse at Home

**Symptoms:**
- Put off work more than when in office
- Hard to get started in the morning
- Easy tasks take forever
- Important tasks never happen

**Root Causes:**
1. No external accountability (no one sees you working)
2. No clear start to the day
3. Task feels overwhelming
4. Perfectionism

**Solutions:**

**1. Create a start-work ritual**
- Same routine every morning
- Coffee, shower, get dressed
- Fake commute (walk around block)
- Signals brain: "Time to work"

**2. Use accountability**
- Work with a buddy (Focusmate is great for this)
- Commit to someone else: "I'll do X by Y time"
- Share your daily priorities in Slack
- Tell someone when you'll start working

**3. Break tasks into smaller steps**
- Too big = overwhelming = procrastination
- Break into 15-30 minute tasks
- Example: "Write report" → "Outline report (15 min), "Write intro (30 min)"

**4. 5-minute rule**
- Commit to doing something for just 5 minutes
- Usually you'll keep going
- Hardest part is starting

**5. Reduce friction**
- Have everything ready before you start
- Open necessary tabs/docs
- Clear your desk
- Make it easy to start

**Prevention:**
- Plan your tomorrow at the end of today
- Start every day with your #1 priority
- Use accountability systems

## 🏠 Category 2: Workspace & Ergonomics

### Problem: Back/Neck Pain from Home Office

**Symptoms:**
- Back pain after working for a few hours
- Neck stiffness
- Wrist pain
- Headaches

**Root Causes:**
1. Poor chair
2. Monitor too low (looking down constantly)
3. Laptop-only setup
4. Sitting too long without movement

**Solutions:**

**1. Fix your chair**
- Lumbar support (lower back)
- Adjustable height (feet flat, knees at 90°)
- If can't afford new chair: Add lumbar pillow

**2. Raise your monitor**
- Top of monitor at or slightly below eye level
- Use books, monitor stand, or laptop stand
- Looking down constantly = neck pain

**3. Get external keyboard and mouse**
- If using laptop: This is essential
- Prevents shoulder strain
- Allows proper monitor height

**4. Take breaks every 30-60 minutes**
- Set a timer
- Stand, stretch, walk around
- Don't work for 3+ hours without moving

**5. Consider standing desk**
- Even a standing desk converter ($150-300)
- Alternate sitting and standing
- Reduces back pain significantly

**Prevention:**
- Invest in ergonomics from day 1
- Your body will thank you
- See the Home Office Setup Guide for complete recommendations

### Problem: Poor Lighting / Eye Strain

**Symptoms:**
- Headaches after working
- Eye strain, blurry vision
- Difficulty focusing on screen
- Looking washed out or shadowy on video calls

**Root Causes:**
1. Working in a dark room
2. Monitor brightness doesn't match room lighting
3. Glare on monitor
4. No natural light

**Solutions:**

**1. Get natural light**
- Position desk near window (but not directly facing)
- Natural light is best for focus and eye comfort

**2. Use daylight bulbs**
- 5000K color temperature (daylight)
- Mimics natural light
- Improves focus

**3. Adjust monitor brightness**
- Should match room brightness
- Too bright = eye strain
- Too dim = eye strain

**4. Reduce glare**
- Don't place monitor directly facing window
- Use curtains to diffuse direct sunlight
- Matte screen reduces glare

**5. Follow 20-20-20 rule**
- Every 20 minutes, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds
- Reduces eye strain
- There are apps that remind you

**Prevention:**
- Set up your workspace with good lighting from day 1
- Adjust monitor brightness to match room
- Take breaks to look away from screen

### Problem: Bad Internet Connection

**Symptoms:**
- Video calls freeze or drop
- Can't share screen
- Slow loading
- Constant frustration

**Root Causes:**
1. Slow internet plan
2. Too many devices using bandwidth
3. WiFi signal issues
4. Router problems

**Solutions:**

**1. Test your internet speed**
- Go to speedtest.net
- Need at least 25 Mbps download, 5 Mbps upload
- If slower: Upgrade plan (usually $20-40/month more)

**2. Use ethernet cable**
- Wired connection is faster and more reliable
- $10-20 for a cable
- Huge improvement

**3. Move router closer to workspace**
- Or use WiFi extender
- Or mesh WiFi system (Eero, Nest Wifi)
- Improves signal strength

**4. Limit other devices during calls**
- Don't stream video/game during important calls
- Ask family/housemates to limit bandwidth during your meetings

**5. Have a backup**
- Mobile hotspot (phone)
- Coffee shop or library
- Coworking space (for consistent needs)

**Prevention:**
- Test internet speed before starting remote work
- Invest in good internet (it's essential)
- Have backup options

## 💬 Category 3: Communication & Collaboration

### Problem: Feeling Disconnected from Team

**Symptoms:**
- Loneliness
- FOMO (fear of missing out)
- Not sure what team is working on
- Feeling like an outsider

**Root Causes:**
1. Missing hallway conversations and informal interactions
2. No face-to-face time
3. All communication is transactional
4. Working in different time zones

**Solutions:**

**1. Keep some meetings**
- Not everything should be async
- Keep 1:1s, team social, complex collaboration
- Some sync is necessary for relationships

**2. Create async social opportunities**
- Slack channel for pets, hobbies, weekend wins
- Share photos of your workspace, view, weekend
- Virtual watercooler conversations

**3. Over-communicate**
- Share what you're working on
- Ask questions publicly (not just DMs)
- Comment on others' updates

**4. Schedule informal video calls**
- Virtual coffee breaks
- Optional happy hours
- Game sessions (Jackbox, etc.)

**5. Consider in-person meetups**
- Quarterly or annual team retreats
- Even once a year makes a huge difference
- If budget allows

**Prevention:**
- Be intentional about relationship-building
- Balance async efficiency with sync connection
- Create opportunities for informal interaction

### Problem: Too Many Meetings

**Symptoms:**
- Calendar full of meetings
- No time for actual work
- Work happens in evenings
- Meeting fatigue

**Root Causes:**
1. Compensating for lack of in-person interaction
2. Poor async communication
3. Meeting culture
4. Lack of trust

**Solutions:**

**1. Audit your meetings**
- Track for one week: What meetings, how long, how many people
- Calculate: Which could have been async?
- Target: Replace 30-50% with async alternatives

**2. Use async templates**
- Daily stand-in instead of standup
- Status updates instead of status meetings
- Written decisions instead of decision meetings
- See the Async Communication Playbook

**3. Decline unnecessary meetings**
- Before accepting: "Could I get the notes async?"
- "Is my presence required, or optional?"
- Protect your focus time

**4. Shorten meetings**
- Default to 30 minutes, not 60
- Start on time, end on time
- Send agenda and pre-work in advance

**5. Meeting-free days**
- One day per week with no meetings
- Entire day for deep work
- Amazing for productivity

**Prevention:**
- Default to async
- Meet only when necessary
- Audit and prune recurring meetings regularly

### Problem: Important Info Gets Lost in Slack

**Symptoms:**
- "Did anyone see my message?"
- Can't find information later
- Decisions get lost
- Constant re-hashing

**Root Causes:**
1. Everything is in Slack
2. No documentation
3. Poor organization
4. Information overload

**Solutions:**

**1. Document important information**
- Decisions: Decision log
- Processes: Team wiki/Notion
- Resources: Shared drive
- Slack is transient, docs are permanent

**2. Create team knowledge base**
- One source of truth
- Organized by topic
- Searchable
- Notion, Confluence, Google Sites, etc.

**3. Use channels effectively**
- Different channels for different topics
- Pinned messages for important info
- Threaded responses (not everything in main channel)

**4. Summarize meetings**
- Send summary after every meeting
- Include: decisions, action items, owners
- Post in relevant channel

**5. Weekly digest**
- Round up important updates
- Share in dedicated channel
- Easy to scan and find

**Prevention:**
- Document as you go
- Create systems from day 1
- Make documentation part of process

### Problem: Different Time Zones Make Collaboration Hard

**Symptoms:**
- Waiting 24+ hours for simple responses
- Meetings at terrible times
- Feeling constantly out of sync
- Resentment builds

**Root Causes:**
1. No respect for time zones
2. Poor async communication
3. Trying to replicate office schedules remotely
4. No handoff processes

**Solutions:**

**1. Respect time zones**
- No meetings outside reasonable work hours
- Include time zones in all invitations
- See the Time Zone Mastery Framework

**2. Daily handoffs**
- End of day update to next time zone
- Use the handoff template
- Link everything
- Be explicit about what's needed

**3. Go fully async**
- If no overlap: Don't meet
- Document everything
- Slack/Email for communication
- Occasional meetings for special occasions only

**4. Find and use overlap**
- If you have 2-4 hours of overlap: Use it for meetings
- Rest of time: Async
- See everytimezone.com for overlap calculator

**5. Rotate meeting times**
- If some people must meet at bad times: Rotate
- Don't make one time zone always sacrifice
- Or record meetings for those who can't attend

**Prevention:**
- Map your team's time zones
- Establish handoff routines
- Respect work hours

## 🧠 Category 4: Mental Health & Boundaries

### Problem: Can't Disconnect from Work

**Symptoms:**
- Checking Slack at 10pm
- Thinking about work during dinner
- Working weekends
- Feeling "always on"

**Root Causes:**
1. No physical separation (work is everywhere)
2. No temporal boundaries (no hard stop)
3. Always-on culture
4. Self-worth tied to work

**Solutions:**

**1. Hard stop time**
- Choose a specific end time (e.g., 6pm)
- Stop working at that time, no exceptions
- Use the Work Shutdown Ritual

**2. Remove work apps from phone**
- Or at least turn off notifications
- Most effective solution
- Breaks the habit loop

**3. Create physical separation**
- Put work away at end of day
- Clear desk, close laptop
- Transform space if multi-purpose

**4. Decompression zone**
- 30-60 minutes between work and life
- Walk, workout, meditate, something tactile
- See the Work-Life Boundary Tools guide

**5. Set boundaries with team**
- Share your work hours explicitly
- No Slack/email after 6pm
- See the boundary communication templates

**Prevention:**
- Establish boundaries from day 1
- Be consistent
- Help others do the same

### Problem: Loneliness and Isolation

**Symptoms:**
- Feeling disconnected from others
- Depression or anxiety
- Lack of motivation
- Missing office social interaction

**Root Causes:**
1. No daily in-person interaction
2. Living alone
3. All communication is work-related
4. No social rituals

**Solutions:**

**1. Maintain non-work relationships**
- Friends, family, community
- Intentional about seeing people
- Don't let work fill the void

**2. Remote work communities**
- Online communities for remote workers
- Slack/Discord groups
- Local remote worker meetups

**3. Coworking spaces**
- Even 1-2 days per week
- Provides social interaction
- Helps with boundaries too

**4. Hobbies and interests outside work**
- Something you do for fun
- Classes, groups, events
- Reminds you there's more to life than work

**5. Consider company**
- Get a pet (if you're ready for the responsibility)
- Or have housemates/family nearby
- Or work from coffee shop sometimes

**Prevention:**
- Be intentional about social connection
- Build relationships outside work
- Recognize when you're isolating and take action

### Problem: Impostor Syndrome is Worse Remotely

**Symptoms:**
- Feeling like you're not good enough
- Comparing yourself to others
- Fear of being "found out"
- Anxiety about performance

**Root Causes:**
1. Less feedback (can't see others working)
2. No visibility into others' struggles
3. Performance = visible output
4. Isolation magnifies doubts

**Solutions:**

**1. Recognize it's common**
- Most remote workers feel this
- You're not alone
- It's not about you, it's about the situation

**2. Seek feedback proactively**
- Ask for regular feedback from manager
- Ask colleagues: "How did I do on X?"
- Don't wait for annual review

**3. Share your struggles**
- Be open about challenges
- You'll find others relate
- Vulnerability builds connection

**4. Track your wins**
- Write down what you accomplish each week
- Review it when you're doubting yourself
- Evidence that you're doing well

**5. Remember: Social media is highlight reel**
- Others share their wins, not their struggles
- You're comparing your insides to others' outsides
- Not fair comparison

**Prevention:**
- Normalize talking about challenges
- Seek regular feedback
- Build self-awareness and self-worth outside work

### Problem: Burnout

**Symptoms:**
- Chronic exhaustion
- Cynicism about work
- Reduced performance
- Physical symptoms (headaches, sleep issues, etc.)

**Root Causes:**
1. No boundaries
2. Always-on culture
3. Lack of rest
4. Perfectionism

**Solutions:**

**1. Take time off**
- Actual time off, no checking in
- At least a week, preferably two
- You need extended rest

**2. Establish and defend boundaries**
- Work hours
- No work on weekends
- Disconnect fully in evenings
- See the Work-Life Boundary Tools guide

**3. Reduce workload**
- Talk to manager about expectations
- Prioritize: What's essential, what can drop?
- Say no to new commitments

**4. Practice self-care**
- Sleep: 7-9 hours/night
- Exercise: 3-4x/week
- Eat well
- Mental health: Therapy, meditation, etc.

**5. Consider whether this job is right for you**
- If job culture is toxic, no amount of self-care will fix it
- Some companies expect burnout as the norm
- Leave if necessary

**Prevention:**
- Boundaries from day 1
- Take regular breaks and time off
- Pay attention to early warning signs

## ⏰ Category 5: Time Management

### Problem: Days Blend Together

**Symptoms:**
- Don't know what day it is
- No structure to your day
- Work and personal time blur together
- Lose track of time

**Root Causes:**
1. No commute to mark day boundaries
2. No rituals to start/end day
3. Same environment for work and life
4. Lack of routine

**Solutions:**

**1. Create a daily routine**
- Wake up at same time every day
- Same morning routine
- Fake commute before work
- Work shutdown ritual at end of day

**2. Wear different clothes**
- Work clothes during day
- Home clothes after work
- Simple but effective signal

**3. Have different weekend routine**
- Don't work on weekends
- Different activities, different pace
- Creates contrast

**4. Plan your tomorrow today**
- Write down tomorrow's priorities
- Makes tomorrow distinct from today
- Helps you remember what day it is

**5. Mark time visually**
- Calendar on wall
- Clock visible in workspace
- Date visible somewhere

**Prevention:**
- Establish routines from day 1
- Create rituals for start/end of day
- Maintain weekend/work week separation

### Problem: Overworking

**Symptoms:**
- Working 10-12 hour days
- Working weekends
- Feeling like you never do enough
- Guilt when not working

**Root Causes:**
1. No clear end to work day
2. Always more to do
3. Self-worth tied to productivity
4. No external stop signal (office closing, etc.)

**Solutions:**

**1. Hard stop time**
- Choose specific end time (e.g., 6pm)
- Stop at that time, no exceptions
- Use shutdown ritual for closure

**2. Track your hours**
- For one week, track every hour
- You're probably working plenty (40-50 hours)
- Data helps you feel okay about stopping

**3. Define "done"**
- At start of day: "I'll be done when I achieve X"
- Not "I'll be done when I feel like I've worked enough"
- Concrete goal = clear stopping point

**4. Focus on output, not hours**
- Did you achieve your goals for the day?
- Yes? Then you're done.
- Working 12 hours ≠ 12 hours of productivity

**5. Remember: Rest is productive**
- Well-rested you does better work than tired you
- Working more hours ≠ working better
- Diminishing returns after 8 hours/day

**Prevention:**
- Set boundaries from day 1
- Track hours regularly
- Focus on priorities, not everything

### Problem: Underworking (Procrastination & Distraction)

**Symptoms:**
- Intend to work but don't
- Constantly distracted
- Work takes way longer than it should
- Feel guilty about lack of productivity

**Root Causes:**
1. No accountability
2. No structure to day
3. Task feels overwhelming
4. Burnout (need rest, not more discipline)

**Solutions:**

**1. Create structure**
- Time block your day
- Focus blocks with no interruptions
- Accountability buddy (Focusmate is great)

**2. Use accountability**
- Tell someone what you'll do and when
- Share daily priorities in Slack
- Check in at end of day

**3. Break tasks into smaller pieces**
- Too big = overwhelming = procrastination
- Break into 15-30 minute chunks
- Start with something easy

**4. Eliminate distractions**
- Block social media during work hours
- Put phone in different room
- Close unnecessary browser tabs

**5. Check for burnout**
- If you're exhausted, you need rest, not more discipline
- Take time off
- Reassess workload

**Prevention:**
- Structure your day
- Use accountability systems
- Take breaks and time off regularly

## 📋 When to Seek Help

### Red Flags: When to Talk to Your Manager

**Schedule a meeting if:**
- You're consistently working 50-60+ hours/week
- You're receiving messages at odd hours regularly
- You're burned out and need a break
- You're not sure what's expected of you
- You need support or resources

**How to have the conversation:**
1. Be specific: "I'm working 60 hours/week and it's not sustainable"
2. Propose solutions: "Here are my proposed work hours and boundaries"
3. Ask for support: "I need [resource/support] to do my job well"

**Good managers will work with you.**
**Bad managers won't.** That tells you something important.

---

### Red Flags: When to Consider Leaving Your Job

**Consider leaving if:**
- Company expects 24/7 availability and won't change
- You're burned out and they won't support you
- Culture is toxic (blaming, shaming, overworking)
- Your boundaries are consistently disrespected
- Your health is suffering

**Before leaving:**
1. Have direct conversation with manager first
2. Give them chance to fix it
3. If they won't or can't: Leave

**Jobs are plentiful. Good mental health is not.**

---

### Red Flags: When to Seek Professional Help

**Consider therapy if:**
- You're experiencing depression or anxiety
- You're unable to function
- You're having thoughts of self-harm
- You're using substances to cope
- You're isolated and lonely

**Resources:**
- BetterHelp (online therapy)
- Psychology Today therapist finder
- Crisis hotline (if in crisis): 988 in US

**There's no shame in seeking help.**
**Remote work can amplify existing mental health challenges.**
**Professional support makes a huge difference.**
