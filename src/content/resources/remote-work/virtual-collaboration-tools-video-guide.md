## Module 2: Real-Time Communication Tools

**Video Length:** 15 minutes
**Format:** Screen Recording + Commentary

### Script Outline

**[INTRO - 2 minutes]**

"Real-time communication tools are your virtual office. They're where the quick conversations, questions, and social interactions happen. But used poorly, they become constant distraction machines.

"Today, we're covering Slack, Microsoft Teams, and Discord—how to configure them for focus, not interruption."

**[DEMO: CHANNEL STRUCTURE - 5 minutes]**

**Screen recording: Setting up Slack/Teams workspace**

"The foundation of a good real-time tool is channel structure. Let me show you a healthy setup:

**#announcements**
One-way communication from leadership. Only admins can post. No reactions needed—this is for information only.

**#general**
Company-wide conversations. Everyone's a member. This is your virtual town square.

**#team-[name]**
Each team has their own channel. This is where day-to-day work conversations happen.

**#project-[name]**
Cross-functional project channels. Include only people working on that project.

**#random**
Social connection. Non-work conversations allowed and encouraged.

**#dist-updates**
Daily standups in written form. No replies needed unless someone's blocked."

**[DEMO: THREADING PROTOCOLS - 3 minutes]**

**Screen recording: Demonstrating threaded conversations**

"The most important feature most teams don't use: Threads. Let me show you why this matters.

"When you respond in the main channel, you're interrupting everyone. When you respond in a thread, you're having a focused conversation.

"Here's the protocol: React with an emoji if you've read and have nothing to add. Start a thread if you have a response. Post in the main channel only if it's relevant to everyone."

**[DEMO: NOTIFICATION SETTINGS - 3 minutes]**

**Screen recording: Configuring notifications**

"Now for the most important part: Notification settings. Let me show you how to configure Slack so it works for you, not against you.

**On Mobile:**
- Notifications off for all channels
- Mentions and DMs only
- Do Not Disturb during focus hours

**On Desktop:**
- All notifications off during deep work blocks
- Channel mentions only for critical channels
- Desktop notifications for mentions and DMs

**Key insight:** Your team needs to know how to reach you urgently. But not everything is urgent."

**[CLOSING - 2 minutes]**

"Your homework: Audit your notification settings. How many times per day are you interrupted? Configure your tools to protect your focus.

"Next module: Async communication—how to collaborate without needing everyone online at the same time."

## Module 3: Async Communication Platforms

**Video Length:** 14 minutes
**Format:** Screen Recording

### Script Outline

**[INTRO - 2 minutes]**

"Async communication is the superpower of remote teams. It means collaborating across time zones without requiring everyone to be online simultaneously. When done right, it's more inclusive, more thoughtful, and more efficient than real-time meetings.

"Today, we're covering written async platforms, recorded video tools, and async-first workflows."

**[DEMO: WRITTEN ASYNC TOOLS - 4 minutes]**

**Screen recording: Notion, Google Docs, Confluence**

"Your written async platform is where decisions get made, where work gets documented, and where your team's collective knowledge lives.

Let me show you a healthy structure:

**Team Home**
Quick links to everything a team member needs

**Decisions Log**
Every decision, documented with context

**Meeting Notes**
All meeting notes, tagged and searchable

**Processes & Playbooks**
How we do things here

**Projects**
Project documentation and status

The key principle: If it's not written down, it didn't happen."

**[DEMO: RECORDED VIDEO TOOLS - 4 minutes]**

**Screen recording: Loom, Vidcast, Volley**

"Sometimes writing isn't the right medium. That's where recorded video comes in. Let me show you how to use Loom effectively:

**Update videos:**
Instead of typing out a status update, record a 2-minute screen share. Show, don't just tell.

**Tutorials:**
When someone asks how to do something, record yourself doing it. Then share the link with anyone who has the same question.

**Feedback:**
Need feedback on something? Record a walkthrough, ask specific questions, get async responses.

The magic happens when you build a library of these videos. Suddenly, knowledge isn't trapped in someone's head—it's shareable."

**[DEMO: ASYNC WORKFLOWS - 3 minutes]**

**Screen recording: Demonstrating an async process**

"Let me show you a complete async workflow:

1. Someone posts a proposal in a shared doc
2. Team members add comments asynchronously over 24-48 hours
3. Author addresses concerns in the doc
4. Decision is made and logged
5. Result is communicated to all stakeholders

This entire process happened without a single meeting. People in 12 time zones all contributed. The whole thing is documented.

This is the power of async."

**[CLOSING - 1 minute]**

"Your challenge: Take one meeting from your calendar this week and convert it to async. Document the process, share the proposal, collect feedback.

"See how it feels. You might be surprised."

## Module 5: Project Management Systems

**Video Length:** 15 minutes
**Format:** Screen Recording

### Script Outline

**[INTRO - 2 minutes]**

"Project management tools are your team's central nervous system. They show who's doing what, by when, and what's blocking progress.

"Today, I'm showing you how to set up Linear, Asana, and Trello for remote team success."

**[DEMO: PROJECT STRUCTURE - 5 minutes]**

**Screen recording: Setting up a project in Linear/Asana**

"A healthy project management system has three levels: **Projects**, **Milestones**, and **Tasks**.

Let me show you this hierarchy:

**Project:** The big initiative with clear outcomes
**Milestones:** Checkpoints on the way to completion
**Tasks:** Individual work items

Each task should have:
- Clear owner (one person, not multiple)
- Definition of done
- Due date
- Dependencies (what needs to happen first)
- Priority level

Let me create a project from scratch to show you..."

**[DEMO: WORKFLOWS & AUTOMATION - 4 minutes]**

**Screen recording: Setting up automation rules**

"The magic happens when you automate routine processes. Let me show you some useful automations:

- When a task is created → assign it based on project
- When a task is completed → notify dependent tasks
- When a task is overdue → escalate to project owner
- When a task is tagged 'urgent' → notify team lead

These automations reduce mental load. Things happen without someone remembering to do them."

**[DEMO: VIEWS & DASHBOARDS - 3 minutes]**

**Screen recording: Creating different views**

"Different people need different views of the same work:

- **Kanban board:** For visual workflow management
- **List view:** For comprehensive task lists
- **Calendar view:** For time-based planning
- **Workload view:** For capacity planning

Let me show you how to set up each view and when to use which..."

**[CLOSING - 1 minute]**

"Your project management tool should answer three questions instantly:
1. What are we working on?
2. Who's working on what?
3. What's at risk?

If it can't answer these, you're using it wrong."

## Module 6: Video Meeting Platforms

**Video Length:** 14 minutes
**Format:** Screen Recording

### Script Outline

**[INTRO - 2 minutes]**

"Video meetings are necessary, but they should be a last resort, not a default. In this module, I'm showing you how to make the most of Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams—while using them less."

**[DEMO: MEETING SETUP BEST PRACTICES - 5 minutes]**

**Screen recording: Configuring Zoom/Meet settings**

"Every video meeting should be set up for success. Let me show you the optimal settings:

**Before the meeting:**
- Enable waiting room
- Require password for security
- Enable recording (transcript if available)
- Set up breakout rooms if needed

**During the meeting:**
- Use gallery view to see everyone
- Enable captions for accessibility
- Use raised hand for orderly discussion
- Chat for parallel processing

**After the meeting:**
- Recording automatically shared
- Transcript created and searchable
- Action items documented

Let me walk through each setting..."

**[DEMO: FACILITATION TOOLS - 4 minutes]**

**Screen recording: Using whiteboards, polls, breakout rooms**

"Video meetings have tools that in-person meetings don't. Let me show you how to use them:

**Breakout rooms:**
Small group discussions during larger meetings. Let me show you how to set them up and randomly or manually assign participants.

**Polls:**
Quick pulse of the room. Multiple choice questions that give instant feedback. Great for decision-making meetings.

**Whiteboards:**
Collaborative visual thinking. Everyone can contribute simultaneously. Let me show you Miro, FigJam, and the built-in options...

**Reactions:**
Non-verbal feedback without interrupting. Thumbs up, clap, raise hand—these keep engagement high without disruption."

**[DEMO: HYBRID MEETING BEST PRACTICES - 2 minutes]**

**Screen recording: Hybrid meeting setup**

"Some people are in person, some are remote. This is the hardest meeting format, and it usually fails for the remote participants.

Let me show you how to make hybrid meetings work:
- Everyone logs in individually, even in-person attendees
- Remote participants go first in discussions
- Use a second screen in the room so remote faces are visible
- Designate a remote facilitator to ensure remote voices are heard"

**[CLOSING - 1 minute]**

"Remember: Before scheduling any meeting, ask: Could this be async? If yes, don't meet. Reserve meetings for what actually requires real-time interaction."
