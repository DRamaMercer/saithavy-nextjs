# PART 1: EARLY WARNING SIGNS ASSESSMENT

## Why This Checklist Matters

I've been there. I thought I could push through. I thought feeling this way was normal. I was wrong.

Burnout doesn't happen overnight. It sends signals. Warning signs. Red flags waving frantically, begging for attention. Most of us ignore them until we collapse.

This checklist is your early detection system. Use it weekly. Use it honestly. Your future self will thank you.

**You don't need to be perfect. You need to be consistent.**

---

## THE 30-ITEM BURNOUT WARNING SIGNS CHECKLIST

### Instructions:
- Review each item
- Check the box if it's been true in the past 2 weeks
- Note the severity indicator: 🟢 Green (Mild), 🟡 Yellow (Moderate), 🔴 Red (Severe)
- Total your checks at the end

---

## SECTION A: PHYSICAL SIGNS (6 items)

**Burnout lives in the body first.** Your brain can rationalize, but your body tells the truth.

- [ ] **1. Chronic fatigue** 🟡
  - Feeling tired even after sleeping
  - Needing caffeine to function
  - Energy crashes in the afternoon

- [ ] **2. Sleep disruption** 🟡
  - Difficulty falling asleep
  - Waking up frequently
  - Feeling unrested after 8+ hours

- [ ] **3. Physical symptoms without clear cause** 🟢
  - Headaches, stomach issues, muscle tension
  - Getting sick more often
  - Unexplained aches and pains

- [ ] **4. Changes in appetite** 🟡
  - Eating too much or too little
  - Using food for comfort
  - Weight fluctuations

- [ ] **5. Neglecting personal care** 🔴
  - Skipping showers, not grooming
  - Ignoring health appointments
  - Wearing dirty clothes repeatedly

- [ ] **6. Physical exhaustion** 🔴
  - Feeling heavy, movement takes effort
  - Shortness of breath from simple tasks
  - Body feels like it's shutting down

---

## SECTION B: EMOTIONAL SIGNS (8 items)

**Emotional exhaustion is the heart of burnout.** It's not just feeling tired—it's feeling empty.

- [ ] **7. Cynicism and detachment** 🟡
  - "What's the point?" attitude
  - Detached from work/meaning
  - Everything feels pointless

- [ ] **8. Loss of enjoyment** 🟡
  - Activities you loved feel flat
  - No excitement about anything
  - Numbness replaces joy

- [ ] **9. Irritability and impatience** 🟢
  - Snapping at people
  - Zero tolerance for minor annoyances
  - Everyone seems incompetent

- [ ] **10. Hopelessness** 🔴
  - "Things will never get better"
  - Dark thoughts about the future
  - Feeling trapped

- [ ] **11. Emotional volatility** 🟡
  - Crying unexpectedly
  - Anger outbursts
  - Mood swings

- [ ] **12. Detachment from others** 🟡
  - Isolating from friends/family
  - Not wanting to be around people
  - Feeling disconnected even when with others

- [ ] **13. Feeling like a failure** 🟡
  - "I'm not good enough"
  - Comparing yourself constantly
  - Imposter syndrome on overdrive

- [ ] **14. Loss of motivation** 🔴
  - Can't make yourself do anything
  - Zero drive or ambition
  - Complete apathy

---

## SECTION C: MENTAL SIGNS (7 items)

**Brain fog is real.** When you're burned out, your cognitive function declines—dramatically.

- [ ] **15. Difficulty concentrating** 🟡
  - Can't focus on tasks
  - Mind wandering constantly
  - Reading same sentence repeatedly

- [ ] **16. Memory problems** 🟢
  - Forgetting appointments, deadlines
  - Can't retain information
  - Words on the tip of your tongue

- [ ] **17. Poor decision-making** 🟡
  - Indecisive about small things
  - Second-guessing yourself
  - Avoiding decisions altogether

- [ ] **18. Reduced creativity** 🟡
  - Can't think outside the box
  - Ideas don't come
  - Feeling uninspired

- [ ] **19. Negative thinking patterns** 🔴
  - Catastrophizing everything
  - Assuming worst outcomes
  - Mental loops of negativity

- [ ] **20. Loss of problem-solving ability** 🟡
  - Problems feel overwhelming
  - Can't see solutions
  - Mental paralysis

- [ ] **21. Dissociation** 🔴
  - Feeling outside your body
  - Time disappears
  - Detached from reality

---

## SECTION D: BEHAVIORAL SIGNS (5 items)

**Actions speak louder than thoughts.** Watch what you DO, not just what you feel.

- [ ] **22. Withdrawal from responsibilities** 🟡
  - Avoiding commitments
  - Calling in sick frequently
  - Dropping balls at work/home

- [ ] **23. Procrastination** 🟡
  - Putting everything off
  - Paralysis on important tasks
  - Last-minute panic mode

- [ ] **24. Using substances to cope** 🔴
  - Drinking more than usual
  - Using drugs to escape
  - Depending on substances to function

- [ ] **25. Avoiding people** 🟡
  - Canceling plans last minute
  - Not responding to messages
  - Isolating yourself

- [ ] **26. Risk-taking or self-destructive behavior** 🔴
  - Reckless decisions
  - Self-harm
  - Dangerous activities

---

## SECTION E: WORK PATTERNS (4 items)

**Work can be a major burnout trigger.** These patterns are red flags.

- [ ] **27. Overworking constantly** 🔴
  - Working weekends, nights
  - Never truly unplugging
  - 60+ hour weeks become normal

- [ ] **28. No boundaries** 🟡
  - Saying yes to everything
  - Available 24/7
  - Can't say no

- [ ] **29. Loss of satisfaction from work** 🟡
  - Work feels meaningless
  - No pride in accomplishments
  - Just going through motions

- [ ] **30. Performance declining** 🔴
  - Missing deadlines
  - Quality suffering
  - Mistakes happening frequently

---

## SCORING YOUR BURNOUT RISK

**Total your checks:**

### 0-5 checks: 🟢 GREEN ZONE - Low Risk
**What this means:** You're managing stress well. Minor symptoms are normal life fluctuations.

**Action:** Maintain healthy habits. Use prevention practices below. Check monthly.

---

### 6-15 checks: 🟡 YELLOW ZONE - Moderate Risk
**What this means:** You're experiencing early burnout symptoms. Your system is overstressed.

**Action:** IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED (see Part 2). Implement prevention practices this week. Recheck in 2 weeks.

**Honest truth:** This is the danger zone. Most people push through until they hit red. Don't be most people.

---

### 16+ checks: 🔴 RED ZONE - Severe Risk
**What this means:** You are likely in burnout. Your system is depleted.

**Action:** EMERGENCY PROTOCOL (see Part 2). Consider professional help immediately.

**Honest truth:** You cannot white-knuckle your way out of this. You need real recovery time (see Part 6).

---

### 🔴 CRITICAL WARNING SIGNS
**If you checked ANY of these, seek help immediately:**
- Feeling hopeless or worthless
- Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
- Dissociation or feeling outside reality
- Substance dependency
- Inability to function in daily life

**Call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or seek emergency care**

# PART 2: IMMEDIATE ACTION ITEMS

## What to Do RIGHT NOW

When you notice burnout signs, speed matters. The earlier you act, the faster you recover.

**Build strength through the reps.** Small actions, taken consistently, transform everything.

---

## 🟢 GREEN ZONE: Maintenance Actions

**When you're doing okay but want to stay that way.**

### Today (5 minutes):
- [ ] Take 5 deep breaths
- [ ] Drink a glass of water
- [ ] Take a 10-minute walk
- [ ] Write down 3 things you're grateful for

### This Week (30 minutes):
- [ ] Schedule 1 activity you enjoy
- [ ] Set 1 work boundary (no email after 7pm, etc.)
- [ ] Connect with 1 friend just to catch up
- [ ] Get 7+ hours of sleep nightly

### This Month (2 hours):
- [ ] Take 1 full day off (no work, no chores)
- [ ] Review your schedule and cut 1 non-essential commitment
- [ ] Plan something to look forward to

---

## 🟡 YELLOW ZONE: Quick Recovery Actions

**When you're sliding into burnout. ACT NOW.**

### Immediately (Next 24 Hours):
- [ ] **Take a mental health day** (or half-day)
  - Call in sick. Use a vacation day. Do it.
  - Sleep. Rest. Do nothing productive.
  - No work email. No chores. Just recovery.

- [ ] **Tell someone**
  - A trusted friend, partner, or family member
  - Say: "I'm struggling and not doing well"
  - You don't need solutions. You need witness.

- [ ] **Basic care triage**
  - When did you last eat? Eat something nutritious.
  - When did you last sleep? Take a nap.
  - When did you last drink water? Hydrate now.

### This Week (7 Days):
- [ ] **Set aggressive boundaries**
  - No work after 6pm
  - Weekend completely off
  - Say no to ALL new commitments
  - Delegate what you can

- [ ] **Schedule recovery time**
  - 30 minutes daily of nothing (phone away, no inputs)
  - 1 activity that brings joy (even if you don't feel like it)
  - 1 session with a therapist or coach

- [ ] **Sleep as a priority**
  - 8+ hours nightly
  - Same bedtime every night
  - No screens 1 hour before bed

- [ ] **Movement**
  - 20-minute walk daily
  - Gentle yoga or stretching
  - Nothing intense—just movement

### Next 2 Weeks:
- [ ] **Evaluate your workload**
  - What can be postponed?
  - What can be delegated?
  - What can be dropped entirely?
  - Talk to your manager about realistic expectations

- [ ] **Reconnect with support**
  - Schedule time with friends who energize you
  - Join a support group
  - Consider therapy if not already in it

---

## 🔴 RED ZONE: EMERGENCY PROTOCOL

**You are in burnout. Your system has crashed.**

**Honest truth:** You cannot recover by working harder. You cannot white-knuckle this. You need REAL recovery time.

### Step 1: STOP (Immediately)
- [ ] Take sick leave NOW
  - Use FMLA if available (covers burnout)
  - Short-term disability if you have it
  - If you don't have these, you still need to stop

- [ ] Medical evaluation
  - See a doctor (burnout has physical symptoms)
  - See a therapist or psychiatrist
  - Get professional support

- [ ] Tell key people
  - Your manager (you need accommodation)
  - Your family/partner (you need support)
  - Close friends (you need community)

**What to say to your manager:**
> "I'm experiencing burnout and need to take medical leave to recover. I'll keep you updated on my timeline, but right now I need to focus on my health. I can help with a smooth transition of my responsibilities."

They may not understand. That's okay. You're not asking for permission. You're informing them of a medical necessity.

### Step 2: STABILIZE (Weeks 1-2)

# PART 3: PREVENTION PRACTICES

## Daily Habits (5-10 minutes)

**Consistency beats intensity.** Small daily practices compound into massive resilience.

### Morning (5 minutes):
- [ ] **3-Minute Mindfulness**
  - Sit comfortably, close your eyes
  - Focus on your breath
  - When thoughts come, notice and return to breath
  - Build the muscle of presence

- [ ] **Intention Setting**
  - Write down 1 priority for the day
  - Not a to-do list—a single focus
  - "Today I'm focused on ______"

### Midday (2 minutes):
- [ ] **Reset Break**
  - Step away from your workspace
  - Take 10 deep breaths
  - Move your body (stretch, walk)
  - No phone—just a mental reset

### Evening (5 minutes):
- [ ] **Shutdown Ritual**
  - Close all tabs, put work away
  - Write tomorrow's 1 priority
  - Transition to personal time with intention

- [ ] **Gratitude Practice**
  - Write 3 good things from today
  - Even hard days have micro-moments
  - Train your brain to see the good

---

## Weekly Rituals (30-60 minutes)

**Weeks disappear without ritual.** Create structure that replenishes you.

### Weekly Review (30 minutes, Sunday evening):
- [ ] **Review the week**
  - What drained my energy?
  - What gave me energy?
  - What did I say yes to that I should have said no to?

- [ ] **Plan the next week**
  - Block recovery time FIRST
  - Schedule 1 activity you enjoy
  - Set 1 boundary to protect

- [ ] **Self-compassion check**
  - Where was I hard on myself?
  - What can I forgive myself for?
  - What am I proud of, even if small?

### Weekly Reset (1 hour, weekend):
- [ ] **Do something completely unrelated to work**
  - Creative activity (art, music, cooking)
  - Nature time (hiking, beach, park)
  - Social time (friends who energize you)
  - No work talk allowed

- [ ] **Physical care**
  - Meal prep for the week
  - Laundry and cleaning
  - Body care (bath, massage, exercise)

---

## Monthly Deep Dives (2-3 hours)

**Monthly reflection prevents quarterly crashes.**

### Monthly Life Audit (2-3 hours):
- [ ] **Energy audit**
  - Rate your energy levels (1-10) each day for the past month
  - Look for patterns—what days were best? Worst?
  - What caused the low days? (Trigger identification)

- [ ] **Commitment review**
  - List all your commitments
  - Which ones energize you?
  - Which ones drain you?
  - What can you quit or delegate?

- [ ] **Financial check**
  - Money stress = burnout risk
  - Review spending, savings, goals
  - Make one financial decision that reduces stress

- [ ] **Relationship inventory**
  - Who supports you?
  - Who drains you?
  - Who do you need more time with?
  - Who do you need boundaries with?

- [ ] **Career alignment**
  - Does your work align with your values?
  - What's one change you can make?
  - Is it time for a bigger conversation?

---

## Quarterly Life Audits (Half-day)

**Every 3 months, step back and look at the whole picture.**

### Quarterly Retreat (Half-day, offsite):
- [ ] **Review the quarter**
  - What were your goals? Did you achieve them?
  - What did you learn about yourself?
  - What drained you? What energized you?

- [ ] **Set next quarter's intentions**
  - 3 major goals (max)
  - 3 habits to build
  - 3 things to quit

- [ ] **Life domains assessment**
  Rate each area 1-10 and set ONE intention for improvement:
  - Health & Fitness
  - Career & Work
  - Finances
  - Relationships & Family
  - Personal Growth
  - Fun & Recreation
  - Physical Environment
  - Spiritual Connection

- [ ] **Schedule the next quarter**
  - Block vacation time NOW
  - Schedule your next quarterly retreat
  - Add recovery weeks to your calendar

# PART 4: WORK ENVIRONMENT AUDIT

## Is Your Workspace Burning You Out?

Your environment shapes your energy. A toxic workspace = guaranteed burnout.

---

## PHYSICAL WORKSPACE ASSESSMENT

### Your Desk/Workspace:
- [ ] **Ergonomics**
  - Chair supports your lower back
  - Monitor at eye level
  - Feet flat on floor
  - Wrists not strained when typing

- [ ] **Lighting**
  - Natural light available
  - Soft, warm lighting (not harsh fluorescent)
  - Reduced blue light in evening

- [ ] **Air quality**
  - Ventilation is good
  - Plants or nature visible
  - Temperature comfortable (not too hot/cold)

- [ ] **Noise level**
  - Quiet enough to concentrate
  - Ability to use headphones if needed
  - Not over-stimulating

- [ ] **Personalization**
  - Photos or items that bring joy
  - Reflects your personality
  - Feels like YOUR space

### At-Home Workspace:
- [ ] **Separation from living areas**
  - Work and life spaces are physically distinct
  - Ability to "leave work" at end of day
  - Not working from bed or couch

- [ ] **Boundaries with others**
  - Family/roommates know when you're working
  - Do not disturb signal (closed door, headphones)
  - Clear start and end times

---

## DIGITAL ENVIRONMENT CHECK

### Your Devices & Tools:
- [ ] **Notification management**
  - Non-essential notifications turned OFF
  - Phone on Do Not Disturb during focus time
  - Email batching (check 2-3x/day max)

- [ ] **Communication boundaries**
  - Slack/Teams status set
  - Clear expectation: not always available
  - Response time communicated

- [ ] **Digital clutter**
  - Desktop organized
  - Tabs managed (not 50+ open)
  - Files archived regularly

### After-Hours Digital Habits:
- [ ] **Work communications end**
  - Work email/Slack off phone after hours
  - No work notifications on personal devices
  - Clear boundary: work time vs. personal time

- [ ] **Screen-free time**
  - 1 hour before bed: no screens
  - Morning routine: phone not first thing
  - Meals: phone away

---

## SCHEDULE & BOUNDARIES REVIEW

### Your Calendar:
- [ ] **Meeting load**
  - Meetings limited to essential only
  - Buffer time between meetings
  - No-meeting blocks protected

- [ ] **Focus time**
  - Deep work blocks scheduled
  - Interruptions minimized during focus
  - Calendar reflects priorities

- [ ] **Break time**
  - Lunch break taken away from desk
  - Short breaks every 90 minutes
  - Full day off each week

### Boundaries:
- [ ] **Work hours**
  - Clear start and end times
  - No early morning or late night work
  - Consistent schedule

- [ ] **Availability**
  - Not checking email/Slack on weekends
  - Vacation time used and respected
  - Sick days taken when needed

- [ ] **Saying no**
  - Comfortable declining non-essential requests
  - Protecting time for priorities
  - Not overcommitting

---

## WORKLOAD ANALYSIS

### Your Responsibilities:
- [ ] **Capacity vs. demand**
  - Workload is realistic for your hours
  - Not consistently working overtime
  - Manager understands true time needed

- [ ] **Role clarity**
  - Clear expectations of your job
  - Not doing work outside your scope
  - Resources to do your job well

- [ ] **Support and resources**
  - Training available for new skills
  - Tools and systems work properly
  - Team support when overwhelmed

### Recognition & Growth:
- [ ] **Value recognized**
  - Contributions acknowledged
  - Feedback is regular and helpful
  - Effort is seen and appreciated

- [ ] **Growth opportunities**
  - Clear path for advancement
  - Skills development supported
  - Challenged but not overwhelmed

# PART 6: RECOVERY PROTOCOL

## I'm Already Burned Out—Now What?

**I've been there.** Here's what I learned: you can't think your way out of burnout. You have to REST your way out.

Burnout recovery isn't a weekend project. It's not a spa day. It's a fundamental rebuilding of your system.

**Honest truth:** Full recovery takes 6-12 months for most people. Partial recovery (functional again) takes 3-6 months. Anyone promising a quick fix is lying.

But here's the good news: you WILL recover. Your body and brain are designed to heal. You just need to give them what they need.

---

## PHASE 1: STABILIZATION (WEEKS 1-2)

**Goal:** Stop the bleeding. Rest your nervous system. Establish basic functioning.

### Medical Foundation:
- [ ] **See a doctor**
  - Full blood work (thyroid, vitamins, cortisol)
  - Discuss symptoms and get diagnosis
  - Rule out other health issues

- [ ] **See a mental health professional**
  - Therapist, psychologist, or psychiatrist
  - Get professional support
  - Consider medication if recommended

### Complete Rest:
- [ ] **Take time off work**
  - 2 weeks minimum (4+ is better)
  - Medical leave if possible
  - No work contact during this time

- [ ] **Sleep as much as needed**
  - No alarm clock
  - 10-12 hours nightly if that's what your body wants
  - Naps are encouraged

- [ ] **Reduce all inputs**
  - No news
  - Limited social media
  - No stressful conversations
  - Sensory deprivation as much as possible

### Basic Functioning:
- [ ] **Eat regularly**
  - 3 meals a day minimum
  - Simple, nourishing food
  - Don't worry about "perfect" nutrition

- [ ] **Hydrate**
  - Water throughout the day
  - Reduce caffeine and alcohol

- [ ] **Gentle movement**
  - Short walks (10-15 minutes)
  - Stretching
  - No intense exercise

- [ ] **Nature time**
  - Sit outside daily
  - Get sunlight
  - Fresh air

### Emotional Support:
- [ ] **Tell people what's happening**
  - Be honest about burnout
  - You don't need to justify or explain
  - You need support, not solutions

- [ ] **Release the pressure**
  - No expectations of productivity
  - Give yourself full permission to do nothing
  - This is healing time, not wasted time

---

## PHASE 2: RECOVERY (WEEKS 3-8)

**Goal:** Rebuild energy gradually. Establish recovery routines. Identify root causes.

### Gradual Activity:
- [ ] **Follow your energy**
  - Do what feels manageable
  - Rest when tired
  - No pushing through

- [ ] **Daily structure**
  - Wake up around the same time
  - Simple meals at regular times
  - Light activity (walk, gentle yoga)

- [ ] **Therapy or coaching**
  - Weekly sessions
  - Work on underlying patterns
  - Build new coping strategies

### Root Cause Analysis:
- [ ] **What caused this burnout?**
  - Workload issues?
  - Toxic environment?
  - Perfectionism?
  - Lack of boundaries?
  - People-pleasing?

- [ ] **What needs to change?**
  - At work?
  - In your schedule?
  - In your relationships?
  - In your mindset?

### Rebuilding Identity:
- [ ] **Who are you outside of work?**
  - List your values
  - Identify activities that bring joy
  - Reconnect with forgotten interests

- [ ] **Set new boundaries**
  - Practice saying no
  - Communicate limits
  - Protect recovery time

### Light Return to Activity:
- [ ] **If still on leave:**
  - No work yet
  - Add one light activity per week (coffee with friend, hobby)
  - Monitor energy levels

- [ ] **If returned to work:**
  - Reduced hours if possible
  - Gradual increase in responsibilities
  - Clear boundaries from day one
  - Regular check-ins with yourself

  - Clear boundaries from the start

  - Regular breaks
  - No overtime

### Lifestyle Changes:
- [ ] **Regular exercise**
  - 3-4 times per week
  - Mix of cardio and strength
  - You enjoy it (not punishment)

- [ ] **Sleep hygiene**
  - Consistent schedule
  - 7-9 hours nightly
  - Wind-down routine

- [ ] **Nutrition**
  - Regular, balanced meals
  - Hydration
  - Limit caffeine and alcohol

### Prevention Systems:
- [ ] **Weekly recovery rituals**
  - One full day off

  - Regular self-care

- [ ] **Boundary maintenance**
  - Say no without guilt
  - Protect your time

# PART 7: 30-DAY PREVENTION CHALLENGE

## Build Sustainable Habits in One Month

**Build strength through the reps.** This challenge isn't about perfection. It's about building a foundation.

Each week, add 3-5 new habits. By day 30, you'll have a complete burnout prevention system.

---

## WEEK 1: AWARENESS

**Goal:** Notice your patterns. Identify burnout signs early.

### Daily Habits (All Week):
- [ ] **Morning energy check**
  - Rate your energy 1-10
  - Note what might affect it (sleep, stress, etc.)
  - Keep a simple log

- [ ] **End-of-day reflection**
  - What drained my energy today?
  - What gave me energy?
  - One thing I'm grateful for

- [ ] **Body scan**
  - Notice tension in your body
  - Where do you hold stress?
  - Take 3 deep breaths to release it

### Weekly Task:
- [ ] **Take the burnout assessment** (Part 1)
  - Be honest with yourself
  - Note your red flags
  - Save this baseline

---

## WEEK 2: BOUNDARIES

**Goal:** Protect your energy. Say no without guilt.

### Daily Habits (All Week):
- [ ] **Set one boundary daily**
  - "I can't make that meeting"
  - "I need to leave on time today"
  - "I'm not checking email after 7pm"

- [ ] **Schedule recovery time**
  - Block 15 minutes of downtime
  - Protect it like a meeting
  - No phone, no inputs

- [ ] **End workday ritual**
  - Close all work tabs
  - Write tomorrow's priority
  - Transition to personal time intentionally

### Weekly Task:
- [ ] **Boundary audit**
  - List all your commitments
  - Which ones violate your boundaries?
  - What can you quit, delegate, or renegotiate?

---

## WEEK 3: RECOVERY

**Goal:** Build rest into your schedule. Recovery isn't optional.

### Daily Habits (All Week):
- [ ] **7+ hours of sleep**
  - Set a bedtime alarm
  - Wind-down routine (no screens)
  - Cool, dark bedroom

- [ ] **Movement break**
  - 10-minute walk
  - Stretch or yoga
  - Midday reset

- [ ] **Nourish your body**
  - Three meals
  - Stay hydrated
  - Limit caffeine after 2pm

### Weekly Task:
- [ ] **Schedule one recovery day**
  - Full day off (no work, no chores)
  - Do something you enjoy
  - Protect this time ruthlessly

---

## WEEK 4: INTEGRATION

**Goal:** Combine all habits. Create your system.

### Daily Habits (All Week):
- [ ] **Morning intention**
  - Set one priority for the day
  - 3-minute mindfulness
  - Energy check

- [ ] **Midday reset**
  - 10-minute walk
  - Away from screens
  - Recharge break

- [ ] **Evening shutdown**
  - Close work completely
  - Gratitude practice
  - Prepare for tomorrow

### Weekly Task:
- [ ] **Monthly review**
  - What worked this month?
  - What didn't?
  - What will you continue?
  - What will you adjust?

- [ ] Daily awareness of your energy levels

- [ ] Recovery time blocked on your calendar
- [ ] Morning and evening routines

## Professional Help Guide

### When to See a Therapist:
- [ ] Burnout symptoms interfering with daily life
- [ ] Feeling hopeless or worthless
- [ ] Using substances to cope
- [ ] Relationships suffering
- [ ] Physical symptoms without clear medical cause
- [ ] Thoughts of self-harm

### Types of Therapists:
- **Psychologist (PhD/PsyD)**
  - Talk therapy, psychological testing
  - Cannot prescribe medication

- **Psychiatrist (MD)**
  - Medical doctor specializing in mental health
  - Can prescribe medication
  - Often combined with therapy

- **Licensed Clinical Social Worker (LCSW)**
  - Talk therapy, case management
  - Often more affordable

- **Licensed Professional Counselor (LPC)**
  - Talk therapy, specialized in specific issues
  - Often more affordable

### Therapy Approaches for Burnout:
- **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)**
  - Change negative thought patterns
  - Practical tools and strategies

- **Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)**
  - Accept difficult emotions
  - Align actions with values

- **Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR)**
  - Mindfulness meditation
  - Stress reduction techniques

### Finding a Therapist:
1. **Start with your insurance**
   - Check your mental health coverage
   - Find in-network providers

2. **Use directories**
   - Psychology Today
   - Your insurance company's website
   - Open Path Collective for low-cost options

3. **Ask questions**
   - Do you have experience with burnout?
   - What's your approach to therapy?
   - Do you have availability?

4. **Try a few**
   - First session is often a fit check
   - It's okay to try multiple therapists
   - Trust your gut

### Cost Considerations:
- **Insurance**: Check copay and deductible
- **Sliding scale**: Many therapists offer reduced rates
- **Community clinics**: Low-cost options
- **University training clinics**: Reduced-rate sessions with supervised trainees
- **Online therapy**: Often more affordable than in-person
