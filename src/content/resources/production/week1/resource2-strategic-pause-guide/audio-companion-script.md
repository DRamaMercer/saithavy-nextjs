## Introduction (2 minutes)

**Welcome.**

I'm so glad you're here.

I'm going to teach you a practice that transformed my leadership—and I've seen it transform hundreds of leaders since.

It's called the **Strategic Pause**.

**[PAUSE 3 seconds]**

Let me be vulnerable with you for a moment.

I used to pride myself on being quick. Fast decisions. Rapid responses. Always ready with an answer.

But here's what I learned:

**Being fast doesn't make you wise.**

In fact, some of my worst leadership moments came from reacting too quickly. I'd say something in a meeting that I couldn't take back. I'd send an email in anger that I spent weeks apologizing for. I'd make a decision that felt urgent but wasn't actually important.

The cost was high.

Damaged relationships. Lost trust. Sleepless nights. Regret.

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

Maybe you can relate.

Maybe you've been in a high-pressure meeting where everyone expected you to decide NOW. Your chest tightens. Your mind races. And you feel this pressure to just... SAY something.

So you do. And sometimes it works. But often—it doesn't.

**Here's the truth:**

Your nervous system wasn't designed for constant high-speed, high-stakes decision-making.

When you're chronically in fight-or-flight mode:
- Your decision quality declines
- Your emotional reactivity increases
- Your creativity shuts down
- Your presence and influence diminish

**The strategic pause is the antidote.**

**[PAUSE 3 seconds]**

**A strategic pause is a brief, intentional interruption of activity designed to:**

1. Regulate your nervous system
2. Clear mental noise
3. Access your full cognitive capacity
4. Choose your response rather than reacting

It's not about relaxation. It's about **clarity**.

It's about accessing your wisest self in the moments you need it most.

And today, I'm going to teach you exactly how to do it.

## The 3-Question Framework (3 minutes)

**[SOUND: Subtle shift in music - slightly more energized]**

The strategic pause is built on **3 simple questions**.

You can ask them in 60 seconds. Anytime. Anywhere.

Let's walk through them.

---

### Question 1: What Do I Know? (Facts vs. Stories)

**[PAUSE 2 seconds]**

**Here's what I know about you:**

Your mind is a story-making machine.

Under pressure, you don't just observe—you interpret.

- They arrive late → **"They don't respect my time"**
- Your boss asks a question → **"They're questioning my competence"**
- A client sends an angry email → **"They hate our work"**

**Here's the problem:**

When you believe a story, your nervous system responds as if it's TRUE.

Your amygdala activates. Your heart races. Your chest tightens.

Your prefrontal cortex—the wise, strategic part of your brain—goes offline.

You're now operating from **survival mode**, not leadership mode.

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**So the first question is: What do I KNOW?**

Not what am I telling myself.
Not what I'm assuming.
Not what I fear is true.

**What are the FACTS?**

What would a video camera capture?

**Example:**

- **Story:** "My team doesn't respect me"
- **Fact:** "Three people arrived late to the meeting"

See the difference?

The story triggers defensiveness. The facts allow for problem-solving.

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

### Question 2: What Do I Feel? (Emotions as Data)

**[PAUSE 2 seconds]**

**Here's something I wish I'd learned earlier:**

Emotions aren't obstacles to leadership. They're **data**.

We're taught to suppress emotions. "Stay professional." "Don't get emotional."

But unfelt emotions don't disappear—they drive unconscious behavior.

- Unexpressed anger → passive-aggressive comments
- Unacknowledged fear → overcontrolling behavior
- Unrecognized disappointment → cynicism and withdrawal

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**So the second question is: What do I FEEL?**

Name the emotion. Feel it in your body. Ask what it needs.

**Example:**

You receive critical feedback. You feel shame.

Instead of pushing it away, you ask:

"What is this shame telling me?"

→ "I care about doing good work"
→ "I want to be seen as competent"
→ "I feel vulnerable when critiqued"

Now you can address the **need**—not just react to the emotion.

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

### Question 3: What's Aligned Action? (Wisdom-Based Decisions)

**[PAUSE 2 seconds]**

**When we're activated, we do one of two things:**

We **freeze**—can't decide, avoid the situation
Or we **react**—send the angry email, make the snap decision

Neither is wisdom.

**Aligned action is the middle path: Responsive. Intentional.**

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**So the third question is: What's ALIGNED action?**

Not: What's safest?
Not: What's fastest?

**What would my wisest self do?**

**Example:**

A direct report delivers poor work.

**Reactive action:** "This is unacceptable! I'm done doing your work for you."

**Aligned action:** "I see this is still a challenge. Let's discuss what support you need. And I need this work to meet standard by Friday."

Wisdom sees: **Care + Accountability > Either/or**

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**Those are the 3 questions:**

1. What do I KNOW? (Facts vs. Stories)
2. What do I FEEL? (Emotions as Data)
3. What's ALIGNED action? (Wisdom-Based Decisions)

Simple. Memorable. Powerful.

Now let's practice them together.

## Guided Practice (5 minutes)

**[SOUND: Music shifts to slower, more contemplative]**

**[VOICE: Slower, more gentle]**

**I invite you to get comfortable.**

Feet flat on the floor. Spine tall but relaxed. Hands resting wherever feels natural.

**Let's take three deep breaths together.**

Inhale deeply... filling your lungs completely

**[PAUSE 2 seconds]**

And exhale slowly... letting the air release fully

**[PAUSE 2 seconds]**

One more time:

Inhale... feeling your chest expand

**[PAUSE 2 seconds]**

Exhale... releasing everything you've been holding

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**Now, I invite you to think of a situation that's causing you stress.**

It could be a difficult conversation coming up.
A decision you're facing.
A relationship challenge.
A work situation.

Just let it come into your mind.

**[PAUSE 10 seconds]**

**Don't try to fix it. Just notice it.**

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**Now, let's practice Question 1: What do I KNOW?**

**Ask yourself:**

What are the FACTS in this situation?
What would a video camera capture?
What would hold up in court?

Not what I'm telling myself about it.
Not what I fear is true.

**Just the facts.**

**[LONG PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**Notice:** What's it like to separate facts from stories?

Is there any relief in just acknowledging what you KNOW?

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**Now, let's practice Question 2: What do I FEEL?**

**Gently scan your body:**

Where do you feel this situation?
- Chest tightness?
- Stomach knot?
- Shoulder tension?
- Heat in your face?

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**Now, name the emotion:**

Am I feeling angry? Sad? Scared? Glad?
Frustrated? Disappointed? Anxious? Excited?

Just name it.

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**Now, ask yourself:**

What is this emotion telling me?
What does it need me to know?
What value is being honored or threatened?

**[LONG PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**Example:**

If you're feeling ANGRY → it might be telling you: "I value clear communication. I'm not being heard."

If you're feeling ANXIOUS → it might be telling you: "I care about doing good work. I want to get this right."

If you're feeling DISAPPOINTED → it might be telling you: "I had high hopes. This matters to me."

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**Now, let's practice Question 3: What's ALIGNED action?**

**I invite you to imagine your wisest self.**

Not your fearful self. Not your reactive self.

The part of you that has perspective. That has seen patterns before. That knows what matters.

**Ask yourself:**

If I weren't afraid, what would I do?
What action aligns with my values?
What would future me thank me for?

**[LONG PAUSE - 20 seconds]**

**Don't force an answer.**

Just let the question sit with you.

Sometimes wisdom is quiet. Sometimes it's just a feeling, not words.

**[PAUSE 10 seconds]**

**Now, I invite you to consider:**

If I made a decision from CLARITY rather than FEAR...
What would I choose?

**[PAUSE 10 seconds]**

**Take one more deep breath:**

Inhale clarity... and exhale confusion.

**[PAUSE 3 seconds]**

Inhale wisdom... and exhale reactivity.

**[PAUSE 3 seconds]**

Inhale peace... and exhale stress.

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**[SOUND: Music begins to fade]**

## Closing (Optional Continuation)

**[VOICE: Warm, encouraging]**

**Thank you for practicing with me today.**

**Here's what I want you to know:**

This gets easier with practice.

The first time you try the strategic pause, it might feel awkward.
You might forget the questions.
You might get stuck distinguishing facts from stories.

**That's okay.**

Build strength through the reps.

**[PAUSE 3 seconds]**

**My invitation to you:**

Practice this for 21 days.

- Week 1: Just notice when you're triggered. Take 3 breaths.
- Week 2: Ask "What do I know?" Separate facts from stories.
- Week 3: Add "What do I feel?" Name your emotions.
- Week 4: Integrate all 3 questions. Access your wisdom.

**Systems before willpower.**

You don't need more self-discipline. You need a system.

The strategic pause is your system.

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**Remember:**

**Your presence is your primary leadership tool.**

The strategic pause helps you show up with intention, respond with wisdom, and lead with integrity.

**You don't have to be perfect. You have to be consistent.**

**[PAUSE 3 seconds]**

**One last thing:**

If this practice resonated with you, I'd love to hear about your experience.

Share your stories. Your breakthroughs. Your challenges.

Because here's what I know for sure:

**We're all learning this together.**

**[PAUSE 5 seconds]**

**Thank you for being here.**

**Thank you for your commitment to leading with presence.**

**Take good care.**

**[SOUND: Music swells and fades out - 15 seconds]**
