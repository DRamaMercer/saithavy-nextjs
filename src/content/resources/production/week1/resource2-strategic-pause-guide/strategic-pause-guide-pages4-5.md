# Pages 4-5: How to Practice + Scenario Examples

---

## The 60-Second Strategic Pause

**You don't need an hour. You need 60 seconds.**

Here's exactly how to practice the strategic pause in real time.

---

### Step 1: Notice the Trigger (5 seconds)

**"I feel activated."**

Common triggers:
- Chest tightening
- Heart racing
- Face feeling hot
- Urgency to decide NOW
- Mind racing
- Urge to interrupt or defend

**The moment you notice:**
- "I feel triggered."
- "I'm going to pause."

**This is the most important step.** Noticing the pattern interrupts the automatic reaction.

---

### Step 2: Choose to Pause (5 seconds)

**"I'm taking a moment."**

You can:
- Say: "Let me think about this for a minute"
- Take a sip of water
- Use a physical anchor: touch thumb and forefinger together
- Step away: "I need to use the restroom"

**Normalize it:**
- "I want to give this the consideration it deserves"
- "Let me reflect on that before I respond"

**Most people respect thoughtful leaders more than reactive ones.**

---

### Step 3: Ask the 3 Questions (30 seconds)

**Question 1: What do I know?** (10 seconds)
- List the facts
- Separate from stories
- "What would a camera capture?"

**Question 2: What do I feel?** (10 seconds)
- Name the emotion
- Feel it in your body
- "What does it need me to know?"

**Question 3: What's aligned action?** (10 seconds)
- Consult your wisest self
- Consider short and long-term
- "What serves the situation?"

---

### Step 4: Act from Clarity (20 seconds)

**Respond from your wisest self.**

Or say: "I need more time to think about this. Let me get back to you by [time]."

**Better to pause and respond well than to react and repair later.**

## Scenario 1: The Urgent Email

**Situation:** An angry client email arrives at 5pm on Friday

---

### Without Strategic Pause

```
REACTION: Immediate defensive response
TIME: 30 minutes writing and rewriting
OUTCOME:
- Ruminating all weekend
- Damaged relationship
- Sleepless Sunday night
- Monday morning regret
```

---

### With Strategic Pause

```
TRIGGER: "I feel defensive. My heart is racing."

PAUSE: "I won't respond now. I'll take 10 minutes."

QUESTION 1: "What do I KNOW?"
→ "Client is frustrated about the delay."
→ "They need reassurance and a timeline."
→ "My story ('They don't trust me') isn't a fact."

QUESTION 2: "What do I FEEL?"
→ "Anxiety about their satisfaction."
→ "Fear that I'm not good enough."
→ "Desire to fix it immediately."
→ "Acknowledgment: 'Of course I feel anxious. I care about doing good work.'"

QUESTION 3: "What's ALIGNED action?"
→ "Acknowledge their frustration."
→ "Share the plan clearly."
→ "Offer a realistic timeline."
→ "Send Monday morning with fresh eyes."

ACTION: Send a thoughtful response Monday morning
OUTCOME:
- Relationship preserved
- Anxiety managed
- Solution delivered
- Weekend protected
```

---

**The Pause That Saved the Weekend:**

60 seconds of pause prevented 48 hours of suffering.

## Scenario 2: The High-Stakes Meeting

**Situation:** Your boss questions your data in front of the executive team

---

### Without Strategic Pause

```
REACTION: Defensive response
TIME: Instant regret
OUTCOME:
- Loss of credibility
- Self-doubt for weeks
- Reputation as "defensive"
- Missed opportunity for growth
```

---

### With Strategic Pause

```
TRIGGER: "My face is hot. I feel attacked."

PAUSE: Take a sip of water. Three breaths.

QUESTION 1: "What do I KNOW?"
→ "She asked about methodology."
→ "She wants to ensure accuracy."
→ "My story ('She's trying to embarrass me') isn't a fact."

QUESTION 2: "What do I FEEL?"
→ "Shame. Embarrassment."
→ "Fear that I'm not prepared enough."
→ "Desire to defend myself."
→ "Acknowledgment: 'Of course I feel shame. I'm being questioned publicly.'"

QUESTION 3: "What's ALIGNED action?"
→ "Acknowledge the question."
→ "Explain the methodology confidently."
→ "Offer to follow up with additional details."
→ "Model curiosity, not defensiveness."

ACTION: "Great question. Let me walk you through our approach..."
OUTCOME:
- Composure under pressure
- Credibility enhanced
- Trust in data increased
- Reputation as "thoughtful"
```

---

**The Pause That Saved Your Reputation:**

60 seconds of pause enhanced your standing.

## Scenario 3: The Difficult Conversation

**Situation:** You need to give critical feedback to a direct report

---

### Without Strategic Pause

```
REACTION: Avoid or deliver clumsily
TIME: Days of dread
OUTCOME:
- Conversation avoided or botched
- Relationship damage
- Performance doesn't improve
- Both people feel bad
```

---

### With Strategic Pause

```
BEFORE THE MEETING:

TRIGGER: "I'm dreading this. I feel tightness in my chest."

PAUSE: Take 3 minutes to prepare before the meeting.

QUESTION 1: "What do I KNOW?"
→ "Performance is below expectations."
→ "They deserve honest feedback."
→ "They're capable of better."

QUESTION 2: "What do I FEEL?"
→ "Fear of their reaction."
→ "Guilt about causing pain."
→ "Desire to be liked."
→ "Acknowledgment: 'Of course I feel these things. I care about them as a person.'"

QUESTION 3: "What's ALIGNED action?"
→ "Care about their growth AND be honest."
→ "Frame feedback as support, not criticism."
→ "Come from curiosity, not judgment."

DURING THE CONVERSATION:

ACTION: "I want to help you succeed. Here's what I'm observing..."

TRIGGER (if they get defensive): "They're pushing back."

PAUSE: Three breaths.

QUESTION 1: "What do I KNOW?"
→ "They're feeling defensive."
→ "This is normal, not personal."

QUESTION 2: "What do I FEEL?"
→ "Urgency to fix it."
→ "Desire to be understood."

QUESTION 3: "What's ALIGNED action?"
→ "Stay curious."
→ "Acknowledge their experience."
→ "Hold the standard."

ACTION: "I hear that you're frustrated. Let's talk about what's happening and how to address it."

OUTCOME:
- Growth conversation
- Trust built
- Performance improves
- Relationship strengthened
```

---

**The Pause That Transformed Leadership:**

60 seconds of pause turned a dreaded conversation into a growth opportunity.

## Physical Integration Practices

**Your body is the fastest way to regulate your nervous system.**

**Use these practices during the pause:**

### 3 Deep Breaths (15 seconds)
- Inhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 2 counts
- Exhale for 6 counts
- Repeat 3 times

**This signals safety to your nervous system.**

### Feel Your Feet (10 seconds)
- Wiggle your toes
- Feel the contact with the floor
- Imagine roots growing into the earth

**This grounds you in the present moment.**

### Open Your Posture (5 seconds)
- Uncross your arms and legs
- Roll your shoulders back
- Lift your chest slightly

**This signals confidence to your nervous system.**

### Touch Your Anchor (3 seconds)
- Touch thumb and forefinger together
- This creates a physical trigger for the pause
- Use it consistently to build the habit

**Build strength through the reps.**

## Practice Schedule

**Week 1: Micro-Pauses (3 breaths)**
- Use 3 deep breaths before responding
- Practice 5 times per day
- Build the awareness habit

**Week 2: Full Pause (60 seconds)**
- Use the full 3-question process
- Practice 3 times per day
- Focus on facts vs. stories

**Week 3: Integrated Practice**
- Use in real situations
- Notice what shifts
- Reflect on outcomes

**Week 4: Team Practice**
- Model the pause for others
- Normalize strategic pauses in meetings
- Share the framework
