# Pages 2-3: The 3 Questions Framework

---

## Question 1: What Do I Know? (Facts vs. Stories)

### The Problem

**Our minds spin stories under pressure.**

- "They're questioning my competence" ← STORY
- "They asked for more data" ← FACT

- "My team doesn't respect me" ← STORY
- "Three people arrived late to the meeting" ← FACT

**Here's what happens:**

When you believe a story, your nervous system responds as if it's true. Your amygdala activates. Your prefrontal cortex (the wise, strategic part of your brain) goes offline.

You're now operating from survival mode, not leadership mode.

**The first question helps you separate signal from noise.**

---

### The Practice

**"Write down exactly what happened, not your interpretation."**

When you feel activated, ask yourself:

1. **"What would a video camera capture?"**
   - Observable facts only
   - No interpretations
   - No mind-reading

2. **"What am I adding that isn't provable?"**
   - Look for words like "always," "never," "they"
   - Notice assumptions about intent
   - Check for predictions about the future

3. **"Can I prove this in court?"**
   - Would this hold up as evidence?
   - Or is it my interpretation?

---

### Real-World Example

**Scenario:** Your boss sends you an email: "Can we discuss your presentation?"

**Without the Strategic Pause:**

```
STORY: "They hated it. I'm going to get fired. I'm not good enough."
EMOTION: Anxiety, shame, fear
REACTION: Defensive email or avoidance
OUTCOME: Self-fulfilling prophecy
```

**With the Strategic Pause:**

```
FACTS: "They sent an email asking to discuss the presentation."
STORY: "They hated it."

QUESTION: "What do I KNOW?"
→ "They want to discuss it. That's all I know."

EMOTION SHIFTS: From panic to curiosity
ACTION: "Thanks for the feedback. When works for you?"
OUTCOME: Constructive conversation
```

**See the difference?** The story triggered a nervous system cascade. The facts allowed for a measured response.

---

### Practice Prompt

**Right now, think of a situation that's bothering you.**

**Ask yourself:**
1. "What are the FACTS—what would a camera capture?"
2. "What are the STORIES—what am I adding?"

**Write down two columns:**

| FACTS (Observable) | STORIES (Interpretations) |
|--------------------|---------------------------|
|                    |                           |

**Notice:** How does your body feel when you separate facts from stories?

## Question 2: What Do I Feel? (Emotions as Data)

### The Problem

**We're taught to suppress emotions in leadership.**

"Stay professional."
"Don't get emotional."
"Leave your feelings at the door."

**Here's the problem:**

Unfelt emotions don't disappear. They drive unconscious behavior.

- Unexpressed anger → passive-aggressive comments
- Unacknowledged fear → overcontrolling behavior
- Unrecognized disappointment → cynicism and withdrawal

**Your emotions ARE data.** They tell you what matters.

---

### The Practice

**"Name the emotion. Feel it in your body. Ask what it needs."**

**Step 1: Name the emotion**

Start simple:
- **Mad:** Angry, frustrated, irritated, resentful
- **Sad:** Disappointed, hurt, grieving, lonely
- **Glad:** Happy, excited, grateful, satisfied
- **Scared:** Anxious, afraid, worried, nervous

**Step 2: Feel it in your body**

- Chest tightness = anxiety, fear
- Stomach knot = worry, dread
- Shoulder tension = burden, responsibility
- Heat in face = anger, shame
- Throat constriction = unspoken words

**Step 3: Ask what it needs**

- "What is this emotion telling me?"
- "What does it need me to know?"
- "What value is being honored or threatened?"

---

### Real-World Example

**Scenario:** You receive critical feedback from a peer.

**Without the Strategic Pause:**

```
EMOTION: Shame (unrecognized)
PHYSICAL: Face hot, chest tight
REACTION: Defensive ("But you said...")
OUTCOME: Relationship damaged, growth blocked
```

**With the Strategic Pause:**

```
EMOTION: "I feel SHAME."
PHYSICAL: "My face is hot. My chest is tight."
INQUIRY: "What is the shame telling me?"

→ "I care about doing good work."
→ "I want to be seen as competent."
→ "I feel vulnerable when critiqued."

ACKNOWLEDGMENT: "Of course I feel shame. I care deeply."
INTEGRATED ACTION: "Thank you for the feedback. I want to learn from this."
OUTCOME: Growth, trust, improved performance
```

**When you feel the emotion, it can drive you. When you acknowledge it, you can choose what to do with it.**

---

### Practice Prompt

**Think of a recent emotional reaction.**

**Ask yourself:**
1. "What emotion am I feeling?" (Name it)
2. "Where do I feel it in my body?" (Locate it)
3. "What is this emotion telling me?" (Understand it)

**Example:**
- "I feel FRUSTRATED."
- "I feel it in my jaw and shoulders."
- "It's telling me: I value clear communication. I'm not being heard."

**Now you can address the need, not just react to the emotion.**

## Question 3: What's Aligned Action? (Wisdom-Based Decisions)

### The Problem

**When we're activated, we either:**

1. **FREEZE** (Paralysis)
   - Can't decide
   - Avoid the situation
   - Hope it goes away

2. **REACT** (Impulsivity)
   - Send the angry email
   - Make a snap decision
   - Say something to relieve the pressure

**Neither is wisdom.**

**Aligned action is the middle path: Responsive, intentional.**

---

### The Practice

**"Pause and ask: What would my wisest self do?"**

**Step 1: Access your wisest self**

Imagine yourself:
- 6 months from now, looking back
- At your best, most grounded
- Free from immediate pressure

**Step 2: Consider multiple timeframes**

- **Short-term:** What's needed right now?
- **Medium-term:** What serves the relationship?
- **Long-term:** What aligns with my values?

**Step 3: Choose action aligned with values, not fear**

Not "What's safest?"
Not "What's fastest?"
**"What's wise?"**

---

### Real-World Example

**Scenario:** A direct report delivers poor work for the third time.

**Without the Strategic Pause:**

```
REACTION: "This is unacceptable! I'm done doing your work for you."
EMOTION: Anger, exhaustion
ACTION: Harsh feedback, ultimatum
OUTCOME: Defensive employee, damaged relationship
```

**With the Strategic Pause:**

```
QUESTION 1: "What do I know?"
→ "Work quality is below expectations. We've discussed this twice."

QUESTION 2: "What do I feel?"
→ "Frustrated. Disappointed. Worried about their success."

QUESTION 3: "What's aligned action?"
→ "My wisest self cares about their growth AND the team's standards."
→ "Aligned action: Clear feedback + support + accountability."

ACTION: "I see this is still a challenge. Let's discuss what support you need to succeed. And I need this work to meet standard by Friday."
OUTCOME: Growth, accountability, relationship preserved
```

**Wisdom sees: Care + Accountability > Either/or**

---

### Practice Prompt

**Think of a decision you're facing.**

**Ask yourself:**
1. "If I weren't afraid, what would I do?"
2. "What action aligns with my values?"
3. "What would future me thank me for?"

**Write down:**
- Reactive impulse: ___________________
- Aligned action: ______________________

**Notice the difference.**
