### Pages 2-3: The 3 Questions Framework

**Purpose:** Deep dive into each question with examples

#### Question 1: What Do I Know? (Facts vs. Stories)

**The Problem:**
- Our minds spin stories under pressure
- "They're questioning my competence" vs. "They asked for more data"
- Stories trigger amygdala, facts engage prefrontal cortex

**The Practice:**
- "Write down exactly what happened, not your interpretation"
- "Separate what you observed from what you inferred"
- "What would a video camera capture?"

**Real-World Example:**
```
STORY: "My team doesn't respect my leadership"
FACTS: "Three people arrived late to the meeting. One interrupted me twice."

See the difference? The story triggers defensiveness. The facts allow problem-solving.
```

**Practice Prompt:**
- "In this situation, what are the facts?"
- "What am I adding that isn't provable?"

#### Question 2: What Do I Feel? (Emotions as Data)

**The Problem:**
- We're taught to suppress emotions in leadership
- But emotions ARE data—they tell us what matters
- Unfelt emotions drive unconscious behavior

**The Practice:**
- "Name the emotion: fear, anger, disappointment, excitement"
- "Feel it in your body: chest tightness, stomach knot, shoulder tension"
- "Ask: What is this emotion telling me?"

**Real-World Example:**
```
SITUATION: "I feel angry about this feedback"

INQUIRY:
"What's the anger telling me?"
→ "I care about doing good work"
→ "I feel misunderstood"
→ "I want to be seen as competent"

Now you can address the need, not just react to the anger.
```

**Practice Prompt:**
- "What am I feeling right now?"
- "What does this emotion need me to know?"

#### Question 3: What's Aligned Action? (Wisdom-Based Decisions)

**The Problem:**
- We either freeze (paralysis) or react (impulsivity)
- Aligned action is the middle path: responsive, intentional
- Your wisest self knows the answer—you just need to access it

**The Practice:**
- "Pause and ask: What would my wisest self do?"
- "Consider both short-term and long-term impact"
- "Choose action aligned with values, not fear"

**Real-World Example:**
```
REACTIVE: "Send an angry email defending myself"
ALIGNED: "Schedule a conversation to understand their perspective. Prepare questions. Show up curious, not defensive."

Wisdom sees: relationship preservation > being right
```

**Practice Prompt:**
- "If I weren't afraid, what would I do?"
- "What action aligns with my values?"
- "What would future me thank me for?"

**Brand Voice:** Reflective + Practical
**Format:** Explanation → Example → Practice prompt for each question

### Pages 4-5: How to Practice + Scenario Examples

**Purpose:** Make the framework actionable with specific scenarios

#### How to Practice: The 60-Second Strategic Pause

**Step-by-Step:**

**1. Notice the Trigger (5 seconds)**
- "I feel my chest tightening"
- "I notice I'm interrupting people"
- "I feel the urge to decide NOW"

**2. Choose to Pause (5 seconds)**
- "I'm going to take a moment"
- Excuse yourself: "Let me think about this for one minute"
- Use a physical anchor: touch your thumb and forefinger together

**3. Ask the 3 Questions (30 seconds)**
- "What do I know?" (List facts)
- "What do I feel?" (Name emotions)
- "What's aligned action?" (Identify wise response)

**4. Act from Clarity (20 seconds)**
- Respond from your wisest self
- Or say: "I need more time to think about this"

**Physical Integration:**
- "Take 3 deep breaths"
- "Feel your feet on the floor"
- "Uncross your arms, open your posture"

#### Scenario 1: The Urgent Email

**Situation:** "An angry client email arrives at 5pm on Friday"

**Without Strategic Pause:**
- Immediate defensive response
- Ruminating all weekend
- Damaged relationship

**With Strategic Pause:**
```
1. NOTICE: "I feel defensive, my heart is racing"
2. PAUSE: "I won't respond now. I'll take 10 minutes."
3. QUESTION 1: "What do I know? Client is frustrated about the delay. They need reassurance."
4. QUESTION 2: "What do I feel? Anxiety about their satisfaction. Fear that I'm not good enough."
5. QUESTION 3: "What's aligned? Acknowledge their frustration. Share the plan. Offer a timeline."
6. ACTION: Send a thoughtful response Monday morning
```

**Outcome:** Relationship preserved. Anxiety managed. Solution delivered.

#### Scenario 2: The High-Stakes Meeting

**Situation:** "Your boss questions your data in front of the executive team"

**Without Strategic Pause:**
- Defensive reaction
- Loss of credibility
- Self-doubt for weeks

**With Strategic Pause:**
```
1. NOTICE: "My face is hot. I feel attacked."
2. PAUSE: Take a sip of water. Three breaths.
3. QUESTION 1: "What do I know? She asked about methodology. She wants to ensure accuracy."
4. QUESTION 2: "What do I feel? Shame. Fear that I'm not prepared enough."
5. QUESTION 3: "What's aligned? Acknowledge the question. Explain the methodology. Offer to follow up with details."
6. ACTION: "Great question. Let me walk you through our approach..."
```

**Outcome:** Composure under pressure. Credibility enhanced.

#### Scenario 3: The Difficult Conversation

**Situation:** "You need to give critical feedback to a direct report"

**Without Strategic Pause:**
- Avoid the conversation
- Or deliver it clumsily
- Relationship damage

**With Strategic Pause:**
```
1. NOTICE: "I'm dreading this. I feel tightness in my chest."
2. PAUSE: Before the meeting, take 3 minutes to prepare
3. QUESTION 1: "What do I know? Performance is below expectations. They deserve honest feedback."
4. QUESTION 2: "What do I feel? Fear of their reaction. Guilt about causing pain. Desire to be liked."
5. QUESTION 3: "What's aligned? Care about their growth AND be honest. Frame feedback as support, not criticism."
6. ACTION: "I want to help you succeed. Here's what I'm observing..."
```

**Outcome:** Growth conversation. Trust built. Performance improves.

**Brand Voice:** Supportive + Practical
**Format:** Step-by-step process followed by detailed scenarios

### Page 6: Troubleshooting + FAQs

**Purpose:** Address common objections and challenges

#### Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "I Don't Have Time to Pause"**

**Reality Check:**
- "You don't have time NOT to pause"
- "A 60-second pause saves 60 minutes of cleanup"
- "Reactive decisions cost more time than thoughtful ones"

**Strategy:**
- Start with micro-pauses: 3 breaths before responding
- Build to 60-second pauses for important decisions
- "What's the cost of reacting vs. investing 60 seconds?"

**Challenge 2: "I Feel Awkward Pausing"**

**Reality Check:**
- "Awkwardness is temporary. Benefits are lasting."
- "Leadership isn't about comfort—it's about effectiveness"
- "People respect thoughtful leaders more than reactive ones"

**Strategy:**
- Normalize it: "I need a moment to think about this"
- Use physical anchors: water, bathroom break
- Practice in low-stakes situations first

**Challenge 3: "I Don't Know What I'm Feeling"**

**Reality Check:**
- "Emotional literacy is a skill, not a talent"
- "Start with: mad, sad, glad, scared"
- "Your body always knows—check your physical sensations"

**Strategy:**
- Use the emotion wheel (include visual)
- Notice body sensations: "chest tightness = anxiety"
- "If this feeling had a name, what would it be?"

**Challenge 4: "I Can't Distinguish Facts from Stories"**

**Reality Check:**
- "Our minds are story-making machines"
- "Facts are observable; stories are interpretive"
- "Ask: Would this hold up in court?"

**Strategy:**
- Write it down: separate facts and stories into two columns
- Use the camera test: "What would a video recording show?"
- Look for words like "always," "never," "they"—these signal stories

**Challenge 5: "I Pause But Still Make Bad Decisions"**

**Reality Check:**
- "Pausing doesn't guarantee perfection"
- "It increases probability of wisdom"
- "Progress is in the direction, not the destination"

**Strategy:**
- Review your paused decisions after the fact
- Ask: "What would I do differently?"
- Notice patterns: "When I feel X, I tend to Y"

#### FAQs

**Q: How long should a strategic pause be?**
A: "Start with 60 seconds. Build to 3-5 minutes for major decisions. Even 3 deep breaths (15 seconds) is better than reacting immediately."

**Q: What if people think I'm slow or indecisive?**
A: "Thoughtful leaders are respected, not criticized. Frame it as 'I want to give this the consideration it deserves.' Most people appreciate thoroughness."

**Q: Can I use this for personal decisions, not just work?**
A: "Absolutely. The strategic pause works for any decision: parenting conversations, personal conflicts, financial choices, relationship decisions."

**Q: How long until this becomes natural?**
A: "Most leaders feel a shift within 1-2 weeks of daily practice. It becomes automatic within 4-6 weeks. Systems before willpower—build the habit, trust the process."

**Q: What if I practice this and still fail sometimes?**
A: "You will. Everyone does. The goal isn't perfection—it's increasing your response-ability from 20% to 60% to 80%. Celebrate progress, not perfection."

**Brand Voice:** Empathetic + Encouraging
**Format:** Challenge → Reality Check → Strategy, followed by FAQs
