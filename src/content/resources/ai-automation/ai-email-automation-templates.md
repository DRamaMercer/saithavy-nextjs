## Introduction

Email remains one of the highest ROI marketing channels, but manual email management doesn't scale. These **AI-powered email automation templates** help you deliver the right message to the right person at the right time—without constant manual effort.

**What's inside:**

## Part 1: Email Types & When to Use Them

### Email Automation Hierarchy

```
                    ┌─────────────────┐
                    │  Transactional   │

                    └────────┬────────┘
                             │
        ┌────────────────────┼────────────────────┐
```

### When to Use Each Type

| Type | Purpose | Frequency | Example |
|------|---------|-----------|---------|
| Transactional | Confirm actions | Immediate | Order confirmation |
| Behavioral | Respond to actions | Real-time | Browse abandonment |
| Lifecycle | Guide through journey | Pre-determined | Welcome series |
| Campaign | Broadcast to list | Ad-hoc | Product launch |

## Part 2: Welcome Series Templates

### The 5-Email Welcome Sequence

#### Email 1: Immediate Welcome (Send Immediately)

**Subject:** Welcome to [Community Name]—let's get started

**AI Prompt Template:**
```
Generate a welcome email for new subscribers who just joined [COMMUNITY NAME].

Context:
- Community purpose: [WHAT YOU DO]
- New subscriber joined from: [SOURCE]
- Tone: [WARM / PROFESSIONAL / ENTHUSIASTIC]
- Next step: [PRIMARY CALL-TO-ACTION]

Email structure:
1. Warm welcome + thank you
2. What they can expect from being subscribed
3. One immediate action they should take
4. Reply invitation (build connection)

Keep it under 200 words, scannable, and genuine.
```

**Template:**
```
Hi [First Name],

Welcome to [Community Name]. I'm genuinely glad you're here.

Here's what you can expect:
- [Frequency] emails filled with [type of content]
- No spam—just actionable insights on [topic]
- Unsubscribe anytime (but I hope you'll stay)

📌 Your first step:
[ONE THING: reply with a question, download a resource, etc.]

I personally read every reply. Hit me back with your biggest challenge
right now related to [topic].

Talk soon,
[Your Name]

P.S. If you only read one email from me, make it this one: [LINK TO BEST CONTENT]
```

**AI Prompt for Personalized Recommendations:**
```
Generate personalized product/content recommendations based on subscriber data.

Subscriber data I have:
- [Past purchases/content consumed]
- [Interests indicated]

- [Engagement level]

2. Why it's relevant to them

```

### Segmentation Strategy

| Segment | Criteria | Content Focus | Send Frequency |
|---------|----------|---------------|----------------|
| New Subscribers | Joined < 30 days | Welcome, education | 3x/week → 1x/week |
| Active Buyers | Purchased in last 90 days | Product tips, upsells | 1x/week |
| Leads | Inquired, haven't bought | Case studies, offers | 2x/month |
| Engaged Non-Buyers | Open emails, no purchase | Value content, soft offers | 1x/week |
| At-Risk | No engagement in 30+ days | Re-engagement, best of | 1x/month |
| VIP | High lifetime value | Exclusive content, early access | As appropriate |

## Part 5: AI-Powered Subject Line Generator

### Subject Line Templates by Type

#### Curiosity Gap
```
Generate 5 subject lines using the "curiosity gap" technique for [TOPIC].

Create curiosity by hinting at something valuable without revealing it fully.

Pattern: [Partial information] + [Curiosity element]

Example patterns:
- "The [number] [things] I wish I knew..."
- "Why most [people] get [topic] wrong"
- "[Unexpected thing] about [topic]"
- "The truth about [topic]"

Make them specific to: [CONTEXT]
```

#### Benefit-Driven
```
Generate 5 benefit-driven subject lines for [TOPIC/BENEFIT].

Pattern: [Specific benefit] + [Timeframe/Method]

Example patterns:
- "Get [result] in [timeframe]"
- "How to [result] without [pain point]"
- "The [method] for [result]"
- "[Result]: Here's how"

Focus on this benefit: [SPECIFIC BENEFIT]
```

#### Question-Based
```
Generate 5 question-based subject lines for [TOPIC].

Pattern: [Provocative question] or [Question about pain point]

Example patterns:
- "Are you making this [topic] mistake?"
- "Want [result]?"
- "What if [scenario]?"
- "Why is [topic] so [adjective]?"

Make questions relevant to: [AUDIENCE'S SITUATION]
```

#### Personalized
```
Generate 5 personalized subject lines for [TOPIC].

Personalization elements to use:
- [First Name]
- [Company]
- [Industry]
- [Specific interest]
- [Past behavior]

Pattern: [Personalization] + [Value proposition]

Example patterns:
- "[Name], this [resource] for [topic]"
- "A [topic] tip for [industry]"
- "[Name], your [item] is waiting"
```

### A/B Testing Framework

| Test | Hypothesis | Metric to Track |
|------|------------|-----------------|
| Short vs Long | Short subject lines get more opens | Open rate |
| Question vs Statement | Questions get higher opens | Open rate |
| Personalized vs Generic | Personalization increases clicks | Click rate |
| Urgency vs Value | Value creates more long-term engagement | Unsubscribe rate |

## Part 7: Implementation Checklist

### Pre-Launch Checklist

- [ ] **Email List Health**
  - [ ] Clean email list (remove bounces, inactive subs)
  - [ ] Segment by engagement level
  - [ ] Verify SPF/DKIM records for deliverability

- [ ] **Technical Setup**
  - [ ] Connect email platform to data sources
  - [ ] Set up tracking pixels and analytics
  - [ ] Configure email preferences/unsubscribe page
  - [ ] Set up automation triggers

- [ ] **Content Preparation**
  - [ ] Write all emails in sequence
  - [ ] Create subject line variations for testing
  - [ ] Design email templates (mobile-responsive)
  - [ ] Prepare fallback content for personalization

- [ ] **Testing**
  - [ ] Test all automation triggers
  - [ ] Send test emails to review on multiple devices
  - [ ] Check all links work correctly
  - [ ] Verify unsubscribe process works
  - [ ] Test with small segment before full launch

### Post-Launch Monitoring

- [ ] **Week 1: Daily monitoring**
  - [ ] Open rates by email
  - [ ] Click rates by link
  - [ ] Unsubscribe rates
  - [ ] Spam complaints
  - [ ] Bounce rates

- [ ] **Week 2-4: Weekly reviews**
  - [ ] Compare to benchmarks
  - [ ] A/B test subject lines
  - [ ] Adjust send times based on engagement
  - [ ] Review replies and respond personally

- [ ] **Month 2+: Monthly optimization**
  - [ ] Identify best/worst performing emails
  - [ ] Update based on learnings
  - [ ] Expand successful sequences
  - [ ] Sunset underperforming automations

## Part 8: Best Practices & Pitfalls

### Do's

- **Personalize beyond first name:** Use purchase history, behavior, interests
- **Send consistently but don't spam:** Find your audience's preferred frequency
- **Mobile-optimize everything:** 50%+ of emails are opened on mobile
- **Test everything:** Subject lines, send times, content, CTAs
- **Monitor deliverability:** Keep bounce rate under 2%, spam rate under 0.1%
- **Honor preferences instantly:** Update segments when preferences change
- **Reply to responses:** Build relationships, not just transactions

### Don'ts

- **Don't buy lists:** They damage deliverability and convert poorly
- **Don't ignore unsubscribes:** They're feedback on your relevance
- **Don't send from no-reply:** It says "we don't care about you"
- **Don't hide the unsubscribe:** Frustrated people mark spam instead
- **Don't over-personalize:** It feels creepy when done wrong
- **Don't send and forget:** Monitor and optimize continuously
- **Don't neglect the preview text:** It's prime real estate for opens
