## Part 1: Marketing Prompts

### Brand & Positioning

**Brand Voice Definition**
```
You are helping me define my brand voice. Ask me 10 questions about my business,
values, target audience, and personality. After I answer, synthesize my responses
into a brand voice guide including:
1. Brand personality (3-5 adjectives)
2. Tone guidelines for different situations
3. Words to use and avoid
4. Example phrases in my brand voice
5. A "this is us / this isn't us" comparison table
```

**Value Proposition Generator**
```
Generate 5 compelling value propositions for my business:

Business: [YOUR BUSINESS]
Target customer: [WHO YOU SERVE]
Key benefit: [MAIN BENEFIT]
Differentiation: [WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT]

For each value proposition:
- Make it specific, not generic
- Focus on outcomes, not features
- Use clear, simple language
- Keep it under 15 words
- Explain WHY it works in 1-2 sentences
```

## Part 6: Prompt Engineering Best Practices

### Getting Better Results

**1. Be Specific**
Instead of: "Write marketing copy"
Try: "Write a 150-word Facebook ad for project management software targeting remote team leads. Focus on reducing meeting time. Tone: professional but not corporate."

**2. Provide Context**
Instead of: "Improve this email"
Try: "This email is to cold prospects at 50-200 person companies. Our average deal size is $10k. We sell B2B SaaS. The current email has a 3% response rate."

**3. Specify Format**
Instead of: "Analyze this"
Try: "Analyze this and provide: 1) Three key insights, 2) Two recommendations, 3) One question to investigate further."

**4. Iterate and Refine**
- First prompt: Get something decent
- Follow-up: "Make it more [specific quality]"
- Follow-up: "Rewrite the third paragraph to be more concise"
- Follow-up: "Add a stronger CTA"

**5. Use Examples**
Instead of: "Write in my brand voice"
Try: "Write in this style: [PASTE EXAMPLE OF YOUR WRITING]. Match the tone, sentence structure, and vocabulary."

## Part 7: Quick Reference Templates

### The Perfect Prompt Formula

```
ROLE: Who should the AI be?
CONTEXT: What background is needed?
TASK: What should it do?
CONSTRAINTS: What are the limits?
FORMAT: How should output look?

Example:
"Act as a senior marketing copywriter (ROLE).
We're launching a B2B SaaS tool for project managers (CONTEXT).
Write a cold email sequence targeting VP-level prospects (TASK).
Keep each email under 150 words. Professional but conversational tone (CONSTRAINTS).
Provide 5 emails with subject lines for each (FORMAT)."
```
