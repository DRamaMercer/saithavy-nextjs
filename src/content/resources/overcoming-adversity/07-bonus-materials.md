## Part 2: Reflection Prompts

### Daily Reflection Prompts (Pick One)

**Morning Prompts:**
1. How do I want to feel today? What's one thing I can do to create that feeling?
2. What am I grateful for this morning?
3. What would make today great?
4. What's one challenge I anticipate, and how will I handle it?
5. What affirmation do I need to hear today?
6. What does my body need today?
7. What am I looking forward to?

**Evening Prompts:**
1. What went well today? What am I proud of?
2. What was challenging, and what did I learn?
3. How did I honor my needs today?
4. What emotion was most present today? What was it telling me?
5. What drained my energy today? What replenished it?
6. What do I need to let go of from today?
7. How was I resilient today?

**Weekly Prompts:**
1. What were my dominant emotions this week? What triggered them?
2. When did I feel most like myself this week? When did I feel least like myself?
3. What patterns do I notice in my thoughts, behaviors, or reactions?
4. What did I learn about myself this week?
5. Where was I too hard on myself?
6. What am I ready to release?
7. What do I want to focus on next week?

**Monthly Prompts:**
1. How have I grown in the past month?
2. What resilience tools are working best for me?
3. What still feels challenging?
4. What do I need to practice more?
5. What am I proud of accomplishing or handling?
6. What relationships need attention?
7. What intention do I want to set for next month?

### Deep Reflection Exercises

#### Exercise: The Resilience Timeline

**Map your resilience journey.**

**Step 1: Timeline**
Draw a timeline of your life. Mark significant events:
- High points (achievements, joys, successes)
- Low points (challenges, losses, struggles)
- Turning points (moments that changed you)

**Step 2: Reflect**
For each significant event, answer:
- What happened?
- How did I respond at the time?
- What did I learn?
- How did it make me stronger?
- What wisdom did I gain?

**Step 3: Identify**
- What patterns do you see in how you handle challenges?
- What strengths have you developed?
- What have you survived that you didn't think you could?
- How have you grown?

**Step 4: Honor**
Write yourself a letter acknowledging your resilience:
"Dear [Name], I've been looking at my life timeline, and I want to honor you for..."

---

#### Exercise: The Future Self Visualization

**Connect with your resilient future self.**

**Close your eyes and take 3 deep breaths.**

**Imagine yourself 5 years from now.**
- You've navigated everything you're currently facing
- You've grown stronger and wiser
- You've developed even more resilience

**Ask your future self:**
- What did you learn?
- What are you most proud of?
- What advice do you have for me right now?
- What gave you strength during difficult times?
- What can you tell me about what's possible?

**Write down what you hear.**

**Return to this visualization when you need perspective.**

---

#### Exercise: The Values Clarification

**Resilience is easier when you're connected to your values.**

**List your top 5 values:**
1. _______________________
2. _______________________
3. _______________________
4. _______________________

## Part 5: Audio Script Suggestions

### Guided Box Breathing (5 minutes)

**[Optional: Add gentle background music]**

**Script:**

"Find a comfortable position, either sitting or lying down. Close your eyes or soften your gaze.

Let's begin by taking three normal breaths, just tuning into your body.

In... and out.
In... and out.
In... and out.

Now, let's begin box breathing.

Inhale through your nose for a count of 4...
2, 3, 4.

Hold that breath for a count of 4...
2, 3, 4.

Exhale through your mouth for a count of 4...
2, 3, 4.

Hold empty for a count of 4...
2, 3, 4.

That's one complete box. Let's continue.

Inhale... 2, 3, 4.
Hold... 2, 3, 4.
Exhale... 2, 3, 4.
Hold... 2, 3, 4.

Inhale... imagine breathing in calm, cool energy.
Hold... let it spread through your body.
Exhale... release tension, stress, worry.
Hold... rest in the space you've created.

Inhale... 2, 3, 4.
Hold... 2, 3, 4.
Exhale... 2, 3, 4.
Hold... 2, 3, 4.

Two more cycles.

Inhale... 2, 3, 4.
Hold... 2, 3, 4.
Exhale... 2, 3, 4.
Hold... 2, 3, 4.

Last cycle.

Inhale... 2, 3, 4.
Hold... 2, 3, 4.
Exhale... 2, 3, 4.
Hold... 2, 3, 4.

Return to normal breathing. Notice how you feel.

Take one more deep breath in... and release.

When you're ready, gently open your eyes.

Thank yourself for taking this time."

---

### Guided Body Scan (10 minutes)

**[Optional: Add soft, calming background music]**

**Script:**

"Find a comfortable position, either lying down or seated with your spine straight. Close your eyes.

Take three deep breaths, letting each exhale be longer than the inhale.

Inhale... exhale slowly.
Inhale... exhale completely.
Inhale... exhale and let go.

Bring your attention to your feet.

Notice any sensation: warmth, coolness, tingling, or perhaps no sensation at all. Just notice.

If there's tension, imagine it softening with each exhale. Let your feet relax completely.

Move your attention up to your legs.

Notice your calves, your knees, your thighs. What do you feel?

Breathe into your legs, and imagine relaxation flowing down with each exhale.

Now, bring attention to your hips and pelvis.

This area often stores tension and emotion. Notice what's here.

Allow your hips to relax, to sink, to soften.

Bring attention to your belly.

Notice it rising and falling with your breath.

If there's tightness here, breathe into it. Let your belly be soft.

Move to your chest.

Notice your heartbeat if you can sense it.

If your chest feels tight, imagine breathing space into it.

With each exhale, let your chest expand and soften.

Bring attention to your back.

Lower back, middle back, upper back.

Notice any areas of tension or discomfort.

Breathe warmth into these areas. Imagine your muscles releasing.

Now, your shoulders.

Are they raised toward your ears? Let them drop.

Let your shoulders be heavy, let them sink.

Feel the tension melting away.

Bring attention to your arms.

From shoulders to elbows to wrists to hands.

Notice any sensation. Let your arms feel heavy and relaxed.

Your hands—your palms, your fingers.

Let them be completely soft.

Bring attention to your neck.

Your neck often holds tremendous tension.

Breathe into your neck. Imagine it lengthening, relaxing.

Now, your face and head.

Your jaw—is it clenched? Let your tongue fall away from the roof of your mouth.

Your eyes—let them be soft.

Your forehead—smooth out any furrows.

Let your entire face be relaxed, smooth, peaceful.

Take a moment to scan your whole body.

From your toes to the top of your head.

Notice how you feel compared to when we started.

Take three more deep breaths, sealing in this relaxation.

Inhale... exhale slowly.
Inhale... exhale completely.
Inhale... exhale and let go.

Wiggle your fingers and toes. Gently stretch.

When you're ready, open your eyes.

Carry this body awareness with you."

### 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding (3 minutes)

**[Optional: Record for emergency use]**

**Script:**

"You're feeling overwhelmed. Let's ground you right now.

Look around you. Name 5 things you can see.

[Pause]

I see...
[Name 5 things]

Now, 4 things you can touch.

Reach out and touch 4 things around you.

[Pause]

I feel...
[Name 4 things with their textures]

Now, 3 things you can hear.

Close your eyes and listen.

[Pause]

I hear...
[Name 3 sounds]

Now, 2 things you can smell.

Take a deep breath in through your nose.

[Pause]

I smell...
[Name 2 scents]

Finally, 1 thing you can taste.

Take a sip of water, or notice the taste in your mouth.

[Pause]

I taste...

You're here. You're safe. You're grounded.

Take one more deep breath.

You can handle this."

## Part 6: Quick Reference Guides

### Technique Selection Guide

**Not sure which technique to use? Follow this flowchart.**

**How intense is your stress?**

**Low (1-3/10):**
- Practice box breathing
- Do a quick body scan
- Use cognitive reframing
- Take a short walk

**Medium (4-7/10):**
- Use 5-4-3-2-1 grounding
- Practice extended box breathing (5-10 min)
- Write in your journal
- Reach out to someone

**High (8-10/10):**
- Cold water shock
- Physiological sigh (multiple)
- Call someone from your support network
- Use your crisis plan if needed

---

**Are you more in your head or your body?**

**In your head (racing thoughts):**
- 5-4-3-2-1 grounding
- Cognitive reframing
- Worry dump journaling
- Reality anchor worksheet

**In your body (physical symptoms):**
- Box breathing
- Progressive muscle relaxation
- Physical release (shake-out, movement)
- Body scan meditation

---

**How much time do you have?**

**30 seconds:**
- Physiological sigh
- One box breathing cycle

**2 minutes:**
- Full box breathing (4-8 cycles)
- Quick body shake-out

**5 minutes:**
- 5-4-3-2-1 grounding
- Short body scan
- Quick journaling

**10+ minutes:**
- Extended practices
- Full journaling session
- Long walk or movement
- Comprehensive body scan

### Professional Help

**When to Consider Professional Support:**

- You're feeling overwhelmed for more than 2 weeks
- Your symptoms are interfering with daily life
- You're having thoughts of self-harm
- You're using substances to cope
- You've experienced trauma
- You want personalized guidance

**Types of Professionals:**

**Therapists/Counselors:**
- Licensed Clinical Social Workers (LCSW)
- Licensed Professional Counselors (LPC)
- Psychologists (PhD or PsyD)
- Marriage and Family Therapists (MFT)

**How to Find One:**
- Psychology Today "Find a Therapist" directory
- Your insurance provider's directory
- Ask for recommendations from trusted friends
- Employee Assistance Programs (EAP)

**Coaches:**
- Life coaches, wellness coaches, resilience coaches
- Focus on growth and skill-building
- Not a replacement for therapy when mental health conditions are present

**Support Groups:**
- Peer-led or professionally facilitated
- Specific to issues (grief, addiction, anxiety, etc.)
- Often free or low-cost
- Check NAMI or local mental health organizations
