# Recovery Routine Checklist
## Daily Recovery to Restore Energy, Focus, and Emotional Steadiness

---

**A structured approach to building daily recovery into your life—because sustained performance requires consistent restoration.**

---

## Why Daily Recovery Matters

**Recovery is not optional.** It's the foundation of sustainable performance, emotional health, and resilience.

**Without recovery:**
- Energy depletes and crashes
- Focus fragments and fails
- Emotions become dysregulated
- Burnout becomes inevitable
- Performance declines over time

**With recovery:**
- Energy stabilizes and renews
- Focus sharpens and sustains
- Emotions regulate smoothly
- Resilience becomes your baseline
- Performance improves consistently

**This checklist helps you build recovery as a daily practice, not an occasional luxury.**

---

## How to Use This Checklist

**Daily Use:**
- Complete the morning section to start your day
- Use the midday reset to maintain energy
- Complete the evening section to close your day
- Track your recovery score

**Weekly Review:**
- Review your patterns at week's end
- Celebrate what worked
- Adjust what didn't

**Monthly Optimization:**
- Update your routine based on what you've learned
- Set new recovery goals

## Section 6: Recovery Boosters

### When You Need Extra Recovery

**Use these when you're depleted, stressed, or pushing hard:**

**[ ] The 20-minute power nap**

Rules: Before 3 PM, max 20 minutes, dark room

**[ ] The nature immersion**

30+ minutes outdoors, no phone, notice your surroundings

**[ ] The social connection**

Quality time with someone who energizes you

**[ ] The creative outlet**

30+ minutes of creation (art, music, writing, building)

**[ ] The physical release**

Exercise that gets you sweaty and releases tension

**[ ] The digital detox**

4+ hours without screens, or a full day per week

**[ ] The rest day**

A day with no obligations, only what feels restorative

---

### Recovery Red Flags

**Check in if you're experiencing:**

**Physical signs:**
- [ ] Consistently tired despite sleep
- [ ] Frequent illness
- [ ] Physical tension or pain
- [ ] Digestive issues

**Emotional signs:**
- [ ] Irritability or short temper
- [ ] Emotional numbness
- [ ] Loss of joy in things you usually enjoy
- [ ] Feeling overwhelmed or anxious

**Mental signs:**
- [ ] Difficulty concentrating
- [ ] Memory problems
- [ ] Indecisiveness
- [ ] Negative thinking patterns

**Behavioral signs:**
- [ ] Withdrawing from people
- [ ] Neglecting responsibilities
- [ ] Increased substance use
- [ ] Skipping self-care

**If you checked 3+ in any category, you need a recovery period.**

**Immediate actions:**
1. Reduce non-essential commitments
2. Increase sleep and rest
3. Process emotions (journal, talk, therapy)
4. Seek support if needed

## Section 7: Customize Your Recovery

### Recovery Type Assessment

**What kind of recovery do you need most?**

**Physical Recovery:**
- Signs needed: Physical fatigue, muscle tension, illness
- Best practices: Sleep, movement, massage, saunas, nature

**Mental Recovery:**
- Signs needed: Brain fog, poor focus, indecision
- Best practices: Meditation, time in nature, digital detox, reading

**Emotional Recovery:**
- Signs needed: Emotional volatility, numbness, irritability
- Best practices: Journaling, therapy, talking, crying, creative expression

**Social Recovery:**
- Signs needed: Loneliness, social fatigue, disconnection
- Best practices: Quality time with friends, community, solitude (if socially drained)

**Spiritual Recovery:**
- Signs needed: Loss of meaning, purpose, connection
- Best practices: Meditation, prayer, time in nature, service, reflection
