## Section 1: Immediate Crisis Protocol

### The STOP Method

When crisis hits, your nervous system may flood. Before you can think clearly, you need to regulate.

---

#### **S - STOP What You're Doing**

- Put down what you're holding
- Step away from the situation if possible
- Literally say "STOP" out loud if needed
- Create a pause between stimulus and response

---

#### **T - TAKE 3 Conscious Breaths**

- 4 counts IN through your nose
- 4 counts HOLD
- 6 counts OUT through your mouth
- Feel your feet on the ground

---

#### **O - OBSERVE What's Happening**

- Name 3 emotions you're feeling: _________________
- Notice where you feel tension in your body: _________________
- Acknowledge: "This is hard. And I'm here."

---

#### **P - PROCEED Mindfully**

- Ask: "What do I need right now?"
- Give yourself what you need (water, walk, cry, vent, reach out)
- Only then, move to next steps

---

### Grounding Techniques

**Choose one or practice in order:**

**5-4-3-2-1 METHOD (1-2 minutes)**
- Name 5 things you can SEE
- Name 4 things you can TOUCH
- Name 3 things you can HEAR
- Name 2 things you can SMELL
- Name 1 thing you can TASTE

---

**BOX BREATHING (1-2 minutes)**
- Inhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Exhale for 4 counts
- Hold empty for 4 counts
- Repeat 4 times

---

**BODY SCAN (2-3 minutes)**
- Close your eyes
- Start at the top of your head
- Slowly scan down your body
- Notice sensations without judgment
- Release tension as you go

---

**PHYSICAL GROUNDING**
- Splash cold water on your face
- Hold ice cubes in your hands
- Walk briskly or do 10 jumping jacks
- Wrap yourself in a heavy blanket

## Section 2: Crisis Type Decision Tree

### What Type of Crisis Is This?

---

**TYPE A: IMMEDIATE SAFETY CRISIS**
*(Self-harm, suicide, harm to others, medical emergency)*

**ACTION REQUIRED NOW:**
- [ ] Call 911 or go to emergency room
- [ ] Call crisis hotline (988)
- [ ] Contact your emergency contact
- [ ] Do NOT be alone

**THIS CANNOT WAIT.** Safety first.

---

**TYPE B: SEVERE EMOTIONAL CRISIS**
*(Intense overwhelm, panic attack, unable to function)*

**ACTION REQUIRED NOW:**
- [ ] Use STOP method and grounding
- [ ] Call your support person
- [ ] Call crisis hotline if needed
- [ ] Remove yourself from triggers if possible
- [ ] Focus on getting through the next hour only

**GOAL:** Regulate and stay safe.

---

**TYPE C: ESCALATING SITUATION**
*(Things are getting worse, heading toward crisis)*

**ACTION REQUIRED SOON:**
- [ ] Use grounding techniques
- [ ] Reach out to support system
- [ ] Activate self-care plan
- [ ] Consider professional support
- [ ] Monitor for escalation to Type B

**GOAL:** De-escalate before crisis.

---

**TYPE D: DIFFICULT MOMENT**
*(Hard time, but not crisis)*

**ACTION HELPFUL:**
- [ ] Acknowledge the difficulty
- [ ] Use one grounding technique
- [ ] Reach out if you want support
- [ ] Practice self-compassion
- [ ] This is hard, and you can handle it

**GOAL:** Navigate with care.

## Section 3: Communication Templates

Use these templates when you need to communicate during crisis or difficult times.

---

### Template A: Notifying Family/Close Friends

**Subject/Opening:** I'm going through a hard time

"Hi [name], I wanted to let you know that I'm going through a difficult time right now with [brief description if comfortable: mental health / a personal challenge / a crisis situation].

I wanted to share this with you because [you're important to me / I trust you / I need support].

Right now, what would help me most is [specific request: someone to check in on me / to talk / distraction / just knowing you're there].

I'm [reaching out to a professional / taking steps to get support / working through this].

Thank you for being in my life.

Love,
[your name]"

---

### Template B: Communicating with Workplace

**To Supervisor/HR:**

"Hi [name], I need to share something personal that affects my work. I'm going through a [mental health challenge / difficult situation / health crisis] that requires my attention right now.

I want to be transparent about this while maintaining my professionalism. I may need [time off / flexible schedule / accommodation].

I'm committed to my role and want to ensure my responsibilities are covered. Can we discuss [options / next steps]?

Thank you for understanding."

---

### Template C: Asking for Specific Help

**To Someone You Trust:**

"Hey [name], I'm struggling right now with [situation/feeling].

I know you can't fix this, and I'm not asking you to. But would you be willing to [specific request]:
- Listen for 15 minutes while I talk this out?
- Check in with me tomorrow?
- Help me with [specific task]?
- Just sit with me for a bit?

It would really help me to [feel less alone / get through this moment / not be isolated right now].

No pressure if you can't. I understand."

---

### Template D: Setting Boundaries During Crisis

**Setting Boundaries:**

"[name], I appreciate your [concern / care / reaching out]. Right now, I need to set a boundary to protect my wellbeing.

I need [space / not to talk about this / to limit contact / to focus on my recovery].

This isn't about you—it's about what I need right now to heal. I'll reach out when I'm ready.

Thank you for respecting this boundary."

---

### Template E: Following Up After Crisis

**To Your Support People:**

"Hi [name], I wanted to follow up and thank you for supporting me through my recent crisis.

Your [listening / presence / help] meant a lot to me. I'm currently [feeling better / working on recovery / taking steps to heal].

I wanted to let you know where I'm at and [what I need now / how you can continue to support me / if I need space right now].

Thank you again for being there."
