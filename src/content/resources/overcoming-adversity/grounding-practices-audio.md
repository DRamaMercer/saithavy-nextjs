# Grounding Practices Audio Collection
## Calming Pack for Grounding Your Nervous System

---

**A curated collection of guided practices to settle your nervous system, find your center, and return to the present moment.**

---

## About This Collection

**Format:** 8 guided audio practices (3-12 minutes each)
**Purpose:** To provide immediate tools for nervous system regulation and emotional grounding

**When to use:**
- Feeling anxious, scattered, or overwhelmed
- Before stressful situations
- After difficult experiences
- As part of your daily self-care routine
- To improve sleep quality

---

## Practice Overview

| Practice | Duration | Best For |
|----------|----------|----------|
| 1. 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding | 5 min | Acute anxiety, dissociation |
| 2. Box Breathing | 4 min | Quick regulation, pre-stress |
| 3. Body Scan Relaxation | 10 min | Tension, body awareness |
| 4. Anchoring Visualization | 8 min | Emotional overwhelm |
| 5. Compassionate Breathing | 7 min | Self-criticism, shame |
| 6. Root to Rise | 9 min | Instability, insecurity |
| 7. The Safe Space | 12 min | Deep overwhelm, trauma response |
| 8. Return to Now | 6 min | General distraction, worry |

## Full Scripts for All Practices

### PRACTICE 1: 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding (5 minutes)

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to begin]**

**GUIDE:** Welcome. This practice uses your five senses to bring you fully into the present moment. It's simple, effective, and can be done anywhere.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Find a comfortable position. You can sit or stand. Let your feet make contact with the floor.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Take a deep breath in... and out.

**[PAUSE - 3 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Now, look around you. Name FIVE things you can see.

I'll give you some time.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Maybe it's a lamp on your desk. The color of the wall. A tree outside your window. Your own hands.

Five things you can see.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Now, close your eyes or lower your gaze. Name FOUR things you can feel.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** The fabric of your shirt against your skin. The temperature of the air. Your feet pressing into the floor. The sensation of your own breathing.

Four things you can feel.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Now, listen. Name THREE things you can hear.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** The hum of a refrigerator. Distant traffic. Your own breath. Birds outside. The sound of silence itself.

Three things you can hear.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Now, notice smell. Name TWO things you can smell.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Maybe coffee in the air. Laundry soap. The rain. Or simply, the smell of the room you're in.

Two things you can smell.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Finally, notice taste. Name ONE thing you can taste.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** The lingering taste of your last meal. Or simply the taste of your own mouth. Or take a sip of water and really taste it.

One thing you can taste.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Take a deep breath in... and out.

**[PAUSE - 3 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** You are here. In this room. In this body. In this present moment.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** When you're ready, open your eyes. You are grounded.

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to end]**

### PRACTICE 2: Box Breathing (4 minutes)

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to begin]**

**GUIDE:** This practice uses a simple breathing pattern to regulate your nervous system. It's called box breathing—each breath has four equal parts.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Find a comfortable seat. Let your spine be tall but not rigid. Place your hands wherever feels comfortable.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** We'll breathe in a pattern of four. Four counts in. Four counts hold. Four counts out. Four counts hold.

**[PAUSE - 3 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Let's begin. Inhale... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Hold... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Exhale... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Hold... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Continue with me. Inhale... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Hold... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Exhale... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Hold... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Notice your mind settling. Your body relaxing. Your breath finding its rhythm.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Inhale... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Hold... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Exhale... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Hold... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** One more cycle. Inhale... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Hold... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Exhale... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Hold... two, three, four.

**[PAUSE - 4 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Return to your natural breathing. Notice how you feel. Calmer. Centered. More present.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** You can return to this practice anytime, anywhere. Your breath is always with you.

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to end]**

### PRACTICE 3: Body Scan Relaxation (10 minutes)

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to begin]**

**GUIDE:** This practice will guide you through a full body scan, releasing tension and bringing awareness to each part of your body.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Find a comfortable position—lying down is ideal, but seated works too. Let your body settle.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Take a deep breath in... and a long exhale out. Letting go of the day so far.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Bring your attention to the very top of your head. Notice any sensations there. Tightness, heat, coolness, or perhaps nothing at all.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Imagine your breath flowing into the top of your head. With each exhale, feel that area softening, relaxing, letting go.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Now, move your attention to your forehead. Notice any tension held here. Gently smooth your brow. Let your forehead become wide and smooth.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Move to your eyes. Notice if they're clenched or strained. Allow them to soften, maybe even close if they haven't already.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Your jaw. Often we hold so much tension here. Unclench your teeth. Let your jaw drop slightly. Your tongue can rest away from the roof of your mouth.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Your neck and throat. Let these areas release. Your head can feel supported, not held up by tension.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Shoulders. Notice if they're raised, tense. Let them drop. Heavy. Relaxed. melting down away from your ears.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Arms and hands. Your upper arms, elbows, forearms, wrists, palms, fingers. Notice any gripping, holding. Let your fingers curl naturally. Let your hands rest heavily.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Chest and upper back. With each breath, feel expansion here. No forcing. Just allowing. Your breath moving freely.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Belly. Often we hold tension here without realizing it. Let your belly be soft. Let it rise and fall naturally with your breath.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Lower back. Let it release into whatever supports you. No holding. Just resting.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Hips and pelvis. Let these heavy areas settle. Relaxation spreading through your hips.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Thighs. Large muscles here. Let them release completely. Heavy, relaxed.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Calves and shins. Letting go of tension carried through walking and standing.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Ankles and feet. Notice any tension here. Let your feet relax completely. Toes unfurling.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Your entire body. Relaxed. Supported. Safe. Here.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Rest here for a moment. Just breathing. Just being.

**[LONG PAUSE - 20 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** When you're ready, take a deep breath in... and out. Gently wiggle your fingers and toes. Stretch if it feels good.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Slowly return to full awareness. Taking this sense of relaxation with you.

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to end]**

### PRACTICE 4: Anchoring Visualization (8 minutes)

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to begin]**

**GUIDE:** This practice uses visualization to create an internal anchor—a place within you that is always steady, always available.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Find a comfortable position. Close your eyes if you wish. Take three deep breaths.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** I want you to imagine a place where you feel completely safe. Completely grounded. A place where nothing can disturb you.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** This might be a place you've been. A beach you've walked. A mountain trail. A room in a home you love. Or it might be imaginary—a meadow, a forest, a sanctuary you create in your mind.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** See this place clearly in your mind's eye. What do you see around you?

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Notice the colors. The light. Is it bright or soft? Morning or evening? Sunny or cloudy?

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** What sounds do you hear in this place? Wind? Water? Birds? Or is it quiet, a peaceful silence?

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** What do you feel? The temperature. The texture of the ground beneath you. The air on your skin.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Notice how you feel in this place. Safe. Grounded. Completely at home in yourself.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Now, I want you to find a physical anchor in this place. A tree, a rock, a bench, a spot on the ground. Something solid, steady, immovable.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** In your mind, walk over to this anchor. Place your hand on it. Feel its solidity. Its steadiness. It has been here before you arrived. It will be here after you leave.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** This anchor is a part of this place. And this place is always within you. You can return here anytime. The anchor will always be waiting.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Take a moment to really feel this. This place, this anchor, are always available. You carry them within you.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Now, I want you to create a gesture—a physical movement you can make to connect with this anchor.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Maybe it's placing your hand on your heart. Or pressing your palms together. Or touching a specific finger to your thumb. Choose a gesture that feels right.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Make this gesture now, as you visualize your anchor. Connect the gesture to the feeling of groundedness, safety, steadiness.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** This gesture is your bridge. Anytime you need to return to this place, anywhere in your daily life, you can make this gesture and immediately reconnect.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Repeat this gesture a few times. Strengthening the connection.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** This anchor is within you. This place is within you. You can return anytime.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Take a deep breath. When you're ready, gently wiggle your fingers and toes. Open your eyes when you're ready.

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to end]**

### PRACTICE 5: Compassionate Breathing (7 minutes)

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to begin]**

**GUIDE:** This practice is for moments of self-criticism, shame, or harshness toward yourself. It's about meeting yourself with kindness instead.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Find a comfortable seat. Place one or both hands over your heart. Let your touch be gentle.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Take a moment to notice how you're feeling toward yourself right now. Without judgment. Just noticing.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Notice the voice in your head. Is it kind? Critical? Neutral? Rushing?

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Whatever you find is okay. This is a practice, not a performance.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Now, I want you to imagine a version of yourself who is deeply compassionate. Who knows all of you—your mistakes, your flaws, your struggles—and still looks at you with complete kindness.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** What does this compassionate self say to you right now?

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Maybe it says: "It's okay to feel this way. You're doing your best. This is hard, and you're handling it."

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** As you breathe in, imagine breathing in compassion for yourself.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Inhaling compassion.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** As you breathe out, imagine breathing out harshness, criticism, the demand to be different than you are.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Exhaling release.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Continue. Breathing in compassion. Breathing out harshness.

**[REPEAT 5 TIMES with PAUSES]**

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Feel the warmth of your hand on your heart. This is your own touch. Your own kindness. You can give this to yourself anytime.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Repeat after me silently: "May I be kind to myself."

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** "May I forgive myself for not being perfect."

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** "May I treat myself with the same kindness I'd offer a friend."

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** "May I remember that I'm doing the best I can."

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Your hand on your heart is a reminder. You are here. You are worthy of your own kindness.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Take one more deep breath. Inhaling compassion. Exhaling anything that doesn't serve you.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** When you're ready, lower your hands. Open your eyes. Carry this kindness with you.

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to end]**

### PRACTICE 6: Root to Rise (9 minutes)

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to begin]**

**GUIDE:** This practice is about finding your foundation—your root—so that you can rise with stability and strength.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Stand up. Find a place where you have a little space. Feet about hip-width apart.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Close your eyes if you feel stable. Or keep a soft gaze on something unmoving.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Bring all your attention to your feet. Notice where they make contact with the floor.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Feel your heels. The balls of your feet. Your toes. All the points of contact.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Imagine roots growing from the soles of your feet. Reaching down into the earth beneath you. Deeper and deeper.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** These roots are your connection to stability. To the earth. To something solid and unshakeable.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Feel yourself anchored. Not stuck—but grounded. Supported from below.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Now, notice your legs. Strong. Capable of holding you. Of carrying you through your life.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Imagine energy rising from those roots. Up through your legs. Into your pelvis. Your torso.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Your spine lengthening gently from that rooted base. Taller, but not rigid. Flexible, like a tree.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Your shoulders dropping away from your ears. Your arms hanging heavy and relaxed.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Your head lifting slightly, as if suspended by a string from above. Chin slightly tucked.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** You are rooted below. Rising above. Connected to earth and sky.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Feel your stability. You cannot be easily knocked over. You have roots.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** From this place of stability, you can handle whatever comes. You can bend without breaking. You can weather storms.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Stand here for a moment. Rooted. Rising. Present in your body.

**[LONG PAUSE - 20 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** When you're ready, take a deep breath. Open your eyes. Notice how you feel. More stable. More present. More yourself.

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to end]**

### PRACTICE 7: The Safe Space (12 minutes)

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to begin]**

**GUIDE:** This is a longer practice for times when you need deeper safety and containment. It's about creating an internal sanctuary that is always yours.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Find a comfortable position where you can fully relax. Lying down is ideal for this practice.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Take a few deep breaths. Letting your body settle. Letting your mind begin to quiet.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** I'm going to guide you to create or visit a safe space in your mind. This space is completely yours. You design it. You control it. Nothing can enter here without your permission.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Begin by visualizing a boundary. A wall, a fence, a circle of light, a river. Something that marks the edge of your safe space. Something that creates a clear container of safety.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** See this boundary clearly. Know that nothing harmful can cross it. You are completely safe within.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Now, inside this boundary, what do you see? Let yourself imagine a space that feels perfectly safe. Perfectly comforting.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Is this space indoors or outdoors? Is it a room you know, a place from nature, or something entirely your creation?

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Look around this space. What do you see? Colors, objects, light, textures. Make it real in your mind.

**[PAUSE - 20 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** What do you hear in this space? Or is it deliciously quiet?

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** What do you feel? The temperature, the textures, the air. Make yourself comfortable in this space. Is there somewhere to sit? To lie down?

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Notice how you feel here. Completely safe. Completely held. Completely free to be yourself.

**[PAUSE - 20 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** In this safe space, there is nothing you need to do. No one you need to please. No way you need to be. You can just be.

**[LONG PAUSE - 30 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Spend some time here. What does this safe space need to be perfect for you? Add anything you like. Remove anything that doesn't belong. This space responds to you.

**[LONG PAUSE - 30 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Notice that you feel calmer just being here. Your nervous system settling. Your mind quieting. This space is within you. You can return anytime.

**[LONG PAUSE - 30 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Before we close, I want you to create a symbol or image that represents this space. Something you can visualize quickly to return here.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** It might be an image of the space itself. Or an object within it. Or a color, a shape, a feeling.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Hold this symbol in your mind. Connect it to the feeling of safety, calm, being home in yourself.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** This symbol is your key. Anytime you need to return to this safe space, simply visualize this symbol.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Know that this space is always here. Waiting. Safe. Yours.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Take a deep breath. When you're ready, begin to bring your awareness back to the room around you.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Wiggle your fingers and toes. Stretch if it feels good.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Open your eyes. Taking the feeling of safety with you.

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to end]**

### PRACTICE 8: Return to Now (6 minutes)

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to begin]**

**GUIDE:** This is a quick practice for when you're caught in thoughts about the past or worries about the future. It's about returning to now.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Find a comfortable position. Let your body be still.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Take a deep breath in... and out.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Notice where your mind is right now. Is it in the past? Replaying conversations, reliving moments?

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Is it in the future? Planning, worrying, imagining scenarios?

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Notice that neither of those places exist right now. The past is gone. The future hasn't arrived.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** What exists is now. This moment.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Bring your attention to your breath. Your breath only happens now.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Feel the sensation of breathing. The air entering. The air leaving. You are here, breathing.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Now, bring your attention to your body. Your body only exists now.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Feel your weight. The contact of your body with the chair or floor. You are here, in your body.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Now, bring your attention to your senses. Your senses only receive information from now.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** What do you hear right now? What do you feel? What do you see?

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** This moment. This breath. This body. These senses. This is now.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** When your mind drifts to past or future—and it will—gently return to now.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** You don't need to fight your thoughts. Just notice them. "Ah, thinking." Then return to now.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Your breath. Your body. Your senses. Now.

**[LONG PAUSE - 30 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** There is nowhere else you need to be. No other time you need to be in.

**[PAUSE - 10 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Just here. Just now. Just this.

**[PAUSE - 15 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** Take one more deep breath. In... and out.

**[PAUSE - 5 seconds]**

**GUIDE:** When you're ready, open your eyes. Here you are.

**[SOUND: Gentle chime to end]**
