# Overcoming Burnout Recovery Plan
## Step-by-Step Roadmap to Recover and Prevent Burnout

---

**A structured approach to recovering from burnout and building a sustainable life that supports your wellbeing.**

---

## Understanding Burnout

**Burnout is not a character flaw.** It's a state of physical, emotional, and mental exhaustion caused by prolonged and excessive stress.

### The Three Dimensions of burnout:

1. **Emotional Exhaustion** – Feeling drained, depleted, unable to cope
2. **Depersonalization** – Detachment, cynicism, treating yourself/others as objects
3. **Reduced Accomplishment** – Feeling ineffective, like nothing you do matters

### Is This Burnout?

**Common signs:**
- Feeling tired even after sleeping
- Loss of motivation and passion
- Cynical or critical attitude
- Difficulty concentrating
- Physical symptoms (headaches, digestive issues, lowered immunity)
- Withdrawal from responsibilities or people
- Using food, drugs, or alcohol to cope
- Feeling like nothing matters or makes a difference

**If you're experiencing these symptoms, this plan is for you.**

## Phase 1: STOP & REST (Weeks 1-2)

### The Triage Protocol

**If you're in active burnout, your first job is to STOP.**

**Immediate Actions:**

**Day 1-3: Radical Rest**
- [ ] Take sick leave if possible
- [ ] Cancel all non-essential commitments
- [ ] Set an auto-responder: "I'm taking time for health and will respond when I return."
- [ ] Tell 1-3 people: "I'm recovering from burnout and need space."
- [ ] Sleep as much as your body wants
- [ ] Do nothing without "should" attached

**Day 4-7: Basic Care Only**
- [ ] Focus on: sleep, food, water, basic hygiene
- [ ] No productivity goals, no self-improvement
- [ ] Gentle movement only (walking, stretching)
- [ ] Low-stimulation activities (nature, simple movies, comfort reading)
- [ ] Allow emotions to surface without fixing

**Week 2: Stabilization**
- [ ] Establish a loose rhythm (sleep/wake times)
- [ ] Add one restorative practice per day
- [ ] Begin processing: "What led to this point?"
- [ ] Identify your core support people

---

### The Permission Package

**You are allowed to:**
- Rest without earning it
- Say no to everything
- Be unproductive for weeks
- Not know what's next
- Feel relieved to have stopped
- Be angry at what caused this
- Take sick days for mental health

**You are NOT allowed to:**
- Shame yourself for resting
- Compare your recovery to others'
- Rush back to "normal"
- Make major life decisions (yet)
- Ignore medical help if needed

---

### Basic Daily Structure (For Recovery)

**Morning:**
- Wake up naturally (no alarm if possible)
- Drink water
- Eat something nourishing
- Gentle movement if it feels good

**Midday:**
- Rest or nap
- Simple meal
- Low-stimulation activity

**Evening:**
- Wind down early
- Comforting activity
- Sleep hygiene routine

**That's it.** This is enough for weeks 1-2.

## Phase 3: REBUILD SLOWLY (Weeks 5-8)

### The Rebuilding Protocol

**Week 5: Add ONE thing**
- Introduce ONE meaningful activity back into your week
- Choose something energizing, not draining
- Notice how it feels
- If it drains you, remove it

**Week 6: Add TWO things**
- Build on Week 5
- Add a second gentle activity
- Maintain rest as the foundation

**Week 7: Add social connection**
- Reach out to ONE person
- Keep it low-pressure
- Energy-conserving (short calls, quiet meetups)

**Week 8: Assess and adjust**
- How is your energy?
- What's working?
- What's too much?
- Plan Phase 4 based on what you've learned

## Phase 4: SUSTAIN & PREVENT (Weeks 9-12+)

### The Prevention Framework

**Build a life that doesn't create burnout:**

**1. Workload Management**
- Realistic deadlines and expectations
- Regular breaks and rest periods
- Delegation and support systems
- Clear boundaries around availability

**2. Energy Management**
- Work with your natural rhythms
- Regular recovery periods
- Physical movement and exercise
- Sleep as non-negotiable

**3. Emotional Management**
- Process emotions regularly (don't accumulate)
- Seek support when needed
- Practice self-compassion
- Maintain therapy or coaching if beneficial

**4. Meaning and Values**
- Regular connection to purpose
- Work aligned with values
- Relationships that nourish
- Growth and learning opportunities

## Daily Recovery Practices

### The 3-Breathe Reset

**Use throughout the day:**
- Inhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Exhale for 6 counts
- Repeat 3 times

**Do this:**
- Before responding to stressful emails
- When transitioning between tasks
- When feeling overwhelmed
- Before making decisions

---

### The Energy Check-In

**3x daily, ask yourself:**

| Check-in | Questions |
|----------|-----------|
| **Morning** | What's my energy level? What's realistic for today? What do I need to feel supported? |
| **Midday** | How am I really? What can I release from today's plan? What does my body need now? |
| **Evening** | Where did I overextend? What went well? What am I carrying that I can release? |

---

### The Good Enough Practice

**Perfectionism fuels burnout. Practice "good enough":**

| Area | Perfect | Good Enough |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Work | Everything 100% | Important things 80%, rest 60% |
| Email | Instant responses | Respond within 24 hours |
| House | Always tidy | Functional is fine |
| Social | Always available | When I have capacity |
| Exercise | Daily intense | 3x week, whatever feels good |

**This week, practice good enough in one area.**

## Support Resources

### When to Seek Professional Help

**If you're experiencing:**
- Depression symptoms for more than 2 weeks
- Thoughts of self-harm
- Inability to function in daily life
- Severe anxiety or panic
- Physical symptoms from stress

**Reach out to:**
- Mental health professional (therapist, psychiatrist)
- Primary care physician
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP)
- Crisis services if needed

**Helplines:**
- **National Suicide Prevention Lifeline:** 988 (US)
- **Crisis Text Line:** Text HOME to 741741
- **International:** https://findahelpline.com
