# Module 2: Resilience Self-Assessment Quiz

## Discover Your Resilience Profile

Welcome to your personal resilience assessment. This quiz is designed to help you understand your current resilience strengths and identify areas for growth.

**Important:**
- Answer honestly—there are no right or wrong answers
- This is for YOUR awareness, not judgment
- Your baseline is just that—a starting point
- Resilience can be built and strengthened at any point

**Time required:** 10-15 minutes

---

## Instructions

For each statement, rate how strongly you agree on a scale of 1-5:

**1 = Strongly Disagree**
**2 = Disagree**
**3 = Neutral**
**4 = Agree**
**5 = Strongly Agree**

Answer based on how you've felt and acted **over the past month**, not how you want to be or how you've been in the distant past.

## Understanding Your Results

### Overall Resilience Score Interpretation

**154-175: Exceptional Resilience**
You have built strong resilience across multiple domains. You likely navigate challenges with relative ease and have effective tools for managing stress. Continue refining your skills and consider supporting others in their resilience journey.

**131-153: Strong Resilience**
You have solid resilience foundations with room for growth in specific areas. You generally handle stress well but may struggle during particularly challenging periods. Focus on strengthening your lower-scoring categories.

**108-130: Moderate Resilience**
You have some resilience skills but may feel inconsistent in your ability to handle stress. You likely navigate everyday challenges well but may feel overwhelmed by significant stressors. This is a great starting point for intentional skill-building.

**85-107: Developing Resilience**
You're in the early stages of building resilience. You may frequently feel overwhelmed and unsure how to cope. The good news: resilience is learnable, and you're about to gain powerful tools.

**Below 85: Resilience Building Needed**
You're currently facing significant challenges that may be impacting your well-being. Consider working with a mental health professional who can provide personalized support as you build your resilience toolkit.

## Important Reminders

### This Score Is Not a Judgment

Your score doesn't determine your worth. It's simply data about where you are right now.

### Resilience Is Dynamic

Your score will fluctuate based on life circumstances, stress levels, and current challenges. This is normal.

### All Scores Are Starting Points

Whether you scored 35 or 175, there's always room to grow. The goal isn't perfection—it's progress.

### You're Not Alone

If you scored low in multiple areas and feel overwhelmed, consider:
- Working with a therapist or counselor
- Joining a support group
- Enlisting a resilience coach
- Reaching out to trusted friends

**Asking for help IS resilience.**
