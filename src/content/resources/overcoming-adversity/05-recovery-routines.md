# Module 5: Recovery Routines

## Daily and Weekly Templates for Sustainable Wellness

**Resilience isn't built in a day.** It's built in small, consistent practices repeated over time.

But here's the challenge: knowing what to do isn't enough. You need systems that make wellness automatic.

**That's what recovery routines do.**

They transform effort into habit. They turn "I should" into "I just do." They make resilience your default setting, not something you have to fight for.

**In this module, you'll create:**
- A morning routine that starts your day with intention
- An evening routine that ensures restorative rest
- A weekly check-in that prevents burnout
- A personalized system that actually fits YOUR life

Let's build routines that stick.

---

## Part 1: Morning Recovery Ritual

### Why Your Morning Matters

How you start your morning sets the tone for your entire day.

Start rushed and reactive → You'll likely feel that way all day
Start intentional and grounded → You'll handle challenges better

**The goal isn't a perfect 2-hour morning routine.** The goal is a intentional 15-30 minutes that:
- Regulates your nervous system before the day's stress hits
- Connects you to your body and mind
- Sets clear intentions for how you want to feel and act
- Builds momentum before demands crowd in

---

### The 15-Minute Morning Foundation

**If you only do one thing from this toolkit, make it this.**

**Minute 1-3: Intentional Awakening**
- Before checking your phone (seriously, don't check it first)
- Take 3 deep breaths: In for 4, out for 6
- Ask yourself: "How do I want to feel today?"
- Choose one word: Calm? Focused? Confident? Present?
- Set one intention: "Today, I choose to stay calm when challenges come"

**Minute 4-7: Body Connection**
- Drink a glass of water (hydrate before caffeine)
- Do 2 minutes of movement:
  - Stretch your arms overhead
  - Roll your neck and shoulders
  - Twist your torso side to side
  - Touch your toes
- Notice how your body feels this morning

**Minute 8-12: Mental Preparation**
- Write down your top 3 priorities for the day
- Not your to-do list—just the 3 things that matter most
- Identify one potential stressor: "What might be challenging today?"
- Prepare your response: "If that happens, I'll ________"
- Remind yourself: "I have tools to handle whatever comes"

**Minute 13-15: Gratitude Anchor**
- Write 3 things you're grateful for
- They can be tiny: "Coffee," "My bed," "The sun coming out"
- This trains your brain to notice the good before the bad
- End with your affirmation: "I am resilient. I can handle today."

**Total: 15 minutes that transform how you experience your entire day.**

---

### The 30-Minute Morning Expansion

**If you have more time and want deeper practice:**

**Minute 1-15: The Foundation**
- Do everything from the 15-minute version above

**Minute 16-20: Box Breathing**
- Practice 5 minutes of box breathing
- Or the body scan meditation
- Regulate your nervous system for the day ahead

**Minute 21-25: Movement or Mindfulness**
- Option A: 5 minutes of exercise (yoga, walk, calisthenics)
- Option B: 5 minutes of meditation
- Option C: 5 minutes of journaling
- Choose based on what you need most

**Minute 26-30: Visualization**
- Close your eyes and visualize your day going well
- See yourself handling challenges with grace
- Feel how you want to feel: confident, calm, capable
- Carry that feeling into your day

### Making Your Morning Ritual Stick

**Challenge #1: "I don't have time"**
**Solution:** You don't have time NOT to do this. 15 minutes of preparation saves hours of reactive, stressed time. Start with 10 minutes if 15 feels impossible.

**Challenge #2: "I keep forgetting"**
**Solution:** Link it to an existing habit. "After I pour my coffee, I will do my morning ritual." Put your journal where you'll see it. Set a phone reminder.

**Challenge #3: "I'm not a morning person"**
**Solution:** You don't have to be. Do this ritual after your first cup of coffee or shower. Just don't check your phone first.

**Challenge #4: "My schedule is unpredictable"**
**Solution:** Create a 10-minute version and a 20-minute version. Do the shorter one on busy days, longer on calm days. Consistency matters more than perfection.

**Challenge #5: "I try for a week then stop"**
**Solution:** Never miss twice. If you miss one morning, get back on track immediately. Also, track your streak—don't break the chain.

## Part 2: Evening Recovery Ritual

### Why Your Evening Matters

How you end your day affects:
- How well you sleep
- how you feel when you wake up
- How much emotional residue you carry into tomorrow
- Your body's ability to repair and restore

**A good evening ritual helps you:**
- Process the day's events
- Release accumulated stress
- Transition from doing to being
- Prepare your mind for restful sleep

---

### The 20-Minute Evening Wind-Down

**Minute 1-5: Transition Ritual**
- Create a clear boundary between "active day" and "rest evening"
- Options:
  - Change out of work clothes
  - Wash your face
  - Take a short walk outside
  - Light a candle or dim the lights
- Signal to your brain: "Active time is over"

**Minute 6-10: The Daily Review**
Answer these questions in your journal:

**1. What went well today?**
- Even small things: "I handled that email well," "I drank enough water"
- Celebrate your wins, no matter how tiny

**2. What was challenging?**
- Acknowledge without judgment
- "I felt overwhelmed in that meeting"
- "I snapped at my partner"

**3. What did I learn about myself?**
- "I need to schedule breaks"
- "I handle stress better when I've eaten"
- "I'm stronger than I thought"

**4. What do I need tonight?**
- Rest? Connection? Solitude? Movement?
- Honor that need

**Minute 11-15: Processing**
Choose one:
- **Option A:** Box breathing (4-8 cycles)
- **Option B:** 5-minute body scan
- **Option C:** Brain dump (write everything on your mind)
- **Option D:** Worry release (write tomorrow's worries, leave them on paper)

**Goal:** Release anything you're still holding from the day

**Minute 16-20: Tomorrow Preview**
- Write your top 3 priorities for tomorrow
- Set out anything you'll need (clothes, lunch, materials)
- End with a positive thought:
  - "I've handled today. I'll handle tomorrow too."
  - "I did my best. Tomorrow I'll do my best again."
  - "I trust myself to figure it out."

---

### The Sleep-Optimized Evening

**If you struggle with sleep, add these elements:**

**1 Hour Before Bed: Digital Sunset**
- Turn off screens (phone, computer, TV)
- Blue light disrupts melatonin production
- Use this time for reading, conversation, or relaxation

**30 Minutes Before Bed: Sleep Preparation**
- Dim the lights in your home
- Do something calming (not work, not stressful news)
- Options: stretch, read fiction, listen to calming music, gentle conversation

**Right Before Bed: The Sleep Ritual**
1. **Your evening hygiene:** brush teeth, wash face, etc.
2. **Gratitude:** Write 3 things from today (no matter how hard the day was)
3. **Release:** Box breathing (4-4-4-4 for 4 cycles)
4. **Intention:** "Tonight, I rest deeply. Tomorrow, I'll handle whatever comes."
5. **Sleep:** When your head hits the pillow, don't think. Just breathe and let go

**During the Night: If You Wake Up**
- Don't check the time (it creates anxiety)
- Don't look at your phone
- Use 4-7-8 breathing if you can't fall back asleep
- If you're awake after 20 minutes, get up and do something boring (read a dull book) until you're tired

## Part 3: Weekly Resilience Check-In

### Why You Need a Weekly Practice

Daily routines keep you grounded. **Weekly check-ins help you grow.**

They let you:
- Zoom out and see patterns
- Celebrate progress you might miss day-to-day
- Course-correct before problems become crises
- Plan intentional growth for the week ahead

**This is your strategic planning meeting with yourself.**

---

### The Sunday Session (30 minutes)

**Pick a consistent time (Sunday evening works well for many).**

**Minute 1-5: Set the Stage**
- Quiet space
- Journal or notebook
- Your beverage of choice
- Maybe calming music
- Take 3 deep breaths to center yourself

**Minute 6-10: Emotional Weather Report**
- What was your dominant emotion this week?
- What triggered it most often?
- How well did you regulate it?
- What emotions were easiest to handle? Hardest?

**Minute 11-15: Resilience Wins**
- When did you use your tools?
- What worked really well?
- What are you proud of?
- What did you learn?

**Minute 16-20: Growth Edges**
- Where did you get triggered or overwhelmed?
- What patterns do you notice? (same situations, same people, same thoughts)
- What do you want to do differently next week?

**Minute 21-25: Energy Audit**
Rate each area (1-10):
- Physical energy (sleep, movement, nutrition): _____
- Mental energy (focus, clarity, decisions): _____
- Emotional energy (mood, regulation, relationships): _____
- Spiritual energy (purpose, meaning, values): _____

For any area below 7:
- What drained it?
- What replenishes it?
- What will you do differently next week?

**Minute 26-30: Next Week's Focus**
Based on everything you've reviewed:
- Pick ONE focus area for next week
- Just one. Not five. One.
- Examples:
  - "I'm going to practice box breathing every morning"
  - "I'm going to use cognitive reframing when I feel anxious"
  - "I'm going to reach out to a friend when I feel isolated"
  - "I'm going to prioritize sleep (7-8 hours nightly)"

**Write your focus and put it somewhere visible.**

---

### Monthly Deep Dive (60 minutes)

**Once a month, do a more thorough review:**

**1. Review Your Resilience Assessment**
- Retake the quiz from Module 2
- Compare with your previous scores
- Celebrate growth
- Identify new focus areas

**2. Review Your Goals**
- What goals did you set last month?
- Which did you accomplish?
- Which fell by the wayside?
- What did you learn about yourself?

**3. Honor Your Journey**
- What was hard this month?
- What are you proud of surviving/handling?
- How have you grown?
- Write yourself a compassionate letter acknowledging your journey

**4. Set Next Month's Intentions**
- Not a massive to-do list
- 2-3 meaningful intentions
- What do you want to focus on?
- What do you want to let go of?
- What support do you need?

## Part 4: Building Habits That Stick

### The Science of Habit Formation

Research shows it takes an average of 66 days to form a habit—but it varies widely from person to person.

**The good news:** You don't have to be perfect. You just have to be consistent.

**The 4 Laws of Behavior Change (from James Clear):**

---

### 1. Make It OBVIOUS

**Visual Cues:**
- Put your journal on your pillow (so you can't miss it)
- Set your clothes out the night before for morning movement
- Put a note on your bathroom mirror with your intention
- Use a habit tracker (see bonus materials)

**Environmental Design:**
- Create a "wellness corner" in your home with your journal, meditation cushion, etc.
- Remove temptations (phone out of the bedroom)
- Add friction to bad habits, remove friction from good ones

**Implementation Intentions:**
- "I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION]"
- Example: "I will do my morning ritual at 7:00am in my kitchen"

**Habit Stacking:**
- "After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]"
- Example: "After I pour my coffee, I will do 3 minutes of box breathing"

---

### 2. Make It ATTRACTIVE

**Pair with Things You Enjoy:**
- Morning ritual + favorite coffee
- Evening journaling + herbal tea
- Body scan + comfortable clothes and calming music
- Weekly check-in + favorite treat

**Join a Culture:**
- Find a community practicing similar habits
- Share your journey with friends
- Create an accountability partnership

**Reframe Your Identity:**
- "I'm the kind of person who takes care of themselves"
- "I'm someone who prioritizes mental wellness"
- "I'm a resilient person"

---

### 3. Make It EASY

**Start Ridiculously Small:**
- Can't do 15 minutes? Do 5.
- Can't do 5? Do 2.
- Can't do 2? Do 30 seconds.
- Build from there once the habit is established

**The 2-Minute Rule:**
- When you start a new habit, it should take less than 2 minutes
- "Read before bed" → "Read one page"
- "Meditate daily" → "Meditate for 2 minutes"
- Once established, you can optimize

**Reduce Friction:**
- Prep everything in advance
- Keep supplies visible and accessible
- Automate what you can (reminders, playlists)

**One-Habit-at-a-Time:**
- Don't change everything at once
- Master one habit, then add another
- This is why we recommend starting with morning OR evening ritual, not both simultaneously

---

### 4. Make It SATISFYING

**Immediate Rewards:**
- Check off your habit tracker (satisfying checkmark!)
- Give yourself verbal praise ("Good job!")
- Do a little celebration when you finish

**Track Your Streaks:**
- "Don't break the chain"
- Use a habit tracker app or calendar
- Watch your streak grow—it becomes motivating

**Celebrate Milestones:**
- 7 days = one week of consistency!
- 30 days = real progress!
- 66 days = it's becoming automatic!

**Share Your Progress:**
- Tell a friend
- Post about it (if you're comfortable)
- External accountability helps

---

### The Never-Miss-Twice Rule

**You will miss days.** It's inevitable.

**The rule:** Never miss twice.

**If you miss one day:**
- Don't beat yourself up
- Get back on track immediately
- One miss is an accident. Two misses is the start of a new habit (a bad one)

**Example:**
- Monday: Do morning ritual ✓
- Tuesday: Sleep through alarm, miss ritual ✗
- Wednesday: DO IT NO MATTER WHAT (don't let two misses happen)

## Troubleshooting Common Challenges

### "I'm too busy in the mornings"

**Solution:** Your morning ritual doesn't need to be long. Even 5 minutes of:
- 3 deep breaths
- Set one intention
- Write one priority

That's it. 5 minutes. You're not too busy for 5 minutes.

---

### "I'm too tired at night"

**Solution:** Your evening ritual can be as simple as:
- 3 deep breaths
- Write: "What went well today?"
- Set out one thing for tomorrow

That's 3 minutes. You're not too tired for 3 minutes.

---

### "My schedule varies too much"

**Solution:** Create two versions:
- **Full version:** For normal days (15-20 minutes)
- **Minimum version:** For chaotic days (5 minutes)

Do the minimum version on hard days. That's still better than nothing.

---

### "I keep forgetting"

**Solutions:**
- Set a recurring phone alarm
- Put your journal on your pillow (evening) or coffee maker (morning)
- Link it to something you already do (habit stacking)
- Use a habit tracker app
- Tell someone you're doing it (external accountability)

---

### "I do it for a week then stop"

**Solutions:**
- Start smaller (you tried to do too much too soon)
- Focus on ONE habit at a time (morning OR evening, not both)
- Use the "never miss twice" rule (if you miss one day, get back on track immediately)
- Track your streak (don't break the chain)
- Find an accountability partner
