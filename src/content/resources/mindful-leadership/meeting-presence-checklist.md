# Meeting Presence Checklist

**Improve Focus, Listening, and Facilitation in Every Meeting**

---

## Introduction: Why Meeting Presence Matters

**The average professional spends 23 hours per week in meetings.** That's more than half your work life. Yet most meetings are characterized by:

- Partial attention (laptops open, phones present, minds elsewhere)
- Reactive participation (speaking without thinking)
- Dominated by a few voices (others never fully engage)
- Poor listening (waiting to talk rather than understanding)

**Meeting presence** is the antidote. When you bring full presence to meetings:

- **You make better decisions** — Full awareness produces higher-quality thinking
- **You build stronger relationships** — People feel seen and heard
- **You model leadership** — Your presence influences others
- **You save time** — Focused, present meetings are shorter and more effective

This checklist is your pre-meeting ritual and in-meeting guide to showing up fully.

## Part 1: Pre-Meeting Preparation (5 minutes)

### The "Before You Enter" Checklist

**Complete these 5 items before every meeting:**

| # | Action | Why It Matters | Done |
|---|--------|----------------|------|
| 1 | **Clear your mental buffer** | Write down what's on your mind so you can be present | [ ] |
| 2 | **Clarify your role** | Are you here to decide? Discuss? Listen? Facilitate? | [ ] |
| 3 | **Set your intention** | How do you want to show up? What quality do you want to embody? | [ ] |
| 4 | **Know the outcome** | What should be different by the end of this meeting? | [ ] |
| 5 | **Ground your body** | 3 breaths, feet flat, shoulders dropped, arrive fully | [ ] |

### Detailed Guidance

#### 1. Clear Your Mental Buffer

**Take 60 seconds to brain dump:**

> What's on my mind right now that could distract me?

1.
2.
3.

**Write it down and set it aside.** Your brain can stop holding it because you've captured it.

---

#### 2. Clarify Your Role

**Your role determines your presence mode:**

| Role | Your Presence Job | How to Show Up |
|------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Facilitator** | Hold the container, guide the process | Stay neutral, manage time, ensure participation |
| **Decision-maker** | Bring clarity and commit | Be decisive, think aloud when helpful, own the call |
| **Contributor** | Add value when you have something unique | Listen first, speak when you can add, hold back otherwise |
| **Listener/Learner** | Absorb and ask questions | Curiosity mode, minimal speaking, maximum attention |

**My role in this meeting is:** _________________________

**Therefore, my presence job is:** _________________________

---

#### 3. Set Your Intention

**Choose one quality to embody:**

Examples:
- Curious
- Decisive
- Supportive
- Challenging
- Clear
- Patient
- Courageous
- Inclusive

**Today, I will show up as:** _________________________

**How people will feel after interacting with me:** _________________________

---

#### 4. Know the Outcome

**What should be different after this meeting?**

> By the end of this meeting, we should have [outcome].

**What does success look like for my participation?**

> I will have participated successfully if [measure].

---

#### 5. Ground Your Body

**The 30-second arrival ritual:**

1. **Feet flat** on the floor, feel the contact
2. **Drop shoulders** away from your ears
3. **Relax your jaw** and unclench your teeth
4. **Take 3 deep breaths:**
   - Inhale for 4 counts
   - Exhale for 6 counts
5. **Set your intention** internally: "I will be fully present."

## Part 2: In-Meeting Presence Skills

### The PRESENCE Framework

**Use this framework during meetings to maintain and restore presence:**

| Letter | Meaning | Practice |
|--------|---------|----------|
| **P** | **Posture** | Grounded feet, open chest, alert but relaxed |
| **R** | **Respiration** | Breathe fully; notice when breath gets shallow |
| **E** | **Eyes** | Soft eye contact, not scanning or avoiding |
| **S** | **Stillness** | Minimize fidgeting; stillness communicates presence |
| **E** | **Ears** | Listen to understand, not to respond |
| **N** | **Notice** | Notice when mind wanders; gently return |
| **C** | **Curiosity** | Ask questions; stay open rather than certain |
| **E** | **Engage** | Participate fully when you have value to add |

---

### Listening Presence

**Most people listen to respond. Shift to listening to understand.**

**The 3-Level Listening Practice:**

| Level | What You're Listening For | Your Job |
|-------|---------------------------|----------|
| **1. Content** | What they're actually saying | Understand the information |
| **2. Meaning** | What they mean by what they're saying | Hear their perspective |
| **3. Emotion** | What they're feeling beneath the words | Connect with their experience |

**Before responding, ask yourself:**
- Did I fully understand what they said?
- Do I know why they're saying it?
- Have I acknowledged their experience?

**Only then respond.**

---

### Speaking Presence

**When you speak, do it with intention:**

**Before Speaking Checklist:**

- [ ] Is this necessary to say, or am I just talking?
- [ ] Does this add value to the conversation?
- [ ] Am I the right person to say this, right now?
- [ ] Can I say it more concisely?

**While Speaking:**
- [ ] One point at a time (avoid rambling)
- [ ] Eye contact with others, not just the leader
- [ ] Notice audience reactions and adjust
- [ ] Pause for emphasis and to allow processing

**After Speaking:**
- [ ] Stop talking (avoid filler and repetition)
- [ ] Invite others in ("What do others think?")
- [ ] Notice the impact of what you said

---

### Mindful Technology Use

**Technology is the enemy of presence. Set guidelines:**

**Best Practices:**

| Practice | Why | How |
|----------|-----|-----|
| **Devices away** | Even silent devices distract attention | Leave laptop/phone unless essential for the meeting |
| **Single screen** | Multitasking divides attention | If you must use technology, only have the meeting screen open |
| **Notifications off** | Interrupts break presence | Silence all notifications during meeting |
| **Video on** | Maintains connection | Keep camera on for virtual meetings unless bandwidth is an issue |
| **Breaks built in** | Sustained presence is draining | For meetings >60 minutes, schedule a 5-minute break every hour |

## Part 3: Facilitating Presence in Others

### If You're Leading the Meeting

**Your presence sets the tone. Lead by example and design.**

**Pre-Meeting:**

1. **Send a clear purpose and agenda** — People arrive more present when they know why they're there
2. **Request pre-work** — Reduce information-overload during the meeting
3. **Specify participation expectations** — "Come prepared to discuss X" vs. "I'll update you on X"

**Meeting Start:**

1. **Begin with arrival** — "Let's take 30 seconds to arrive fully. Feet flat, breath, clear the mental buffer."
2. **State expectations** — "This meeting works best when we're all fully present. I invite you to close other windows and put phones away."
3. **Set the container** — "Our goal today is [outcome]. Here's how we'll use our time [brief process]."

**During Meeting:**

1. **Notice energy** — If attention is flagging, pause and reset: "Let's take a breath. Where are we?"
2. **Invite quiet voices** — "I'd love to hear from those who haven't spoken yet."
3. **Manage dominators** — "Thank you for that. Let's hear from others before you add more."
4. **Reinforce presence** — Acknowledge when someone brings full presence: "That was a thoughtful question that really gets to what matters."

**Meeting End:**

1. **Review decisions and actions** — Presence is wasted if nothing is captured
2. **End with intentional closure** — "Let's take one breath together before we return to our days."
3. **Appreciate the presence** — "Thank you all for bringing your full attention to this."

## Part 4: Recovery and Reset

### When You Lose Presence (You Will)

**Presence is not a permanent state. It's a practice of returning.**

**Signs you've lost presence:**
- You're thinking about something else
- You realize you haven't been listening
- You feel reactive or defensive
- You notice others have checked out
- You're physically uncomfortable

**The 60-Second Reset:**

1. **Notice** — "I'm not present right now."
2. **Breathe** — Take 3 full breaths (inhale 4, exhale 6)
3. **Ground** — Feel your feet on the floor
4. **Return** — Re-enter the meeting: "Sorry, can you repeat that?" or just start listening fully again

**No drama, no apology needed.** Just return to presence.

---

### Post-Meeting Integration

**Before your next meeting or task:**

1. **Capture any actions** for you personally
2. **Note any follow-up** you need to do
3. **Clear your mind** before moving on — take 30 seconds to transition
4. **Reflect briefly** (optional): "What did I do well in that meeting? What would serve me to improve?"

## Part 5: Meeting-Specific Guidance

### 1:1 Meetings

**Presence is especially critical in one-on-ones.**

**Before:**
- Review previous notes and commitments
- Clear your mind of other concerns
- Set intention: How do you want the other person to feel?

**During:**
- Remove all distractions (phones away, laptop closed)
- Maintain eye contact
- Listen more than you speak (70/30 rule is good)
- Notice what's NOT being said

**After:**
- Note any commitments made
- Follow through promptly

---

### Team Meetings

**Your presence models team culture.**

**Focus on:**
- Inclusive participation (draw out quieter voices)
- Maintaining focus and energy
- Facilitating constructive dialogue
- Modeling listening and curiosity

**Avoid:**
- Dominating the conversation
- multitasking
- checking devices
- shutting down dissent

---

### Decision Meetings

**Presence enables better decisions.**

**Focus on:**
- Full understanding before deciding
- Pausing before committing
- Checking alignment with values/strategy
- Making decisions, not just discussing

**Key presence move:**
Before finalizing any decision, ask: "Are we all truly aligned on this, or is there unspoken disagreement?"

---

### Difficult Meetings

**Presence under pressure is leadership presence.**

**When conflict arises:**
- Pause before responding
- Lower your voice (slower, quieter = more presence)
- Acknowledge emotions: "This clearly matters to people. Let's slow down."
- Stay curious about differences rather than defensive

**When you're triggered:**
- Breathe
- Notice your activation
- Request a pause if needed: "I want to respond thoughtfully. Can I take a moment?"

## Part 6: Quick Reference

### The Ultimate Meeting Presence Checklist

**Before Every Meeting:**
- [ ] Cleared mental buffer (brain dump distractions)
- [ ] Clarified my role (contributor, facilitator, decision-maker, listener)
- [ ] Set intention for how I'll show up
- [ ] Know the desired outcome
- [ ] Grounded body (3 breaths, feet flat, shoulders dropped)

**During Every Meeting:**
- [ ] Devices away or minimized
- [ ] Listening to understand, not respond
- [ ] Speaking only when adding value
- [ ] Noticing when I lose presence and returning
- [ ] Maintaining eye contact and open body language

**After Every Meeting:**
- [ ] Captured my action items
- [ ] Noted any follow-up needed
- [ ] Took 30 seconds to transition before next thing

### Presence Challenges and Solutions

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| **Mind wandering** | Notice without judgment, return to breath, re-engage listening |
| **Feeling impatient** | Acknowledge it, remember the purpose, find curiosity |
| **Dominating the conversation** | Count to 3 before speaking, ask questions instead |
| **Withdrawing** | Set intention to speak once, prepare one contribution |
| **Feeling defensive** | Breathe, get curious about the other perspective |
| **Checking out mentally** | Ground in body, take notes, ask a question |
| **Multitasking** | Close other windows, turn phone face down |
| **Virtual meeting fatigue** | Take breaks, limit back-to-backs, turn video on to stay engaged |

## Part 7: Team Implementation

### Bringing This to Your Team

**If you want to improve meeting presence across your team:**

**Step 1: Share the why**
> "We spend over half our work time in meetings. I want to make sure that time is well spent. Let's experiment with bringing more presence to our meetings together."

**Step 2: Establish shared agreements**
- Devices away unless essential
- Video on for virtual meetings
- Start and end on time
- One conversation at a time
- Listen to understand, not just respond

**Step 3: Model it**
- Be the most present person in every meeting
- Call out good presence when you see it
- Acknowledge when you lose presence and recover

**Step 4: Review and adjust**
- After 2-4 weeks, discuss: "How is our meeting presence?"
- Adjust agreements based on what's working

---

**Remember:** Meeting presence is not about perfection. It's about the quality of attention you bring and how quickly you return when you lose focus.

**Every meeting is an opportunity to practice.**
