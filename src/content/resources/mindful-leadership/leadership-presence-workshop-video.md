**[Visual: HOST on camera in a quiet, reflective setting.]**

"Welcome back. Before we talk about what you DO with your body and voice, we need to talk about what's happening INSIDE.

### Section 1: The Presence Mindset Shift (4 min)

**[Visual: "Reactive vs. Responsive" comparison on screen.]**

**HOST:**

"The foundation of presence is a mindset shift: moving from REACTIVE to RESPONSIVE.

**Reactive leaders** are triggered by their environment. Someone says something challenging, they react. Something goes wrong, they panic. They're constantly responding to external stimuli.

**Responsive leaders** have a gap between stimulus and response. In that gap, they choose. They're not controlled by their environment—they regulate themselves and choose their response.

This gap is where presence lives. And here's how you build it:"

**[Visual: 3-step framework appears: NOTICE → NAME → CHOOSE]**

**STEP 1: NOTICE**

"First, you have to notice when you're triggered. Your body will tell you: tension in your chest, your jaw clenching, your heart racing. These are signals that you've moved into reactive mode."

**STEP 2: NAME**

"Second, name what's happening. 'I'm feeling defensive right now.' 'I'm worried about how this will be received.' When you name it, you tame it."

**STEP 3: CHOOSE**

"Third, choose your response. What does this situation call for? What's aligned with who you want to be as a leader?"

**Question 3: What am I pretending not to know about myself?**

This is the hard one. What are you hiding? What are you afraid others will see? Because here's the thing: your shadow side will show up whether you acknowledge it or not. Better to own it than be controlled by it."

**[Visual: Animation of "values → behaviors → presence"]**

## Video 3: Physical Presence & Body Language

### Opening (1 min)

**[Visual: Side-by-side comparison: leader with closed posture vs. open, grounded posture.]**

**HOST:**

"Before you speak, your body has already spoken. In fact, research shows that body language accounts for more than 50% of communication.

Your body is always broadcasting. The question is: what message is it sending?

In this video, we're breaking down the physical foundations of presence: posture, movement, and the subtle signals that shape how others experience you."

### Section 1: Grounded Posture (5 min)

**[Visual: HOST demonstrates proper posture. Diagram shows "grounding" through feet.]**

**HOST:**

"Let's start with your foundation: how you stand or sit.

**The Presence Posture has three elements:**

**1. Grounded Feet**

Whether standing or sitting, your feet should be flat and about shoulder-width apart. Feel the contact with the floor. This isn't just physical—it signals to your nervous system that you're supported and stable.

**2. Relaxed Shoulders**

Tension in your shoulders communicates stress, defensiveness, or discomfort. Let them drop. If you need a reminder, exhale deeply and feel them settle.

**3. Open Chest**

Collapsed shoulders or crossed arms signal closing down. An open chest signals openness and confidence.

**[Visual: Before/After comparison]**

**HOST (voiceover):**

"When you adopt this posture, two things happen:

First, others perceive you as more present and confident.

Second—and this is fascinating—your own psychology shifts. Research by Amy Cuddy shows that adopting expansive, open posture actually changes your hormone levels, increasing testosterone (confidence) and decreasing cortisol (stress).

Your posture doesn't just communicate presence—it CREATES it."

### Section 2: Hand Gestures and Movement (4 min)

**[Visual: Examples of effective vs. ineffective gestures.]**

**HOST:**

"Let's talk about your hands. What should you do with them?

**Effective gestures:**

- **Open palms:** Signal honesty, openness, and collaboration
- **Steepling:** Touching fingertips together can signal thoughtfulness and precision
- **Illustrative gestures:** Using hands to paint a picture as you speak adds emphasis and memorability

**Gestures to avoid:**

- **Pointing:** Can feel aggressive or accusatory
- **Crossed arms:** Signals defensiveness or disengagement
- **Fidgeting:** Communicates nervousness or lack of focus
- **Hiding hands:** Put your hands where people can see them—hidden hands unconsciously trigger distrust"

**[Visual: HOST demonstrates the "gesture zone" - between waist and chest]**

**HOST:**

"Keep your gestures in the zone between your waist and chest. Gestures above your chest can feel over-the-top; gestures below your waist can lack impact. The sweet spot is right in front of your torso."

### Section 3: Facial Expressions and Eye Contact (5 min)

**[Visual: Close-ups of various facial expressions. Eye contact examples.]**

**HOST:**

"Your face is your most expressive presence tool. Let's use it intentionally.

**The Eyes:**

- **Duration:** Maintain eye contact for about 3-5 seconds per person in group settings; longer in one-on-ones
- **Distribution:** In groups, make eye contact with multiple people, not just the decision-maker
- **Softness:** A soft gaze is better than an intense stare—you want to feel present, not intimidating

**The Mouth:**

- **Genuine smiles:** When authentic, smiles build connection and warmth
- **Relaxed jaw:** Tension in your jaw communicates stress
- **Nodding:** Gentle nodding shows you're listening, but don't overdo it—it can feel like you're rushing

**The Brows:**

- **Raised eyebrows:** Can signal surprise or inquiry
- **Furrowed brows:** Can communicate concern or deep thought"

**[Visual: "Micro-expressions" graphic]**

**HOST (voiceover):**

"Micro-expressions—tiny, fleeting facial movements—communicate your true feelings. You can't completely control them, but you can work on alignment: if your words say 'I'm excited' but your face says 'I'm skeptical,' people will believe your face. Presence requires alignment between your internal state and your external expression."

### Section 4: Movement and Use of Space (3 min)

**[Visual: Examples of purposeful vs. aimless movement.]**

**HOST:**

"How should you move when you're leading?

**Purposeful movement:**

- Move to emphasize transitions or new points
- Step closer when you want to create intimacy or intensity
- Step back when you want to create space or allow reflection
- Use the full space available to you—don't get stuck behind a podium or table

**Movement that undermines presence:**

- Pacing without purpose (nervous energy)
- Rocking back and forth (lack of groundedness)
- Hiding behind furniture or objects (lack of confidence)

**[Visual: Host demonstrates "anchoring" - standing still while making key points]**

**HOST:**

"Your most powerful moments of presence will often come when you're completely STILL. When you stop moving, you signal: this matters. When you combine stillness with direct eye contact and a slightly lowered voice, you create maximum impact."

### Closing (2 min)

**[Visual: Summary checklist on screen.]**

**HOST:**

"Physical presence checklist:

- ✓ Grounded feet, relaxed shoulders, open chest
- ✓ Open palms in the gesture zone
- ✓ Soft eye contact, distributed across the room
- ✓ Facial expressions aligned with your message
- ✓ Purposeful movement, anchored stillness for emphasis

Practice these elements individually, then integrate them. And remember: don't try to perform all of this at once. Start with one element, master it, then add another.

In the next video, we're covering your VOICE—how to use tone, pacing, and silence to amplify your presence.

See you in Video 4."

## Video 4: Vocal Presence & Communication

### Opening (1 min)

**[Visual: Sound wave animation. HOST with microphone.]**

**HOST:**

"Your voice is your primary instrument of leadership. Every day, you use it to inspire, direct, challenge, comfort, and persuade.

But are you using it intentionally?

Most leaders have never been trained in vocal presence. They speak how they've always spoken—missing opportunities to amplify their impact.

This video changes that. We're covering tone, pacing, volume, and the strategic use of silence."

### Section 1: The Five Elements of Vocal Presence (5 min)

**[Visual: Five icons appear: Tone, Pace, Volume, Pause, Articulation]**

**HOST:**

"Vocal presence has five elements. Let's break them down.

**1. TONE**

Your tone communicates emotion and attitude. Is your tone warm or cold? Certain or uncertain? Frustrated or curious?

The key is ALIGNMENT—your tone should match your message. If you're delivering difficult feedback, a warm but clear tone works better than harshness OR false cheer.

**2. PACE**

Most people speak too fast when they're nervous. But pace is a tool, not just a reflex.

- Slower pace: Signals gravity, importance, thoughtfulness
- Faster pace: Signals energy, excitement, urgency

Vary your pace intentionally. Slow down for key points. Speed up for energetic moments.

**3. VOLUME**

Volume isn't just about being loud. It's about DYNAMICS.

- Projected voice: Signals confidence and authority
- Softer voice: Creates intimacy and draws people in
- Volume variation: Keeps attention and emphasizes importance

**4. PAUSE**

The pause is the most underused vocal tool. A well-placed pause:

- Gives people time to absorb what you've said
- Creates emphasis around important points
- Shows confidence—you're not rushing to fill silence
- Allows YOU to breathe and think

**5. ARTICULATION**

Clear articulation signals clarity of thought. Crisp consonants and complete words communicate that you're present and intentional."

### Section 2: Finding Your Authentic Voice (4 min)

**[Visual: HOST demonstrates vocal exercises.]**

**HOST:**

"Here's something important: don't try to sound like someone else. The goal isn't a generic 'leader voice.' The goal is YOUR best voice.

Let's find it:

**Exercise 1: Your Natural Pitch**

Count from 1 to 5. Then count again, one note lower. Again, one note lower.

Find the lowest pitch that still feels natural and resonant. This is your optimal speaking pitch—grounded, authoritative, but not forced.

**Exercise 2: Breath Support**

Place your hand on your abdomen. Take a deep breath into your belly, not your chest.

Now say 'AAAAHHH' on a full exhale. Feel your hand move? That's diaphragm support.

Speaking from your diaphragm gives your voice warmth, resonance, and sustainability.

**Exercise 3: The Resonance Test**

Say 'MMMM-hmm' while humming. Feel the vibration in your chest, your throat, your nasal cavity?

That vibration is resonance. It's what makes your voice rich and full, not thin and weak."

### Section 3: Strategic Pausing (4 min)

**[Visual: Clock animation showing seconds ticking. Examples of effective pauses.]**

**HOST:**

"I want to focus more on pausing because it's that important.

Here's where to pause strategically:

**BEFORE a key point** - This signals that something important is coming and creates anticipation.

**AFTER a key point** - This gives the listener time to absorb and remember.

**BEFORE answering** - This shows thoughtfulness and prevents reactive responses.

**AFTER someone asks a question** - This honors their question and shows you're really considering it.

**[Visual: Examples of effective pauses in famous speeches]**

**HOST (voiceover):**

"How long should you pause? Longer than feels comfortable. Usually 2-4 seconds.

It will feel like an eternity to you. To your audience, it will feel like confident thoughtfulness."

### Section 4: Vocal Presence in Different Situations (4 min)

**[Visual: Three scenarios: One-on-one, Team meeting, Presentation]**

**HOST:**

"Different situations call for different vocal approaches.

**ONE-ON-ONES**

- Softer volume, more intimacy
- More listening, less talking
- Tone should be warm and supportive
- Use pauses to show you're thinking about their specific situation

**TEAM MEETINGS**

- Projected voice so everyone can hear clearly
- Crisp articulation and energetic pace
- Vary your tone to maintain engagement
- Direct your voice around the room, include everyone

**PRESENTATIONS**

- Maximum vocal variety to maintain attention
- Strategic pauses before and after key messages
- Louder volume for emphasis and projection
- Slower pace for complex points, faster for stories"

### Section 5: Common Vocal Mistakes (2 min)

**[Visual: "X" marks appear over common mistakes.]**

**HOST:**

"Quick mistakes to avoid:

**Uptalk** - Rising intonation at the end of statements. It makes you sound uncertain. End statements with a downward pitch.

**Vocal fry** - That creaky, low voice at the end of sentences. It undermines your credibility. Complete your sentences with clear tone.

**Filler words** - 'Um,' 'uh,' 'like,' 'you know.' These signal lack of confidence. Replace them with silence.

**Monotone** - Flat delivery signals disengagement. Vary your pitch and pace.

**Not breathing** - Speaking without enough breath makes your voice thin and strained. Breathe fully before speaking."

### Closing (2 min)

**[Visual: Summary on screen. HOST on camera.]**

**HOST:**

"Vocal presence takes practice. Here's your daily routine:

- 5 minutes of vocal warm-up (humming, pitch exercises)
- Record yourself speaking and identify one thing to improve
- Practice pausing—count to 3 silently before responding

In the next video, we're putting it all together: presence in high-stakes situations. Conflict, presentations, difficult conversations—when the pressure is on, how do you maintain presence?

See you in Video 5."

## Video 5: Presence in High-Stakes Situations

### Opening (1 min)

**[Visual: High-pressure scenarios: Boardroom, crisis meeting, presentation stage.]**

**HOST:**

"Presence is easy when things are calm. The real test is when the pressure is on.

Conflict. High-stakes presentations. Difficult conversations. Crises.

In these moments, your nervous system wants to pull you into reactivity. Your instinct is to fight, freeze, or flee.

But this is exactly when presence matters most. Your ability to stay grounded and intentional in difficult moments is what defines your leadership."

### Section 1: The Physiology of Stress (3 min)

**[Visual: Nervous system diagram. Fight/flight/freeze response.]**

**HOST:**

"Let's quickly understand what happens in high-pressure situations.

When you're stressed, your amygdala (the threat detector) activates your fight-flight-freeze response:

- Heart rate increases
- Breathing becomes shallow
- Blood flows to major muscle groups (away from your brain)
- Your prefrontal cortex (the thinking brain) literally shuts down

This is why you say things you regret, lose your train of thought, or freeze up. Your body is preparing you to SURVIVE, not to lead.

**Presence is the ability to override this response.** To regulate your nervous system and access your thinking brain even under pressure."

### Section 2: The 90-Second Reset (4 min)

**[Visual: Clock counting down. HOST demonstrates the reset.]**

**HOST:**

"Neuroscientist Jill Bolte Taylor discovered that the emotional charge of an event lasts only 90 seconds in the body—unless you keep re-triggering it with your thoughts.

This is the foundation of the 90-Second Reset:

**Step 1: Notice**

Recognize the activation. Your heart racing, your chest tightening, your thoughts racing. Name it: 'I'm activated right now.'

**Step 2: Breathe**

Take 5 deep, slow breaths. Inhale for 4, exhale for 6. This signals to your nervous system that you're safe.

**Step 3: Ground**

Feel your feet on the floor. Look around the room. Name 3 things you can see. This pulls you out of spiral and into the present.

**Step 4: Choose**

Now that you've reset, choose your response. What does this situation need from you?

**[Visual: The 90-second cycle illustrated]**

**HOST (voiceover):**

"You can do this reset in 90 seconds, right in the middle of a high-pressure situation. No one even needs to know you're doing it. But it will completely change your capacity for presence."

### Section 3: Presence in Conflict (4 min)

**[Visual: Conflict scenarios. "Presence vs. Reactivity" comparison.]**

**HOST:**

"Conflict is where presence is most powerful—and most difficult.

When someone challenges you, attacks you, or disagrees strongly:

**Your reactive impulse:** Defend, attack back, shut down, escalate

**Your presence-based response:**

- Stay grounded in your body (feel your feet)
- Get curious about their perspective
- Lower your voice and slow your pace
- Listen fully before responding
- Acknowledge what you've heard

**[Visual: Sample dialogue]**

**HOST (demonstrating):**

"Instead of: 'That's not what I said! You're misrepresenting me!'

Try: 'Let me make sure I understand what you're hearing. [Reflect back]. What I actually meant was...'

This small shift—from defense to clarification—is presence in action. It de-escalates rather than escalates."

### Section 4: Presence During Presentations (5 min)

**[Visual: Presentation scenarios. Nervous speaker vs. present speaker.]**

**HOST:**

"Public speaking triggers more anxiety than almost anything else. Here's how to stay present:

**BEFORE: Set Yourself Up**

- Arrive early and stand in the space. Own it before anyone arrives.
- Do a vocal warm-up and some movement to discharge nervous energy.
- Set a clear intention: How do you want people to feel?

**DURING: Return to Presence**

- If you lose your train of thought, PAUSE. Don't ramble. Just pause, breathe, and return.
- Make eye contact with ONE person at a time, not the whole room.
- Use your body—move with purpose, gesture naturally.
- Remember: your audience WANTS you to succeed. They're on your side.

**[Visual: "PSA Framework" appears]**

**HOST:**

"When you're presenting, use the PSA Framework:

**P - PAUSE** before answering questions or moving to new points
**S - SPEAK** slowly and clearly
**A - ATTUNE** to the room—read energy and adjust

This keeps you responsive rather than locked in your script."

### Section 5: Presence in Difficult Conversations (3 min)

**[Visual: One-on-one conversation setting.]**

**HOST:**

"Delivering difficult news, giving critical feedback, having a tough one-on-one—these conversations test your presence.

**Key principles:**

- **Prepare:** Know your message and your intention. But don't over-script—presence requires responsiveness.
- **Start with presence:** Take a breath. Make eye contact. Set a tone of care, not attack.
- **Listen more than you speak:** Their reaction matters. Let them express it.
- **Stay with discomfort:** You don't have to fix their feelings. Just stay present.

**[Visual: Sample phrases for difficult conversations]**

**HOST:**

"Presence phrases for difficult conversations:

- 'This conversation is difficult for me too, and it's important that we have it.'
- 'I'm going to pause for a moment to gather my thoughts.'
- 'I can see this is hard to hear. I want to hear your reaction.'
- 'I don't have all the answers, but I'm committed to working through this with you.'"

### Closing (2 min)

**[Visual: Summary of high-stakes presence principles.]**

**HOST:**

"Presence under pressure comes down to this:

- Understand your nervous system and how to reset it
- Have practices you can use in the moment (breathing, grounding, pausing)
- Remember: reactivity escalates, presence de-escalates
- Your presence communicates safety to others—helping them regulate too

In our final video, we're creating a personal development plan. How do you practice and build these skills over time?

See you in Video 6."

## Video 6: Practice Exercises & Integration

### Opening (1 min)

**[Visual: HOST on camera, welcoming tone.]**

**HOST:**

"You've learned the what, why, and how of leadership presence. Now comes the most important part: practice.

Presence is a skill, not knowledge. You can't think your way into being more present—you have to PRACTICE your way there.

In this final video, I'm giving you a complete development plan: daily practices, weekly exercises, and ways to track your progress."

### Section 1: Daily Presence Practice (4 min)

**[Visual: Daily practice checklist. Morning/evening routines.]**

**HOST:**

"Building presence requires consistent, small practices. Here's your daily routine:

**MORNING: 5-Minute Presence Setup**

1. **Body Scan:** 2 minutes scanning from head to toe, releasing tension
2. **Intention Setting:** Ask 'How do I want to show up today?' Choose ONE quality
3. **Vocal Warm-Up:** 2 minutes of humming and pitch variation
4. **Posture Check:** Grounded feet, relaxed shoulders, open chest

**DURING THE DAY: Micro-Practices**

- **Before each meeting:** 3 deep breaths, set intention for how you'll participate
- **Before speaking:** One breath to collect your thoughts
- **In conversations:** Full eye contact, no multitasking
- **In stress:** 90-second reset (notice, breathe, ground, choose)

**EVENING: 5-Minute Reflection**

Answer these questions:

1. When was I most present today? What created that?
2. When was I most reactive today? What triggered me?
3. What one thing will I do differently tomorrow?

**[Visual: Daily practice template appears on screen]**

**HOST:**

"This daily practice takes 15 minutes total. 15 minutes a day to transform your presence. That's a massive ROI."

### Section 2: Weekly Development Exercises (5 min)

**[Visual: Weekly focus areas with specific exercises.]**

**HOST:**

"In addition to your daily practice, choose ONE focused exercise each week.

**Week 1: Physical Presence**

- **Exercise:** Record yourself speaking for 2 minutes. Watch with the sound off.
- **What to notice:** Posture, gestures, facial expressions, movement
- **Goal:** Identify and practice ONE improvement

**Week 2: Vocal Presence**

- **Exercise:** Record yourself speaking. Listen with the video off.
- **What to notice:** Pace, tone, volume, pauses, filler words
- **Goal:** Reduce filler words by 50%, add 3 strategic pauses

**Week 3: Presence Under Pressure**

- **Exercise:** Intentionally put yourself in a low-stakes pressure situation (speak up in a meeting, give short presentation)
- **What to notice:** Your nervous system response, your ability to regulate
- **Goal:** Use the 90-second reset at least once

**Week 4: Listening Presence**

- **Exercise:** In 3 conversations, practice fully present listening
- **What to notice:** Your impulse to interrupt, your internal commentary
- **Goal:** Don't interrupt once in each conversation

**[Visual: 12-week progression plan appears]**

**HOST (voiceover):**

"Rotate through these focus areas for 12 weeks, and you'll see dramatic improvement in your presence."

### Section 3: The Presence Project (3 min)

**[Visual: Project framework: Skill → Practice → Feedback → Iterate]**

**HOST:**

"For accelerated growth, take on a Presence Project:

**Choose ONE specific presence skill** to focus on for 30 days.

Examples:
- Eliminating filler words
- Mastering the strategic pause
- Improving eye contact distribution
- Developing a more resonant voice
- Staying grounded in conflict

**For each day of your 30-day project:**
