# Leadership Values Clarification Exercise

**Discover Your Core Values, Test Your Decisions, and Lead with Complete Alignment**

---

## Welcome to Your Values Clarification Journey

I've seen leaders who embody their values so completely that you can feel their integrity before they speak. Their decisions are clear. Their teams trust them completely. Their leadership feels authentic because it is.

I've also seen leaders who've never done this work. They say they value "transparency" but withhold information. They claim to prioritize "team development" but make all decisions themselves. They wonder why their teams feel confused, distrustful, or disengaged.

The difference isn't character—it's clarity.

**The central leadership challenge of our time is alignment.** Alignment between who you are and how you lead. Between your stated values and your daily behaviors. Between what you say matters and what you actually do.

This exercise will guide you through a structured process to:

1. **Discover your core leadership values** from 100+ possibilities
2. **Prioritize ruthlessly** to identify your top 5-7 non-negotiable values
3. **Test your current leadership** against these values
4. **Create a decision-testing framework** for future choices
5. **Communicate your values** to your team with clarity
6. **Live your values** through a 30-day embodied practice

**Time Investment:** 2-3 hours total (can be done in one session or across multiple sittings)

**What You'll Need:** Quiet space, honesty, pen, and paper

## Part 1: Values Discovery

### Understanding Leadership Values

Leadership values are the principles that guide your behavior as a leader. They're your internal compass—the non-negotiable truths that inform how you show up, make decisions, and treat others.

When your leadership aligns with your values:
- You feel energized and authentic
- Decisions feel clearer (even when difficult)
- Your team trusts you because you're consistent
- You lead from a place of integrity, not pretense

When your leadership contradicts your values:
- You feel drained, conflicted, or "off"
- Decisions feel like constant compromise
- Your team senses inconsistency (even if you hide it)
- You lead from performance, not presence

**Your presence is your primary leadership tool.** Values alignment is what makes that presence trustworthy.

## Part 6: 30-Day Values Living Challenge

### The Challenge Structure

**Lead yourself first.** This 30-day challenge will transform your values from words on a page into embodied leadership practices.

**How it works:**
- **Daily focus:** Each day, you'll focus on one specific value-alignment practice
- **Morning intention:** Start each day by setting your values intention
- **Evening reflection:** End each day by reflecting on your alignment
- **Weekly audit:** Every 7 days, assess your progress and adjust
- **Real-world testing:** Apply your values to actual leadership situations

**The commitment:** 10 minutes morning + 5 minutes evening = 15 minutes/day for 30 days

---

### Week 1: Values Activation

**Day 1: The Declaration**
- **Morning:** Choose your #1 value. Write it on a card, post-it, or phone wallpaper
- **Practice:** Before your first meeting, pause and ask: "How would a leader who embodies [Value] show up to this meeting?"
- **Evening reflection:** When did I most/least embody my #1 value today?

**Day 2: Values-Based Decision**
- **Morning:** Identify one decision you'll make today. Test it against your values using the framework from Exercise 7
- **Practice:** Make the decision that best aligns with your values (even if it's harder)
- **Evening reflection:** How did values-based decision-making feel different?

**Day 3: Values Communication**
- **Morning:** Choose one value you want to demonstrate through communication today
- **Practice:** In one conversation, explicitly state: "For me, [Value] is important here, so I want to..."
- **Evening reflection:** How did naming the value change the interaction?

**Day 4: Values Under Pressure**
- **Morning:** Identify one stressful situation you anticipate today
- **Practice:** When stress hits, pause and ask: "What would [Value] look like in this moment?"
- **Evening reflection:** What happens to my values under pressure? How can I stay aligned?

**Day 5: Values in Feedback**
- **Morning:** Identify feedback you need to give (positive or constructive)
- **Practice:** Deliver the feedback through a values lens: "Because I value [Value], I want to share..."
- **Evening reflection:** How does grounding feedback in values change the conversation?

**Day 6: Values and Boundaries**
- **Morning:** Identify one boundary you need to set or reinforce today
- **Practice:** Set the boundary by referencing your values: "To honor my value of [Value], I need to..."
- **Evening reflection:** How does values clarity make boundary-setting easier or harder?

**Day 7: Weekly Values Audit**
- **Morning:** Review your alignment scores from Exercise 5. Have they changed?
- **Practice:** For each value, write: "This week, I most demonstrated [Value] when..." and "This week, I missed [Value] when..."
- **Evening reflection:** What patterns do I notice? Which values need more attention next week?

---

### Week 2: Values Embodiment

**Day 8: Values Presence Practice**
- **Morning:** Choose one value to embody through your physical presence today
- **Practice:** Before interactions, adjust your presence: "If I were fully [Value], how would I stand/speak/listen?"
- **Evening reflection:** How does my physical presence change when I embody different values?

**Day 9: Values in Conflict**
- **Morning:** Reflect on a situation where two of your values are in tension (e.g., transparency vs. kindness)
- **Practice:** When the tension arises, name it: "I'm feeling tension between [Value 1] and [Value 2] here..."
- **Evening reflection:** How do I navigate values conflicts? Which value takes precedence?

**Day 10: Values and Delegation**
- **Morning:** Identify something you're holding onto that you should delegate
- **Practice:** Delegate through a values lens: "Because I value [Value], I'm trusting you with..."
- **Evening reflection:** How does my values system support (or block) effective delegation?

**Day 11: Values in Meetings**
- **Morning:** Choose one value to bring into every meeting today
- **Practice:** In your first meeting, explicitly demonstrate that value through behavior
- **Evening reflection:** How does intentionally bringing values into meetings change the dynamics?

**Day 12: Values Recognition**
- **Morning:** Identify someone on your team who demonstrated one of your core values recently
- **Practice:** Acknowledge them specifically: "I saw you demonstrate [Value] when you [specific behavior]..."
- **Evening reflection:** How does values-based recognition differ from general praise?

**Day 13: Values and Mistakes**
- **Morning:** Reflect on a recent mistake you made (or make one today)
- **Practice:** Respond to the mistake through a values lens: "Because I value [Value], I want to address this by..."
- **Evening reflection:** How do my values inform how I handle failure?

**Day 14: Weekly Values Audit**
- **Morning:** Review your Week 2 patterns
- **Practice:** For each value, rate your current alignment (1-10) vs. last week
- **Evening reflection:** Which values are becoming more natural? Which still feel hard?

### Week 4: Values Mastery

**Day 22: Values Legacy Reflection**

## Part 8: Team Facilitation Guide

### For Leaders Running This Exercise With Their Teams

**Why facilitate values clarification for your team:**
- Creates shared language and understanding
- Builds trust through vulnerability
- Reveals alignment gaps before they become problems
- Strengthens culture by making values explicit
- Accelerates team cohesion and performance

**Preparation:**
- Set aside 2-3 hours for the full exercise
- Create a safe, confidential environment
- Have everyone complete Parts 1-5 independently first
- Come together for Parts 6-8 as a group
- Emphasize that there are no "right" values

**Facilitation Tips:**
- Go first. Share your values openly to model vulnerability
- Notice resistance without judging it
- Celebrate differences in values
- Look for shared themes across the team
- Document team values and make them visible
- Revisit team values quarterly

**Common Challenges:**
- **Cynicism:** Some may see this as "fluffy." Acknowledge this, then share a real example of how values clarity improved a decision or situation.
- **Uniformity:** Watch for people choosing "safe" values they think you want to hear. Reinforce authenticity.
- **Overlap:** Multiple people will have similar values. Explore the nuances—how does each person uniquely define that value?
- **Conflict:** You'll discover conflicting values. Don't resolve it prematurely. Explore the tension and how to navigate it.

**Follow-Up:**
- Create a team values statement
- Reference team values in decisions and meetings
- Start meetings with a values check-in
- End projects with a values reflection
- Hire and promote based on values alignment
- Review team values in performance evaluations
