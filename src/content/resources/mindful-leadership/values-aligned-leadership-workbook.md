# Values-Aligned Leadership Workbook

**Define Your Leadership Values and Translate Them Into Daily Action**

---

## Welcome to the Values-Aligned Leadership Workbook

**The central leadership challenge of our time is not strategy, execution, or innovation—though all of these matter. The central challenge is alignment.**

Alignment between who you are and how you lead. Between your stated values and your daily behaviors. Between what you say matters and what you actually do.

This workbook will guide you through a structured process to:

1. **Clarify your core leadership values** – What truly matters to you as a leader
2. **Define what each value LOOKS LIKE** in practice – Not abstract ideals, concrete behaviors
3. **Identify alignment gaps** – Where your current leadership is inconsistent with your values
4. **Create an action plan** – Specific changes to close those gaps

## Part 5: Daily Values Alignment Practice

### Morning Alignment Practice (5 minutes)

**1. Connect to your values:**
> "Today, I will lead with [Value 1] and [Value 2]."

**2. Set a specific intention:**
> "My specific demonstration of [Value 1] today will be:"

**3. Identify a challenge:**
> "Today, I expect my values to be tested by [situation]. I will stay aligned by:"

**4. Commit to correction:**
> "If I notice myself out of alignment today, I will:"

---

### Evening Reflection Practice (5 minutes)

**1. Review your day:**
> "Today, I most demonstrated [Value] when I [specific example]."

**2. Identify gaps:**
> "Today, I was least aligned with [Value] when I [specific example]."

**3. Explore without judgment:**
> "What was happening in that moment? What triggered me? What need was I trying to meet?"

**4. Plan for tomorrow:**
> "Tomorrow, I will do [specific action] to realign with [Value]."

## Part 7: Sharing Your Values with Others

### Communicating Your Values to Your Team

**Instructions:** Once you've clarified your values, sharing them with your team creates accountability and builds trust.

**Template for sharing your leadership values:**

> "I've been reflecting on what matters most to me as a leader, and I want to share my top five values with you. These are the principles I strive to embody in my leadership:
>
> **1. [Value]** — [What it means to you]
> **2. [Value]** — [What it means to you]
> **3. [Value]** — [What it means to you]
> **4. [Value]** — [What it means to you]
> **5. [Value]** — [What it means to you]
>
> I share these with you for two reasons:
>
> First, I want to be accountable. If you ever see me acting out of alignment with these values, I invite you to let me know.
>
> Second, I want these values to shape our team culture. I'd love to hear: what values do you bring to your work? How can we build a team that reflects all of our values?"

---

### Values Alignment Conversation Guide

**Use these questions in one-on-ones to explore values alignment with your team:**

1. "What values are most important to you in your work?"
2. "When have you felt most aligned with your values in your role here?"
3. "When have you felt out of alignment? What created that?"
4. "What could I do as a leader to better support your values?"
5. "How can our team better reflect the values that matter to you?"

## Closing Reflection

**You have now:**

- ✓ Clarified your top 5 leadership values
- ✓ Defined what each value means in behavioral terms
- ✓ Assessed your current alignment
- ✓ Identified gaps and triggers
- ✓ Created an action plan to improve alignment
- ✓ Established daily, weekly, and ongoing practices

**The work now is to LIVE these values.** Not perfectly—that's impossible. But consistently, with awareness, honesty, and a commitment to realignment when you fall off course.

**Values-aligned leadership is the foundation of authentic influence.** People follow leaders who are consistent, trustworthy, and clear about what they stand for. You now have clarity on what YOU stand for.

**The question is: will you live it?**
