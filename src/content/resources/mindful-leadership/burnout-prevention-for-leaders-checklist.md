# Burnout Prevention for Leaders Checklist

**Protect Your Energy While Maintaining High Impact**

---

## Introduction: The Leader's Burnout Paradox

**Leaders face an impossible equation:**

The demands on you are increasing while your capacity is finite. You're expected to be always available, always decisive, always strong. Meanwhile, the boundary between work and life has eroded, and technology has made you accessible 24/7.

**The result?** Leaders are burning out at alarming rates.

But here's the paradox: **the more you give without replenishing, the less you have to give.** Your effectiveness, decision quality, relationships, and health all decline.

**Burnout prevention isn't self-indulgence—it's leadership sustainability.** You cannot lead others well if you're running on empty.

This checklist gives you a systematic approach to protecting your energy while maintaining your impact.

## Part 5: Emergency Burnout Response Plan

**If you're in crisis (red zone), use this immediate response plan:**

### Immediate Actions (This Week)

1. **[ ] Inform key stakeholders** that you're taking emergency time
2. **[ ] Cancel non-essential commitments** for the next 2 weeks
3. **[ ] Schedule time with a mental health professional**
4. **[ ] Communicate with your team** about reduced availability
5. **[ ] Commit to minimum sleep hygiene** (7+ hours nightly)
6. **[ ] Remove all work from personal spaces** (phone, home)
7. **[ ] Identify ONE person** who can support you through this

---

### Short-Term Recovery (2-4 Weeks)

**[ ] Reduced work schedule** (maximum 40 hours, ideally less)

**[ ] Daily renewal practice** (even 10 minutes)

**[ ] Weekly professional support** (therapy, coaching)

**[ ] Delegation of non-essential tasks**

**[ ] Reassessment of workload** with manager/board

**[ ] No new commitments** until energy recovers

---

### Long-Term Prevention (3+ Months)

**[ ] Redesign role** to be sustainable

**[ ] Build stronger team** to share load

**[ ] Establish permanent boundaries**

**[ ] Create accountability system** for maintaining boundaries

**[ ] Regular check-ins** with support system

## Closing Reminder

**Burnout is not a failure of character.** It's what happens when human capacity meets unlimited demand without sufficient boundaries, renewal, or support.

**Preventing burnout is leadership work.** When you protect your energy:

- You make better decisions
- You model sustainable leadership for your team
- You sustain high performance over the long term
- You protect your health and relationships
- You maintain your passion and purpose

**Use this checklist weekly.** Make adjustments. Ask for support. Your sustainability matters—for you, for your team, and for everyone counting on your leadership.

---

**Resources for Additional Support:**

- Mental Health Professionals: Psychology Today directory, local resources
- Leadership Coaching: ICF-certified coaches
- Peer Support: EO, YPO, Vistage, or similar groups
- Crisis Support: National helplines available 24/7

**If you're experiencing severe symptoms (depression, anxiety, suicidal thoughts), please seek professional help immediately.**
