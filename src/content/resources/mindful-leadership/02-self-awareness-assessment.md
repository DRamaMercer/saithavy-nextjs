# Self-Awareness Assessment for Mindful Leaders

## Assessment Overview

This assessment will help you understand your current leadership style, strengths, and growth areas. It takes approximately 10 minutes to complete.

**Instructions:**
- Answer each question honestly
- Don't overthink—go with your first instinct
- Rate each statement on a scale of 1-5 (1 = Strongly Disagree, 5 = Strongly Agree)
- There are no right or wrong answers—this is for your self-awareness

## Interpreting Your Results

### Overall Score Interpretation

**225-250: Exceptional Mindful Leader**
You demonstrate outstanding self-awareness and consistently apply mindful leadership principles. You're a role model for others and likely already coach or mentor other leaders.

**Your Next Steps:**
- Share your expertise with others
- Explore advanced leadership development
- Consider mentoring emerging leaders
- Document your leadership philosophy and practices

---

**200-224: Strong Mindful Leader**
You have solid self-awareness and regularly practice mindful leadership. You experience the benefits of intentional, conscious leadership.

**Your Next Steps:**
- Identify your 2-3 lowest-scoring areas and focus development there
- Share your practices with your team
- Seek stretch assignments that challenge you
- Build a structured development plan

---

**175-199: Developing Mindful Leader**
You're on the journey of mindful leadership with some areas of strength and clear growth opportunities. You likely see the value of self-awareness but haven't fully integrated it yet.

**Your Next Steps:**
- Choose ONE area from each category to develop
- Establish daily reflection practices
- Find an accountability partner or coach
- Focus on consistency over intensity

---

**150-174: Emerging Mindful Leader**
You're beginning to develop self-awareness but often lead on autopilot. You may feel reactive rather than responsive and want more intentionality in your leadership.

**Your Next Steps:**
- Start with the basics: daily reflection and values clarification
- Focus on your lowest-scoring category first
- Build one habit at a time (don't try to change everything)
- Seek feedback from trusted colleagues

---

**Below 150: Starting Your Journey**
Self-awareness is a new area of focus for you. You may feel overwhelmed, uncertain, or reactive in your leadership role. The good news: you're starting from an honest place and can build from here.

**Your Next Steps:**
- Start with Part 1 (Emotional Self-Awareness) - this is your foundation
- Commit to ONE daily practice: 5 minutes of reflection
- Find a mentor or coach who can guide you
- Be patient with yourself - this is a journey, not a destination

## Category-Specific Interpretations

### Part 1: Emotional Self-Awareness

**Score 40-50:** You're highly emotionally intelligent and use emotions as valuable data.

**Score 30-39:** You have good emotional awareness but may miss subtle cues or reactions.

**Score 20-29:** You're aware of strong emotions but miss more subtle feelings and triggers.

**Score Below 20:** You likely react to emotions without understanding them. Start with emotional vocabulary building and body awareness.

**Growth Actions:**
- Keep an emotions journal (what triggered you, how did it feel, what did you do?)
- Practice naming your emotions throughout the day
- Learn to recognize your physical stress signals
- Ask others for feedback on your emotional responses

---

### Part 2: Leadership Values & Purpose

**Score 40-50:** Your values are clear and guide your leadership consistently.

**Score 30-39:** You know your values but sometimes compromise them under pressure.

**Score 20-29:** You have a vague sense of values but haven't clearly defined them.

**Score Below 20:** Your values are unclear or disconnected from your daily leadership.

**Growth Actions:**
- Complete a values clarification exercise
- Write your personal leadership purpose statement
- Create a "values decision filter" for important choices
- Regularly ask: "Does this align with what I value?"

---

### Part 3: Impact & Influence Awareness

**Score 40-50:** You deeply understand how you affect others and use this intentionally.

**Score 30-39:** You're generally aware of your impact but may miss individual differences.

**Score 20-29:** You have some awareness of your impact but haven't explored it deeply.

**Score Below 20:** You may be surprised by how others perceive you versus your intent.

**Growth Actions:**
- Seek specific feedback about your impact
- Practice perspective-taking exercises
- Ask trusted team members: "What's it like to be led by me?"
- Regularly check for understanding after communicating

---

### Part 4: Growth & Learning Orientation

**Score 40-50:** You're a continuous learner who actively seeks development.

**Score 30-39:** You're open to learning but may not be systematic about it.

**Score 20-29:** You learn when necessary but don't actively seek growth opportunities.

**Score Below 20:** You may see learning as optional or feel threatened by development needs.

**Growth Actions:**
- Create a 90-day development plan
- Identify your blind spots through 360-degree feedback
- Find a mentor or join a peer learning group
- Set learning goals alongside performance goals

---

### Part 5: Mindful Leadership Practices

**Score 40-50:** Mindfulness is integrated into your daily leadership approach.

**Score 30-39:** You use mindful practices but may not do them consistently.

**Score 20-29:** You see the value of mindful practices but rarely apply them.

**Score Below 20:** Mindful practices are unfamiliar or feel unnecessary to you.

**Growth Actions:**
- Start with just ONE practice: 3 deep breaths before meetings
- Build reflection into your calendar (even 5 minutes)
- Try a mindfulness app for guided practice
- Practice "single-tasking" instead of multitasking

## Retaking This Assessment

**Recommended Timeline:** Retake this assessment every 3-6 months to track your growth.

**What to Look For:**
- Which scores increased the most?
- Which areas remain challenging?
- What new growth areas have emerged?
- How has your leadership impact changed?

**Pro Tip:** Keep your previous assessments to compare progress over time. This creates a powerful record of your leadership journey.
