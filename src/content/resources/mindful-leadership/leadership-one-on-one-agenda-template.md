# Leadership One-on-One Agenda Template

**Structure Meaningful Coaching Conversations and Build Trust**

---

## Introduction: Why One-on-Ones Matter

**The one-on-one meeting is the single most important leadership ritual.**

It's your dedicated time to:
- Build trust and relationship
- Coach and develop your team member
- Address concerns before they become problems
- Align on priorities and expectations
- Understand what motivates and challenges them

Yet most one-on-ones are ineffective—wandering conversations with no clear purpose, dominated by status updates rather than development.

**This template transforms one-on-ones from catch-ups into coaching conversations.**

---

## The Template Structure

### Recommended Format

| Element | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Personal Check-In** | 5 min | Build connection, assess energy |
| **Wins & Accomplishments** | 5 min | Celebrate success, build confidence |
| **Priorities & Progress** | 10 min | Review work, identify obstacles |
| **Development & Coaching** | 15 min | Growth, skill-building, challenges |
| **Feedback & Support** | 10 min | Two-way feedback, resources needed |
| **Closing & Next Steps** | 5 min | Commitments, scheduling |

**Total:** 50 minutes (adjust based on your cadence—30-60 minutes typical)

## Best Practices for Effective One-on-Ones

### Before the Meeting

- [ ] Schedule consistent time (same day/time is ideal)
- [ ] Block enough time (45-60 minutes recommended)
- [ ] Review notes from previous meeting
- [ ] Prepare focus topic/questions
- [ ] Review their recent work/accomplishments

### During the Meeting

- [ ] Start on time, end on time
- [ ] Close laptop, put phone away
- [ ] Take notes (shows you value what they say)
- [ ] Listen more than you speak (70/30 rule)
- [ ] Ask open-ended questions
- [ ] Coach rather than solve
- [ ] Give specific, actionable feedback
- [ ] End with clear commitments

### After the Meeting

- [ ] Send summary of commitments (if helpful)
- [ ] Follow through on your commitments
- [ ] Note topics for next meeting
- [ ] Observe and acknowledge progress

## Common One-on-One Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Problematic | What to Do Instead |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Canceling frequently** | Signals they're not important | Protect this time ruthlessly |
| **Dominating the conversation** | Misses their perspective | Aim for 70/30 split (they talk 70%) |
| **Focus only on status** | Misses development opportunities | Prioritize coaching over updates |
| **Solving rather than coaching** | Creates dependency | Ask questions first, offer solutions second |
| **Avoiding difficult topics** | Problems compound | Address concerns directly and compassionately |
| **Not taking notes** | Forget commitments | Document key points and follow through |
| **No clear focus** | Wandering, unproductive | Use this agenda template |
| **Skipping development** | Stagnates growth | Always include a development focus |

## Sample One-on-One Conversation Flow

**Leader:** "Thanks for meeting. How are you doing today—genuinely?"

**Team Member:** [Shares]

**Leader:** "Thanks for sharing. Let's start with wins. What are you proud of since we last met?"

**Team Member:** [Shares wins]

**Leader:** "That's great work. I noticed [specific strength] in how you handled that. Let's talk priorities. What's most important on your plate right now?"

**Team Member:** [Shares priorities]

**Leader:** "Those make sense. Any obstacles I can help remove?"

**Team Member:** [Shares or says no]

**Leader:** "Good. Now for our main focus: development. I've been thinking about [skill/growth area]. What are your thoughts?"

**[Development coaching conversation ensues]**

**Leader:** "Let's also talk about feedback. First, from me to you: [SBI feedback]. And I'd love your feedback on how I'm supporting you."

**Team Member:** [Gives feedback]

**Leader:** "Thank you for that feedback. I'll [action]. Finally, what do you need from me to be successful this month?"

**Team Member:** [Requests support]

**Leader:** "I can do that. Let's capture our commitments and schedule our next meeting. Appreciate your time and the work you're doing."
