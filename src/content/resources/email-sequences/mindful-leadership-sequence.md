## Email 2: Self-Awareness (Day 1)

**Subject:** The leadership skill most executives lack

**Preheader:** 3 practices to build self-awareness in 10 minutes/day

---

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Yesterday you discovered your leadership EQ profile. Today, we're building **self-awareness**—the foundation of all mindful leadership.

Here's what most leaders don't understand: Self-awareness isn't navel-gazing. It's a strategic advantage. Leaders who lack it make decisions from unconscious patterns, react instead of respond, and wonder why their teams disengage.

**SELF-AWARENESS PRACTICE #1: DAILY LEADERSHIP REFLECTION**

Spend 10 minutes at the end of each day answering these questions:

1. **What emotions did I experience today?**
   - Name them specifically (not "bad" but "frustrated," "anxious," "excited")

2. **What triggered those emotions?**
   - Specific situations, people, or events

3. **How did my emotions affect my decisions?**
   - What decisions did I make while emotional?
   - Were those decisions effective?

4. **What went well? What could I improve?**
   - Be honest with yourself

5. **What am I grateful for today?**
   - End with positivity

**Time investment:** 10 minutes/day
**Return:** Increased emotional intelligence, better decisions, reduced reactive behavior

---

**SELF-AWARENESS PRACTICE #2: THE STOP METHOD**

Use this technique before important meetings, when triggered, or when feeling overwhelmed:

**S - Stop what you're doing**
**T - Take a few deep breaths**
**O - Observe your thoughts and feelings**
**P - Proceed with intention**

**Time investment:** 30-60 seconds
**Return:** Reduced reactivity, increased clarity, better decisions

---

**SELF-AWARENESS PRACTICE #3: VALUES CLARIFICATION**

1. List your top 5 leadership values
2. For each value, write: What does this look like in practice?
3. Rate yourself: How well am I living each value?
4. Choose ONE value to focus on this week

**YOUR HOMEWORK:**

Pick ONE self-awareness practice and commit to it for 7 days.

## Email 3: Emotional Regulation (Day 2)

**Subject:** Stay calm when everyone else is freaking out

**Preheader:** The emotional regulation techniques top leaders use

---

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Great work committing to self-awareness! Today, we're tackling **emotional regulation**—the ability to manage your emotions effectively, especially under pressure.

**EMOTIONAL REGULATION TECHNIQUE #1: BOX BREATHING**

Used by Navy SEALs and elite athletes. Use before high-stakes conversations.

**The Practice:**
1. Inhale for 4 seconds through your nose
2. Hold for 4 seconds
3. Exhale for 4 seconds through your mouth
4. Hold for 4 seconds
5. Repeat 4 times

**Why it works:** Activates parasympathetic nervous system, lowers heart rate, reduces cortisol.

---

**EMOTIONAL REGULATION TECHNIQUE #2: NAME IT TO TAME IT**

Labeling emotions reduces amygdala activation by up to 50%.

**The Practice:**
1. Notice the emotion in your body
2. Name it specifically ("I'm feeling frustrated")
3. Take one deep breath
4. Choose your response

---

**EMOTIONAL REGULATION TECHNIQUE #3: THE 90-SECOND RULE**

The chemical surge of an emotion moves through the body in 90 seconds.

**The Practice:**
1. Notice the emotion rising
2. Label it
3. Breathe through it for 90 seconds
4. Choose your next action

**YOUR HOMEWORK:**

Identify ONE situation where you typically get triggered. Use box breathing before it.

## Email 4: Compassionate Communication (Day 3)

**Subject:** The feedback mistake 90% of leaders make

**Preheader:** How to communicate so people actually hear you

---

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

You're building self-awareness and regulating emotions. Now let's put it all together with **compassionate communication**.

**COMMUNICATION FRAMEWORK #1: DELIVERING DIFFICULT FEEDBACK**

**The Template:**
"When [specific behavior], I feel [emotion] because [impact]. I would appreciate [request]."

**Example:**
"When you interrupt in meetings, I feel frustrated because it cuts off diverse perspectives. I would appreciate letting people finish their thoughts."

---

**COMMUNICATION FRAMEWORK #2: ACTIVE LISTENING**

1. **Listen** - Give full attention
2. **Reflect** - "What I'm hearing is..."
3. **Clarify** - "Did I get that right?"
4. **Validate** - "I can understand why you'd feel that way"
5. **Respond** - Now respond thoughtfully

---

**COMMUNICATION FRAMEWORK #3: APPRECIATION FORMULA**

"Thank you for [action]. I appreciate [quality] because [impact]."

**YOUR HOMEWORK:**

Give specific appreciation to 3 people this week.
