## Email 3: Habit Formation (Day 2)

**Subject:** How to make habits stick (even when you're not motivated)

**Preheader:** The science of lasting behavior change

---

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

You've identified your vision and your gaps. Now let's talk about **habit formation**—the engine that drives lasting transformation.

Here's the truth: Motivation gets you started. Habit keeps you going.

**HABIT FORMATION STRATEGY #1: IMPLEMENTATION INTENTIONS**

Most people rely on willpower: "I'll exercise tomorrow." Then tomorrow comes, and they're tired.

Implementation intentions remove willpower from the equation.

**The Formula:**
"I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION]."

**Examples:**
- ❌ "I'll exercise more"
- ✅ "I will walk for 10 minutes at 7am in my neighborhood"

- ❌ "I'll read more"
- ✅ "I will read for 15 minutes at 9pm in my bedroom"

**Why it works:** You decide WHEN and WHERE before you're in the situation. No willpower required.

---

**HABIT FORMATION STRATEGY #2: HABIT STACKING**

Anchor new habits to existing routines.

**The Formula:**
"After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]."

**Examples:**
- "After I pour my morning coffee, I will meditate for 2 minutes"
- "After I brush my teeth, I will write down my top 3 priorities for the day"
- "After I eat lunch, I will take a 5-minute walk"

**Why it works:** You leverage existing neural pathways. The new habit becomes automatic.

---

**HABIT FORMATION STRATEGY #3: START STUPIDLY SMALL**

Most people set goals that are too big. They fail, feel discouraged, and quit.

Instead, make habits so small you can't say no.

- ❌ "Exercise for an hour daily"
- ✅ "Put on running shoes when I get home"

- ❌ "Read a book a week"
- ✅ "Read one page before bed"

**Why it works:** Success builds momentum. Small wins lead to big changes.

---

**HABIT FORMATION STRATEGY #4: THE 2-MINUTE RULE**

When starting a new habit, it should take less than 2 minutes to do.

- Want to exercise? Start with 2 minutes of stretching
- Want to read? Read one page
- Want to meditate? Meditate for 2 minutes

Once the habit is automatic, you can scale up.

---

## Email 4: Mindset Shift (Day 3)

**Subject:** The belief that's sabotaging your success

**Preheader:** How to think like someone who never fails

---

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

You've got vision. You've got habits. There's one final piece: **mindset**.

Here's what separates people who transform from those who stay stuck: Not talent. Not resources. Not luck.

It's how they think about setbacks.

---

**THE GROWTH MINDSET:**

People with a fixed mindset believe:
- "I'm just not good at this"
- "Failure means I'm not capable"
- "I should stick to what I'm good at"

People with a growth mindset believe:
- "I can improve with practice"
- "Failure is feedback—I'm learning"
- "Challenges help me grow"

**The truth:** Your abilities are not fixed. You can change, grow, and transform.

---

**MINDSET SHIFT #1: REFRAME SETBACKS**

When you hit a setback, your brain says: "See? You can't do this."

Reframe it:

**Fixed mindset:** "I failed. I'm not good enough."
**Growth mindset:** "I haven't figured this out YET. What can I learn?"

**Fixed mindset:** "This is too hard."
**Growth mindset:** "This is challenging my brain to grow."

**Fixed mindset:** "I'll never change."
**Growth mindset:** "Every expert was once a beginner. I'm in the learning phase."

---

**MINDSET SHIFT #2: THE POWER OF "YET"**

Add one word to transform your mindset:

"I can't do this... **yet**."
"I'm not good at this... **yet**."
"I haven't figured this out... **yet**."

"Yet" acknowledges where you are while holding open the possibility of change.

---

**MINDSET SHIFT #3: CELEBRATE SMALL WINS**

Most people only celebrate the big milestones. Wrong.

Celebrate EVERY small win:

- Did you show up? Celebrate.
- Did you take one step forward? Celebrate.
- Did you learn something new? Celebrate.

**Why:** Dopamine reinforces behavior. Celebrating small wins creates momentum for big ones.

---

**MINDSET SHIFT #4: CHALLENGE LIMITING BELIEFS**

We all have limiting beliefs:

- "I'm too old to change"
- "I don't have enough time"
- "I'm not disciplined enough"
- "I've always been this way"

**Challenge them:**

1. **Is this absolutely true?** (Or just a story I'm telling myself?)
2. **What evidence do I have?** (For and against this belief)
3. **What would I do if this weren't true?**
4. **Who could I be if I let this go?**

---

**YOUR HOMEWORK:**

Identify ONE limiting belief. Challenge it using the 4 questions above.

Replace it with a growth mindset alternative.
