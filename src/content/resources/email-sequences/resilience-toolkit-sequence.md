**Subject:** What to do when stress hits RIGHT NOW

**Preheader:** 5 immediate techniques that work in under 2 minutes

---

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Yesterday you discovered your resilience profile. Today, we're building your **stress response toolkit**—the immediate techniques you can use the moment stress hits.

Here's the truth: You can't always control when stress arrives. But you *can* control how you respond to it.

**THE STRESS RESPONSE TOOLKIT:**

**1. BOX BREATHING (4-4-4-4)**
Best for: Acute stress, panic, anxiety

Inhale for 4 seconds → Hold for 4 → Exhale for 4 → Hold for 4 → Repeat 4 times

*Why it works:* Slows heart rate, activates parasympathetic nervous system

**2. 5-4-3-2-1 GROUNDING**
Best for: Anxiety, overwhelm, dissociation

Name 5 things you SEE → 4 you TOUCH → 3 you HEAR → 2 you SMELL → 1 you TASTE

*Why it works:* Engages all 5 senses, brings you to present moment

**3. PHYSICAL RESET**
Best for: Tension, anger, frustration

Roll shoulders back 10x → Shake out hands → Stretch arms overhead → 3 deep breaths

*Why it works:* Releases physical tension, resets nervous system

**4. COGNITIVE SHIFT**
Best for: Racing thoughts, worry

Ask: "Will this matter in 5 years?"
Ask: "What's within my control right now?"
Name 3 things you're grateful for

*Why it works:* Shifts perspective, reduces catastrophic thinking

## Email 3: Emotional Regulation (Day 2)

**Subject:** The 5 exercises that regulate your emotions

**Preheader:** Practical techniques you can use anywhere

---

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Great work on the stress response techniques! Today, we're diving into **emotional regulation**—the ability to manage difficult emotions effectively.

Emotions aren't good or bad—they're information. But sometimes they can overwhelm us. These exercises help you process and regulate emotions so they don't control your behavior.

**EXERCISE 1: BOX BREATHING**

When to use: Feeling anxious, angry, or overwhelmed

1. Inhale for 4 seconds through your nose
2. Hold for 4 seconds
3. Exhale for 4 seconds through your mouth
4. Hold for 4 seconds
5. Repeat 4 times

This activates your vagus nerve and calms your nervous system in under 2 minutes.

---

**EXERCISE 2: BODY SCAN**

When to use: Carrying tension, feeling disconnected

1. Close your eyes and start at your toes
2. Notice sensations without judging them
3. Slowly move your attention up your body
4. Release tension in each area as you go
5. Finish at the top of your head

Takes 5-10 minutes. Perfect before bed or during breaks.

---

**EXERCISE 3: COGNITIVE REFRAMING**

When to use: Negative thoughts, catastrophizing

1. Identify the negative thought
2. Ask: "Is this 100% true? What evidence do I have?"
3. Ask: "Is there another way to see this?"
4. Ask: "What's the most helpful interpretation?"
5. Choose the perspective that serves you best

---

**EXERCISE 4: JOURNALING PROMPTS**

When to use: Processing emotions, gaining clarity

Use these prompts:
- "What am I feeling right now and why?"
- "What's one lesson from today?"
- "What am I grateful for in this moment?"
- "What's one thing I can control right now?"
- "What would I tell a friend in this situation?"

---

**EXERCISE 5: PHYSICAL RELEASE**

When to use: Bottled-up energy, frustration

- Shake out your hands and arms
- Roll your shoulders and neck
- Stretch deeply (touch your toes, reach for the sky)
- Take a brisk 5-minute walk
- Do 10 jumping jacks

Physical movement releases tension and processes emotions through the body.

---

## Email 4: Recovery Routines (Day 3)

**Subject:** The recovery routines high-performers swear by

**Preheader:** Daily and weekly practices that restore your energy

---

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

You've got stress response techniques down. You're learning to regulate emotions. Now, let's talk about **prevention**—recovery routines that keep you from burning out in the first place.

Here's what high performers know: Resilience isn't just about bouncing back. It's about not getting depleted in the first place.

**RECOVERY ROUTINE #1: MORNING RITUAL (10 minutes)**

Your morning sets your tone for the entire day. Don't leave it to chance.

• **5 minutes of movement** - stretching, walking, yoga, or exercise
• **3 minutes of stillness** - meditation, breathing, or quiet reflection
• **2 minutes of intention** - set 3 goals for the day

Total investment: 10 minutes
Return: Calm focus, clarity, sustained energy

---

**RECOVERY ROUTINE #2: EVENING SHUTDOWN**

Create a boundary between work and rest.

• **Stop working 1 hour before sleep** - no email, no tasks
• **Transition activity** - read, stretch, connect with family
• **Reflect on the day** - 3 wins, 1 lesson, tomorrow's priority
• **Prepare for sleep** - dark room, cool temperature, no screens

Total investment: 1 hour
Return: Better sleep, faster recovery, mental clarity

---

**RECOVERY ROUTINE #3: MICRO-BREAKS THROUGHOUT THE DAY**

Schedule 5-minute breaks every 90 minutes.

• Step away from your workspace
• Move your body (stretch, walk, shake it out)
• Connect with someone (or nature, or a pet)
• Do something restorative (not just scrolling)

Total investment: 40 minutes/day
Return: Sustained focus, reduced stress, better decisions

---

**RECOVERY ROUTINE #4: WEEKLY REVIEW**

Every Sunday evening, review your week:

• What drained my energy?
• what energized me?
• What did I tolerate that I shouldn't have?
• What's one thing I'll do differently next week?

Total investment: 30 minutes
Return: Intentional living, continuous improvement

**BUILDING YOUR RESILIENCE SUPPORT NETWORK:**

**TIER 1: INNER CIRCLE (3-5 people)**
Who they are: Close friends, family, partner

What they provide: Emotional safety, unconditional support, honest feedback

**TIER 2: PEER SUPPORT (2-3 people)**
Who they are: Colleagues, mentors, people who "get it"

What they provide: Professional guidance, shared experience, practical advice

Your action: Schedule a regular check-in (weekly or biweekly) with one peer.

---

**TIER 3: PROFESSIONAL SUPPORT (1-2 people)**
Who they are: Therapist, coach, mentor

What they provide: Expert guidance, accountability, structured growth

Your action: If you don't have professional support, consider finding someone. It's an investment, not an expense.
