## 📝 Content Type Voice Guidelines

### For PDF Guides

**Tone:** Comprehensive + Strategic
- Mix of "I" (experience) and "you" (guidance)
- Direct instructions with explanations
- Real examples from testing/usage
- Vulnerable sharing: "I made this mistake for months..."

**Example Opening:**
> "After years of testing dozens of productivity systems, I've discovered that most complexity is self-inflicted. This guide breaks down the 5 essential systems that actually move the needle—none of which require buying expensive tools."

### For Templates

**Tone:** Clear + Direct
- Helpful, concise, encouraging
- Step-by-step structure
- Minimal fluff, maximum actionability
- "Do this. Then this." approach

**Example Opening:**
> "You don't need a complex system to track your goals. You need a template that works. Fill in one section at a time, in any order—this template is designed to work with your brain, not against it."

### For Audio Content

**Tone:** Supportive + Engaging
- Warm, conversational style
- Personal stories and vulnerability
- Encouraging without being dismissive
- Reflective questions with action steps

**Example Opening:**
> "Hi, I'm Saithavy. When I first started working remotely, I felt isolated and unproductive. In this audio, I'll share the 3 practices that helped me thrive—and how you can adapt them to your situation."

## 🔧 Content Production Framework

### Rebuild Formula (For Resilience Resources)

Apply this to all **Overcoming Adversity** and **Personal Growth** content:

1. **Truth:** Name the struggle plainly
   > "Imposter syndrome doesn't mean you're a fraud. It means you're growing."

2. **Reframe:** What it's teaching / what it reveals
   > "That feeling? It's evidence you're pushing your boundaries."

3. **Practice:** 1-3 actionable steps
   > "Step 1: Document three wins this week. Step 2: Share one with a trusted peer. Step 3: Repeat."

4. **Anchor:** Calm, confident closing
   > "You're allowed to be a beginner. You don't need to stay there."

### Alignment-to-Action Formula (For Leadership Resources)

Apply this to all **Mindful Leadership** content:

1. **Pattern:** What most people do
   > "Most leaders manage from a place of urgency and reactivity."

2. **Pivot:** What aligned leaders do instead
   > "Mindful leaders create space before responding."

3. **Tool:** Framework/checklist
   > "Use this 3-question pause before your next meeting."

4. **CTA:** One decision to make today
   > "Try this pause in your next 3 meetings. Notice the difference."

### Calm Authority Formula (For How-To Resources)

Apply this to all **AI + Automation** and **Remote Work** content:

1. **Define:** The problem in one sentence
   > "Most remote workers struggle with context switching between deep work and communication."

2. **Explain:** The mechanism (why it happens)
   > "Your brain takes 23 minutes to refocus after an interruption. Constant interruptions = constant shallow work."

3. **Give:** The steps (how to fix it)
   > "Step 1: Schedule deep work blocks. Step 2: mute notifications. Step 3: communicate availability proactively."

4. **Provide:** A simple metric (how to know it's working)
   > "You'll know it's working when you complete 3 hours of focused work in one session."

## 🚫 Quality Gates (What the Voice Never Does)

### For All Resource Content

**AVOID:**
- ❌ Hustle-culture aggression ("grind harder", "push through")
- ❌ Overly corporate language ("leverage synergistic workflows")
- ❌ Mystical vagueness without steps
- ❌ Overpromising ("guaranteed results", "instant transformation")
- ❌ Performative perfection (no rough edges, all polish)

**ENSURE:**
- ✅ Authenticity (real experience, admits imperfections)
- ✅ Sustainability (lasting systems over hustle)
- ✅ Innovation (creative problem-solving)
- ✅ Vulnerability (shares failures alongside successes)
- ✅ Clarity (specific examples, not abstract advice)
