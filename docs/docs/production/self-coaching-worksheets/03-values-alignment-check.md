### PURPOSE

### PERSONAL NOTE FROM THE CREATOR

For years, I thought I valued "success" - so I chased achievements, promotions, and external validation. I burned out repeatedly, wondering why accomplishment felt so empty.

When I finally did a values check-in, I discovered my actual core values: authenticity, connection, growth, and freedom. I'd been living someone else's values.

The hardest part wasn't identifying what mattered - it was admitting how much of my life was built around things that didn't. I had to let go of relationships, opportunities, and even parts of my identity that felt "successful" but weren't aligned.

Here's what nobody tells you: **Living your values will disappoint people.**

Not everyone will understand your choices. Some will think you're being selfish or unrealistic. That's okay. You're not here to live someone else's values.

Every season is training. The misalignment you feel now isn't failure - it's feedback. Your values evolve as you grow. This worksheet isn't a one-time exercise. It's a practice you return to again and again.

**Remember:** Alignment isn't perfection. It's direction. Small corrections compound into transformation.

### VALUES CLARITY PROMPTS

*Use these when you feel stuck or disconnected:*

**When facing a decision:**
"Which option honors my core values most?"

**When feeling drained:**
"Which value am I betraying right now?"

**When feeling confused:**
"What would someone who values [X] do in this situation?"

**When setting goals:**
"Does this goal align with my values or someone else's expectations?"

### VALUES IN DIFFICULT TIMES

**When values conflict:**
- Values clarity doesn't make conflict disappear - it helps you choose which value to honor in THIS moment
- Example: Honesty vs. Kindness → Truth delivered with compassion
- Example: Freedom vs. Security → Strategic choices that create long-term freedom

**When you can't live all your values at once:**
- Choose the ONE that matters most in THIS situation
- Trust that seasons change, and you'll honor others later
