### PERSONAL NOTE FROM THE CREATOR

I spent years trying to manage time instead of energy. I had perfect schedules, color-coded calendars, and productivity systems - but I was constantly exhausted.

The breakthrough came when I tracked my energy for two weeks and discovered:
- I write 3x faster between 8-11am
- Meetings drain me, even if I like the people
- I need 20 minutes of alone time after lunch to function
- Exercise gives me more energy than it costs

Here's the counterintuitive truth: **Rest is productive.**

I used to feel guilty for "wasting time" on walks, naps, or doing nothing. Now I recognize these as energy investments. When I honor my energy patterns, I get more done in less time - and enjoy it more.

Your energy isn't infinite. It's a renewable resource you must manage intentionally. This worksheet helps you stop fighting your natural rhythms and start working WITH them.

**Remember:** You're not a machine. You're a human with biological rhythms, emotional needs, and a nervous system that requires care.

Every season is training - but training requires recovery.

### ENERGY AUDIT QUICK TIPS

**Signs you're running on empty:**
- Irritability over small things
- Difficulty concentrating
- Sleep that doesn't refresh
- relying on caffeine/sugar to function
- Dreading things you usually enjoy
- Physical tension (shoulders, jaw, stomach)

**Quick energy resets (5-15 minutes):**
- 5 minutes outside
- 10 deep breaths
- Drink a glass of water
- Stretch or walk
- Listen to a favorite song
- 5-minute meditation

**Energy vs. Time:**
- Time management: "How much can I fit in?"
- Energy management: "What can I actually sustain?"

---

### DESIGN YOUR DAY AROUND ENERGY

**Energy-first scheduling:**
- **High-energy blocks** → Deep work, creative work, important decisions
- **Medium-energy blocks** → Meetings, calls, administrative work
- **Low-energy blocks** → Email, routine tasks, research
- **Recovery blocks** → Non-negotiable rest

**The rule:** Never schedule high-focus work during low-energy times

---

### ENERGY FOR DIFFERENT SEASONS

**High-growth seasons:**
- Expect temporary energy depletion
- Build in extra recovery time
- Lower standards in other areas
- Communicate your capacity honestly

**Maintenance seasons:**
- Focus on sustainable rhythms
- Rebuild depleted reserves
- Strengthen energy practices
- Prepare for next growth phase

**Recovery seasons:**
- Rest IS the work
- Release guilt about "productivity"
- Focus on healing, not achieving
- Trust that energy will return
