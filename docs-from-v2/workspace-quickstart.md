## **🚀 Quick Start: 10 Critical Fixes**

### **1. Monitor at Eye Level (5 minutes)**
**What:** Top of your screen should be at or slightly below eye level.

**How:**
- Close one eye
- Your open eye should naturally gaze at the **top bezel** of your monitor
- If you're looking down, raise your monitor
- If you're looking up, lower your monitor

**Quick Fix:**
- Laptop: Stack of books, sturdy box, or monitor riser
- Desktop: Adjust stand height or use riser

**Why:** Looking down even 15° adds 30 pounds of pressure on your neck. Do this now.

---

### **2. Monitor at Arm's Length (3 minutes)**
**What:** Monitor should be about 20-26 inches from your eyes.

**How:**
- Sit back in your chair
- Extend your arm
- Your palm should rest comfortably on the screen
- If you can't reach, pull monitor closer
- If you're leaning forward, push monitor back

**Why:** Being too close causes eye strain. Being too far makes you lean forward (neck strain).

---

### **3. Elbows at 90° (5 minutes)**
**What:** Your elbows should be at right angles when typing.

**How:**
- Sit with your elbows relaxed at your sides
- Bend your elbows to 90°
- Adjust chair height until your forearms are parallel to the floor
- Your keyboard should be directly under your hands, no reaching

**Quick Fix:**
- Chair too high? Lower it or use a footrest
- Chair too low? Raise it or add seat cushion
- Desk too high? Raise your chair
- Desk too low? Lower your chair

**Why:** Reaching forward causes shoulder and neck pain. This fixes it immediately.

---

### **4. Lumbar Support (4 minutes)**
**What:** Chair should fill the gap behind your lower back.

**How:**
- Sit all the way back in your chair
- There should be no gap behind your lower back
- Chair should naturally maintain your spine's slight curve

**Quick Fix:**
- No lumbar support? Roll up a towel and place it behind your lower back
- Cheap chair? Buy a lumbar support pillow ($20 on Amazon)

**Why:** Without support, you'll slouch. Slouching causes back pain.

---

### **5. Feet Flat on Floor (3 minutes)**
**What:** Both feet should be flat on the floor, not dangling.

**How:**
- Sit with your back against the chair
- Both feet should be flat on the floor
- Knees should be at 90° (same angle as elbows)

**Quick Fix:**
- Feet don't reach? Use a footrest (box, stool, or buy one)
- Chair too high? Lower it
- Chair too low? Raise it or add seat cushion

**Why:** Dangling feet causes lower back strain. This is an instant fix.

---

### **6. Shoulders Relaxed (2 minutes)**
**What:** Your shoulders should be loose, not hunched up.

**How:**
- Drop your shoulders
- They should feel relaxed, not tense
- If your chair has armrests, adjust them so your shoulders stay relaxed

**Quick Fix:**
- Shoulders hunched? Raise your chair or lower your desk
- Armrests too high? Lower them or don't use them while typing

**Why:** Hunched shoulders cause neck and upper back pain.

---

### **7. Wrists Neutral (3 minutes)**
**What:** Your wrists should be straight, not bent up or down.

**How:**
- Float your hands above the keyboard
- Don't rest your wrists on the desk or wrist rest while typing
- Your hands should form a straight line from your elbow to your knuckles

**Quick Fix:**
- Wrist rest? Use it only when pausing, not while typing
- Laptop? Use external keyboard and mouse

**Why:** Bent wrists cause carpal tunnel. This prevents it.

---

### **8. Lighting Check (3 minutes)**
**What:** No glare on your screen, adequate ambient light.

**How:**
- Look at your screen
- Do you see windows or lights reflected? That's glare
- Move your monitor or adjust blinds to eliminate glare

**Quick Fix:**
- Glare from window? Move monitor so window is to the side, not behind or in front
- Too dark? Add a lamp to the side of your monitor
- Too bright? Close blinds slightly

**Why:** Glare causes eye strain and headaches.

---

### **9. Set Movement Timer (2 minutes)**
**What:** Remind yourself to move every 30-60 minutes.

**How:**
- Set a timer on your phone for 45 minutes
- When it goes off, stand up and move for 2 minutes
- Repeat throughout the day

**Quick Fix:**
- Use your phone's built-in timer
- Use a Pomodoro app (Tomato Timer, Forest)
- Use calendar reminders

**Why:** Sitting still is terrible for your body. Movement breaks prevent injury.

---

### **10. Water at Your Desk (1 minute)**
**What:** Keep water within arm's reach.

**How:**
- Fill a water bottle or glass
- Put it within arm's reach of your desk
- Drink from it throughout the day

**Quick Fix:**
- Any water bottle or glass works
- Preferably one with a lid (prevents spills)

**Why:** Dehydration causes headaches, fatigue, and brain fog.

## **🚨 What Pain Means**

**Neck pain:** Monitor too low or too far
**Shoulder pain:** Reaching forward, elbows not at 90°
**Upper back pain:** Hunched shoulders, poor lighting (leaning forward)
**Lower back pain:** No lumbar support, feet not flat, slouching
**Wrist pain:** Bent wrists, reaching for mouse
**Eye strain:** Screen too close, glare, poor lighting

**Don't ignore pain. It's your body telling you something is wrong.**

## **🆘 Quick Troubleshooting**

**"I can't get my monitor high enough"**
- Use more books or a sturdier box
- Buy a monitor riser ($20-30)
- Consider wall-mounting (VESA mount)

**"My chair is terrible"**
- Add lumbar support pillow ($20)
- Add seat cushion to raise height ($20)
- Plan to upgrade chair first (priority #1)

**"My desk is wrong height"**
- Raise chair and add footrest
- Lower chair and raise monitor
- Build a simple platform (wood + cinder blocks)

**"I use a laptop"**
- Monitor riser is mandatory (non-negotiable)
- External keyboard and mouse are mandatory (non-negotiable)
- Laptop stands are worth every penny
