# Worksheet 5: Courageous Conversation Prep
## For Difficult Conversations That Matter

---

### PURPOSE
This worksheet helps you prepare for and navigate difficult conversations with clarity, compassion, and confidence. Whether it's setting boundaries, giving feedback, addressing conflict, or sharing hard truths - you'll be ready to speak your truth while preserving the relationship.

**Time to complete:** 25-35 minutes
**Best used when:** You need to have a conversation you've been avoiding, or you're preparing for a talk that feels high-stakes

### PART 2: PREPARE YOUR TRUTH (10 minutes)

**6. Use the SBI Model to prepare what you'll say:**

**S - Situation (When/Where):**
"The specific situation is..."
*(Stick to facts, not interpretations. "In Tuesday's meeting," not "When you attacked me")*


<br>
<br>

**B - Behavior (What happened):**
"The specific behavior was..."
*(Observable actions, not judgments. "You spoke over me three times," not "You were disrespectful")*


<br>
<br>

**I - Impact (How it affected you):**
"The impact was..."
*(Your experience, not accusations. "I felt unheard and withdrew from the conversation," not "You made me feel small")*


<br>
<br>

**7. What's the REQUEST underneath all this?**
*(Not a demand, but a clear ask. "Would you be willing to...?")*


<br>

<br>

**9. What's my part in this?**
*(How have I contributed? What could I have done differently?)*

### PART 7: EMERGENCY SCRIPTS

**If they get defensive:**
"I can see this feels difficult. I'm not trying to attack you - I want to work through this together. Can we slow down?"

<br>

**If they shut down:**
"I notice you've gone quiet. I know this is uncomfortable. Take your time. I'm here whenever you're ready."

<br>

**If they attack back:**
"I hear that you're upset. I want to understand your perspective too. Can we take turns sharing so we both feel heard?"

<br>

**If you get overwhelmed:**
"I need a moment. Let's take a 5-minute break and come back to this."

### PERSONAL NOTE FROM THE CREATOR

The hardest conversation I ever had was telling a business partner that our working arrangement wasn't working anymore. I avoided it for months, making excuses, hoping things would magically improve.

They didn't. Resentment built up. The quality of our work suffered. I started dreading every interaction.

When I finally had the conversation, it was messy. They were hurt. I stumbled through my words. It was uncomfortable.

But here's what happened instead of the catastrophe I imagined: We both felt relief. The unspoken tension was finally in the open. We figured out a new arrangement that worked better for both of us.

I learned that day: **The conversation you're avoiding is usually the one you need most.**

The anticipation is almost always worse than the reality. Your mind creates worst-case scenarios that rarely happen. Even when conversations go poorly, you can handle it. You've handled hard things before.

This worksheet captures the preparation process that's helped me navigate hundreds of difficult conversations since then. Not perfectly - but with more clarity, compassion, and courage.

**Remember:** Avoiding conflict doesn't preserve relationships - it erodes them. Honest communication, even when uncomfortable, is the foundation of real connection.

You're allowed to have needs. You're allowed to set boundaries. You're allowed to speak your truth.

### CONVERSATION FRAMEWORKS

**For giving feedback:**
"I notice when you [specific behavior], and the impact is [impact]. Would you be open to [suggestion]?"

**For setting boundaries:**
"I care about [relationship/work], and I need to be clear about [boundary]. Going forward, [specific request]."

**For apologizing:**
"I realize I [specific behavior], and I see that it caused [impact]. I want to acknowledge this and [how I'll repair]."

**For receiving feedback:**
"Thank you for telling me. Let me make sure I understand - you're saying [paraphrase back]. Is that right?"

---

### SIGNALS YOU NEED THIS CONVERSATION

- You're ruminating on what you wish you'd said
- You feel resentful but haven't communicated your needs
- You're avoiding someone or certain topics
- You've complained to others but not the person involved
- You feel tense before interacting with them
- The issue keeps coming up in your mind
