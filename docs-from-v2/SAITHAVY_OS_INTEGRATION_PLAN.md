# Brand Voice Application Guide for Resources

## Purpose
Ensure all 62 resources consistently reflect Saithavy brand voice across personality traits, tone sliders, and signature lines.

## Brand Voice Quick Reference

### Core Personality Traits
- **Empathetic:** "I know how overwhelming... here's a template that breaks it down"
- **Insightful:** "After testing dozens of... I've discovered these patterns"
- **Supportive:** "You're not alone in struggling... this worksheet will help"
- **Confident:** "I've used this framework with 50+ clients... here's what works"
- **Visionary:** "Imagine what's possible when your systems work seamlessly"

### Tone Sliders by Category

**Mindful Leadership:**
- Range: Reflective → Tactical (middle)
- Key phrases: "Lead yourself first", "Your presence is your primary tool"

**AI + Automation:**
- Range: Practical → Visionary (slightly tactical)
- Key phrases: "Turn struggle into system", "Innovation isn't about new tools"

**Personal Growth:**
- Range: Soft → Direct (emotional first, then action)
- Key phrases: "You're allowed to start small", "Every season is training"

**Remote Work:**
- Range: Direct → Pragmatic (action-oriented)
- Key phrases: "Build a life that can hold your ambition", "Systems before willpower"

**Overcoming Adversity:**
- Range: Reflective → Soft (empathy-first)
- Key phrases: "If you're overwhelmed, that's not a character flaw", "Adversity reveals what you're made of"

## Voice Application Process

### Step 1: Identify Resource Category
- Determine which of 5 categories the resource belongs to
- Note the corresponding tone slider range
- Select appropriate signature lines

### Step 2: Choose Brand Voice Formula

**Rebuild Formula** (for adversity/growth resources)
1. Truth: Name the struggle plainly
2. Reframe: What it's teaching/revealing
3. Practice: 1-3 actionable steps
4. Anchor: Calm, confident closing

**Alignment-to-Action Formula** (for leadership resources)
1. Pattern: What most people do
2. Pivot: What aligned leaders do instead
3. Tool: Framework/checklist
4. CTA: One decision to make today

**Calm Authority Formula** (for how-to resources)
1. Define: The problem in one sentence
2. Explain: The mechanism (why it happens)
3. Give: The steps (how to fix)
4. Provide: A simple metric (how to know it's working)

### Step 3: Apply to Resource Content

**Introduction:**
- Use formula structure for opening
- Incorporate 2-3 personality traits
- Include signature line
- Keep to 100-150 words

**Body Content:**
- Maintain consistent tone throughout
- Use second-person perspective ("you")
- Balance empathy with authority
- Include real examples/stories

**Conclusion:**
- Reinforce key message
- Add encouraging closing
- Include call-to-action
- End with signature line

### Step 4: Quality Check

**Voice Consistency Checklist:**
- [ ] Empathetic to challenges?
- [ ] Insightful from experience?
- [ ] Supportive and encouraging?
- [ ] Confident in what works?
- [ ] Visionary about possibilities?

**Quality Gates:**
- [ ] No hustle-culture aggression
- [ ] No overly corporate language
- [ ] No mystical vagueness without steps
- [ ] No overpromising results
- [ ] Authenticity present (real experience shared)

## Examples by Resource Type

### Workbook Example: Mindful Leadership Reflection Journal

**Opening (Alignment-to-Action Formula):**
"Most leaders manage from a place of urgency and reactivity, constantly putting out fires instead of building systems that prevent them. I spent years in this cycle, feeling like I was always one step behind.

Mindful leaders create space before responding. They pause, reflect, then act with intention. This 30-day journal contains the exact prompts that transformed my leadership from reactive to intentional. Your presence is your primary leadership tool."

**Tone:** Reflective → Tactical
**Traits:** Insightful (shares experience), Supportive (offers tool), Visionary (presence as tool)
**Signature Line:** "Your presence is your primary leadership tool"

### Checklist Example: Remote Onboarding Checklist

**Opening (Calm Authority Formula):**
"Most remote teams struggle with new hire integration because they lack structured onboarding processes. Your brain takes 23 minutes to refocus after an interruption, and poor onboarding creates constant interruptions for everyone.

This checklist covers the 15 elements that create seamless remote onboarding. When followed, new team members reach productivity 2x faster and managers save 5+ hours per week in avoidable questions. Systems before willpower."

**Tone:** Direct → Pragmatic
**Traits:** Confident (cites data), Insightful (knows the mechanism), Practical (actionable)
**Signature Line:** "Systems before willpower"
