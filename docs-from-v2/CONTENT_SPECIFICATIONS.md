## 1. Universal Content Standards

### Tone and Voice

**Overall Tone:**
- Empowering and encouraging
- Clear and actionable
- Professional yet accessible
- Supportive and understanding
- Practical over theoretical

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Second-person perspective ("you")
- Active voice (avoid passive)
- Present tense (where applicable)
- Conversational but professional
- Inclusive and respectful

**Language Level:**
- Reading grade level: 8th-10th grade
- Avoid jargon (or explain it clearly)
- Simple sentence structure
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Clear headings and subheadings

### Writing Style Guidelines

**Sentence Structure:**
- Average length: 15-20 words
- Maximum: 30 words (rare exceptions)
- One idea per sentence
- Vary sentence length for flow
- Use transitions between paragraphs

**Paragraph Structure:**
- Topic sentence first
- Supporting sentences follow
- 2-4 sentences per paragraph
- One concept per paragraph
- White space between paragraphs

**Headings:**
- H1: Main title (once per document)
- H2: Major sections
- H3: Subsections
- H4: Minor points (rare)
- Clear hierarchy and progression

**Lists:**
- Use numbered lists for sequences
- Use bullet points for options
- Keep list items parallel in structure
- 3-7 items per list
- Introduce lists with clear lead-in

### Formatting Standards

**Typography:**
- Headings: Bold, larger font
- Emphasis: **Bold** for key terms
- Italics: For terms, definitions, examples
- Underline: Use sparingly (links only)
- ALL CAPS: Never (except acronyms)

**White Space:**
- Margins: 1" minimum (0.5" for checklists)
- Line spacing: 1.15-1.5 for body text
- Paragraph spacing: 6-12pt after
- Section breaks: 24-36pt before H2

**Color Usage:**
- Category-specific accent colors
- Consistent color coding
- High contrast for readability
- Color-blind friendly combinations
- Test grayscale conversion

### Accessibility Standards

**WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance:**
- Color contrast: ≥4.5:1 for normal text
- Color contrast: ≥3:1 for large text (18pt+)
- Alt text: All images and graphics
- Headings: Logical structure (H1→H2→H3)
- Links: Descriptive text (not "click here")
- Tables: Row and column headers
- Lists: Proper markup (not visual only)

**Language Accessibility:**
- Simple, clear language
- Explain acronyms first use
- Define technical terms
- Provide examples for abstract concepts
- Avoid idioms and cultural references

### Brand Consistency

**Brand Voice:**
- Empowering: "You can do this"
- Encouraging: "Here's how to succeed"
- Clear: "Follow these steps"
- Professional: "Based on research"
- Inclusive: "Whatever your situation"

**Preferred Terminology:**
- Use: "Mindfulness practice" instead of "Meditation"
- Use: "Remote work" instead of "WFH"
- Use: "Team members" instead of "Employees"
- Use: "Challenges" instead of "Problems"
- Use: "Opportunities" instead of "Issues"

**Avoid:**
- Jargon and buzzwords
- Clichés and overused phrases
- Gender-specific language (use "they" singular)
- Absolute statements ("always", "never")
- Judgmental language ("should", "must" - use "consider", "try")

## 2. PDF Specifications

### Document Structure

**Standard PDF Structure (8-12 sections):**

1. **Cover Page**
   - Resource title
   - Category icon/branding
   - Version number
   - Copyright notice
   - Visual element (category-specific)

2. **Table of Contents**
   - All sections and subsections
   - Page numbers
   - Clickable links (if digital)

3. **Introduction**
   - What this resource is
   - Who it's for
   - How to use it
   - What you'll get out of it
   - Time required to complete

4. **Main Content Sections**
   - Organized by theme/topic
   - Clear section breaks
   - Progressive difficulty (basic → advanced)
   - Practical examples throughout

5. **Exercises/Worksheets** (if applicable)
   - Clear instructions
   - Fillable fields
   - Example responses
   - Space for reflection
   - Action items

6. **Key Takeaways**
   - Summary of main points
   - Action items list
   - Next steps
   - Related resources

7. **Resources Section**
   - Recommended tools
   - Further reading
   - Helpful links
   - Citations (if applicable)

8. **About the Author**
   - Brief bio
   - Credentials
   - Contact information
   - Website/social media

9. **Copyright & License**
   - Copyright notice
   - License terms
   - Attribution requirements
   - Disclaimer (if applicable)

10. **Appendix** (optional)
    - Extended examples
    - Additional exercises
    - Reference materials
    - Templates

### Page Layout Specifications

**Page Size:**
- Primary: US Letter (8.5" x 11")
- Secondary: A4 (210mm × 297mm)
- Margins: 0.75" (sides), 1" (top/bottom)

**Grid System:**
- Columns: 2-3 column grid
- Gutter: 0.25" between columns
- Baseline grid: 12pt or 14pt
- Alignment: Left-aligned text

**Typography:**
- **Headings:**
  - H1: 32-40pt, bold, category color
  - H2: 24-28pt, bold, category color
  - H3: 18-20pt, bold, dark gray
  - H4: 14-16pt, bold, dark gray

- **Body Text:**
  - Font size: 11-12pt
  - Line height: 1.4-1.5
  - Font: Inter, Open Sans, or similar
  - Color: #1B263B (dark navy)

- **Captions/Notes:**
  - Font size: 9-10pt
  - Line height: 1.3
  - Style: Italic
  - Color: #5E6472 (medium gray)

**Visual Elements:**
- Images: 300 DPI minimum
- Charts: Clear labels, category colors
- Icons: Lucide or similar (consistent style)
- Dividers: Category accent color
- Callouts: Light background, colored border

### Content Length Guidelines

**By Resource Type:**

**Simple Guides (5-8 pages):**
- Word count: 1,500-2,500 words
- Sections: 4-6 main sections
- Exercises: 2-3 simple exercises
- Examples: 2-3 brief examples
- Production time: 6-12 hours

**Standard Guides (8-12 pages):**
- Word count: 2,500-4,000 words
- Sections: 6-8 main sections
- Exercises: 3-5 exercises
- Examples: 4-6 detailed examples
- Production time: 12-18 hours

**Comprehensive Workbooks (15-30 pages):**
- Word count: 4,000-8,000 words
- Sections: 8-12 main sections
- Exercises: 8-12 exercises
- Examples: 8-12 examples
- Worksheets: 5-8 pages
- Production time: 20-30 hours

### Content Sections by Type

**Self-Reflection/Journal Workbooks:**
- Introduction to the practice
- Guided reflection prompts
- Journaling exercises
- Progress tracking pages
- Weekly review templates
- Goal-setting worksheets
- Habit tracking calendars
- Celebration pages

**Educational/Guidance PDFs:**
- Learning objectives
- Core concepts (3-5)
- Step-by-step instructions
- Real-world examples
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Pro tips and best practices
- FAQ section
- Next steps/resources

**Framework/Methodology PDFs:**
- Theoretical foundation
- Framework overview diagram
- Step-by-step process
- Decision trees
- Case studies
- Implementation guide
- Troubleshooting section
- Success stories

### Interactive Elements

**Fillable Fields:**
- Clear visual indicators (underlines, boxes)
- Sufficient space for writing (0.25" minimum)
- Instructions in italics
- Example text in gray
- Consistent formatting

**Checkboxes:**
- Size: 12pt square minimum
- Clear borders
- Sufficient spacing (0.15" between items)
- Grouped by category
- Progress indicators

**Progress Trackers:**
- Visual progress bars
- Milestone markers
- Celebration pages
- Date tracking
- Notes sections

### File Specifications

**Technical Requirements:**
- **Format:** PDF/A-1b or PDF/A-2b (ISO standard)
- **Compatibility:** Adobe Reader X or later
- **File Size:** <10MB (optimize if larger)
- **Color Mode:** RGB for digital, CMYK for print
- **Resolution:** 300 DPI for images

**Metadata:**
- Title: Resource title
- Author: Your company name
- Subject: Category and type
- Keywords: 5-7 relevant tags
- Creation Date: Current date

**Optimization:**
- Compress images without quality loss
- Subset fonts (embed only used characters)
- Remove unnecessary layers
- Optimize PDF version compatibility
- Test on multiple devices

## 3. Template Specifications

### Template Types

**Spreadsheet Templates (Google Sheets/Excel):**
- Budget trackers
- Project planners
- Habit trackers
- Goal-setting canvases
- Audit checklists
- Calculation tools

**Document Templates (Google Docs/Word):**
- Worksheets
- Journals
- Plans
- Agendas
- Reports
- Contracts

**Digital Templates (Notion/Airtable):**
- Databases
- Kanban boards
- Calendars
- Note-taking systems
- Project trackers

### Spreadsheet Template Structure

**Standard Layout:**
- **Header Row:** Frozen, bold, category color background
- **Column Widths:** Auto-fit where possible, min 100px
- **Row Height:** Auto-fit, min 40px for data rows
- **Frozen Panes:** Top row and first column (if applicable)

**Color Coding:**
- **Headers:** Category accent color background
- **Input Cells:** Light yellow background (#FFF9C4)
- **Calculated Cells:** Light green background (#C8E6C9)
- **Required Fields:** Light red background (#FFCDD2)
- **Example Data:** Gray text (#9E9E9E)

**Cell Formatting:**
- **Text:** Left-align
- **Numbers:** Right-align, 2 decimals (currency)
- **Dates:** MM/DD/YYYY format
- **Percentages:** 0 decimal places
- **Boolean:** Checkboxes or drop-down

**Formulas:**
- **Error Handling:** IFERROR() for all calculations
- **Validation:** Data validation for drop-downs
- **Conditional Formatting:** Visual feedback for thresholds
- **Named Ranges:** For complex formulas
- **Comments:** For complex logic

**Protection:**
- **Instructions:** Sheet with locked instructions
- **Input Areas:** Unlocked cells
- **Formulas:** Locked and protected
- **Structure:** Locked rows/columns
- **Warnings:** If invalid data entered

### Document Template Structure

**Standard Layout:**
- **Title Page:** Resource name, instructions
- **Section Pages:** Dividers with clear titles
- **Content Pages:** Structured content areas
- **Footer:** Page numbers, copyright

**Formatting:**
- **Styles:** Use consistent paragraph styles
- **Headings:** H1-H6 defined styles
- **Body:** Normal style
- **Instructions:** Italics, distinct color
- **Placeholders:** [BRACKETS] and underlined

**Sections:**
1. **Overview/Instructions**
   - Purpose statement
   - How to use this template
   - Tips for best results
   - Example section

2. **Main Content Area**
   - Structured sections
   - Clear headings
   - Guiding questions
   - Example content (removable)

3. **Action Items/Next Steps**
   - To-do list format
   - Priority indicators
   - Due dates
   - Owner assignment

### Template Components

**Common Elements:**

**Date Fields:**
- Format: MM/DD/YYYY
- Validation: Valid dates only
- Default: Today's date (dynamic)
- Placeholder: [Date]

**Text Fields:**
- Size: Appropriate for content (short, medium, long)
- Validation: Text length limits
- Placeholder: [Your answer here]
- Instructions: Gray helper text

**Dropdown Menus:**
- Options: 3-10 items
- Default: "Select..." or first option
- Validation: List only
- Color coding: By category

**Checkboxes:**
- Size: Large enough to click easily
- Grouping: Related items grouped
- Default: Unchecked
- Labels: Clear and descriptive

**Calculations:**
- Formulas: Hidden or protected
- Results: Highlighted visually
- Rounding: 2 decimal places
- Units: Clearly labeled ($, %, etc.)

### Template Instructions

**Instruction Style:**
- Write in second person ("You will...")
- Use action verbs ("Enter...", "Select...", "Choose...")
- Be concise (1-2 sentences)
- Provide examples
- Use formatting (bold, color) for emphasis

**Instruction Placement:**
- Top of document/spreadsheet
- Before each section
- In comments/notes
- In example cells
- In separate instruction sheet

**Example Sections:**
- Clearly labeled "EXAMPLE"
- Realistic data
- Related to use case
- Easy to understand
- Removable

## 4. Guide Specifications

### Guide Structure

**Standard Guide Outline (7-9 sections):**

1. **Title Page**
   - Catchy title
   - Subtitle (benefit-driven)
   - Estimated time to read
   - Difficulty level
   - Category badge

2. **Introduction** (150-250 words)
   - Hook: Problem statement
   - Promise: What you'll learn
   - Target audience: Who this is for
   - Prerequisites: What you need before starting

3. **Quick Overview** (100-150 words)
   - 3-5 key takeaways (bullet points)
   - Time investment
   - Difficulty level (Beginner/Intermediate/Advanced)
   - Tools needed (if any)

4. **Main Content** (1,500-4,000 words total)
   - **Section 1: Foundation** (300-500 words)
     - Core concepts
     - Context and background
     - Why this matters

   - **Section 2: Step-by-Step** (500-1,000 words)
     - Numbered steps (5-10 steps)
     - Clear instructions
     - Examples for each step
     - Screenshots/diagrams

   - **Section 3: Pro Tips** (300-500 words)
     - Best practices
     - Common mistakes
     - Expert insights
     - Time-savers

   - **Section 4: Real-World Example** (300-500 words)
     - Case study
     - Before/After
     - Results achieved
     - Key lessons

5. **Implementation Checklist** (100-200 words)
   - Action items (5-10 items)
   - Priority order
   - Time estimates
   - Success criteria

6. **FAQ** (200-400 words)
   - 3-7 common questions
   - Clear, concise answers
   - Links to resources

7. **Next Steps** (100-150 words)
   - Related resources
   - Further reading
   - Action items
   - Call-to-action

### Content Depth by Type

**Quick Start Guides (1,500-2,000 words):**
- Focus: Immediate action
- Sections: 4-5 main sections
- Depth: Surface level, actionable
- Examples: 2-3 brief examples
- Time to read: 5-8 minutes
- Best for: Beginners, quick wins

**Comprehensive Guides (2,500-4,000 words):**
- Focus: Thorough understanding
- Sections: 6-8 main sections
- Depth: Detailed, nuanced
- Examples: 4-6 detailed examples
- Time to read: 10-15 minutes
- Best for: Practitioners, implementation

**Deep Dive Guides (4,000-6,000 words):**
- Focus: Mastery
- Sections: 8-12 main sections
- Depth: Expert-level detail
- Examples: 8+ case studies
- Time to read: 20-30 minutes
- Best for: Advanced users, professionals

### Visual Elements

**Screenshots:**
- **Frequency:** 1 per 500 words (minimum)
- **Format:** PNG with transparency
- **Resolution:** 1920x1080 or higher
- **Annotations:** Callouts, arrows, highlights
- **Consistency:** Same style throughout

**Diagrams:**
- **Purpose:** Illustrate processes, concepts
- **Style:** Clean, modern, category colors
- **Format:** SVG or high-res PNG
- **Labels:** Clear, readable (12pt+)
- **Legend:** If multiple elements

**Callout Boxes:**
- **Purpose:** Highlight key information
- **Style:** Light background, colored border
- **Types:** Tips, Warnings, Examples, Quotes
- **Icons:** Consistent icon system
- **Placement:** Relevant to content

**Tables:**
- **Purpose:** Organize information
- **Style:** Clean rows, alternating colors
- **Headers:** Bold, category color
- **Alignment:** Left-align text, right-align numbers
- **Captions:** Above or below

### Writing Style

**Introduction:**
- Start with hook (problem or question)
- State benefit clearly
- Set expectations
- Build credibility
- Keep it concise (150-250 words)

**Step Instructions:**
- Use imperative mood ("Click...", "Enter...", "Select...")
- Number each step
- One action per step
- Be specific and precise
- Include expected outcomes
- Add screenshots for complex steps

**Examples:**
- Real-world scenarios
- Before/After comparisons
- Specific metrics/results
- Relatable situations
- diverse perspectives

**Tips and Best Practices:**
- Actionable advice
- Expert insights
- Time-saving shortcuts
- Common pitfalls to avoid
- Pro techniques

## 5. Audio Specifications

### Audio Content Structure

**Standard Audio Structure:**

1. **Intro** (20-30 seconds)
   - Welcome and greeting
   - What this session is about
   - What you'll get out of it
   - How to use this audio
   - Background music: Upbeat, fades out

2. **Session Overview** (30-60 seconds)
   - Session agenda
   - Key topics covered
   - Expected duration
   - Any materials needed
   - Background music: Subtle, fades

3. **Main Content** (Varies by type)
   - **For Guided Practices:**
     - Introduction to practice (1-2 min)
     - Step-by-step guidance (main portion)
     - Pause for reflection (30-60 seconds)
     - Return to guidance
     - Closing (1 min)

   - **For Instructional:**
     - Concept explanation (2-3 min)
     - Real-world examples (2-3 min)
     - Action steps (2-3 min)
     - Implementation tips (1-2 min)

   - **For Stories:**
     - Setup and context (1-2 min)
     - Challenge/struggle (2-3 min)
     - Turning point (1-2 min)
     - Resolution and lessons (2-3 min)

4. **Key Takeaways** (1-2 minutes)
   - Recap main points (3-5 items)
   - Action items
   - How to apply learning

5. **Outro** (30-60 seconds)
   - Encouragement
   - Next steps
   - Related resources
   - Call-to-action
   - Background music: Returns, fades out

### Technical Specifications

**Audio Quality:**
- **Sample Rate:** 44.1kHz (CD quality) or 48kHz
- **Bit Depth:** 16-bit or 24-bit
- **Bitrate:** 128-192 kbps (MP3), 1411 kbps (WAV)
- **Channels:** Stereo (preferred) or Mono (acceptable for voice)
- **Format:** MP3 (distribution) + WAV (master)

**Recording Standards:**
- **Voice:** Clear, centered, consistent volume
- **No Background Noise:** Silent recording environment
- **No Clipping:** Peak levels at -6dB to -3dB
- **Consistency:** Same mic distance throughout
- **Pacing:** Natural, conversational (140-160 words/minute)

**Music and Sound Effects:**
- **Background Music:** -20dB to -15dB below voice
- **Genre:** Ambient, instrumental, category-appropriate
- **Transitions:** Fade in/out (2-3 seconds)
- **Sound Effects:** Minimal, purposeful
- **Licensing:** Epidemic Sound, AudioJungle, or CC0

**File Specifications:**
- **Format:** MP3 for distribution, WAV for master
- **File Size:** <50MB per episode
- **Metadata:** ID3 tags populated
- **Naming:** [category]-[resource-name]-v[version].mp3
- **Duration:** 5-30 minutes optimal

### Content Types

**Guided Meditations (5-15 minutes):**
- Calming voice, slow pace
- Clear instructions for each step
- Pause for reflection (30-60 seconds)
- Background music: Ambient, soft
- Structure: Intro → Practice → Reflection → Outro

**Coaching Sessions (10-20 minutes):**
- Conversational tone
- Stories and examples
- Actionable advice
- Energy: Encouraging, motivating
- Structure: Problem → Context → Advice → Action → Encouragement

**Instructional Content (10-30 minutes):**
- Educational focus
- Clear explanations
- Examples and analogies
- Pacing: Moderate (allowing comprehension)
- Structure: Concept → Examples → Steps → Tips → Summary

**Audio Stories (10-20 minutes):**
- Narrative style
- Emotional engagement
- Vivid descriptions
- Voice acting (different tones for characters)
- Structure: Setup → Struggle → Turning Point → Resolution → Lessons

### Script Writing

**Script Format:**
```
[Intro Music: 5 seconds, fades under]

HOST (enthusiastic):
"Welcome to [resource name]. I'm [name], and today we're exploring [topic].

By the end of this session, you'll [specific outcome]. All you need is [prerequisites].

Let's get started."

[Music fades out]

[Section 1: Topic Introduction]
HOST:
"So, what is [topic]? Simply put, [explanation].

Think of it this way: [analogy or metaphor].

Here's why this matters: [reason and benefit]."

[Background music: Subtle, -20dB]

[Section 2: Main Content]
HOST:
"Now, let's dive into the [main point].

First, [step 1].

Then, [step 2].

Finally, [step 3]."

[Pause for reflection: 30 seconds of music]

HOST:
"Take a moment to reflect on [question].

[Pause 5 seconds]

How did that feel? [Validation]."

[Section 3: Key Takeaways]
HOST:
"To recap, here are the key points from today:

Number one: [takeaway 1]

Number two: [takeaway 2]

Number three: [takeaway 3]"

[Outro music: Swells, -15dB]

HOST:
"Thanks for listening. If you found this helpful, check out [related resource].

Until next time, [encouraging closing statement]."

[Outro music: Fades to silence over 5 seconds]
```

### Voice Performance

**Characteristics:**
- **Tone:** Warm, friendly, professional
- **Pacing:** Natural, conversational (140-160 WPM)
- **Energy:** Matched to content type
  - Meditation: Calm, slow
  - Coaching: Energetic, enthusiastic
  - Instructional: Clear, moderate pace
- **Articulation:** Clear, enunciated
- **Emotion:** Appropriate to content

**Microphone Technique:**
- **Distance:** 6-12 inches from mic
- **Position:** Slightly off-axis (prevents plosives)
- **Consistency:** Maintain distance throughout
- **Environment:** Quiet, no echo
- **Posture:** Sit/stand comfortably

**Recording Tips:**
- Do practice runs before recording
- Take breaks every 15-20 minutes
- Stay hydrated (water at room temp)
- Record in segments (easier to edit)
- Save multiple takes for best sections

### Post-Production

**Editing Process:**
1. **Import** raw recording into editing software
2. **Clean up** breaths, clicks, mistakes
3. **Normalize** audio to -16 LUFS
4. **Add** music and sound effects
5. **Mix** voice and music (voice -6dB to -3dB)
6. **Fade** music in/out (2-3 seconds)
7. **Export** to MP3 (128-192 kbps) and WAV (master)

**Quality Checks:**
- Listen on multiple devices (phone, headphones, speakers)
- Check for clipping or distortion
- Verify smooth transitions
- Test at different volumes
- Get feedback from others

**Transcription:**
- Required for accessibility
- Create time-stamped transcript
- Include speaker labels
- Note sound effects in [brackets]
- Save as TXT or PDF

### Metadata and Tagging

**ID3 Tags:**
- **Title:** Resource name
- **Artist:** Your company name
- **Album:** Category name
- **Track Number:** Order in series (if applicable)
- **Genre:** Spoken Word, Education, or Self-Help
- **Year:** Current year
- **Comments:** Brief description
- **Cover Art:** 1400x1400px (square)
